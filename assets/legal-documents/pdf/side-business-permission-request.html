4. Legal Framework

4.1 Article 7:653a BW: Prohibition on Ancillary Activities Clauses

Since 1 August 2022, Article 7:653a of the Dutch Civil Code (Burgerlijk Wetboek, "BW") provides that a clause prohibiting an employee from performing work for others outside working hours is null and void, unless the employer can demonstrate an objective justification for the restriction. This provision implements the EU Directive on Transparent and Predictable Working Conditions (2019/1152) and reflects the principle that employees have the right to work outside their contracted hours.

An employer may only restrict an employee's ancillary activities where there is an objective justification, such as: - Health and safety concerns (e.g., Working Time Act compliance); - Protection of business confidentiality; - Avoidance of genuine conflicts of interest; - Compliance with integrity rules.

I believe none of these grounds appear to apply in the current circumstances. FutureAtoms has no revenue, no clients, and operates in a different product space than Axelera's core business. The proposed conditions set out in Section 3 above further eliminate any conceivable ground for concern.

4.4 Good Employer Practices (Goed Werkgeverschap)

Article 7:611 BW imposes on employers the obligation to conduct themselves as a goed werkgever (good employer). In the context of side activity requests, this means:

  1. Proportionality: Any restriction on side activities must be proportionate to the employer's legitimate interest. Given that FutureAtoms operates in a non-competing domain with zero revenue, a blanket refusal would be disproportionate.
  2. Equal treatment: Permitting side activities for some employees while denying them to others without objective distinction violates the goed werkgeverschap obligation.
  3. Transparency: The employer must provide clear, objective reasons for any refusal, as required by both Art. 7:611 BW and Art. 7:653a BW.
