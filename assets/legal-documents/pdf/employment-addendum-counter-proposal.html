PART A: SIDE BUSINESS PERMISSION

(Deel A: Toestemming Nevenactiviteiten)

Article A1: Disclosure and Approval of Side Business (Openbaarmaking en Goedkeuring Nevenactiviteiten)

A1.1. Employee hereby formally discloses that he owns and operates the sole proprietorship (eenmanszaak) FutureAtoms, registered with the Kamer van Koophandel under number KvK: 99153289 (hereinafter: "FutureAtoms").

A1.2. FutureAtoms' principal activity is the development, licensing, and commercialization of ChipOS, a vendor-neutral agentic orchestration platform for semiconductor design workflows, as further defined in Article A4.

A1.3. Employer hereby grants Employee written approval (schriftelijke toestemming) to continue operating FutureAtoms and to continue developing, marketing, licensing, and commercializing ChipOS and related products, subject to the conditions set forth in this Addendum.

A1.4. This approval shall remain in effect for the duration of the employment relationship unless revoked in writing for cause, provided that revocation shall only be permissible in the event of a material breach of the conditions set forth in this Part A that remains uncured after a written notice period of thirty (30) calendar days.

A1.5. Any restriction on side activities must comply with the requirements of Article 7:653a BW, which provides that an ancillary activities restriction (nevenwerkzaamhedenbeding) may only be invoked if justified by objective reasons (objectieve rechtvaardiging).

Article A2: Separation of Activities (Scheiding van Activiteiten)

A2.1. Employee undertakes that all work for FutureAtoms, including but not limited to development, marketing, customer engagement, and administrative tasks, shall be performed exclusively outside of Employee's working hours (buiten werktijd) as defined in the employment agreement and any applicable working time arrangements.

A2.2. Employee shall not use any of the following for FutureAtoms activities: - (a) Employer's equipment, hardware, software licenses, or infrastructure (apparatuur, hardware, softwarelicenties of infrastructuur); - (b) Employer's networks, servers, cloud environments, or development environments (netwerken, servers, cloudomgevingen of ontwikkelomgevingen); - (c) Employer's confidential information or trade secrets (vertrouwelijke informatie of bedrijfsgeheimen) as defined in the employment agreement and the Dutch Trade Secrets Act (Wet bescherming bedrijfsgeheimen).

A2.3. Employee shall not solicit, approach, or divert any customers, clients, business partners, or prospects of Employer for FutureAtoms business purposes (geen relatiebeding-schending).

A2.4. Employee shall not use any trade secrets (bedrijfsgeheimen) of Employer in the development, marketing, or commercialization of ChipOS, Wingman, or any other FutureAtoms products.

A2.5. Employee shall promptly disclose to Employer any situation that may give rise to a conflict of interest (belangenconflict) between Employee's duties under the employment agreement and FutureAtoms' business activities. Such disclosure shall be made in writing within five (5) business days of Employee becoming aware of the potential conflict.

Article A2.6: Objective Justification of Restrictions (Objectieve Rechtvaardiging Beperkingen)

A2.6. In accordance with Article 7:653a BW, the restrictions set forth in Articles A2.1 through A2.5 are each supported by the following objective justifications:

  • (a) Article A2.1 (outside working hours): justified by the need to ensure adequate time and resource separation between employment duties and side business activities, protecting Employer's legitimate interest in Employee's full availability during contracted working hours;
    • (b) Article A2.2 (no use of Employer resources): justified by the protection of Employer's trade secrets and confidential information (bescherming bedrijfsgeheimen) and the prevention of commingling of business assets;
    • (c) Article A2.3 (no solicitation): justified by the protection of Employer's customer relationships and business goodwill (relatiebescherming);
    • (d) Article A2.4 (no use of trade secrets): justified by the protection of Employer's trade secrets within the meaning of the Dutch Trade Secrets Act (Wet bescherming bedrijfsgeheimen);
    • (e) Article A2.5 (conflict disclosure): justified by the avoidance of conflicts of interest (belangenconflicten) and the duty of good faith inherent in the employment relationship.

Article A3: Intellectual Property Boundary (Intellectueel Eigendom Afbakening)

This Article constitutes the agreed delineation of intellectual property ownership between the Parties.

A3.1. Employer's IP (IE van Werkgever): Pursuant to the "unless otherwise agreed" (tenzij tussen partijen anders is overeengekomen) provision of Article 7 of the Auteurswet and the applicable provisions of Article 12(1) of the Rijksoctrooiwet 1995, the Parties agree that all intellectual property, including but not limited to inventions, works of authorship, software, designs, and know-how, that is: - (a) created in the performance of Employee's employment duties (in de uitoefening van de dienstbetrekking) as described in the employment agreement; and - (b) created using Employer's time, equipment, networks, or resources;

shall be and remain the exclusive property of Employer. The Parties intend this cumulative test (requiring both (a) and (b)) to replace the default statutory rule of Article 7 Auteurswet, which applies the single criterion of creation "in the service of" the employer. This agreed departure is made in accordance with Article 7:610 BW (employment relationship), Article 7 of the Auteurswet (copyright in employment), and Article 12(1) of the Rijksoctrooiwet 1995 (patents in employment).

A3.2. Employee's / FutureAtoms' IP (IE van Werknemer / FutureAtoms): All intellectual property, including but not limited to inventions, works of authorship, software, designs, and know-how, that is: - (a) created outside of Employee's working hours (buiten werktijd); and - (b) created using Employee's own equipment, resources, and infrastructure;

shall be and remain the exclusive property of Employee and/or FutureAtoms. The fact that such intellectual property may relate to a field of activity similar to Employer's business shall not, by itself, alter this classification, provided that the cumulative conditions of Article A3.1(a) and (b) are not met.

A3.3. ChipOS and all its components, modules, source code, documentation, trademarks, and associated intellectual property, as defined in Article A4 below, are hereby acknowledged and confirmed by Employer to be the exclusive intellectual property of Employee / FutureAtoms, having been developed independently of the employment relationship.

A3.4. Testing and Demonstration Exception (Uitzondering voor Testen en Demonstratie): The fact that Employee has tested, demonstrated, or evaluated ChipOS or any FutureAtoms product on Employer's hardware for pilot, evaluation, proof-of-concept, or demonstration purposes shall NOT: - (a) create, transfer, or imply any ownership rights in favor of Employer over ChipOS or any FutureAtoms intellectual property; - (b) constitute creation "in the performance of employment duties" within the meaning of Article 7 Auteurswet or Article 12 Rijksoctrooiwet 1995; - (c) alter the classification of ChipOS or any FutureAtoms product as Employee's / FutureAtoms' IP under Article A3.2.

A3.5. In the event of any dispute regarding the classification of specific intellectual property under this Article A3, the burden of proof that such IP was created in the performance of employment duties using employer time and equipment shall rest with Employer.

A3.6. Mixed-Scenario Clarification (Verduidelijking Gemengde Situaties). In the event that the creation of intellectual property involves elements of both Article A3.1 and Article A3.2 (for example, work performed partly during and partly outside working hours, or using a combination of Employer and Employee resources):

(a) The intellectual property shall be classified as Employer's IP under Article A3.1 only if Employer demonstrates that both cumulative conditions (A3.1(a) and A3.1(b)) are satisfied for the substantial and material part of the creative work. Incidental or de minimis use of Employer time or resources (e.g., a brief test on Employer hardware, reference to a work email) shall not, by itself, satisfy condition A3.1(b).

(b) Where the creative work cannot reasonably be attributed predominantly to either category, the Parties shall negotiate in good faith to reach a fair allocation of rights, taking into account the relative contributions of each Party's time and resources to the creation of the IP.

(c) The burden of proof that the cumulative conditions of Article A3.1 are met for the substantial part of the work shall rest with Employer (consistent with Article A3.5).

Article A4: ChipOS Definition (Definitie ChipOS)

A4.1. For the purposes of this Addendum, "ChipOS" means the vendor-neutral agentic orchestration platform for semiconductor design workflows, developed by Employee through FutureAtoms, maintained in the git repository at github.com/FutureAtoms/ChipOS.git (extensive independent commit history, predominantly by FutureAtoms), including but not limited to: - (a) the core orchestration engine and framework (custom VS Code distribution with FastAPI backend and MCP server); - (b) all application modules, plugins, extensions, and App Packs (including but not limited to the 11 AI-native App Packs for semiconductor design workflows: CPU Development, RTL Verification, RTL Debug, Physical Design, PD Verification, Spec Developer, Hardware Programmer, Voyager SDK, Raspberry Pi, Web Development, and Documentation); - (c) all APIs, interfaces, protocols, and data schemas; - (d) all documentation, specifications, and user guides; - (e) all associated source code, object code, libraries, and dependencies; - (f) the FutureAtoms brand, trademarks, trade names, and associated goodwill; - (g) all derivative works, improvements, and future versions thereof; - (h) all cloud infrastructure, deployment scripts, CI/CD pipelines, and build systems associated with ChipOS.

A4.2. ChipOS is and shall remain the exclusive intellectual property of Employee / FutureAtoms. Employer has no claim, right, title, or interest in ChipOS, except as expressly granted under Part B of this Addendum with respect to the Wingman application only.

A4.3. Nothing in the employment agreement, any prior or subsequent addendum, or any company policy shall be construed to transfer, assign, or license any rights in ChipOS to Employer, except as expressly set forth in Part B.

PART B: WINGMAN DELIVERY

B1.2. The delivered version (Wingman v1.0.0) will be a standalone, self-contained codebase that Employer can operate independently.

B1.3. The version of Wingman subject to this Addendum is version 1.0.0 (hereinafter: "Wingman v1.0.0").

EU Directive 2009/24/EC Art. 1(2), confirmed by CJEU C-406/10, SAS Institute v. WPL: ideas and principles underlying a computer program are not protectable by copyright; applied in Dutch law via richtlijnconforme interpretatie). The Wingman License granted under Article B3 includes a license to use such Shared Development Patterns solely as part of and in connection with Wingman v1.0.0 for the Permitted Purpose.
