5. Why NOT to Switch to Contractor Status

4.1 HSM Permit Is Tied to Employment

  • The HSM (kennismigrant) permit is granted on the basis of employment with a recognized sponsor (erkend referent) under Art. 3.30a Vreemdelingenbesluit 2000.
  • Axelera AI B.V. holds recognized sponsor status with the IND.
  • Switching to self-employment means the legal basis for the HSM permit ceases. In practice, the IND must be notified and will initiate a withdrawal procedure, but the permit does not lapse instantaneously; however, the legal basis is lost and continued residence on the HSM permit becomes precarious.
  • A different permit type (verblijfsvergunning als zelfstandige) is required for self-employed persons.
  • The zelfstandige permit has distinct and much stricter requirements.

4.2 Zelfstandige Permit Requirements

The self-employment residence permit (verblijfsvergunning regulier voor bepaalde tijd onder de beperking 'arbeid als zelfstandige') is assessed by the Netherlands Enterprise Agency (RVO) on whether the applicant's business serves the essential interest of the Dutch economy (wezenlijk Nederlands belang). The RVO assessment considers three broad categories (see IND self-employed residence permit page for the current assessment framework and any applicable scoring methodology):

  • Personal experience (education, entrepreneurial history, income history)
  • Business plan (market analysis, financial projections, viability)
  • Added value to the Dutch economy (innovation, job creation, investment)

Critical problem: FutureAtoms currently has EUR 0 revenue. This makes it very difficult to demonstrate a sustainable, viable business that serves the essential interest of the Dutch economy. While individual circumstances and the strength of a business plan can influence the outcome, a zero-revenue applicant faces a steep uphill assessment and the application would carry a high risk of rejection.

4.3 Gap in Legal Residence

  • During any transition period between permit types, there may be no valid residence basis.
  • The legal basis for the HSM permit ceases when employment ends (though the IND follows a formal withdrawal procedure). The zelfstandige permit has an IND decision period of 90 days (extendable), not including preparation and RVO assessment time.
  • A gap in legal residence poses a serious risk to the naturalization timeline.
  • Depending on duration and circumstances, a gap may reset or delay the 5-year continuous residence clock required for Dutch citizenship under Art. 8(1)(c) Rijkswet op het Nederlanderschap. The IND has some discretion in assessing short interruptions (e.g., brief administrative gaps between permits may be tolerated), but any gap should be avoided.
  • During a gap, the right to work may also be suspended.

4.4 Loss of Financial Benefits

Switching to contractor status means the immediate and permanent loss of:

  • SARs / stock appreciation rights (currently offered as part of compensation)
  • Pension contributions (employer pension scheme)
  • Illness coverage under the Ziektewet (2 years continued salary during illness)
  • Holiday allowance (8% vakantiebijslag, Art. 15 Wet minimumloon en minimumvakantiebijslag (WML))
  • 13th month salary (if applicable under employment contract or CLA)
  • Dutch employment protection (ontslagbescherming under Art. 7:669 BW et seq.)
  • Transition payment (transitievergoeding) upon termination
  • Unemployment benefits eligibility (WW-uitkering)
  • Disability insurance (WIA coverage)

4.5 30% Ruling (30%-regeling)

The 30% ruling (30%-regeling) under Art. 31a(2)(e) Wet op de loonbelasting 1964, as further implemented in the Uitvoeringsregeling loonbelasting 2011 and Belastingdienst guidance, allows qualifying employees recruited from abroad to receive up to 30% of their gross salary as a tax-free allowance, intended to compensate for extraterritorial costs (housing, travel, cost-of-living differences).

Key implications for status change:

  • The 30% ruling is only available to employees; self-employed persons and contractors do not qualify.
  • If the employment relationship with Axelera AI ends (whether by resignation, termination, or switch to contractor status), the 30% ruling lapses unless a new qualifying employment with a different employer is found within three months (the transfer window).
  • If the three-month transfer window expires without new qualifying employment, the remaining entitlement for that period lapses and cannot be reinstated. Note: a new 30% ruling application may be possible with a subsequent qualifying employer, but the elapsed months are not recoverable.

4.6 Schijnzelfstandigheid (False Self-Employment) Risk

  • Under the Wet deregulering beoordeling arbeidsrelaties (Wet DBA), if a contractor works primarily for one client, tax authorities (Belastingdienst) may reclassify the relationship as employment.
  • If Axelera AI is the primary or sole client of FutureAtoms, this risk is very high.
  • Reclassification creates tax liability for both parties: back-payment of payroll taxes, social security contributions, and potential penalties.
  • Enforcement resumed from 1 January 2025 after years of a moratorium. The government announced a transitional approach: 2025 focused on correction and back-taxes rather than fines (geen boetes bij handhaving schijnzelfstandigheid in 2025; see Rijksoverheid announcement 18 December 2024). 2026 is a "halfzachte landing" (semi-soft landing): no verzuimboetes (administrative fines for failure to comply) are imposed; vergrijpboetes (fines for intentional or grossly negligent non-compliance / opzet of grove schuld) are possible from 1 January 2026; back-assessments (naheffingsaanslagen) can be levied back to 1 January 2025. The full penalty regime (including verzuimboetes) is not expected until 2027.
  • The Deliveroo ruling (Hoge Raad, ECLI:NL:HR:2023:443) has broadened the criteria for establishing an employment relationship.

6.2 Naturalization Requirements (Art. 8 Rijkswet op het Nederlanderschap)

  • Valid residence permit at the time of application
  • 5 years continuous legal residence in the Netherlands
  • Sufficient integration: civic integration exam (inburgeringsexamen) passed, or exemption (vrijstelling) based on education or other grounds
  • No criminal record issues: no conviction for certain offenses, no pending criminal proceedings
  • Note on income: while "sufficient and sustainable income" is not listed as a formal naturalisation requirement under Art. 8 RWN (the core requirements are continuous residence, integration, no criminal objections, and nationality renunciation), the IND may in practice consider the applicant's means of existence as part of the broader assessment. Stable employment income is generally favorable. Consult the current IND naturalisation requirements for the definitive list of conditions. This reflects current IND practice as of March 2026; IND assessment criteria may evolve. Verify against the current IND naturalisation guidance before relying on this factor.
  • Renunciation of previous nationality: Indian nationality must be renounced (India generally requires renunciation; limited exceptions apply under Dutch law)
  • No threat to public order, public morals, or national security

6.3 Why Permanent Employment Strengthens the Citizenship Application

  • Provides the cleanest proof of continuous residence (uninterrupted payslips, employer records)
  • Provides the easiest proof of sufficient and sustainable income (employment contract + salary exceeds norms)
  • Eliminates any gaps in residence history that could disqualify or delay the application
  • A stable employment history with a recognized sponsor demonstrates integration and contribution to Dutch society
  • The HSM permit remains valid through November 2027, covering the period until citizenship eligibility
