### Article A3: Intellectual Property Boundary (Intellectueel Eigendom Afbakening)

*This Article constitutes the agreed delineation of intellectual property ownership between the Parties.*

A3.1. **Employer's IP (IE van Werkgever):** Pursuant to the "unless otherwise agreed" (*tenzij tussen partijen anders is overeengekomen*) provision of Article 7 of the Auteurswet and the applicable provisions of Article 12(1) of the Rijksoctrooiwet 1995, the Parties agree that all intellectual property, including but not limited to inventions, works of authorship, software, designs, and know-how, that is:
   - (a) created in the performance of Employee's employment duties (in de uitoefening van de dienstbetrekking) as described in the employment agreement; **and**
   - (b) created using Employer's time, equipment, networks, or resources;

   shall be and remain the exclusive property of Employer. The Parties intend this cumulative test (requiring both (a) and (b)) to replace the default statutory rule of Article 7 Auteurswet, which applies the single criterion of creation "in the service of" the employer. This agreed departure is made in accordance with **Article 7:610 BW** (employment relationship), **Article 7 of the Auteurswet** (copyright in employment), and **Article 12(1) of the Rijksoctrooiwet 1995** (patents in employment).

A3.2. **Employee's / FutureAtoms' IP (IE van Werknemer / FutureAtoms):** All intellectual property, including but not limited to inventions, works of authorship, software, designs, and know-how, that is:
   - (a) created **outside of Employee's working hours** (buiten werktijd); **and**
   - (b) created using Employee's **own equipment, resources, and infrastructure**;

   shall be and remain the **exclusive property of Employee and/or FutureAtoms**. The fact that such intellectual property may relate to a field of activity similar to Employer's business shall not, by itself, alter this classification, provided that the cumulative conditions of Article A3.1(a) and (b) are not met.

A3.3. **ChipOS** and all its components, modules, source code, documentation, trademarks, and associated intellectual property, as defined in Article A4 below, are hereby acknowledged and confirmed by Employer to be the **exclusive intellectual property of Employee / FutureAtoms**, having been developed independently of the employment relationship.

A3.4. **Testing and Demonstration Exception (Uitzondering voor Testen en Demonstratie):** The fact that Employee has tested, demonstrated, or evaluated ChipOS or any FutureAtoms product on Employer's hardware for pilot, evaluation, proof-of-concept, or demonstration purposes shall **NOT**:
   - (a) create, transfer, or imply any ownership rights in favor of Employer over ChipOS or any FutureAtoms intellectual property;
   - (b) constitute creation "in the performance of employment duties" within the meaning of Article 7 Auteurswet or Article 12 Rijksoctrooiwet 1995;
   - (c) alter the classification of ChipOS or any FutureAtoms product as Employee's / FutureAtoms' IP under Article A3.2.

A3.5. In the event of any dispute regarding the classification of specific intellectual property under this Article A3, the burden of proof that such IP was created in the performance of employment duties using employer time and equipment shall rest with Employer.
