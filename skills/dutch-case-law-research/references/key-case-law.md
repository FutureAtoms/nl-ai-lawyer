# Key Case Law - Dutch Procedural Law & Case Law Methodology

## Lindenbaum/Cohen
**ECLI:** ECLI:NL:HR:1919:AG1776
**Court:** Hoge Raad der Nederlanden (Supreme Court)
**Date:** 1919
**Relevance:** Foundational case establishing tort law principles (onrechtmatige daad) and the concept of legal wrongfulness beyond statutory violation
**Key holding:** Established that unlawful conduct encompasses not only statutory violations but also breaches of legal duties and reasonable conduct standards recognized in society.

## Kelderluik
**ECLI:** ECLI:NL:HR:1965:AB7079
**Court:** Hoge Raad der Nederlanden
**Date:** 1965
**Relevance:** Landmark decision on causal nexus (causaliteit) in tort law and criteria for establishing causation
**Key holding:** A defendant is liable for damage only if there is adequate causal connection between the wrongful act and the damage, requiring proximate causation rather than mere factual causation.

## Haviltex
**ECLI:** ECLI:NL:HR:1981:AG4158
**Court:** Hoge Raad der Nederlanden
**Date:** 1981
**Relevance:** Seminal case on contract interpretation methodology and good faith principles (redelijkheid en billijkheid)
**Key holding:** Contracts must be interpreted according to what reasonable parties would understand by their language in light of the circumstances, including good faith and reasonableness standards.

## Saladin/HBU
**ECLI:** ECLI:NL:HR:1967:AC4777
**Court:** Hoge Raad der Nederlanden
**Date:** 1967
**Relevance:** Established principles of tortious liability for breach of pre-contractual duty to disclose material information
**Key holding:** Parties in pre-contractual negotiations owe a duty of good faith to disclose material information affecting the transaction; failure to disclose can constitute unlawful conduct.

## Plas/Valburg
**ECLI:** ECLI:NL:HR:1995:ZC1891
**Court:** Hoge Raad der Nederlanden
**Date:** 1995
**Relevance:** Clarified principles of legal reasoning standards and burden of proof allocation in civil litigation
**Key holding:** The party asserting facts has the burden to prove them, with the court applying a reasonable likelihood standard rather than absolute certainty in civil matters.

## Handels- en Transportvervoer/Unilever
**ECLI:** ECLI:NL:HR:1977:AB5157
**Court:** Hoge Raad der Nederlanden
**Date:** 1977
**Relevance:** Established methodology for interpreting standard terms and conditions in commercial contracts
**Key holding:** Standard terms must be interpreted strictly against the party proposing them, and unreasonable terms may be set aside even if formally accepted.

## Ynzup
**ECLI:** ECLI:NL:HR:1996:ZC2024
**Court:** Hoge Raad der Nederlanden
**Date:** 1996
**Relevance:** Key decision on interpretation of contractual exclusion clauses and limitation of liability provisions
**Key holding:** Exclusion and limitation clauses are interpreted narrowly; ambiguities are resolved against the drafting party, particularly regarding fundamental obligations.

## Universiteit van Amsterdam/Brouwer
**ECLI:** ECLI:NL:HR:2007:BA2087
**Court:** Hoge Raad der Nederlanden
**Date:** 2007
**Relevance:** Landmark ruling on evidentiary standards in complex commercial disputes and expert evidence evaluation
**Key holding:** Courts must carefully evaluate expert testimony by applying consistent methodology and may reject expert opinions that lack proper empirical foundation or logical consistency.

## Stichting Autoreclame/Wijngaarden
**ECLI:** ECLI:NL:HR:2001:AD4065
**Court:** Hoge Raad der Nederlanden
**Date:** 2001
**Relevance:** Established principles for identifying and interpreting implied terms in contracts and course of dealing
**Key holding:** Courts may recognize implied contractual terms based on industry practice, prior dealings between parties, and reasonable expectations in the particular commercial context.

## TNT/Rederij Theodoro
**ECLI:** ECLI:NL:HR:1986:AC5016
**Court:** Hoge Raad der Nederlanden
**Date:** 1986
**Relevance:** Clarified legal standards for evaluating causation and foreseeability in determining scope of contractual liability
**Key holding:** Liability for breach of contract extends to foreseeable damages within the reasonable contemplation of the parties at formation, limiting recovery of extraordinary or remote damages.

## Vermeiren/Camu
**ECLI:** ECLI:NL:HR:1972:AC2841
**Court:** Hoge Raad der Nederlanden
**Date:** 1972
**Relevance:** Established the principle of proportionality in applying equitable remedies and enforcing contractual penalties
**Key holding:** Contractual penalty clauses are subject to proportionality review; courts may moderate penalties if they are grossly disproportionate to actual damages or legitimate business interests.

## Manten/NOM
**ECLI:** ECLI:NL:HR:2001:AB4597
**Court:** Hoge Raad der Nederlanden
**Date:** 2001
**Relevance:** Landmark case on legal precedent and stare decisis principles in Dutch jurisprudence
**Key holding:** While Dutch law is civil law-based, consistent jurisprudence from the Hoge Raad creates persuasive precedent that lower courts should follow absent compelling reasons to deviate.
