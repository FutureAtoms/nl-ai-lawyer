## 1. Non-Conformity in Purchase of Immovable Property (Art. 7:17 BW)

### 1.1 HR 23 november 2007, ECLI:NL:HR:2007:BB3733 (Ploum/Smeets)
- **Court**: Hoge Raad
- **Date**: 23 November 2007
- **Key principle**: The buyer's duty of investigation (onderzoeksplicht) versus the seller's duty of disclosure (mededelingsplicht) in the sale of contaminated land. The Hoge Raad held that the seller's failure to disclose known soil contamination prevailed over the buyer's own duty to investigate, confirming that the mededelingsplicht generally takes precedence.
- **Relevance**: Foundation case for Art. 7:17 BW disputes involving hidden defects in immovable property.

### 1.2 HR 25 februari 2005, ECLI:NL:HR:2005:AR5383 (Fabels/Meenderink -- "lekkage-arrest")
- **Court**: Hoge Raad
- **Date**: 25 February 2005
- **Key principle**: Whether persistent water leakage in a residential dwelling constitutes non-conformity under Art. 7:17 BW. The Hoge Raad confirmed that a buyer may expect that a dwelling is suitable for normal habitation, and structural leakage can render the property non-conformant.
- **Relevance**: Sets the standard for what a buyer may reasonably expect regarding the condition of residential property ("normaal gebruik als woning").

### 1.3 HR 14 november 2008, ECLI:NL:HR:2008:BF0407 (Van Dalfsen/Gemeente Kampen)
- **Court**: Hoge Raad
- **Date**: 14 November 2008
- **Key principle**: The scope of the conformity requirement where public-law restrictions (bestemmingsplan limitations) affect the purchased property. The Supreme Court clarified how building restrictions relate to Art. 7:17 BW and the parties' reasonable expectations.
- **Relevance**: Important for disputes about planning law constraints that were not disclosed before purchase.

## 2. Commercial Lease -- Rent Review (Art. 7:303 BW -- Huurprijsherziening)

### 2.1 HR 10 juni 2011, ECLI:NL:HR:2011:BQ1677 (Halfords)
- **Court**: Hoge Raad
- **Date**: 10 June 2011
- **Key principle**: The method for determining the nadere huurprijs (revised rent) for Art. 7:290 BW commercial premises, and the role of deskundigenadvies. The HR clarified that comparable premises (vergelijkingspanden) must be truly comparable in location, size, and use.
- **Relevance**: Leading authority on the methodology for rent review proceedings under Art. 7:303 BW.

### 2.2 HR 17 december 2004, ECLI:NL:HR:2004:AR3636 (Toko Mitra/PMT)
- **Court**: Hoge Raad
- **Date**: 17 December 2004
- **Key principle**: When calculating the revised rent, the court should assess the average rent of comparable business premises over the preceding five years, not merely one reference date. This prevents distortion by short-term market fluctuations.
- **Relevance**: Essential for practitioners advising on huurprijsherziening procedures.

### 2.3 HR 1 februari 2008, ECLI:NL:HR:2008:BB8098
- **Court**: Hoge Raad
- **Date**: 1 February 2008
- **Key principle**: Interaction between freely negotiated incentives (huurvrije perioden, bijdrage inrichtingskosten) and the statutory rent review mechanism. The HR ruled that such incentives may be taken into account when determining whether the current rent deviates substantially from market rent.
- **Relevance**: Guides commercial lease negotiations on rent incentives.

## 3. Termination of Lease for Non-Payment (Ontbinding Huurovereenkomst)

### 3.1 HR 28 september 2018, ECLI:NL:HR:2018:1810 (Eigen Haard)
- **Court**: Hoge Raad
- **Date**: 28 September 2018
- **Key principle**: Ontbinding of a residential lease is not automatically justified by any breach; the court must always assess whether the breach, given its special nature or minor significance ("bijzondere aard of geringe betekenis"), justifies termination. This applies even where the tenant has accumulated a rent arrear.
- **Relevance**: Tempers strict application of Art. 6:265 BW in housing contexts, reflecting the social function of housing.

### 3.2 HR 30 november 2012, ECLI:NL:HR:2012:BX5585 (LCCM/A)
- **Court**: Hoge Raad
- **Date**: 30 November 2012
- **Key principle**: The tenzij-clause of Art. 6:265 BW applies to commercial lease terminations as well. Courts must engage in a proportionality assessment even in purely commercial lease disputes.
- **Relevance**: Ensures proportionality analysis in all lease terminations.

## 4. Erfpacht (Long Lease / Ground Lease) Canon Revision

### 4.1 Gerechtshof Amsterdam 29 juni 2010, ECLI:NL:GHAMS:2010:BN2643
- **Court**: Gerechtshof Amsterdam
- **Date**: 29 June 2010
- **Key principle**: Challenge to the Gemeente Amsterdam's method of drastically increasing erfpacht canon upon renewal. The court assessed whether the municipality's policy (Eeuwigdurende Erfpacht 2000) was in accordance with reasonableness and fairness (redelijkheid en billijkheid).
- **Relevance**: High-profile dispute affecting thousands of Amsterdam homeowners with erfpacht on municipal ground.

### 4.2 HR 26 maart 1999, ECLI:NL:HR:1999:ZC2874 (Erfpacht Stichting/Gemeente)
- **Court**: Hoge Raad
- **Date**: 26 March 1999
- **Key principle**: The enforceability of canon revision clauses in erfpacht conditions. The HR addressed whether a clause allowing the ground owner to unilaterally revise the canon must be tested against Art. 6:248 lid 2 BW (derogating function of reasonableness and fairness).
- **Relevance**: Framework for challenging one-sided canon increases as unreasonable.

### 4.3 Rechtbank Amsterdam 10 april 2019, ECLI:NL:RBAMS:2019:2755
- **Court**: Rechtbank Amsterdam
- **Date**: 10 April 2019
- **Key principle**: Collective action by erfpachters against the Gemeente Amsterdam's transition to "eeuwigdurende erfpacht" and the resulting canon calculation methods. The court examined the reasonableness of the offered transitional arrangements.
- **Relevance**: Directly relevant to ongoing erfpacht transition disputes in Amsterdam.

## 5. Kadaster Registration Disputes

### 5.1 HR 20 februari 2004, ECLI:NL:HR:2004:AN8074 (Kadaster -- grensbepaling)
- **Court**: Hoge Raad
- **Date**: 20 February 2004
- **Key principle**: The evidentiary weight of Kadaster boundary registration (kadastrale grens) in disputes over property boundaries. The HR confirmed that the kadastrale registration creates a presumption but can be overturned by evidence of long-standing actual use (bezit) under Art. 3:105 BW (acquisitive prescription / verkrijgende verjaring).
- **Relevance**: Central to boundary disputes between neighbors.

### 5.2 HR 24 februari 2017, ECLI:NL:HR:2017:309 (Verjaring grond Gemeente)
- **Court**: Hoge Raad
- **Date**: 24 February 2017
- **Key principle**: An occupier who acquires ownership of municipal land through extinctive prescription (bevrijdende verjaring, Art. 3:105 jo. 3:306 BW) may, in certain circumstances, be liable for damages to the municipality under Art. 6:162 BW (onrechtmatige daad). The court recognized a potential schadevergoedingsplicht consisting of "re-transfer" of the land.
- **Relevance**: Landmark ruling curbing abusive reliance on verjaring to acquire public land without compensation.

## 6. Construction and Development Disputes

### 6.1 HR 13 juli 2018, ECLI:NL:HR:2018:1176 (Aanneming van werk -- verborgen gebreken)
- **Court**: Hoge Raad
- **Date**: 13 July 2018
- **Key principle**: Allocation of risk for hidden construction defects discovered after oplevering (delivery). The HR interpreted the interaction between the UAV 2012 (Uniforme Administratieve Voorwaarden) and Title 7.12 BW (aanneming van werk).
- **Relevance**: Governs liability for construction defects in large-scale real estate development.

### 6.2 HR 21 juni 2019, ECLI:NL:HR:2019:1016 (Fraanje/Alukon)
- **Court**: Hoge Raad
- **Date**: 21 June 2019
- **Key principle**: Whether a subcontractor owes a direct duty of care (rechtstreekse zorgplicht) to the principal, based on Art. 6:162 BW, for construction defects. The Hoge Raad applied a multi-factor test (proximity, foreseeability) to determine whether a special relationship existed.
- **Relevance**: Important for understanding liability chains in real estate development involving multiple contractors.

## 7. VvE (Vereniging van Eigenaars) and Appartement Law

### 7.1 HR 10 maart 1995, ECLI:NL:HR:1995:ZC1657 (VvE -- nietigheid besluit)
- **Court**: Hoge Raad
- **Date**: 10 March 1995
- **Key principle**: The distinction between nietige (void) and vernietigbare (voidable) resolutions of the VvE under Art. 5:130 BW. A VvE resolution that conflicts with the splitsingsakte or splitsingsreglement is void; a resolution that merely violates procedural requirements may be voidable within the statutory time limit.
- **Relevance**: Fundamental for disputes within apartment complexes (appartementsrechten).

### 7.2 HR 24 november 2017, ECLI:NL:HR:2017:3016 (VvE -- onderhoudsbijdrage)
- **Court**: Hoge Raad
- **Date**: 24 November 2017
- **Key principle**: The obligation of apartment owners to contribute to the VvE reserve fund (reservefonds) and the VvE's power to levy additional contributions for maintenance. The HR confirmed that the VvE vergadering can adopt binding maintenance resolutions, and dissenting owners cannot refuse payment.
- **Relevance**: Critical for real estate due diligence involving apartments.

## 8. Acquisitive Prescription (Verjaring) -- Additional Reference

### 8.1 HR 4 september 2015, ECLI:NL:HR:2015:2463
- **Court**: Hoge Raad
- **Date**: 4 September 2015
- **Key principle**: Requirements for bezit te goeder trouw (possession in good faith) under Art. 3:99 BW for verkrijgende verjaring of immovable property. The HR required that the possessor's good faith must exist at the commencement of the possession period and clarified the objective standard for assessing good faith in boundary disputes.
- **Relevance**: Supplements the 2017 verjaring ruling (section 5.2 above); relevant for neighbor boundary disputes and land registration.
