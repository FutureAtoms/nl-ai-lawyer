# Dutch Real Estate Purchase Agreement (Koopovereenkomst)

## Legal Framework
- **Art. 7:2 BW** - Written requirement for consumer real estate purchases
- **BW Boek 3, Titel 4** - Transfer of property (overdracht)
- **BW Boek 5** - Property rights (eigendom)
- **Kadasterwet** - Registration requirements

---

## Key Steps in a Dutch Property Purchase

### 1. Agreement (Koopovereenkomst)
- **Art. 7:2 BW**: Purchase of residential property by a consumer (natural person, not acting in business capacity) must be in writing
- Oral agreements for residential property are NOT binding on consumer buyers
- Standard form: **NVM koopakte** (model koopovereenkomst) widely used in practice
- Real estate agent (makelaar) typically drafts the koopovereenkomst
- No notarial deed required at this stage (private deed / onderhandse akte)

### 2. Cooling-Off Period (Bedenktijd - Art. 7:2 lid 2 BW)
- Consumer buyer has a **3 calendar days** cooling-off period after receiving a signed copy of the koopovereenkomst
- During this period, the buyer may withdraw without reason and without costs
- Period starts the day AFTER the buyer receives the signed agreement
- Weekends and public holidays: if the period would end on a Saturday, Sunday, or public holiday, it is extended to the next business day
- No cooling-off period for commercial buyers or in auctions

### 3. Resolutive Conditions (Ontbindende voorwaarden)
Common resolutive conditions in the koopovereenkomst:
- **Financieringsvoorbehoud** (financing condition): buyer can dissolve if unable to obtain mortgage financing. Standard period: 4-6 weeks. Must be invoked in writing with supporting documentation (at least 2 rejection letters from lenders)
- **Bouwkundige keuring** (structural survey condition): dissolution right if inspection reveals serious defects
- **Nationale Hypotheek Garantie (NHG)**: condition that NHG guarantee is obtained
- **Huisvestingsvergunning**: condition for required housing permit

### 4. Deposit / Bank Guarantee (Waarborgsom / Bankgarantie)
- Typically **10% of purchase price**
- Payable within agreed period after signing (usually 4-6 weeks)
- Held by notaris in escrow (derdengelden)
- Alternative: bank guarantee (bankgarantie) from buyer's bank
- If buyer defaults after expiry of conditions: 10% contractual penalty (boete, Art. 10 NVM model)

### 5. Notarial Transfer (Levering)
- **Art. 3:89 BW**: Transfer of registered property requires:
  1. Valid title (geldige titel - the koopovereenkomst)
  2. Notarial deed (notariele akte van levering)
  3. Registration in the public registers (kadaster)
- Transfer is only complete upon registration at the kadaster
- Notaris performs:
  - Identity verification (Wwft compliance)
  - Title search (eigendomsonderzoek)
  - Mortgage search (hypotheekonderzoek)
  - Municipal search (gemeentelijke lasten)
  - Preparation and execution of the deed
  - Registration at kadaster
  - Financial settlement (afrekening)

## Key Purchase Agreement Provisions

### Conformity (Art. 7:17 BW)
- Property must conform to what the buyer could reasonably expect
- Seller must disclose known defects (mededelingsplicht)
- Buyer has duty of investigation (onderzoeksplicht)
- **Balance**: seller's duty to disclose prevails over buyer's duty to investigate (HR Baris/Riezenkamp principle)

### NVM Model - Standard Clauses

#### Article 5 - State of Delivery (Staat van oplevering)
- Property delivered in the condition at time of agreement
- All rights and burdens (erfdienstbaarheden, kwalitatieve verplichtingen) are transferred

#### Article 6 - Risk and Insurance
- Risk of the property (schade/tenietgaan) transfers at delivery
- Between signing and delivery: usually governed by property insurance

#### Article 10 - Contractual Penalty (Boetebeding)
- Default: 10% of purchase price as penalty for breach
- Plus additional damages if actual loss exceeds the penalty
- Either party can invoke

#### Article 11 - Ingebrekestelling
- Written notice of default required before penalty can be claimed
- Defaulting party gets 8 days to cure

### Special Clauses
- **Ouderdomsclausule**: Disclaimer for older properties regarding hidden defects due to age
- **Asbestclausule**: Buyer accepts risk of asbestos in older buildings
- **Anti-speculation clause**: Municipality may impose resale restrictions on new-build social housing

## Transfer Tax (Overdrachtsbelasting)

### Rates (as of current legislation)
| Buyer Category | Rate |
|---------------|------|
| Owner-occupier (eigen woning), 18-35 years, first-time buyer, below threshold | 0% (startersvrijstelling, up to EUR 510,000 in 2024) |
| Owner-occupier (eigen woning) | 2% |
| Non-residential / investment property | 10.4% |

- Tax base: purchase price or WOZ value (whichever is higher for residential)
- Paid by the buyer via the notaris at time of delivery

## Mortgage (Hypotheek)

### Registration
- Mortgage (hypotheekrecht) is a limited property right (beperkt zakelijk recht)
- Created by notarial deed registered at kadaster
- Provides the bank with a right of priority (voorrang) on the property

### Key Provisions
- **Art. 3:260 BW**: Mortgage created by notarial deed
- **Art. 3:268 BW**: Mortgagee has the right to foreclose (executie) without prior court order
- **Art. 3:264 BW**: Huurbeding (rental clause): mortgagee can include a clause that any rental without prior consent can be nullified in foreclosure

### Nationale Hypotheek Garantie (NHG)
- Government-backed mortgage guarantee (via Stichting WEW)
- Available for mortgages below the NHG limit (EUR 435,000 in 2024)
- Lower interest rates
- Protection for both borrower and lender in case of residual debt after forced sale

## Apartment Rights (Appartementsrecht - BW Boek 5, Titel 9)

### Structure
- Building divided into apartment rights (appartementsrechten)
- Each apartment right = share in the building (aandeel in de gemeenschap) + exclusive use right (exclusief gebruiksrecht) for a specific unit
- **VvE** (Vereniging van Eigenaren): mandatory owners' association for all apartment buildings
- **Splitsingsakte**: notarial deed of division establishing the apartment rights
- **Splitsingsreglement**: rules governing the apartment complex (maintenance, use, restrictions)

### VvE Obligations
- VvE must hold annual meetings, maintain financial reserves, insure the building
- VvE contributions (servicekosten/bijdragen) are mandatory
- Active VvE is a condition for mortgage approval at most banks

## Pre-registration (Vormerkung - Art. 7:3 BW)
- After signing the koopovereenkomst, the buyer can register the purchase agreement at the kadaster (Vormerkung)
- Protects against: seller bankruptcy, double sale, seizure by seller's creditors, new encumbrances
- Protection period: 6 months from pre-registration
- Common practice for the period between signing and delivery
