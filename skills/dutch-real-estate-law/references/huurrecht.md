# Dutch Rental Law (Huurrecht) - BW Boek 7, Titel 4

## Three Categories of Rental Property

Dutch rental law distinguishes three categories with very different levels of tenant protection:

| Category | BW Provisions | Protection Level |
|----------|--------------|-----------------|
| Woonruimte (residential) | Art. 7:232-282 | Very high (dwingend recht) |
| Middenstandsbedrijfsruimte (retail/hospitality) | Art. 7:290-310 | High (semi-dwingend) |
| Overige bedrijfsruimte (other commercial) | Art. 7:230a | Limited (ontruimingsbescherming only) |

---

## 1. Residential Rental (Woonruimte - Art. 7:232-282)

### Rent Price Regulation

#### Regulated Sector (Gereguleerde/sociale huur)
- Applies to properties scoring **below 186 points** on the WWS (Woningwaarderingsstelsel) - threshold as of mid-2024 expansion
- Maximum rent determined by WWS points system
- Annual rent increase limited by government-set percentage
- Tenant can challenge rent at the **Huurcommissie** (Rent Tribunal)
- Initial rent can also be challenged within 6 months of commencement

#### Liberalized Sector (Vrije sector / geliberaliseerd)
- Properties scoring **187+ points** on WWS (and rented above the liberalization threshold)
- Initial rent freely negotiable
- Annual increases per contract terms (often CPI + percentage)
- Since 2024: annual rent increase in free sector capped by law (Wet maximering huurprijsverhogingen geliberaliseerde huurovereenkomsten - initially temporary, extended)
- Limited Huurcommissie jurisdiction (mainly service costs and temporary contracts)

### Tenant Protection Against Termination

#### Art. 7:271-272 BW - Termination
- **Landlord cannot freely terminate**: can only terminate on limited statutory grounds
- Landlord must give notice (opzegging) by registered letter or bailiff's writ (deurwaardersexploot)
- Notice period: minimum **3 months** + 1 month for each year of tenancy (max 6 months total for landlord; 1 month for tenant after first year, otherwise equal to payment period with minimum 1 and max 3 months)

#### Art. 7:274 BW - Grounds for Termination by Landlord
1. **Slecht huurderschap** (bad tenancy) - tenant fails to behave as a good tenant (e.g., nuisance, late payment, illegal activities)
2. **Dringend eigen gebruik** (urgent own use) - landlord or close family urgently needs the property. Must offer suitable alternative housing (if available). Court may award relocation compensation (verhuis- en inrichtingskosten)
3. **New rental agreement** (reasonable offer rejected by tenant)
4. **Bestemmingsplanwijziging** (zoning change) - property to be used according to new zoning plan
5. **Hospitaverhuur** (lodger termination) - special rules for renting a room within landlord's own home

#### Art. 7:272 BW - Court Involvement
- If tenant does not agree with termination: landlord must go to court
- Contract continues until court has ruled (huurbescherming)
- Court may grant or deny termination, possibly with conditions

### Temporary Rental Contracts (since Wet doorstroming huurmarkt 2016)
- Fixed-term rental without open-ended continuation:
  - Self-contained (zelfstandige woonruimte): max **2 years**
  - Non-self-contained (onzelfstandige/kamer): max **5 years**
- Landlord must notify tenant (aanzeggen) at least 1 month and at most 3 months before end date
- If not notified: contract becomes indefinite
- Only one temporary contract per tenant per property

### Key Tenant Rights
- **Huurcommissie**: Tenant can challenge rent level, service costs, and maintenance deficiencies
- **Gebrekenregeling** (Art. 7:204-210): Landlord must repair defects; tenant may claim rent reduction
- **Medehuur** (Art. 7:266-268): Spouse/registered partner is automatic co-tenant; others may apply
- **Woningruil** (Art. 7:270): Tenant can request permission to exchange rental properties
- **Onderhuur** (subletting): Generally prohibited without landlord consent; unauthorized subletting is ground for termination

## 2. Commercial Retail/Hospitality (Middenstandsbedrijfsruimte - Art. 7:290-310)

### What Qualifies (Art. 7:290 BW)
Premises used as:
- Retail space (winkelruimte) accessible to the public
- Restaurant, cafe, hotel (horeca)
- Camp site, take-away establishment
- Craft workshop (ambachtsbedrijf) accessible to the public
- **Key criterion**: Public accessibility for direct supply of goods/services

### Duration and Termination

#### Art. 7:292 BW - Minimum Duration
- Lease is entered into for **5 years** (first period) - minimum
- After first period: automatically renewed for **5 years** (second period)
- Total initial protection: **10 years** (5+5)
- After 10 years: lease continues for indefinite period

#### Art. 7:293-296 BW - Termination by Landlord
- **After first 5 years**: Only possible on grounds of:
  - Bad tenancy (slecht huurderschap)
  - Urgent own use (dringend eigen gebruik) - court must deny if interests of tenant outweigh landlord's
- **After 10 years**: Court has broader discretion (belangenafweging - weighing of interests); all circumstances considered
- Notice period: at least **1 year** before end of the current period

#### Art. 7:297 BW - Relocation Compensation
Court may award tenant a relocation and fitting-out compensation (tegemoetkoming in verhuis- en inrichtingskosten)

### Rent Review (Art. 7:303 BW)
- Either party can request rent adjustment to market rate (nadere huurprijsvaststelling)
- Only after the initial agreed rent period (usually 5 years)
- Expert opinion required (deskundigenbericht)
- Adjustment calculated based on comparable properties in the area

### Indeplaatsstelling (Art. 7:307 BW)
- Tenant who sells their business can request court permission to substitute a new tenant
- Landlord's consent not required if court considers the new tenant adequate

## 3. Other Commercial Space (Overige bedrijfsruimte - Art. 7:230a BW)

### What Qualifies
- Office space (kantoorruimte)
- Warehouse/storage (opslagruimte)
- Factory/industrial premises
- Medical practices (not publicly accessible retail)
- Any premises not qualifying as woonruimte or Art. 7:290 middenstandsbedrijfsruimte

### Limited Protection
- **No rent protection** - rent freely negotiable
- **No termination protection** as in 7:290
- **Only ontruimingsbescherming** (eviction protection - Art. 7:230a BW):
  - After lease termination, tenant can request 1-year postponement of eviction (up to 3x, max 3 years total)
  - Postponement unless court finds tenant's interests do not outweigh landlord's

## 4. Wet goed verhuurderschap (Good Landlordship Act - 2023)

### Key Obligations for Landlords
- Prohibition of discrimination in tenant selection
- Maximum security deposit: 2 months' rent (base rent)
- Written lease agreement mandatory
- Prohibition of intimidation
- Mandatory information provision in writing
- Municipal enforcement powers (boetes up to EUR 90,000+)

---

## 5. Huurcommissie (Rent Tribunal)

### Jurisdiction
- Rent assessment for regulated sector
- Service costs disputes
- Maintenance defect assessment
- Temporary contract disputes
- Initial rent check (within 6 months of commencement)

### Procedure
- Low-cost procedure (EUR 25 for tenants, EUR 300 for landlords as of 2024)
- Not a court but an independent advisory/decision body
- Decisions binding unless challenged in court within 8 weeks

## 6. Practical Checklist

### For Landlords
- [ ] Classify the property correctly (woonruimte / 290 / 230a)
- [ ] Check municipal regulations (huisvestingsverordening, zoning)
- [ ] Use appropriate standard contract (ROZ model recommended)
- [ ] Register rental income for tax purposes
- [ ] Maintain the property (gebrekenregeling obligations)
- [ ] Comply with Wet goed verhuurderschap requirements

### For Tenants
- [ ] Check rent level against WWS points (if residential regulated)
- [ ] Request Huurcommissie review if needed (within 6 months for initial rent)
- [ ] Document maintenance defects in writing
- [ ] Understand notice periods and termination rights
- [ ] Check whether subletting is permitted
