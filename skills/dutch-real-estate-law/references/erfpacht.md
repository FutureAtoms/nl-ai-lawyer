# Erfpacht (Leasehold / Ground Lease)

## Legal Basis
- **Art. 5:85 BW**: Definition - erfpacht is a right in rem (zakelijk recht) entitling the holder (erfpachter) to hold and use immovable property (onroerende zaak) belonging to another person (grondeigenaar / bloot eigenaar)
- **Art. 5:85-5:100 BW**: Complete legal framework for erfpacht in Burgerlijk Wetboek, Boek 5, Titel 7
- **Kadasterwet**: Registration requirements for erfpacht rights

---

## Core Characteristics

### Nature of the Right
- Erfpacht is a **beperkt zakelijk recht** (limited right in rem) - not a lease (huur)
- The erfpachter has near-complete enjoyment of the property, similar to ownership
- The right is registered at the kadaster and is transferable and inheritable
- Erfpacht can be encumbered with a mortgage (hypotheek) - essential for financing
- The grondeigenaar retains bare ownership (bloot eigendom)

### Key Legal Provisions
- **Art. 5:86 BW**: Erfpacht can be established for a fixed period or perpetually
- **Art. 5:87 BW**: Termination provisions - erfpachter may terminate by notification; grondeigenaar may only terminate in case of non-payment of canon (2+ years arrears) or serious breach of conditions
- **Art. 5:88 BW**: Erfpachter must not act in conflict with the purpose (bestemming) of the property
- **Art. 5:89 BW**: Canon - the periodic payment by the erfpachter to the grondeigenaar
- **Art. 5:91 BW**: Transfer restrictions can be included in the erfpacht conditions (toestemmingsvereiste)
- **Art. 5:92 BW**: The erfpacht conditions (erfpachtvoorwaarden) are set in the vestigingsakte
- **Art. 5:93 BW**: Upon termination, the erfpachter is entitled to compensation for buildings and improvements (vergoeding voor opstallen), unless conditions provide otherwise
- **Art. 5:94 BW**: Grondeigenaar's right of first refusal (voorkeursrecht) may be stipulated
- **Art. 5:99 BW**: Compensation upon termination - mandatory for buildings and plantings
- **Art. 5:100 BW**: Canon is a priority claim (bevoorrechte vordering)

## Types of Erfpacht

### Tijdelijke Erfpacht (Fixed-Term Leasehold)
- Established for a specific duration (commonly 50, 75, or 99 years)
- Expires automatically at end of term unless renewed
- Common in various municipalities and private erfpacht
- Risk: uncertainty about renewal terms and canon upon expiration

### Voortdurende Erfpacht (Perpetual Leasehold with Periodic Revision)
- No end date, but canon is periodically revised (herzien)
- Typically revision every 25 or 50 years (depending on conditions)
- The Amsterdam system before 2016 was a form of voortdurende erfpacht
- Risk: **canonical shock** at revision moments when ground values have risen sharply

### Eeuwigdurende Erfpacht (Perpetual Leasehold with Fixed Canon)
- No end date and no canon revision
- Canon is fixed for the entire duration (or paid off as a lump sum - afkoop)
- Amsterdam system since 2016 reform
- Most favorable for erfpachter in terms of certainty

## Amsterdam Erfpacht System

Amsterdam is the largest municipal ground lessor (grondeigenaar) in the Netherlands. The Gemeente Amsterdam owns approximately 80% of the land within the city.

### Historical Background
- Amsterdam has used erfpacht since 1896
- Purpose: retain control over land use, capture land value increases for the community
- Virtually all residential and commercial property in Amsterdam is on erfpacht land

### Algemene Bepalingen (General Conditions)

#### AB1915 / AB1934 / AB1966
- Older conditions still applicable to many existing erfpacht rights
- Tijdelijk erfpacht with 75-year terms (AB1915) or 50-year terms (AB1966)
- Canon revision at end of term, leading to significant increases

#### AB1994 / AB2000
- Voortdurende erfpacht (perpetual, with periodic canon revision)
- Canon revision every 50 years (AB2000) based on residuele grondwaarde
- Resulted in so-called **canonsprong** (canon leap) for many homeowners
- Provisions for canonical herziening caused significant public concern

#### AB2016 - Eeuwigdurende Erfpacht
- Introduced 1 July 2016 by the Gemeente Amsterdam
- **Eeuwigdurend** (perpetual without revision): no end date, no canon revision
- Canon is either:
  - Paid annually (vast jaarlijks canon) - fixed amount, indexed only with CPI
  - Bought off in a lump sum (eeuwigdurende afkoop) - one-time payment
- Applies automatically to all new erfpacht issuances after 1 July 2016
- Existing erfpachters can voluntarily convert (overstappen) to the new system

### Canon Calculation Methodology (Amsterdam)

#### Residuele Grondwaarde Method
The canon is based on the **residuele grondwaarde** (residual ground value):
1. Determine market value of the property (marktwaarde)
2. Subtract the depreciated replacement value of the building (afgeschreven herbouwwaarde)
3. The remainder is the ground value (grondwaarde)
4. Apply the canon percentage (canonpercentage) to the ground value
5. Result = annual canon

#### Canon Percentage
- Set by the Gemeente Amsterdam based on the government bond yield (rente staatsleningen) plus a risk surcharge
- 2024/2025 rates: approximately 2.39% for residential (verify for current year - rates are updated periodically)
- Different rates may apply for commercial erfpacht

#### Factors Affecting Ground Value
- Location (buurt/wijk)
- Zoning/use (bestemming)
- Surface area (oppervlakte)
- WOZ value as reference
- Market conditions at moment of calculation

### Overstapregeling (Transition Arrangement - Amsterdam)

#### Eligibility
- Erfpachters with AB2000 or older conditions (voortdurend erfpacht)
- Residential properties (woningen) in Amsterdam
- Application window opened in 2017

#### Process
1. Erfpachter receives a BSQ (buurtstraatquote) from the Gemeente
2. BSQ determines the ratio of ground value to property value for the neighborhood
3. The WOZ value is used as a proxy for market value
4. Erfpachter can choose: annual canon or full afkoop
5. Existing residual canonical obligations are settled

#### Financial Options
- **Jaarlijkse canon**: Fixed annual amount, CPI-indexed only
- **Eeuwigdurende afkoop**: One-time lump sum based on net contante waarde of future canonical stream
- **Partial afkoop**: Some erfpacht conditions allow partial prepayment

### Canonical Herziening Risks (Canon Revision)
- Under AB2000 and older conditions, canon is revised periodically
- Revision based on current ground value at the moment of revision
- In rising real estate markets, this can result in a **canonsprong** (canon shock):
  - Example: erfpachter paying EUR 500/year suddenly faces EUR 5,000/year after revision
- This was the primary motivation for the 2016 reform to eeuwigdurende erfpacht
- Erfpachters who did NOT convert to AB2016 still face revision risks

## Other Municipal Erfpacht Systems

### Rotterdam
- Gemeente Rotterdam issued erfpacht historically but has largely stopped for residential
- Many existing properties still on erfpacht (especially in older neighborhoods)
- Typically tijdelijke erfpacht (50 years)
- Rotterdam offers the option to purchase the ground (bloot eigendom kopen) in many cases
- Canon revision at expiry of term based on actual ground value

### Den Haag (The Hague)
- Limited erfpacht system compared to Amsterdam
- Primarily used for specific developments and social housing
- Gemeente Den Haag has offered erfpachters the right to purchase ground outright
- Less prevalent; many former erfpacht properties have been converted to vol eigendom

### Utrecht
- Historically used erfpacht for certain neighborhoods
- Gemeente Utrecht has offered conversion to vol eigendom since the 2000s
- Remaining erfpacht properties have periodic canon revision
- Policy focus on gradual elimination of erfpacht system

### Other Municipalities
- Leiden, Groningen, Amersfoort, and others have limited erfpacht
- Each municipality sets its own erfpachtvoorwaarden
- Trend nationally is toward offering purchase of ground or conversion to ownership
- Private erfpacht (by non-government parties) also exists but is less common

## Buyer Due Diligence Checklist for Erfpacht Properties

### Essential Checks Before Purchase
1. **Type of erfpacht**: tijdelijk, voortdurend, or eeuwigdurend?
2. **Which Algemene Bepalingen apply**: AB1966, AB2000, AB2016, or other?
3. **Current canon amount**: What is the annual canon? When was it last revised?
4. **Next revision date**: When is the next canonical herziening? What is the expected increase?
5. **Remaining term** (for tijdelijk): When does the erfpacht expire? What are the renewal terms?
6. **Overstap possibility**: Has the erfpachter converted to AB2016? If not, is conversion still possible and at what cost?
7. **Erfpachtvoorwaarden**: Review the actual conditions in the vestigingsakte at the kadaster
8. **Bestemming restrictions**: Are there use restrictions in the erfpacht conditions?
9. **Toestemmingsvereiste**: Is municipal consent required for transfer? (Art. 5:91 BW)
10. **Afkoopsom**: If canon has been prepaid, for how long? When does the afkoop expire?
11. **Mortgage implications**: Does the bank accept the erfpacht conditions for financing?
12. **VvE and erfpacht**: For apartments, both VvE and erfpacht conditions apply - check interaction

### Red Flags
- Canon revision due within 5 years under old conditions (potential canonical shock)
- Tijdelijk erfpacht expiring within 10 years without clear renewal terms
- Transfer restrictions (toestemmingsvereiste) that are onerous
- Private erfpacht with unclear or unfavorable conditions
- Erfpacht from non-municipal parties (stichtingen, private landowners) - less standardized

## Financial Implications

### Canon Deductibility (Fiscaal)
- **Annual canon is tax-deductible** in Box 1 as eigenwoningkosten (Art. 3:120 Wet IB 2001) if the property is the owner's primary residence (eigen woning)
- Afkoopsom for erfpacht is NOT directly deductible but may be treated as acquisition cost
- Canon increases are immediately deductible in the year of payment
- Note: deductibility of eigenwoningkosten (including canon) is being gradually limited (Wet Hillen afbouw)

### Impact on Property Valuation
- Properties on erfpacht are generally valued LOWER than vol eigendom properties
- The discount depends on:
  - Type of erfpacht (eeuwigdurend afkoop = smallest discount; tijdelijk approaching expiry = largest)
  - Current and expected canon amounts
  - Remaining term / revision date proximity
- Mortgage lenders may apply a lower loan-to-value ratio for erfpacht properties
- Some banks refuse to finance properties with unfavorable erfpacht conditions
- WOZ value includes erfpacht in the valuation (gemeente takes erfpacht into account)

### Afkoopsom Calculation
- The afkoopsom (lump sum to buy off future canon) is calculated as:
  - Net present value (netto contante waarde) of all future annual canon payments
  - Using the canonical discount rate set by the municipality
  - For eeuwigdurende afkoop (Amsterdam AB2016): perpetuity calculation
- Typical amounts in Amsterdam (2024/2025 - verify for current year):
  - Can range from EUR 10,000 to EUR 200,000+ depending on property and location
  - Financing of the afkoopsom is possible through mortgage

## Notarial Requirements and Kadaster Registration

### Vestiging (Establishment)
- Erfpacht is established by **notariele akte** (notarial deed) and registration at the kadaster (Art. 3:98 jo. 3:89 BW)
- The vestigingsakte must contain:
  - Identity of grondeigenaar and erfpachter
  - Description of the property (kadastrale aanduiding)
  - Duration of the erfpacht
  - Canon amount and payment terms
  - Applicable erfpachtvoorwaarden (or reference to Algemene Bepalingen)

### Overdracht (Transfer)
- Transfer of erfpacht requires:
  - Notariele akte van levering
  - Registration at kadaster
  - Consent of grondeigenaar if toestemmingsvereiste applies (Art. 5:91 BW)
- The notaris must verify all erfpacht conditions and any canon arrears
- Outstanding canon debt transfers with the right (potential liability for buyer)

### Wijziging (Amendment)
- Changes to erfpacht conditions require agreement of both parties
- Documented in a notarial deed of amendment (akte van wijziging)
- Must be registered at kadaster to have effect against third parties

### Opzegging (Termination)
- By erfpachter: notification to grondeigenaar (Art. 5:87 lid 1 BW)
- By grondeigenaar: only for serious breach or 2+ years' canon arrears (Art. 5:87 lid 2 BW)
- Court intervention required for grondeigenaar-initiated termination
- Upon termination: settlement of compensation for buildings/improvements (Art. 5:99 BW)

## Practical Guidance

### For Buyers
- Always request the erfpachtakte and applicable Algemene Bepalingen before making an offer
- Calculate the total housing costs including canon (not just mortgage costs)
- Factor in potential canon increases when assessing affordability
- Consider the afkoop option if financially feasible
- Ensure your mortgage advisor understands erfpacht implications

### For Sellers
- Provide all erfpacht documentation to the buyer proactively
- If canon revision is imminent, be transparent about expected increases
- Consider converting to AB2016 before selling (Amsterdam) to increase marketability
- Disclose any known issues with the erfpacht (mededelingsplicht)

### For Advisors
- Verify erfpacht conditions with the kadaster (not just the seller's documents)
- Check for any pending canonical herziening or overstapaanbieding
- Assess the impact of erfpacht on the client's total financial picture
- Consider tax implications of canon vs. afkoop for the client's specific situation
