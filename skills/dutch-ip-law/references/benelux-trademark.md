# BVIE - Benelux Convention on Intellectual Property (Trademarks and Designs)

## Legal Framework
- **Benelux-Verdrag inzake de Intellectuele Eigendom (BVIE)** - Benelux IP Convention (2005, entered into force 2006)
- Covers both **trademarks** (merken) and **designs** (tekeningen en modellen) in the Benelux territory (Belgium, Netherlands, Luxembourg)
- **Benelux Office for Intellectual Property (BOIP / BBIE)** - Registration office in The Hague
- Coexists with EU Trademark (EUTM) system via EUIPO

---

## Benelux Trademark (Benelux-merk)

### Art. 2.1 BVIE - What Can Be a Trademark
A trademark is a sign capable of:
1. Distinguishing goods or services of one undertaking from those of other undertakings
2. Being represented in the Benelux trademark register in a manner enabling determination of the precise subject of protection

Signs include: words, names, letters, numerals, colors, figurative elements, shapes, sounds, patterns, and combinations thereof.

### Art. 2.2 BVIE - Absolute Grounds for Refusal
A sign cannot be registered as a trademark if it:
- Lacks distinctive character (onderscheidend vermogen)
- Is purely descriptive of the goods/services
- Has become customary in the relevant trade
- Is deceptive (misleidend)
- Is contrary to public order or morality
- Consists exclusively of a functional shape or characteristic

### Art. 2.3 BVIE - Acquired Distinctiveness
A sign that lacks inherent distinctiveness can be registered if it has acquired distinctiveness through use (inburgering).

## Registration Process

### Filing
1. **Application** at BOIP (online via boip.int)
2. **Classification** of goods/services per Nice Classification
3. **Formal examination** by BOIP (completeness, absolute grounds)
4. **Publication** for opposition purposes
5. **Opposition period**: 2 months from publication
6. **Registration** if no opposition or opposition unsuccessful

### Timeline
- Approximately 4 months from filing to registration (if no opposition)
- Filing fee: from EUR 244 (online, one class) + EUR 27 per additional class

### Duration and Renewal
- **Art. 2.9 BVIE**: Registration valid for 10 years from filing date
- Renewable indefinitely for successive 10-year periods
- Renewal fee payable within 6 months before expiry (grace period of 6 months after expiry with surcharge)

## Rights of the Trademark Holder

### Art. 2.20 BVIE - Exclusive Rights
The holder can oppose:

1. **Double identity** (lid 1 sub a): Use of an identical sign for identical goods/services
2. **Likelihood of confusion** (lid 1 sub b): Use of an identical or similar sign for identical or similar goods/services where there exists a likelihood of confusion (verwarringsgevaar)
3. **Reputation/dilution** (lid 1 sub c): Use of an identical or similar sign for any goods/services where the mark has a reputation (bekendheid) in the Benelux and the use without due cause takes unfair advantage of, or is detrimental to, the distinctive character or repute of the mark
4. **Non-commercial use** (lid 1 sub d): Use of an identical or similar sign other than for distinguishing goods/services if without due cause and taking unfair advantage or being detrimental

### Art. 2.23 BVIE - Limitations
The holder cannot oppose:
- Use of the user's own name or address (in accordance with honest practices)
- Use of descriptive indications (quality, quantity, intended purpose, geographic origin, etc.)
- Use necessary to indicate the intended purpose of a product/service (e.g., spare parts, compatible with)
- Referential use in comparative advertising (within Directive 2006/114/EC)

### Art. 2.23 BVIE - Exhaustion
Trademark rights exhausted for goods put on the market in the EEA by or with consent of the holder. Exception: legitimate reasons to oppose further commercialization (e.g., condition of goods changed after being placed on the market).

## Trademark Use Requirement

### Art. 2.26 BVIE - Revocation for Non-Use
- Trademark can be revoked if not genuinely used in the Benelux for a continuous period of **5 years**
- Use must be genuine (werkelijk gebruik) - token use insufficient
- Use in one Benelux country suffices for the entire Benelux
- Burden of proof of use: on the trademark holder

---

## Opposition and Cancellation

### Opposition (Oppositie - Art. 2.14 BVIE)
- Filed at BOIP within 2 months of publication
- Grounds: prior trademark rights (earlier Benelux, EU, or international registration)
- BOIP decides; appeal to Benelux Gerechtshof (Benelux Court of Justice)

### Cancellation (Doorhaling)
- **Invalidity** (nietigheid, Art. 2.28): absolute or relative grounds
- **Revocation** (vervallenverklaring, Art. 2.26): non-use, deceptiveness, genericization
- Can be requested at BOIP or court

## Benelux Design (Benelux-tekening of -model)

### Art. 3.1 BVIE - Protection
Design protection for the appearance of a product or part thereof (lines, contours, colors, shape, texture, materials).

### Requirements:
- **Novelty** (nieuwheid): not identical to any design made available to the public
- **Individual character** (eigen karakter): overall impression differs from any earlier design

### Duration:
- 5 years from filing, renewable up to maximum **25 years** (5 x 5-year periods)

### Unregistered Design:
- 3 years of protection against copying (not against independent creation) - EU Unregistered Community Design

## Trademark Landscape Options

| Option | Territory | Office | Duration |
|--------|-----------|--------|----------|
| Benelux trademark | BE, NL, LU | BOIP | 10 years, renewable |
| EU Trademark (EUTM) | All 27 EU states | EUIPO | 10 years, renewable |
| International (Madrid) | Designated countries | WIPO | 10 years, renewable |
| National (if available) | Single country | National office | Varies |

### Choosing Between Benelux and EUTM
- **Benelux**: Lower cost, faster, sufficient if only Benelux market is relevant. Use as basis for Madrid Protocol international extension.
- **EUTM**: Single registration covering all EU. Higher cost, but more cost-effective if broad EU coverage needed. Vulnerability: can be challenged/revoked for the entire EU.
- **Strategy**: Often both Benelux AND EUTM filed for maximum protection.

## Enforcement

### Court Proceedings
- Civil courts (Rechtbank) for infringement actions
- **Kort geding** (preliminary injunction) frequently used in trademark disputes
- Customs detention: EU Regulation 608/2013 for seizure of counterfeit goods at borders

### Remedies
- Injunction (verbod op inbreuk)
- Damages or account of profits
- Recall, removal from channels, destruction of infringing goods
- Publication of judgment
- Reasonable legal costs (based on Indicatietarieven IE-zaken)

### Benelux Court of Justice (Benelux-Gerechtshof)
- Interprets BVIE provisions
- Preliminary questions from national courts
- Appeals from BOIP opposition decisions
