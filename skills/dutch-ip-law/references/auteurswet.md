# Dutch Copyright Act (Auteurswet) - Key Provisions

## Overview
The **Auteurswet (Aw)** is the Dutch Copyright Act, originally enacted in 1912 and amended numerous times to implement EU directives. It protects works of literature, science, and art (werken van letterkunde, wetenschap of kunst).

---

## Key Provisions

### Art. 1 - Definition of Copyright
Copyright is the exclusive right of the author (maker) of a work of literature, science, or art, or their successors in title, to make that work public (openbaar maken) and to reproduce it (verveelvoudigen), subject to the limitations prescribed by law.

### Art. 2 - Transfer of Copyright
- Copyright is transferable by **akte** (written deed) - formal requirement
- Transfer of copyright requires a written document specifically mentioning the transfer
- Mere delivery of a physical copy does not transfer copyright
- Partial transfer is possible (e.g., for specific exploitation forms, territories, or time periods)

### Art. 3 - Co-authorship
- If a work is created by two or more persons as a joint work (gemeenschappelijk werk), copyright belongs to the co-authors jointly
- Cannot be exercised without consent of all co-authors (unless unreasonably withheld)

---

## Ownership Rules

### Art. 4 - Maker (Author)
- The **maker** is the person who created the work
- If no name is indicated on the work: the person who introduces the work to the public is deemed the maker (until proven otherwise)
- If the work is published under a pseudonym: the publisher is deemed the authorized representative

### Art. 6 - Works Made Under Commission (Opdrachtgever)
- If a work is made following a design by another person and under that person's direction and supervision (ontwerp en leiding en toezicht), that person is the maker
- **Important**: This article has a narrow scope; simply commissioning a work does NOT transfer copyright to the commissioner
- A freelance designer or developer retains copyright unless explicitly assigned by akte

### Art. 7 - Works Made in Employment (Werkgever)
- If labor performed in the service of another consists of the making of certain works of literature, science, or art, the **employer** is deemed the maker of those works, unless otherwise agreed
- Requirements: (1) employment relationship, (2) making the work was part of the job duties
- Does NOT apply to commissioned works by independent contractors (ZZP'ers)

### Art. 8 - Legal Entity as Author
- A **legal entity** (rechtspersoon) that makes a work public as its own without naming a natural person as the maker, is deemed to be the maker
- Often applied to compilations, databases, and corporate publications

---

## Exclusive Rights

### Art. 12 - Right of Publication (Openbaarmakingsrecht)
Making a work public includes:
- Distribution of copies
- Public performance (opvoering, voordracht)
- Broadcasting (uitzending)
- Making available online (beschikbaarstelling via internet)

### Art. 13 - Right of Reproduction (Verveelvoudigingsrecht)
Reproduction includes:
- Copying (kopieen maken)
- Translation
- Adaptation (bewerking)
- Any fixation in any form

### Art. 14 - Derivative Works (Bewerkingen)
- Adaptations, arrangements, translations, and other transformations are protected as separate works
- BUT: creating them without permission infringes the original author's copyright

---

## Moral Rights (Persoonlijkheidsrechten - Art. 25)

### Art. 25 - Moral Rights
Even after transfer of copyright, the author retains:
1. **Right of attribution** (recht op naamsvermelding) - right to be identified as author
2. **Right to object to modifications** - right to object to changes that could harm the author's reputation or honor
3. **Right to object to distortion** (recht zich te verzetten tegen misvorming/verminking)
4. **Right to object to publication under another's name**

### Waiver of Moral Rights
- Art. 25 lid 3: The author may waive moral rights under (1), (2), and (4) to the extent permitted
- Right to object to distortion/mutilation that harms reputation (Art. 25 lid 1 sub d) **cannot be waived**
- Waiver must be specific (general blanket waiver may not be enforceable)

---

## Limitations and Exceptions

### Art. 15 - Quotation Right (Citaatrecht)
- Quotation for purposes of critique, polemic, review, or scientific discourse is permitted
- Source and author must be mentioned
- Quotation must be proportionate

### Art. 15a - News Reporting
- Reproduction for reporting on current events is permitted

### Art. 16 - Education
- Reproduction for educational purposes (within limits set by ministerial regulation)

### Art. 16b - Private Copy (Thuiskopie)
- Reproduction for personal use, study, or practice
- Compensated via the thuiskopieheffing (private copying levy) collected by Stichting de Thuiskopie

### Art. 16c - Reprographic Reproduction
- Photocopying of limited portions for own use

### Art. 13a - Temporary Technical Copies
- Temporary copies that are transient or incidental, part of a technological process, with no independent economic significance

### Art. 18a - Parody
- Use of a work for purposes of caricature, parody, or pastiche (implementing InfoSoc Directive Art. 5(3)(k))

---

## Duration (Art. 37-39)

- **General rule**: 70 years after the death of the author (Art. 37)
- **Anonymous/pseudonymous works**: 70 years after first lawful publication (Art. 38)
- **Works by legal entities (Art. 8)**: 70 years after first lawful publication (Art. 38)
- **Joint works**: 70 years after death of the last surviving co-author

---

## Enforcement

### Art. 26d - Injunction (Verbod)
- Author can obtain injunction against infringement

### Art. 27 - Damages
- Infringer liable for damages (schadevergoeding)
- Court may also order:
  - Account of profits (winstafdracht)
  - Recall and destruction of infringing copies
  - Publication of the judgment

### Art. 28 - Seizure (Beslag)
- Infringing copies may be seized by the copyright holder

### Art. 31-32 - Criminal Sanctions
- Willful copyright infringement is a criminal offense (overtreding)
- Fine of up to EUR 22,500 and/or detention of up to 6 months
- Commercial-scale infringement: higher penalties

---

## Software Copyright (Art. 45h-45n)

Specific provisions for computer programs (implementing EU Software Directive 2009/24/EC):
- **Art. 45h**: Computer programs are protected as literary works
- **Art. 45i**: Lawful user may make backup copy, observe/study/test functionality
- **Art. 45j**: Decompilation (reverse engineering) permitted only for interoperability purposes
- **Art. 45k**: Contractual provisions contrary to Arts. 45i-45j are null and void

### Important for Software:
- **Art. 7 Aw** applies: employer owns software copyright if created by employee as part of duties
- For independent developers: copyright stays with the developer unless explicitly transferred by akte
- Open source license compliance is a copyright matter under Dutch law
