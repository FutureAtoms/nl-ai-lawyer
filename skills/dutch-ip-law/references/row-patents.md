# Rijksoctrooiwet 1995 (ROW) - Dutch Patent Act Overview

## Legal Framework
- **Rijksoctrooiwet 1995 (ROW 1995)** - National patent law
- **European Patent Convention (EPC)** - European patent applications via EPO (Munich)
- **Unitary Patent / Unified Patent Court (UPC)** - Since June 2023 (Netherlands participates)
- **Patent Cooperation Treaty (PCT)** - International patent applications
- **Octrooicentrum Nederland / Netherlands Patent Office (part of RVO)** - National patent office

---

## Patent Requirements (Art. 2-6 ROW)

### Art. 2 ROW - Patentable Inventions
Patents are granted for inventions that are:
1. **Nieuw** (Novel) - not part of the state of the art (stand van de techniek)
2. **Inventief** (Inventive step) - not obvious to a person skilled in the art
3. **Industrieel toepasbaar** (Industrially applicable) - can be made or used in any industry

### Art. 2 lid 2 - Not Patentable
The following are NOT regarded as inventions:
- Discoveries, scientific theories, mathematical methods
- Aesthetic creations
- Schemes, rules, methods for mental acts, games, business activities
- Computer programs **as such** (but technical effects of software may be patentable)
- Presentations of information

### Art. 3 ROW - Exceptions to Patentability
No patents for:
- Inventions contrary to public order or morality (openbare orde of goede zeden)
- Plant or animal varieties (but biological processes may be patentable)
- Essentially biological processes for producing plants or animals
- Methods of medical treatment of the human or animal body (but medical products/devices are patentable)

### Art. 4 ROW - Novelty (Nieuwheid)
- Invention is novel if not part of the state of the art
- State of the art: everything made available to the public before the filing date (priority date)
- Includes: publications, public use, oral disclosures, earlier patent applications
- **Grace period**: None under Dutch/European patent law (unlike US). Any disclosure before filing destroys novelty.

### Art. 6 ROW - Inventive Step (Inventiviteit)
- Invention involves an inventive step if, having regard to the state of the art, it is not obvious to a person skilled in the art
- Assessment method: Problem-solution approach (EPO method applied by Dutch courts)

## Types of Dutch Patents

### 1. National Patent (Nederlands octrooi)
- Filed at Octrooicentrum Nederland
- **6-year patent** (Art. 2 lid 1): Granted after formal examination only (no substantive examination of novelty/inventive step); valid for 6 years from filing
- **20-year patent** (Art. 2a): Requires substantive examination; valid for 20 years from filing
- Since 2008: applicants choose between the two tracks

### 2. European Patent (Europees octrooi)
- Filed at EPO under the EPC
- Substantively examined by EPO
- Once granted: must be validated in the Netherlands (translation requirements, fee)
- After validation: treated as a Dutch patent
- Duration: 20 years from filing date

### 3. European Patent with Unitary Effect (Unitair octrooi)
- Since June 2023 (UPC Agreement)
- Single patent covering all participating EU member states
- Filed at EPO; unitary effect requested after grant
- Enforced through the Unified Patent Court (UPC)
- Netherlands participates in the UPC system

## Ownership (Art. 12 ROW)

### Art. 12 - Employer Ownership
- Inventions made by an employee are owned by the **employer** if:
  - The nature of the employment involves creating inventions, OR
  - The invention was made in the context of the employment
- Employee entitled to **billijke vergoeding** (fair compensation) if the invention's importance exceeds what could reasonably be expected given the employee's salary and conditions

### Art. 12 lid 6 - Commissioner
- Inventions made under a commission: patent right belongs to the **commissioner** (opdrachtgever), unless otherwise agreed
- This differs from copyright law (where the contractor retains copyright)

### Art. 12 lid 3 - University Employees
- Special rules for university researchers: the university is the patent holder, but the researcher has certain rights

---

## Rights of the Patent Holder (Art. 53 ROW)

### Art. 53 - Exclusive Rights
The patent holder has the exclusive right to:
1. **Make** (vervaardigen) the patented product
2. **Use** (gebruiken) the patented product or process
3. **Sell, offer for sale, or supply** (verkopen, te koop aanbieden, afleveren)
4. **Import** (invoeren) into the Netherlands
5. **Stock** (in voorraad hebben) for the above purposes

### Art. 53a - Process Patents
For process patents: the patent extends to products directly obtained by the patented process

### Art. 53b - Indirect Infringement
Supplying essential elements (wezenlijke bestanddelen) of the invention to a non-licensee, knowing or having reason to know they will be used for patent infringement

---

## Exceptions and Limitations

### Art. 53 lid 3 - Exhaustion
- Patent rights exhausted after first sale in the EEA by or with consent of the patent holder

### Art. 53 lid 4 - Exceptions
- Private, non-commercial use
- Experimental use (proefnemingen) relating to the subject matter of the invention
- Preparation of medications in pharmacies for individual prescriptions (magistrale bereiding)
- Prior use right (voorgebruiksrecht, Art. 55): person who was using the invention before the filing date may continue

### Art. 57 ROW - Compulsory License
- Can be granted by the court if:
  - Patent is not sufficiently worked in the Netherlands
  - Public interest requires it
  - Dependent patent situation

## Enforcement

### Jurisdiction
- **Rechtbank Den Haag**: Exclusive first-instance jurisdiction for patent infringement in the Netherlands (Art. 80 ROW)
- **Gerechtshof Den Haag**: Appeal
- **Unified Patent Court (UPC)**: For European patents with unitary effect and opted-in European patents

### Remedies
- Injunction (verbod)
- Damages (schadevergoeding) or account of profits (winstafdracht)
- Recall and destruction of infringing products
- Publication of judgment
- Preliminary injunction (kort geding) for urgent cases
- Cross-border injunctions possible under certain circumstances (GAT/LuK, Solvay/Honeywell CJEU case law)

### Validity Challenge
- Patents can be challenged via:
  - Nullity proceedings (nietigheidsactie) at Rechtbank Den Haag
  - Opposition at EPO (within 9 months of grant for European patents)
  - As a defense in infringement proceedings

---

## Supplementary Protection Certificate (SPC / ABC)
- **Aanvullend beschermingscertificaat**: Extends patent protection for up to 5 years for medicinal and plant protection products
- Compensates for regulatory approval delays
- Filed at Octrooicentrum Nederland

---

## Practical Notes
- Patent applications must be filed BEFORE any public disclosure
- European patent route is most common for commercially significant inventions
- Patent attorneys (octrooigemachtigden) are required for EPO proceedings
- Search prior art databases (Espacenet, Google Patents) before filing
- Consider both national and UPC routes for enforcement strategy
