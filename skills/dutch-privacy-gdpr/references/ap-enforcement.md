# Autoriteit Persoonsgegevens (AP) - Enforcement Practice

## About the AP
The **Autoriteit Persoonsgegevens (AP)** is the Dutch independent supervisory authority for data protection, established under Art. 51 AVG and the UAVG.

- **Chair**: Aleid Wolfsen (as of latest information)
- **Website**: autoriteitpersoonsgegevens.nl
- **Address**: The Hague (Den Haag)

---

## Powers of the AP

### Investigative Powers (Art. 58(1) AVG)
- Order controllers and processors to provide information
- Carry out data protection audits
- Access premises and data processing equipment
- Obtain access to personal data and information needed for supervisory tasks

### Corrective Powers (Art. 58(2) AVG)
- Issue warnings (waarschuwing)
- Issue reprimands (berisping)
- Order compliance with data subject requests
- Order rectification, erasure, or restriction of processing
- Impose temporary or definitive ban on processing
- Order suspension of data flows to third country
- **Impose administrative fines** (bestuurlijke boete):
  - Up to EUR 10 million or 2% worldwide annual turnover (for procedural violations)
  - Up to EUR 20 million or 4% worldwide annual turnover (for substantive violations)
- **Impose penalty payments** (last onder dwangsom) for ongoing non-compliance

## AP Enforcement Strategy

### Priority Areas (Focus Gebieden)
The AP publishes annual focus areas. Recent priorities include:
1. **Data trading and profiling** - Online tracking, real-time bidding, adtech
2. **Government and digital government** - Processing of citizen data by public authorities
3. **Healthcare data** - Security of medical records, health apps
4. **Children's privacy** - Social media, educational technology
5. **Employee data** - Employee monitoring, HR analytics
6. **AI and algorithms** - Automated decision-making, facial recognition
7. **International data transfers** - Post-Schrems II enforcement

### Enforcement Procedure
1. **Signal/Complaint** - AP receives a complaint from data subject, notification from other DPA, or initiates own investigation
2. **Preliminary investigation** - AP assesses whether further action warranted
3. **Investigation** - Formal investigation with information requests, access to systems
4. **Report of findings** (rapport van bevindingen) - Shared with the organization
5. **View/intention** (voornemen) - AP communicates intention to impose sanction
6. **Hearing** (zienswijze) - Organization may respond
7. **Decision** (besluit) - AP imposes fine or other corrective measure
8. **Objection** (bezwaar) - Organization may object to the AP itself
9. **Appeal** (beroep) - To the Rechtbank (administrative law section)
10. **Higher appeal** (hoger beroep) - To the Afdeling Bestuursrechtspraak van de Raad van State (ABRvS)

### Key Enforcement Themes

#### Cookie Compliance
- AP strictly enforces cookie consent requirements (Art. 11.7a Telecommunicatiewet)
- Tracking cookies require prior informed consent
- Cookie walls (requiring consent as condition for access) generally not permitted
- AP has investigated multiple websites and ad-tech companies

#### Government Data Processing
- AP has been active in investigating government processing:
  - SyRI case (Systeem Risico Indicatie) - struck down by court as violating ECHR
  - Toeslagenaffaire (childcare benefit scandal) - discriminatory data processing
  - Municipal data processing (WiFi tracking, camera surveillance)

#### Employee Monitoring
- AP guidance on employee monitoring:
  - Must have legal basis (legitimate interest, with DPIA)
  - Must be proportionate and subsidiair
  - Covert monitoring only in exceptional circumstances
  - Biometric data for access: requires DPIA, consent often insufficient due to employment relationship

#### Healthcare
- AP has investigated hospitals and healthcare providers for data breaches
- Focus on electronic health record (EPD) access logging
- Unauthorized access by healthcare personnel to patient records

### AP Fine Calculation Method
The AP uses the following framework:
1. **Base category** (I-IV) based on nature and severity of the violation
   - Category I: EUR 0 - 200,000
   - Category II: EUR 120,000 - 500,000
   - Category III: EUR 300,000 - 750,000
   - Category IV: EUR 450,000 - 1,000,000 (or up to EUR 20M / 4% turnover)
2. **Aggravating factors**: Duration, number of data subjects, intent, previous violations, non-cooperation
3. **Mitigating factors**: Cooperation, remediation, first offense, small enterprise

## Practical Tips for Compliance

1. **Document everything**: Records of processing, DPIAs, legitimate interest assessments, consent records
2. **Respond to data subject requests promptly**: Within 1 month (extendable by 2 months)
3. **Report data breaches within 72 hours**: Use the AP's online notification form
4. **Appoint a DPO if required**: Register with the AP
5. **Monitor AP publications**: The AP regularly updates its guidance and enforcement priorities
6. **Conduct DPIAs proactively**: Do not wait for AP to require it
7. **Review international transfers**: Ensure adequate safeguards post-Schrems II
