## 1. Fiscale Eenheid (Fiscal Unity) -- Art. 15 Wet Vpb 1969

### 1.1 HR 17 december 2004, ECLI:NL:HR:2004:AQ3810
- **Court**: Hoge Raad (belastingkamer)
- **Date**: 17 December 2004
- **Key principle**: Requirements for maintaining a fiscale eenheid: the parent company must hold at least 95% of the legal and economic ownership of shares. The HR addressed what constitutes sufficient "juridische en economische eigendom" and when interposition of intermediate entities breaks the chain.
- **Relevance**: Foundational for structuring corporate groups under the fiscal unity regime.

### 1.2 HR 22 februari 2008, ECLI:NL:HR:2008:BB6186
- **Court**: Hoge Raad (belastingkamer)
- **Date**: 22 February 2008
- **Key principle**: Consequences of involuntary dissolution ("ontvoeging") of a fiscal unity due to share disposal. The HR clarified the timing of de-consolidation and the treatment of intercompany transactions at the moment of dissolution.
- **Relevance**: Critical for M&A transactions involving fiscale eenheid members.

### 1.3 CJEU 22 February 2018, C-398/16 and C-399/16 (X BV and X NV)
- **Court**: Court of Justice of the European Union
- **Date**: 22 February 2018
- **Key principle**: The Dutch fiscal unity rules had to be extended to include certain cross-border situations (per-element approach) to comply with EU freedom of establishment. This forced the Dutch legislator to adopt "spoedreparatiemaatregelen" effective 1 January 2018.
- **Relevance**: Led to major changes in the Dutch fiscal unity regime; essential EU law overlay.

## 2. Fraus Legis (Abuse of Law in Tax Structuring)

### 2.1 HR 26 mei 1926, B.3874 (Cursus Belastingrecht -- origin of fraus legis)
- **Court**: Hoge Raad (belastingkamer)
- **Date**: 26 May 1926
- **Key principle**: Original articulation of the fraus legis doctrine: a taxpayer's transaction may be disregarded for tax purposes if (a) the transaction has no business purpose other than tax avoidance (doorslaggevende beweegreden), and (b) it conflicts with the object and purpose (doel en strekking) of the tax law.
- **Relevance**: The foundational Dutch anti-avoidance doctrine, still applied today.

### 2.2 HR 21 november 1984, ECLI:NL:HR:1984:BH7867 (Cessna-arrest)
- **Court**: Hoge Raad (belastingkamer)
- **Date**: 21 November 1984
- **Key principle**: Application of fraus legis to a circular financing structure designed solely to generate deductible interest. The HR set the standard that the "doorslaggevende beweegreden" (predominant motive) for the transaction must be tax avoidance.
- **Relevance**: Core precedent on the subjective test element of fraus legis.

### 2.3 HR 10 juli 2009, ECLI:NL:HR:2009:BJ2199
- **Court**: Hoge Raad (belastingkamer)
- **Date**: 10 July 2009
- **Key principle**: Fraus legis applied to a leveraged buyout structure where the acquisition debt was artificially routed through the Netherlands. The HR reaffirmed the two-pronged test: (1) predominant tax motive and (2) conflict with the purpose of the law.
- **Relevance**: Modern application of fraus legis in acquisition financing.

## 3. 30% Ruling (30%-regeling) -- Art. 31a Wet LB 1964

### 3.1 HR 28 april 2006, ECLI:NL:HR:2006:AU2304
- **Court**: Hoge Raad (belastingkamer)
- **Date**: 28 April 2006
- **Key principle**: The "specifieke deskundigheid" (specific expertise) requirement for the 30% ruling: the employee must possess expertise that is scarce or not available on the Dutch labor market. The HR upheld the Belastingdienst's assessment that this must be objectively demonstrable.
- **Relevance**: Guides eligibility assessments for incoming expat employees.

### 3.2 Gerechtshof Den Haag 22 maart 2017, ECLI:NL:GHDHA:2017:721
- **Court**: Gerechtshof Den Haag
- **Date**: 22 March 2017
- **Key principle**: Whether the salary threshold (minimum inkomensnorm) for the 30% ruling must be applied pro rata when an employee starts mid-year. The court confirmed that the statutory threshold applies on an annualized basis.
- **Relevance**: Practical impact on structuring start dates for incoming knowledge migrants.

### 3.3 HR 14 oktober 2022, ECLI:NL:HR:2022:1436
- **Court**: Hoge Raad (belastingkamer)
- **Date**: 14 October 2022
- **Key principle**: Reduction of the 30% ruling duration from 8 to 5 years and the transitional provisions. The HR considered whether legitimate expectations were violated for taxpayers who had relied on the prior 8-year term.
- **Relevance**: Key for advising expats on the reduced duration and any grandfathering provisions.

## 4. Deelnemingsvrijstelling (Participation Exemption) -- Art. 13 Wet Vpb 1969

### 4.1 HR 22 november 2002, ECLI:NL:HR:2002:AD8488 (Falcons-arrest)
- **Court**: Hoge Raad (belastingkamer)
- **Date**: 22 November 2002
- **Key principle**: The distinction between a "deelneming" (qualifying participation of 5%+) and a "belegging" (portfolio investment). The HR introduced the "deelnemingsoogmerk" test: whether the shareholding is held with the aim of serving the enterprise of the holder through a durable connection.
- **Relevance**: Fundamental for determining when the participation exemption applies to dividends and capital gains.

### 4.2 HR 8 april 2022, ECLI:NL:HR:2022:526
- **Court**: Hoge Raad (belastingkamer)
- **Date**: 8 April 2022
- **Key principle**: Application of the compartimenteringsleer: when a shareholding transitions from qualifying to non-qualifying (or vice versa) for the deelnemingsvrijstelling, the capital gains or losses must be allocated to the respective periods.
- **Relevance**: Essential for M&A tax planning involving changes in participation percentage.

## 5. Cross-Border Loss Relief and EU Freedoms

### 5.1 CJEU 13 December 2005, C-446/03 (Marks & Spencer)
- **Court**: Court of Justice of the European Union
- **Date**: 13 December 2005
- **Key principle**: A Member State may restrict cross-border loss relief (group relief) to domestic subsidiaries, but not where the losses are "final" -- i.e., the subsidiary has exhausted all possibilities to use the losses in its state of residence. The "final losses" exception applies.
- **Relevance**: Landmark CJEU ruling influencing Dutch corporate tax treatment of foreign subsidiary losses; directly relevant to Art. 13d and 13e Wet Vpb 1969.

### 5.2 CJEU 21 February 2013, C-123/11 (A Oy)
- **Court**: Court of Justice of the European Union
- **Date**: 21 February 2013
- **Key principle**: Clarification and narrowing of the Marks & Spencer "final losses" doctrine for cross-border mergers. The CJEU held that the host state may require proof that losses are truly final.
- **Relevance**: Dutch Vpb practice applies this ruling when Dutch parent companies claim deductions for losses of EU subsidiaries.

## 6. VAT / BTW -- Deduction Rights and Abuse

### 6.1 CJEU 21 February 2006, C-255/02 (Halifax)
- **Court**: Court of Justice of the European Union
- **Date**: 21 February 2006
- **Key principle**: The principle of prohibition of abuse of rights in VAT law. Transactions carried out solely to obtain a tax advantage (BTW deduction) that would be contrary to the purpose of the VAT Directive may be disregarded.
- **Relevance**: Applied by Dutch courts to deny input VAT deduction in artificial structures.

### 6.2 HR 2 december 2011, ECLI:NL:HR:2011:BQ7099
- **Court**: Hoge Raad (belastingkamer)
- **Date**: 2 December 2011
- **Key principle**: Right to deduct input BTW (voorbelasting) in the context of mixed-use immovable property. The HR addressed how to calculate the pro rata deduction when a building is used partly for taxed and partly for exempt supplies.
- **Relevance**: Key for real estate developers and investors dealing with BTW on mixed-use properties.

### 6.3 CJEU 16 October 2019, C-329/18 (Altic)
- **Court**: Court of Justice of the European Union
- **Date**: 16 October 2019
- **Key principle**: The taxable person's obligation to verify the reliability of their supplier in the context of carousel fraud (BTW-carrouselfraude). The CJEU ruled that knowledge or constructive knowledge of participation in fraud can result in denial of the right to deduct input VAT.
- **Relevance**: Applied in Dutch BTW fraud cases where the Belastingdienst denies deduction.

## 7. Transfer Pricing and Arm's Length (Art. 8b Wet Vpb 1969)

### 7.1 HR 22 januari 2010, ECLI:NL:HR:2010:BK6053
- **Court**: Hoge Raad (belastingkamer)
- **Date**: 22 January 2010
- **Key principle**: Application of the arm's length principle (Art. 8b Wet Vpb 1969) to intercompany transactions within a multinational group. The HR confirmed that the Belastingdienst may make a transfer pricing adjustment when the conditions of an intercompany transaction deviate from what unrelated parties would have agreed under comparable circumstances, following the OECD Transfer Pricing Guidelines.
- **Relevance**: Core precedent on transfer pricing disputes in the Netherlands.

### 7.2 HR 18 december 2015, ECLI:NL:HR:2015:3603
- **Court**: Hoge Raad (belastingkamer)
- **Date**: 18 December 2015
- **Key principle**: Whether informal capital (informeel kapitaal) arises when a parent company provides a benefit to its subsidiary at non-arm's length terms. The HR held that where a shareholder-related benefit exists, the arm's length principle requires an adjustment to the taxable profit of the subsidiary, and the excess benefit must be treated as a capital contribution.
- **Relevance**: Governs the intersection of transfer pricing and informal capital doctrine.
