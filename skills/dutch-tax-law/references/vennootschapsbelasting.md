# Corporate Income Tax (Vennootschapsbelasting - Wet VPB 1969)

## Scope

### Taxpayers (Art. 2-3 Wet VPB)
- **Resident taxpayers** (binnenlandse belastingplichtigen): entities established in the Netherlands
  - BV, NV, cooperatie, stichting (if conducting enterprise), open CV, open FGR
- **Non-resident taxpayers** (buitenlandse belastingplichtigen): foreign entities with Dutch-source income
  - Permanent establishment (vaste inrichting) income
  - Dutch real estate income
  - Certain Dutch-source portfolio income

### Tax Rates (current as of 2024)
| Taxable income | Rate |
|---------------|------|
| Up to EUR 200,000 | 19% |
| Above EUR 200,000 | 25.8% |

*Note: Rates and brackets are adjusted regularly; verify current year rates.*

---

## Taxable Base (Belastbare winst)

### Art. 7-8 Wet VPB - General
- Taxable profit = total profit minus deductible losses from previous years
- Total profit determined according to **goed koopmansgebruik** (sound commercial practice) - the Dutch equivalent of accounting standards for tax purposes
- Starting point: commercial accounting profit, with tax adjustments

### Key Adjustments
- Depreciation differences (fiscale afschrijving vs. commercieel)
- Non-deductible expenses
- Participation exemption income (exempt)
- Innovation box income (reduced rate)
- Loss carry-forward limitations

---

## Participation Exemption (Deelnemingsvrijstelling - Art. 13 Wet VPB)

### Purpose
Prevents economic double taxation of corporate profits flowing through a corporate chain.

### Requirements
- Taxpayer holds at least **5% of the nominal paid-up share capital** of the subsidiary
- Not held as a portfolio investment (belegging)

### Effect
- All benefits from the participation are **exempt** from VPB:
  - Dividends received
  - Capital gains on disposal of shares
  - Write-downs are not deductible (corresponding to the exemption)

### Anti-abuse (Art. 13 lid 9-17)
- **Niet-kwalificerende beleggingsdeelneming**: If the subsidiary is a passive investment vehicle without substance, the exemption may not apply
- Low-taxed (< 9% effective rate) + predominantly passive income = potential denial of participation exemption (but subject to substance-over-form test)

---

## Fiscal Unity (Fiscale eenheid - Art. 15 Wet VPB)

### Concept
Parent and one or more subsidiaries are treated as a single taxpayer for VPB purposes.

### Requirements
- Parent must hold at least **95%** of shares (juridisch en economisch eigendom) in the subsidiary
- Both parent and subsidiary must be Dutch tax-resident (or have a Dutch PE)
- Same financial year
- Same profit determination methods
- Request must be filed with the Belastingdienst

### Benefits
- Consolidation of profits and losses within the group
- Intercompany transactions are ignored for tax purposes
- Single tax return filed by the parent
- Intercompany asset transfers without immediate recognition

### Risks/Limitations
- Joint and several liability for the entire group's tax debt
- Entry and exit consequences (voegings- and ontvoeging effects)
- Cross-border fiscal unity: limited (following CJEU case law, only for definitive losses of EU subsidiaries in certain circumstances)

## Innovation Box (Innovatiebox - Art. 12b Wet VPB)

### Purpose
Reduced effective tax rate for profits from qualifying innovative activities.

### Effective Rate
- **9%** (instead of the standard rate) on qualifying innovation box profits

### Qualifying IP
- Patented inventions (or inventions with positive R&D declaration - S&O-verklaring)
- IP from R&D activities for which an S&O-verklaring has been issued
- Software copyright developed by the taxpayer
- Plant variety rights
- Medicinal product marketing authorizations
- Other IP with S&O-verklaring

### Nexus Approach (modified nexus)
- Qualifying profits limited by the nexus ratio: own R&D expenditure / total R&D expenditure (including outsourced and acquired IP)
- Uplift of 30% for own R&D expenditure

### Threshold
- First EUR 25,000 of qualifying profits automatically taxed at innovation box rate (forfeitsregeling)
- Above EUR 25,000: taxpayer must calculate qualifying profits using direct or indirect profit allocation method

## Loss Compensation (Verliesverrekening - Art. 20 Wet VPB)

### Rules (since 2022)
- **Carry-back**: 1 year
- **Carry-forward**: Indefinite (no time limit)
- **Limitation**: Losses can be fully offset against the first EUR 1 million of taxable profit. Above EUR 1 million, only 50% of the excess can be offset.

### Example
If taxable profit is EUR 3 million and available losses are EUR 5 million:
- First EUR 1 million: fully offset
- Remaining EUR 2 million: 50% = EUR 1 million offset
- Total offset: EUR 2 million
- Remaining taxable: EUR 1 million
- Remaining loss carry-forward: EUR 3 million

## Anti-Avoidance Provisions

### Art. 10a Wet VPB - Anti-Base Erosion (Interest Deduction Limitation)
- Interest on loans from related parties is not deductible if the loan is connected to certain tainted transactions (related-party acquisitions, capital contributions, profit distributions)
- Unless: business motives test OR compensating levy test is met

### Art. 10b Wet VPB - Hybrid Loans
- Interest on certain hybrid loans (loans with profit-linked features, > 50 years, subordinated) is treated as non-deductible dividend

### Art. 15b Wet VPB - Earnings Stripping (ATAD Implementation)
- Net interest expense deductible only up to the higher of:
  - 20% of fiscal EBITDA, OR
  - EUR 1 million (threshold)
- Group exemption and specific exemptions available
- Applies to both related-party and third-party interest

### Art. 15c-15e - CFC Rules (ATAD)
- Controlled Foreign Company rules for low-taxed entities
- Undistributed income of CFC included in Dutch parent's taxable base

### Fraus Legis (General Anti-Abuse Doctrine)
- Court-developed doctrine: transactions undertaken predominantly for tax avoidance that are contrary to the purpose and spirit of the law may be recharacterized

## Key Compliance

### Filing
- Annual VPB return (aangifte) filed with the Belastingdienst
- Financial year = calendar year (unless deviating financial year approved)
- Filing deadline: 5 months after financial year end (extensions available, typically up to 12 months)
- Corporate taxpayers generally must file electronically (XBRL format)

### Transfer Pricing
- Arm's length principle (Art. 8b Wet VPB, following OECD Guidelines)
- Documentation obligation (TP documentation master file and local file required above certain thresholds)
- Country-by-Country Reporting (CbCR) for groups with consolidated revenue > EUR 750 million

### 30% Ruling Interaction
The 30% ruling (extraterritoriale kostenregeling) is technically a payroll tax measure, but affects VPB through the deductibility of salary costs. See immigration law skill for details.
