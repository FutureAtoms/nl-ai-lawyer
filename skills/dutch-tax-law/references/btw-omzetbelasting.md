# VAT / Turnover Tax (BTW / Omzetbelasting - Wet OB 1968)

## Legal Framework
- **Wet op de omzetbelasting 1968 (Wet OB)** - Dutch VAT Act
- **EU VAT Directive 2006/112/EC** - Harmonized EU VAT system
- **Uitvoeringsbesluit OB** - VAT Implementation Decree
- **Uitvoeringsbeschikking OB** - VAT Implementation Regulation
- Dutch VAT law must be interpreted in conformity with the EU VAT Directive and CJEU case law

---

## VAT Rates (Current)

| Rate | Percentage | Applicable to |
|------|-----------|--------------|
| Standard (algemeen tarief) | 21% | Most goods and services |
| Reduced (verlaagd tarief) | 9% | Food, water, medicines, books, hotels, cultural events, passenger transport, repair of bicycles/clothing/shoes |
| Zero rate (nultarief) | 0% | Exports, intra-EU supplies, certain international services |
| Exemption (vrijstelling) | N/A | Healthcare, education, financial services, insurance, immovable property (rental/sale with exceptions) |

## Scope of Dutch VAT (Art. 1 Wet OB)

VAT is levied on:
1. **Supplies of goods** (leveringen van goederen) within the Netherlands by an entrepreneur
2. **Supplies of services** (diensten) within the Netherlands by an entrepreneur
3. **Intra-EU acquisitions** (intracommunautaire verwervingen) in the Netherlands
4. **Imports** (invoer) from outside the EU

### Art. 7 Wet OB - Entrepreneur (Ondernemer)
- Anyone who independently carries out a business or professional activity
- Includes: companies, freelancers, partnerships, foundations (if conducting business activities)
- Does NOT include: employees, public authorities (for certain activities)

## Key VAT Principles

### Place of Supply Rules

#### Goods
- **Art. 5 Wet OB**: Supply where transportation begins
- **Intra-EU supply** (intracommunautaire levering): 0% rate in NL; acquisition taxed in destination member state
- **Export** (uitvoer): 0% rate

#### Services (Art. 6 Wet OB)
- **B2B (default)**: Place where recipient is established (Art. 6 lid 1 Wet OB)
- **B2C (default)**: Place where supplier is established (Art. 6 lid 2 Wet OB)
- **Exceptions**:
  - Immovable property services: where the property is located
  - Passenger transport: where transport takes place
  - Cultural/entertainment events: where the event takes place
  - Restaurant/catering: where physically provided
  - Electronic services (B2C): where customer resides (One-Stop Shop/OSS)

### Input VAT Deduction (Voorbelasting - Art. 15 Wet OB)
- Entrepreneurs can deduct VAT charged to them on business purchases
- Requirements:
  1. Valid VAT invoice
  2. Goods/services used for VAT-taxable business activities
  3. Supply actually took place
- Mixed use: pro rata deduction based on taxable vs. exempt activities (pro rata berekening)
- Correction for private use (privégebruik correctie)

### Reverse Charge (Verleggingsregeling - Art. 12 Wet OB)
- Recipient accounts for VAT instead of supplier in certain situations:
  - Intra-EU B2B services
  - Certain domestic supplies (construction, temporary agency work, certain waste materials)
  - Supplies by non-resident entrepreneurs to Dutch businesses

## VAT Exemptions (Art. 11 Wet OB)

### Without Input VAT Recovery (Oneigenlijke vrijstelling)
- Medical and healthcare services (Art. 11 lid 1 sub g)
- Education (Art. 11 lid 1 sub o)
- Financial services (Art. 11 lid 1 sub i/j)
- Insurance and reinsurance (Art. 11 lid 1 sub k)
- Immovable property rental and sale (Art. 11 lid 1 sub a/b) - with opt-in possibilities
- Postal services
- Betting and gambling

### With Input VAT Recovery (Eigenlijke vrijstelling / Nultarief)
- Exports (uitvoer)
- Intra-EU supplies of goods
- International transport
- Supplies to vessels and aircraft

---

## Immovable Property and VAT

### Sale of Immovable Property
- **Exempt** by default (Art. 11 lid 1 sub a Wet OB)
- **Taxed at 21%** for:
  - New buildings (delivered before, on, or within 2 years after first occupation)
  - Building land
- **Opt-in** (optie belaste levering): parties can jointly opt for VAT-taxed transfer if buyer uses the property for at least 90% VAT-taxable activities (Art. 11 lid 1 sub a, 2e Wet OB)
  - If opted: 21% VAT instead of 10.4% overdrachtsbelasting (which is then exempted by Art. 15 lid 1 sub a WBR)

### Rental of Immovable Property
- **Exempt** by default (Art. 11 lid 1 sub b Wet OB)
- **Opt-in** (optie belaste verhuur): landlord and tenant can jointly opt for VAT-taxed rental if tenant uses premises for at least 90% VAT-taxable activities
  - Benefit: landlord can deduct input VAT on construction/renovation; tenant deducts rental VAT
  - 90% requirement monitored annually; correction if not met

## Intra-EU Trade

### Intra-Community Supply (ICS / Intracommunautaire levering)
- 0% rate if:
  1. Goods physically transported to another EU member state
  2. Customer has valid VAT identification number in another member state
  3. Supplier has proof of transport
  4. Included in recapitulative statement (opgaaf ICP)

### Intra-Community Acquisition (ICA / Intracommunautaire verwerving)
- Buyer accounts for VAT in the Netherlands (Art. 17a-17h Wet OB)
- Simultaneously deductible as input VAT (if used for taxable activities)
- Net effect: zero (but must be properly accounted for in VAT return)

### Recapitulative Statement (Opgaaf ICP)
- Monthly or quarterly filing listing all intra-EU supplies and services

## VAT Registration and Compliance

### VAT Number (BTW-nummer)
- Format: NL + 9 digits + B + 2 digits (e.g., NL123456789B01)
- Sole proprietors also receive a separate btw-id for invoicing (no BSN)

### VAT Returns (BTW-aangifte)
| Category | Filing Frequency |
|----------|-----------------|
| Standard | Quarterly |
| Large businesses (> EUR 15,000/quarter or > EUR 50,000/year VAT due) | Monthly |
| Small businesses (< EUR 1,883 VAT per year) | Annual (kleineondernemersregeling, if applicable) |

### Filing Deadline
- Last business day of the month following the period (e.g., Q1 return due by April 30)
- Electronic filing mandatory

### Small Business Scheme (Kleineondernemersregeling - KOR)
- Turnover threshold: EUR 20,000 per year
- If below threshold: can apply for exemption from VAT
- No VAT charged, no input VAT deducted, no VAT returns required
- Not available to entities (only to natural persons and certain partnerships)
- *Note: Since 2020, the new KOR replaced the old deduction-based system*

## Import VAT

### Article 23 Permit (Vergunning art. 23 Wet OB)
- Allows licensed importers to account for import VAT through the VAT return (verlegging) instead of paying at the border
- Significant cash flow advantage
- Available to regular importers with good compliance history

---

## Common VAT Issues

### Invoice Requirements (Art. 35a Wet OB)
A valid VAT invoice must contain:
- Date of issue and sequential number
- Supplier's name, address, and VAT number
- Customer's name and address (and VAT number for reverse charge)
- Description of goods/services
- Quantity and nature
- Date of supply
- Taxable amount per rate
- VAT rate and amount
- Total including VAT

### Correction of Errors (Suppletie)
- Errors in previous VAT returns: voluntary disclosure (suppletieaangifte) can be filed
- No penalty if disclosed voluntarily before discovery by Belastingdienst (for errors < EUR 20,000, can be corrected in next regular return)
