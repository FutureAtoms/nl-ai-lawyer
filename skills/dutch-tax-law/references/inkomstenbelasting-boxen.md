# Dutch Income Tax Box System (Inkomstenbelasting - Boxenstelsel)

## Legal Framework
- **Wet inkomstenbelasting 2001 (Wet IB 2001)**: Primary legislation governing income tax
- The Dutch income tax system uses a **boxenstelsel** (box system) that categorizes income into three separate boxes
- Each box has its own tax rates and rules; losses in one box generally cannot be offset against income in another box
- Annual income tax return: **aangifte inkomstenbelasting** filed with the Belastingdienst

---

## Box 1: Inkomen uit Werk en Woning (Income from Employment and Home)

### Categories of Box 1 Income
1. **Loon uit dienstbetrekking** (employment income): salary, bonuses, benefits in kind, pension income
2. **Winst uit onderneming** (business profits): income from sole proprietorship or partnership
3. **Resultaat uit overige werkzaamheden** (income from other activities): freelance income, ROW
4. **Periodieke uitkeringen en verstrekkingen** (periodic payments): alimony, social security benefits
5. **Inkomsten uit eigen woning** (income from own home): eigenwoningforfait minus hypotheekrenteaftrek

### Progressive Tax Rates (Schijventarief)

#### 2024 Rates (verify for current year)
| Taxable Income (belastbaar inkomen) | Rate |
|-------------------------------------|------|
| Up to EUR 75,518 | 36.97% |
| Above EUR 75,518 | 49.50% |

- These rates include premies volksverzekeringen (AOW, Anw, Wlz) in the first bracket
- For taxpayers born before 1 January 1946 or who have reached AOW age: lower first bracket rate (approximately 19.07%) because AOW premies no longer apply
- Rates are inclusive of the solidariteitsbijdrage (which was previously separate)

#### 2025 Rates (verify for current year)
| Taxable Income | Rate |
|----------------|------|
| Up to approximately EUR 76,817 | 35.82% (subject to final legislation) |
| Above approximately EUR 76,817 | 49.50% |

- Bracket boundaries are indexed annually

### Eigen Woning (Own Home - Art. 3:110-3:123a Wet IB 2001)

#### Eigenwoningforfait (Imputed Rental Value)
- Homeowners must add a deemed income (eigenwoningforfait) based on the WOZ value of their home:

| WOZ Value | Eigenwoningforfait Rate (2024) |
|-----------|-------------------------------|
| Up to EUR 12,500 | 0% |
| EUR 12,500 - EUR 25,000 | 0.10% |
| EUR 25,000 - EUR 50,000 | 0.20% |
| EUR 50,000 - EUR 75,000 | 0.25% |
| EUR 75,000 - EUR 1,200,000 | 0.35% |
| Above EUR 1,200,000 | EUR 4,200 + 2.35% of excess |

#### Hypotheekrenteaftrek (Mortgage Interest Deduction)
- Interest on a mortgage for the eigen woning is deductible from Box 1 income
- **Maximum deduction rate**: 36.97% in 2024 (previously higher; has been gradually reduced since 2014)
- The aftrek has been limited over the years (Wet Hillen is being phased out)
- Conditions for deductibility:
  - The loan must be for acquisition, improvement, or maintenance of the eigen woning
  - Since 2013: new mortgages must be at least annuitair aflossend (annuity repayment) within 30 years to qualify
  - Maximum deduction period: 30 years per loan
- **Wet Hillen**: previously, homeowners with no/low mortgage debt received a full deduction for eigenwoningforfait. This is being phased out (afbouw) over 30 years starting 2019

#### Bijleenregeling (Art. 3:119aa Wet IB 2001)
- When selling one home and buying another: any profit from the sale must be reinvested in the new home
- Failure to reinvest reduces the maximum mortgage eligible for hypotheekrenteaftrek
- Prevents homeowners from extracting equity and maintaining full deduction

### Winst uit Onderneming (Business Profits)
- Self-employed individuals (IB-ondernemers) report business profits in Box 1
- Available deductions and facilities:
  - **Zelfstandigenaftrek**: EUR 3,750 (2024, being phased out - verify annually)
  - **Startersaftrek**: EUR 2,123 additional deduction for the first 3 years
  - **MKB-winstvrijstelling**: 13.31% of profits exempt (2024 - verify, being reduced)
  - **FOR (Fiscale oudedagsreserve)**: Reserved for retirement (being phased out from 2023)
  - **Investeringsaftrek (KIA, EIA, MIA)**: Deductions for qualifying investments

## Box 2: Inkomen uit Aanmerkelijk Belang (Income from Substantial Shareholding)

### What Is an Aanmerkelijk Belang? (Art. 4.6-4.7 Wet IB 2001)
- A taxpayer has an aanmerkelijk belang if they hold (directly or indirectly):
  - **5% or more** of the shares in a company (BV or NV)
  - **5% or more** of the profit-sharing certificates (winstbewijzen)
  - Options to acquire 5% or more of the shares
- Also applies to shares held by a **fiscaal partner** (together 5%+)
- Applies to both Dutch and foreign companies

### Box 2 Tax Rates

#### 2024 Rates (verify for current year)
| Taxable Income from Aanmerkelijk Belang | Rate |
|----------------------------------------|------|
| Up to EUR 67,000 | 24.5% |
| Above EUR 67,000 | 33% |

- The EUR 67,000 threshold applies per taxpayer (not per company)
- For fiscal partners: combined threshold of EUR 134,000

#### 2025 Rates
| Taxable Income | Rate |
|----------------|------|
| Up to EUR 67,000 | 24.5% |
| Above EUR 67,000 | 33% |

### Types of Box 2 Income
1. **Reguliere voordelen**: Dividends (dividenduitkeringen) from the BV
2. **Vervreemdingsvoordelen**: Capital gains on the sale of shares
3. **Fictieve vervreemding**: Deemed disposal upon emigration or other events

### DGA Salary Requirements (Gebruikelijk loon - Art. 12a Wet LB 1964)

#### The Customary Salary Rule
- A **DGA** (directeur-grootaandeelhouder / director-major shareholder) must receive a salary that is "customary" for the work performed
- Minimum customary salary (2024): **EUR 56,000** (verify annually - indexed)
- The salary must be set at the highest of:
  1. 100% of the salary of the most comparable employment position
  2. The highest salary of other employees of the company (or related companies)
  3. EUR 56,000 (2024 minimum)
- The DGA may argue for a lower salary only if 100% of comparable salary is lower, and then the salary can be set at 75% of comparable salary (doelmatigheidsmarge)

#### Planning Considerations
- DGA salary is taxed in Box 1 (progressive rates up to 49.50%)
- Profits retained in the BV are taxed at corporate rates (vennootschapsbelasting: 19% up to EUR 200,000 / 25.8% above)
- Distribution as dividend is taxed in Box 2 (24.5% / 33%)
- Combined tax burden of corporate tax + Box 2 vs. Box 1 must be compared for optimization
- **Excessief lenen bij eigen vennootschap**: Since 2023, loans from own BV exceeding EUR 500,000 are treated as a deemed Box 2 distribution (Wet excessief lenen)

### AB Claim and Emigration
- Upon emigration: a **conserverende aanslag** (preserving assessment) is imposed on unrealized capital gains in Box 2
- Within the EU/EEA: payment is deferred (no guarantee required)
- Outside EU/EEA: payment may be deferred for max 10 years with interest and security
- Tax treaties may limit the Netherlands' ability to tax upon actual disposal after emigration

## Box 3: Inkomen uit Sparen en Beleggen (Income from Savings and Investments)

### The Box 3 System - Major Reform Post-Kerstarrest

#### The Kerstarrest (HR 24 December 2021, ECLI:NL:HR:2021:1963)
- The Hoge Raad ruled that the old Box 3 system (forfaitaire rendementsheffing) violated:
  - **Art. 1 Eerste Protocol EVRM** (right to property)
  - **Art. 14 EVRM** (prohibition of discrimination)
- The old system taxed a fictional return regardless of actual return earned
- This led to a fundamental overhaul of Box 3
- Affected taxpayers (2017-2022) are entitled to compensation through **rechtsherstel** (legal redress)

#### Current System: Transitional Rules (Overbruggingswetgeving, 2023-2026)
Until the new Box 3 system takes effect (expected 2027 at earliest), a transitional system applies:

##### Three Asset Categories
1. **Banktegoeden** (bank deposits): savings accounts, current accounts at banks
   - Deemed return rate (2024): approximately 1.03% (based on average interest rates - verify annually)
2. **Overige bezittingen** (other assets): investments in shares, bonds, real estate (not eigen woning), crypto, claims, etc.
   - Deemed return rate (2024): approximately 6.04% (verify annually)
3. **Schulden** (debts): deductible liabilities
   - Deemed interest rate (2024): approximately 2.47% (verify annually)

##### Calculation Method
1. Determine total assets in each category as of **1 January** (peildatum)
2. Apply the deemed return percentage to each category
3. Calculate the weighted average return (gewogen gemiddeld forfaitair rendement) based on the actual composition of assets
4. Subtract the heffingvrij vermogen (tax-free threshold)
5. Apply the Box 3 tax rate to the calculated income

##### Heffingvrij Vermogen (Tax-Free Threshold)
- **EUR 57,000 per person** (2024 - verify annually)
- **EUR 114,000 for fiscal partners** combined
- Applied to the grondslag sparen en beleggen (the net taxable base)

##### Box 3 Tax Rate
- **36%** (2024 - verify annually)
- The rate has been increasing in recent years (was 31% in 2021, 32% in 2023)

#### Planned New System (Wet werkelijk rendement, expected 2027+)
- Taxation based on **actual returns** (werkelijk rendement) rather than fictional returns
- Will include: realized and unrealized capital gains, dividends, interest, rental income
- Bank deposits may still use a deemed return (forfait)
- Significant legislative complexity; implementation has been delayed multiple times
- Current expectation: effective from 2027 or 2028 (verify for actual timeline)

### What Falls Under Box 3?
**Included:**
- Bank savings accounts (banktegoeden)
- Listed and unlisted shares (effecten) not qualifying as aanmerkelijk belang
- Bonds (obligaties)
- Investment funds (beleggingsfondsen)
- Real estate NOT being the eigen woning (beleggingspanden, second homes)
- Cryptocurrency and digital assets
- Life insurance policies (kapitaalverzekeringen) not in Box 1
- Receivables (vorderingen)
- Foreign assets (worldwide)

**Excluded (taxed elsewhere or exempt):**
- Eigen woning (Box 1)
- Aanmerkelijk belang shares (Box 2)
- Business assets (Box 1 winst uit onderneming)
- Certain exempt assets: art/antiques for personal use, green investments (vrijgesteld), etc.
- Pension rights, AOW, lijfrente

### Debts Deductible in Box 3
- Debts are deductible from Box 3 assets, reducing the tax base
- A **drempel** (threshold) of EUR 3,700 per person (EUR 7,400 for partners) applies: debts below this amount are not deductible (2024 - verify)
- Mortgage on eigen woning is NOT a Box 3 debt (it is in Box 1)
- Student debt (DUO studieschuld) is a Box 3 debt

## General Provisions

### Fiscaal Partnerschap (Art. 5a Wet IB 2001)
- Married couples and registered partners are mandatory fiscal partners
- Cohabiting couples with a notarized cohabitation agreement (samenlevingscontract) and shared household are fiscal partners
- Partners may allocate Box 2 and Box 3 income between them in the most favorable ratio
- The heffingvrij vermogen and Box 2/3 thresholds are doubled for partners

### Heffingskortingen (Tax Credits - Art. 8.1 e.v. Wet IB 2001)
- **Algemene heffingskorting**: General tax credit (EUR 3,362 in 2024 - income dependent, phased out above EUR 75,518)
- **Arbeidskorting**: Employment tax credit (max EUR 5,532 in 2024, income dependent)
- **Inkomensafhankelijke combinatiekorting (IACK)**: For working parents with young children
- **Ouderenkorting**: For taxpayers above AOW age
- **Jonggehandicaptenkorting**: For young disabled persons (Wajong)
- Tax credits reduce the total tax payable (not the taxable income)

### Voorlopige Aanslag and Aangifte
- **Voorlopige aanslag**: Preliminary tax assessment based on estimated income (monthly payments)
- **Definitieve aanslag**: Final assessment after filing the annual return
- Filing deadline: generally 1 May of the following year (uitstel possible)
- Digital filing via Mijn Belastingdienst portal

### Interaction Between Boxes
- Income is allocated to the box based on the source, not by taxpayer choice
- No loss offsetting between boxes (verliesverrekening only within the same box, subject to specific rules)
- Box 1 losses: carryback 3 years, carryforward 9 years (for business losses - winstverlies)
- Box 2 losses: carryback 1 year, carryforward 6 years
- Box 3 losses: no carryforward/carryback (but negative income leads to no Box 3 tax)

## Practical Guidance

### For Employees
- Box 1 is the most relevant: salary is taxed progressively
- Maximize hypotheekrenteaftrek if you have a mortgage on your eigen woning
- Consider whether voluntary pension contributions (lijfrente) can reduce Box 1 income
- Heffingskortingen are applied automatically by the employer for loonheffing purposes

### For DGAs
- Balance between Box 1 salary (DGA loon) and Box 2 dividends
- Consider the combined tax burden: VPB + Box 2 vs. Box 1 marginal rate
- Be aware of the Wet excessief lenen: keep loans from own BV below EUR 500,000
- Plan dividend distributions to stay within the 24.5% bracket (EUR 67,000 per person / EUR 134,000 for partners)

### For Investors / Savers
- Box 3 composition matters under the transitional rules: banktegoeden are taxed at a much lower deemed return than overige bezittingen
- Restructuring assets between bank deposits and investments can significantly affect Box 3 tax
- Fiscal partners should optimize the allocation of Box 3 assets between them
- Consider whether assets can be moved to a BV (Box 2 structure) for tax efficiency
