## 1. Kennismigrant (Highly Skilled Migrant) -- Salary Threshold Disputes

### 1.1 ABRvS 25 maart 2013, ECLI:NL:RVS:2013:BZ7441
- **Court**: Afdeling bestuursrechtspraak van de Raad van State (ABRvS)
- **Date**: 25 March 2013
- **Key principle**: Interpretation of the salary threshold requirement for the kennismigrantenregeling. The ABRvS confirmed that the salary threshold must be met at the time of application and must be an actual, unconditional salary (not including variable components such as bonuses or stock options, unless guaranteed).
- **Relevance**: Foundational for employers structuring compensation packages for knowledge migrants.

### 1.2 ABRvS 5 juni 2019, ECLI:NL:RVS:2019:1844
- **Court**: ABRvS
- **Date**: 5 June 2019
- **Key principle**: Whether holiday allowance (vakantiegeld) of 8% may be included in the calculation of the gross annual salary threshold for the kennismigrantenregeling. The ABRvS ruled that the statutory vakantiegeld may be included if it is contractually guaranteed and actually paid.
- **Relevance**: Practical impact on borderline salary threshold calculations.

### 1.3 ABRvS 11 december 2019, ECLI:NL:RVS:2019:4165
- **Court**: ABRvS
- **Date**: 11 December 2019
- **Key principle**: Revocation of a kennismigrant permit due to the employer's failure to continue paying the minimum salary threshold after the employee's role changed. The ABRvS held that the IND may revoke (intrekken) the permit if the salary drops below the threshold, even temporarily.
- **Relevance**: Warning for employers: continuous compliance with the salary threshold is mandatory.

## 2. MVV (Machtiging tot Voorlopig Verblijf) Refusal and Appeal

### 2.1 ABRvS 6 februari 2008, ECLI:NL:RVS:2008:BC4636
- **Court**: ABRvS
- **Date**: 6 February 2008
- **Key principle**: The scope of judicial review of MVV refusals. The ABRvS confirmed that courts apply a full review (volledige toetsing) of factual and legal grounds, including proportionality, when assessing MVV refusals -- not merely a marginal review.
- **Relevance**: Ensures meaningful judicial protection for MVV applicants.

### 2.2 ABRvS 22 februari 2012, ECLI:NL:RVS:2012:BV6928
- **Court**: ABRvS
- **Date**: 22 February 2012
- **Key principle**: The hardheidsclausule (hardship clause) in MVV refusals under Art. 3.71 lid 4 Vb 2000: the IND has discretion to waive the MVV requirement where refusal would lead to a "onbillijkheid van overwegende aard" (injustice of a preponderant nature). The ABRvS held that the court can review this discretion on proportionality grounds.
- **Relevance**: Safety valve for exceptional cases where strict application of MVV requirements would cause disproportionate hardship.

## 3. Family Reunification -- Income Requirements

### 3.1 CJEU 4 March 2010, C-578/08 (Chakroun)
- **Court**: Court of Justice of the European Union
- **Date**: 4 March 2010
- **Key principle**: The income threshold for family reunification under Directive 2003/86/EC must not be applied in a way that undermines the directive's objective of promoting family reunification. The CJEU ruled that the Netherlands could not apply a higher income threshold for family formation (gezinsvorming) than for family reunification (gezinshereniging) where the directive does not make such a distinction. Member States must assess income on an individual basis.
- **Relevance**: Landmark CJEU ruling that forced the Netherlands to align its income requirements for family migration; directly affected Art. 3.22 Vb 2000.

### 3.2 ABRvS 4 april 2012, ECLI:NL:RVS:2012:BW0791
- **Court**: ABRvS
- **Date**: 4 April 2012
- **Key principle**: Implementation of Chakroun in Dutch practice. The ABRvS confirmed that the IND must apply the norm of 100% of the minimum wage (not 120%) for family reunification and must take into account the stability and regularity of income, not just the absolute amount at the time of application.
- **Relevance**: Operationalizes Chakroun in Dutch administrative practice.

### 3.3 ABRvS 26 maart 2014, ECLI:NL:RVS:2014:1145
- **Court**: ABRvS
- **Date**: 26 March 2014
- **Key principle**: Whether a self-employed sponsor (zelfstandige) can meet the income requirement for family reunification based on projected future income. The ABRvS held that self-employed income may qualify, provided that the sponsor can demonstrate stable and sufficient income over the preceding period and a realistic prospect of continued income.
- **Relevance**: Important for entrepreneurs and freelancers sponsoring family members.

## 4. IND Processing Delays

### 4.1 ABRvS 30 mei 2018, ECLI:NL:RVS:2018:1778
- **Court**: ABRvS
- **Date**: 30 May 2018
- **Key principle**: Application of the dwangsom bij niet tijdig beslissen (Art. 4:17 Awb) to IND decisions. The ABRvS confirmed that the IND is subject to the same dwangsom regime as other administrative bodies when it fails to decide within the statutory decision period (beslistermijn).
- **Relevance**: Empowers applicants to enforce timely IND decisions through financial penalties.

### 4.2 Rb. Den Haag (zittingsplaats Middelburg) 10 september 2021, ECLI:NL:RBDHA:2021:10102
- **Court**: Rechtbank Den Haag
- **Date**: 10 September 2021
- **Key principle**: Systemic IND processing delays in asylum and regular migration cases. The court found that prolonged delays (exceeding the statutory term by months) constitute a failure to act (niet tijdig beslissen) and ordered the IND to decide within a specified period, on pain of a dwangsom.
- **Relevance**: Part of a broader pattern of judicial intervention addressing the IND's structural capacity problems.

## 5. Interaction Between 30% Ruling and Immigration Status

### 5.1 Gerechtshof Den Haag 30 januari 2018, ECLI:NL:GHDHA:2018:148
- **Court**: Gerechtshof Den Haag
- **Date**: 30 January 2018
- **Key principle**: The interaction between the tax law 30% ruling and the immigration law kennismigrantenregeling. The court addressed whether loss of the 30% ruling due to a salary recalculation (netting-up) affects the immigration status if the gross salary before the ruling's benefit falls below the kennismigrant threshold.
- **Relevance**: Warns employers and employees about the tax-immigration nexus: a change in tax treatment can trigger immigration consequences.

### 5.2 ABRvS 18 juli 2018, ECLI:NL:RVS:2018:2410
- **Court**: ABRvS
- **Date**: 18 July 2018
- **Key principle**: Whether the reduced salary threshold for kennismigranten under 30 years of age applies continuously or only at the time of first admission. The ABRvS confirmed that the age-specific threshold applies at the time of the application, and the migrant does not need to shift to the higher threshold upon turning 30 during the permit's validity.
- **Relevance**: Practical certainty for young knowledge migrants and their employers.

## 6. Asylum and International Protection (Supplementary References)

### 6.1 ABRvS 13 april 2016, ECLI:NL:RVS:2016:890
- **Court**: ABRvS
- **Date**: 13 April 2016
- **Key principle**: The credibility assessment framework in asylum cases. The ABRvS refined the "integrale geloofwaardigheidsbeoordeling" (integral credibility assessment) methodology, requiring the IND to consider the applicant's account as a whole rather than rejecting it based on isolated inconsistencies.
- **Relevance**: Foundational for asylum credibility assessments under Art. 31 Vw 2000.

### 6.2 CJEU 2 March 2021, C-18/20 (XY -- Staat der Nederlanden)
- **Court**: Court of Justice of the European Union
- **Date**: 2 March 2021
- **Key principle**: Procedural guarantees in subsequent asylum applications (herhaalde aanvraag). The CJEU ruled that the right to be heard (Art. 41 EU Charter) must be respected before an application is declared inadmissible as a subsequent application, and that new elements must be substantively assessed.
- **Relevance**: Strengthens procedural protections for repeat asylum seekers in the Netherlands.

## 7. Intra-Corporate Transfer (ICT) and EU Blue Card

### 7.1 ABRvS 10 oktober 2018, ECLI:NL:RVS:2018:3285
- **Court**: ABRvS
- **Date**: 10 October 2018
- **Key principle**: Interpretation of the intra-corporate transfer (ICT) directive (2014/66/EU) requirements as implemented in the Netherlands. The ABRvS addressed whether a third-country national transferred to a Dutch branch of a multinational met the "specialist or manager" qualification threshold and the required period of prior employment with the group.
- **Relevance**: Guides companies using the ICT permit route for senior staff transfers.

### 7.2 CJEU 18 December 2014, C-202/13 (McCarthy -- free movement and family members)
- **Court**: Court of Justice of the European Union
- **Date**: 18 December 2014
- **Key principle**: The conditions under which third-country national family members of EU citizens are exempt from national visa and residence permit requirements when exercising the right of free movement. The CJEU clarified that Member States may verify identity documents but may not impose additional conditions on family members holding a valid residence card issued by another Member State.
- **Relevance**: Directly applicable in the Netherlands for EU citizens and their non-EU family members relying on Directive 2004/38/EC.
