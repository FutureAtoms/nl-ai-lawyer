# Expat Cross-Domain Workflow: BV Formation → IND Sponsorship → Employment → 30% Ruling

This reference covers the interconnected legal domains that expats encounter when relocating to the Netherlands for work, particularly through a Dutch BV structure.

---

## 1. BV Formation by Non-Residents / Expats

### Process and Timeline
1. **Engage a Dutch notaris** (civil-law notary) — mandatory for BV incorporation
2. **Legalization of identity documents** — apostille or legalization required for non-EU documents
3. **Draft articles of association** (statuten) — typically 1-2 weeks
4. **Execute notarial deed** — can be done by proxy (volmacht) if founder cannot be physically present
5. **KVK registration** — immediate upon incorporation (within 8 days, Art. 2:180 BW)
6. **Share capital deposit** — minimum EUR 0.01 (Flex-BV Act 2012); no bank statement required for cash contributions

### Costs (Indicative, 2025)
| Item | Cost Range |
|------|-----------|
| Notaris fees (incorporation) | EUR 500 - 2,500 |
| KVK registration | EUR 75 (one-time) |
| Share capital | EUR 0.01 minimum |
| Legal advice (optional) | EUR 1,000 - 5,000 |
| Apostille/legalization | EUR 50 - 300 |

### BSN Requirement
- A BSN (Burgerservicenummer) is **not required** to incorporate a BV
- However, a BSN **is required** for the director (bestuurder) to be registered at KVK with full details
- Non-residents can initially register as UBO without BSN, but must obtain BSN upon arrival in NL

**Cross-reference**: See `dutch-corporate-law/references/bv-nv-structure.md` for full BV formation requirements.

## 2. Recognized Sponsor (Erkend Referent) Application

### Who Can Apply
- Any Dutch legal entity (BV, NV, stichting, etc.) registered at KVK
- The entity must demonstrate: **continuity**, **solvency**, **reliability**, and **compliance history**
- Newly formed BVs **can** apply but face additional scrutiny on continuity and solvency

### Timeline and Costs
| Step | Duration | Cost |
|------|----------|------|
| Application to IND | — | EUR 4,588 (2025) |
| IND processing | 4-8 weeks (standard) | — |
| Registration in public register | Upon approval | — |

### Requirements for Newly Formed BVs
- IND will assess whether the BV has realistic business prospects
- Financial projections, client contracts, and business plan may be requested
- A BV with no employees and no revenue will face higher rejection risk
- Having at least one active client contract or revenue significantly strengthens the application

### Ongoing Obligations (Art. 2e Vw 2000)
- **Zorgplicht**: Ensure migrant workers can meet residence conditions
- **Informatieplicht**: Notify IND of changes within 4 weeks (termination, salary changes, departure)
- **Administratieplicht**: Maintain records for 5 years post-sponsorship

## 3. Self-Sponsorship: DGA Cannot Sponsor Themselves

### The Critical Rule
**A director-major shareholder (DGA / directeur-grootaandeelhouder) of their own BV CANNOT be sponsored by that BV as a kennismigrant.**

### Legal Basis
- Art. 1d BuWav exempts kennismigranten from the TWV (work permit) requirement
- However, the IND interprets the kennismigrant scheme as requiring a **genuine employer-employee relationship** (gezagsverhouding)
- A DGA who holds a majority stake (typically 50%+ or effective control) lacks the subordination element required for an employment relationship under Art. 7:610 BW
- The IND consistently denies kennismigrant applications where the applicant is also the controlling shareholder

### Alternatives for DGA/Founders
1. **Self-employed residence permit** (zelfstandige verblijfsvergunning) — scored on points system, requires viable business plan
2. **Start-up visa** (startupverblijfsvergunning) — requires facilitation by a recognized facilitator (erkend facilitator), 1-year permit
3. **Orientation year** (zoekjaar) — if graduated from NL institution or top-200 university within 3 years
4. **EU Blue Card** — does not solve the self-employment issue (also requires genuine employment)
5. **Hire a controlling co-founder** who holds majority stake, making the applicant a minority shareholder (risky restructuring)

### Practical Implications
- Founders who want to relocate to NL often need to be employed by **another** erkend referent (e.g., their own BV employs them but a different entity sponsors them — this generally fails the IND test)
- The safest route: be hired as a kennismigrant by an **unrelated** employer first, then start a BV on the side (check permit conditions for secondary activities)

## 4. Change of Employer: Dual Risk

### Immigration Risk (IND)
- Kennismigrant permit is tied to the sponsoring employer
- Changing employers requires a **new application** by the new employer (who must also be an erkend referent)
- There is **no automatic transfer** of the residence permit
- While the new application is pending, the worker may continue working for the new employer (Art. 3.31 Vb 2000) — but only if the application was filed **before** starting with the new employer
- Gap between employers without a pending application: work rights lapse immediately

### Tax Risk (30% Ruling)
- Changing employers requires a **new 30% ruling application** with the Belastingdienst
- The new application must be filed within **3 months** of the end of the old employment (Belastingdienst policy)
- The total 60-month period is **not** reset — the clock continues from the original start date
- If there is a gap of more than 3 months: the 30% ruling may be **permanently lost**
- The new employer must meet all conditions independently (salary threshold, etc.)

### Practical Checklist for Employer Change
1. Secure new employment contract **before** resigning (or within notice period)
2. Verify new employer is erkend referent (check IND public register)
3. New employer files IND application (kennismigrant VVB change of employer)
4. New employer files 30% ruling application with Belastingdienst
5. File both applications **within the first month** of new employment
6. Verify salary meets both IND threshold and 30% ruling threshold at new employer
7. Keep copies of all termination documents from old employer

## 5. Job Loss: Grace Period and Consequences

### 3-Month Search Period (Art. 3.31a Vb 2000)
- Upon termination of employment (by employer or by operation of law), the kennismigrant has a **3-month grace period**
- During this period:
  - The residence permit remains valid
  - The worker may seek new employment
  - No work is permitted unless a new application is pending
- After 3 months without new employment: IND may withdraw the residence permit

### Important Nuances
- The 3-month period starts from the **actual end date of employment** (not notice date)
- The former employer must notify IND of the termination within 4 weeks (informatieplicht)
- **Freelancing/ZZP is NOT permitted** on a kennismigrant permit — the permit is specifically for employment
- Starting a BV and self-employing does not maintain the kennismigrant permit (see self-sponsorship section above)

### 30% Ruling During Job Loss
- The 30% ruling continues to run during the gap (the 60-month clock does not stop)
- If new employment + new ruling application is filed within 3 months: ruling continuity is preserved
- If gap exceeds 3 months: the ruling is likely lost permanently

## 6. Application Timing: The 4-Month Rule

### 30% Ruling Application Deadline
- The joint application (employer + employee) must be filed within **4 months** of the first day of Dutch employment
- Filed within 4 months: ruling applies **retroactively** from the first day of employment
- Filed after 4 months: ruling applies from the first day of the **month following** the application — retroactive benefit is lost
- This can result in thousands of euros of lost tax benefit

### IND Application Timing
- No hard deadline for the IND kennismigrant application, but:
  - Non-EU nationals need a valid residence permit to work legally
  - Working without a valid permit is illegal for both employer and employee
  - Penalties: up to EUR 8,000 fine per violation for the employer (Wav)

### Coordinated Timeline (Ideal)
| Step | Timeline | Filed By |
|------|----------|----------|
| Employment contract signed | Day 0 | Employer + Employee |
| IND kennismigrant application | Day 1 - 14 | Employer (as erkend referent) |
| 30% ruling application | Day 1 - 120 (within 4 months) | Employer + Employee jointly |
| BSN registration at gemeente | Within 5 days of arrival | Employee |
| Health insurance (zorgverzekering) | Within 4 months of registration | Employee |

## 7. M-Form: Migration Year Tax Return

### What Is the M-Form?
- The **M-form** (M-biljet / Migratieformulier) is a special income tax return for the year of immigration to or emigration from the Netherlands
- It replaces the standard P-form (for residents) or C-form (for non-residents) for the migration year

### When to File
- For the calendar year in which the taxpayer became a Dutch resident (immigration) or ceased to be a resident (emigration)
- Filing deadline: same as regular tax returns (1 May of the following year, or later if extension granted)

### Key Aspects
- The year is split into:
  - **Non-resident period**: only Dutch-source income is taxable (buitenlands belastingplichtige)
  - **Resident period**: worldwide income is taxable (binnenlands belastingplichtige)
- **30% ruling**: if approved, applies from the first day of employment (or first of month after late application)
- **Mortgage interest deduction**: only for the resident period (and only for the eigenwoningregeling qualifying home)
- **Box 3**: assessed on reference date (1 January) — if not resident on 1 January, no Box 3 for non-resident period
- **Partieel buitenlands belastingplichtige**: if elected, applies from the start of the 30% ruling period
  - ⚠️ Abolished for new rulings from 1 January 2025 (Belastingplan 2025)

### Practical Tips
- Hire a tax advisor familiar with expat M-forms — errors are common and costly
- Gather all foreign income documentation for the non-resident period
- Report worldwide income for the resident period
- If arriving mid-year: Box 3 is often zero (reference date is 1 January)
- Coordination with home country tax return is essential (avoid double taxation)

## 8. Full Expat Workflow Timeline

```
Week 1-2:  BV incorporation (if applicable) — notaris + KVK
Week 2-3:  Erkend referent application filed with IND (if new sponsor)
Week 6-10: Erkend referent status granted (4-8 weeks processing)
Week 6-10: Kennismigrant TEV/VVB application filed
Week 8-12: Kennismigrant permit approved (2-4 weeks for erkend referent fast-track)
Week 8-12: Employee arrives in NL, registers at gemeente (BSN)
Week 8-16: 30% ruling application filed (within 4 months of employment start)
Week 12-20: 30% ruling approved (2-4 months processing)
Month 4:   Health insurance arranged (zorgverzekering)
Year 1:    M-form tax return filed for migration year
Year 5:    30% ruling expires — plan for increased tax burden
```
