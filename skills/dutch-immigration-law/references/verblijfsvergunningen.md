# Dutch Residence Permits (Verblijfsvergunningen)

## Overview of the Permit System

Dutch immigration law distinguishes between temporary residence permits (verblijfsvergunning
voor bepaalde tijd, VVB) and permanent residence permits (verblijfsvergunning voor onbepaalde
tijd, VVO). The legal basis is found in the Vreemdelingenwet 2000 (Vw 2000), Vreemdelingenbesluit
2000 (Vb 2000), and Vreemdelingencirculaire (Vc).

## Categories of Residence Permits

### 1. Reguliere Verblijfsvergunning (Regular Residence Permit)

Governed by **Art. 14 Vw 2000** (temporary) and **Art. 20 Vw 2000** (permanent).

**Purposes (verblijfsdoelen):**

| Purpose | Legal Basis (Vb) | Key Conditions |
|---------|-------------------|----------------|
| Work (arbeid in loondienst) | Art. 3.31 Vb | TWV or GVVA required; labor market test |
| Highly skilled migrant (kennismigrant) | Art. 3.30a Vb | Recognized sponsor; salary threshold |
| Study | Art. 3.41 Vb | Enrolled at designated institution; sufficient funds |
| Family reunification (gezinshereniging) | Art. 3.13-3.22 Vb | Income requirement; genuine relationship |
| Family formation (gezinsvorming) | Art. 3.14-3.22 Vb | Income 100% minimumloon; age 21+ |
| Self-employment (zelfstandig ondernemer) | Art. 3.30 Vb | Points system; essential interest for NL economy |
| Medical treatment | Art. 3.46 Vb | Treatment not available in home country |
| Au pair | Art. 3.43 Vb | Host family; age 18-30; max 1 year |
| Orientation year (zoekjaar) | Art. 3.42 Vb | Recent graduate Dutch institution or top-200 university |
| Exchange/cultural | Art. 3.43a Vb | Recognized program |

### 2. Asiel Verblijfsvergunning (Asylum Residence Permit)

Governed by **Art. 28 Vw 2000** (temporary) and **Art. 33 Vw 2000** (permanent).

**Grounds for asylum (Art. 29 Vw 2000):**
- **Art. 29 lid 1 sub a** - Refugee status: well-founded fear of persecution (Vluchtelingenverdrag)
- **Art. 29 lid 1 sub b** - Subsidiary protection: real risk of serious harm (Art. 3 EVRM)
- **Art. 29 lid 2** - Family reunification for asylum status holders (nareis)

**Key features:**
- Temporary asylum permit valid for 5 years (Art. 28 lid 2 Vw)
- After 5 years: eligible for permanent asylum permit (Art. 33 Vw)
- Housing, health insurance, and civic integration obligations apply
- Work allowed immediately upon granting of the permit

### 3. EU/EER Verblijfsrecht (EU/EEA Right of Residence)

Governed by **EU Directive 2004/38/EC**, implemented in Dutch law via **Art. 8.7-8.25 Vb 2000**.

**Categories of EU/EEA/Swiss nationals:**

| Duration | Basis | Conditions |
|----------|-------|------------|
| < 3 months | Art. 8.9 Vb | Valid ID; no formalities |
| 3 months - 5 years | Art. 8.12 Vb | Economic activity, or sufficient resources + health insurance |
| > 5 years (permanent) | Art. 8.17 Vb | 5 years continuous lawful residence |

**Family members of EU citizens (Art. 8.7 lid 2 Vb):**
- Spouse or registered partner (geregistreerd partner)
- Direct descendants under 21 or dependents
- Dependent direct ascendants
- Third-country national family members receive an Article 10 residence card (verblijfskaart)

## MVV Requirement (Machtiging tot Voorlopig Verblijf)

### What is an MVV?
An MVV is an entry visa (authorization for temporary stay) required before entry into the
Netherlands (Art. 1 sub h Vw 2000). It must be collected at a Dutch embassy or consulate
in the country of origin or habitual residence.

### MVV-Exempt Nationalities (Art. 17 Vw 2000 jo. Art. 3.1 VV 2000)
The following nationals are exempt from the MVV requirement:
- EU/EEA/Swiss nationals and their family members
- Nationals of: Australia, Canada, Japan, Monaco, New Zealand, South Korea, UK, USA, Vatican City
- Holders of certain EU long-term resident permits from other Member States

### MVV-Exempt Categories (Art. 17 lid 1 Vw 2000)
Regardless of nationality, the following are exempt:
- Children under 12 joining a parent in NL
- Holders of a Dutch nationality who also hold another nationality
- Persons with a hardship exemption (onbillijkheid van overwegende aard)
- Persons covered by certain EU directives (ICT, Blue Card holders from other MS)
- Turkish nationals under the Ankara Agreement standstill clause

### TEV Procedure (Toegang en Verblijf)
Since the Wet modern migratiebeleid (2013), the MVV and VVB applications are combined into
a single TEV procedure (Art. 2a Vw 2000):
1. Application filed in NL by sponsor or abroad by applicant
2. IND assesses both MVV and VVB conditions simultaneously
3. If approved: MVV issued at embassy; upon arrival VVB granted
4. Processing time: 90 days (standard) or shorter for recognized sponsors

## Conditions Per Purpose of Stay

### Work-Related Permits

**Combined permit (GVVA - Gecombineerde vergunning voor verblijf en arbeid):**
- Art. 1 Wet arbeid vreemdelingen (Wav)
- Combines residence permit and work permit in one document
- Requires: employer holds TWV (tewerkstellingsvergunning) or is exempt
- Labor market test (arbeidsmarkttoets): employer must demonstrate no suitable workers
  available in NL or EU/EEA (prioriteitgenietend aanbod)
- Valid for maximum 1 year (extendable for same employer/work)

**Exemptions from labor market test:**
- Kennismigrant (highly skilled migrant) - Art. 1d BuWav
- Intra-corporate transferee (ICT) - Art. 1f BuWav
- European Blue Card holder - Art. 1g BuWav
- Scientific researcher (onderzoeker in Richtlijn 2005/71) - Art. 1e BuWav

### Study

**Conditions (Art. 3.41 Vb 2000):**
- Enrolled at a designated educational institution (instelling voor hoger onderwijs)
- Institution must be recognized sponsor (erkend referent)
- Sufficient financial means (per 2024: approximately EUR 13,000 per year or EUR 1,083/month)
- Health insurance coverage
- Study progress requirements apply
- Work allowed: max 16 hours/week or full-time during June/July/August

### Family Reunification and Formation

**Income requirement (Art. 3.22 Vb 2000):**
- Family reunification: 100% of net minimum wage (netto minimumloon)
  - Per 2024: approximately EUR 1,934.40 per month (net)
- Income must be independent, sufficient, and durable (zelfstandig, voldoende, duurzaam)
- Durable: employment contract for at least 1 year, or 3 years of sufficient income

**Additional requirements for family formation:**
- Both partners must be 21 years or older (Art. 3.14 Vb)
- Sponsor must have resided in NL for at least 1 year (Art. 3.15 Vb)
- Civic integration abroad (basisexamen inburgering buitenland) for certain nationalities

**Genuine relationship test:**
- Married or registered partners: marriage/partnership certificate
- Unmarried partners: durable and exclusive relationship, cohabitation evidence

## Duration and Extension

### Standard Validity Periods

| Permit Type | Initial Duration | Maximum Extension |
|-------------|-----------------|-------------------|
| Kennismigrant | Max 5 years | 5 years per extension |
| Study | Duration of study (max 1 year) | Yearly renewal |
| Family reunification | 1 year (first) then 5 years | 5 years |
| GVVA (work) | 1 year | 1 year |
| Au pair | 1 year | Not extendable |
| Orientation year | 1 year | Not extendable |
| Asylum (temporary) | 5 years | Converts to permanent |

### Extension Procedure (Verlenging)
- Application must be filed before expiry (Art. 3.82 Vb 2000)
- If filed on time: lawful residence continues pending decision (Art. 3.87 Vb)
- Late filing: may result in gap in lawful residence
- IND processing time: standard 90 days
- Conditions for extension must still be met at time of decision

### Change of Purpose (Wijziging beperking)
- Possible to change the purpose of an existing permit (Art. 3.4 lid 3 Vb)
- Example: from study to kennismigrant after graduation
- Must meet all conditions of the new purpose
- Application can be filed while still holding current permit

## Permanent Residence (Onbepaalde Tijd)

### Regulier Onbepaalde Tijd (Art. 21 Vw 2000)
**Conditions:**
- 5 years continuous lawful residence on a non-temporary purpose
- Passed civic integration exam (inburgeringsexamen) or exempt
- Sufficient and durable income
- No public order objections (openbare orde)
- Note: study and au pair are temporary purposes and do not count

### EU Long-Term Resident (Art. 21 lid 1 sub b Vw / Directive 2003/109/EC)
- 5 years continuous lawful residence in NL
- Stable and regular resources
- Health insurance
- Confers right to move to another EU Member State under facilitated conditions

### Civic Integration Requirement (Wet inburgering 2021)
- Applies to most third-country nationals aged 16-67
- Three learning routes: B1-route, Onderwijsroute, Zelfredzaamheidsroute
- Must be completed within 3 years (extendable)
- Failure may result in fines or refusal of permanent permit
- Exemptions: EU/EEA/Swiss nationals, Turkish nationals (Ankara Agreement), certain diplomas

## Key IND Procedural Rules

### Application Fees (Leges) - 2024/2025
- Kennismigrant: EUR 210 (recognized sponsor filing)
- Family reunification/formation: EUR 210 (TEV) or EUR 192 (VVB only)
- Study: EUR 210
- Naturalization: EUR 970 (single) / EUR 1,220 (couple)
- Permanent residence: EUR 192

### Processing Times
- Standard: 90 days from complete application
- Recognized sponsors: shortened to 2 weeks for kennismigrant applications
- Asylum: varies (track system, currently 6-15 months due to backlogs)

### Legal Remedies
- Objection (bezwaar) to IND within 4 weeks of decision (Art. 69 Vw)
- Appeal (beroep) to Rechtbank Den Haag (all court locations) within 4 weeks
- Higher appeal (hoger beroep) to Afdeling bestuursrechtspraak Raad van State (ABRvS)
- Provisional measures (voorlopige voorziening) possible during proceedings

### Recognized Sponsor System (Erkend Referent)
- Mandatory for: kennismigrant, study, ICT, researcher, au pair
- Benefits: faster processing, reduced documentation requirements
- Obligations: duty of care (zorgplicht), duty to inform (informatieplicht),
  duty of administration (administratieplicht)
- Penalties for non-compliance: fines up to EUR 6,000 per violation (bestuurlijke boete)
