# Knowledge Migrant Scheme (Kennismigrantenregeling)

## Legal Basis

The highly skilled migrant scheme is governed by:
- **Art. 3.30a Vb 2000** - Conditions for the kennismigrant residence permit
- **Art. 1d BuWav** - Exemption from work permit (TWV) requirement
- **Wet modern migratiebeleid** - Modern Migration Policy Act (framework)
- **IND policy rules** in the Vreemdelingencirculaire (Vc B6)

## Core Requirements

### 1. Recognized Sponsor (Erkend Referent)

**Mandatory requirement (Art. 2c Vw 2000):**
The employer must be a recognized sponsor (erkend referent) with the IND before applying
for a kennismigrant permit.

**Application for recognition:**
- Filed by the employer at IND
- Assessment of: continuity, solvency, reliability, compliance history
- Processing time: approximately 4-8 weeks
- Fee: EUR 4,466 (2024/2025)
- One-time application; valid indefinitely (subject to compliance)
- Registration in the IND public register (openbaar register erkende referenten)

**Obligations of recognized sponsors (Art. 2e Vw 2000):**
- **Zorgplicht** (duty of care): ensure the migrant can meet residence conditions
- **Informatieplicht** (duty to inform): notify IND of relevant changes within 4 weeks,
  including: termination of employment, salary changes below threshold, departure from NL
- **Administratieplicht** (duty of administration): maintain records for 5 years after
  end of sponsorship, including employment contracts, payslips, identity documents

**Penalties for non-compliance:**
- Administrative fines (bestuurlijke boete): up to EUR 6,000 per violation
- Suspension or withdrawal of recognized sponsor status
- Systematic inspections by IND

### 2. Salary Thresholds (Salariscriterium)

## Salary Thresholds (as of 1 January 2025 - verify annually at ind.nl)
⚠️ These thresholds are adjusted annually by the IND. Check ind.nl for current values.

The migrant must earn at least the minimum salary threshold. Thresholds are adjusted
annually on 1 January based on CBS wage indices.

**2024 Salary Thresholds (gross monthly, excluding 8% holiday allowance):**

| Category | Monthly Gross | Annual Gross (approx.) |
|----------|--------------|----------------------|
| Standard (age 30+) | EUR 5,331 | EUR 63,972 |
| Reduced (age < 30) | EUR 3,909 | EUR 46,908 |
| Orientation year graduates | EUR 2,801 | EUR 33,612 |
| European Blue Card | EUR 6,245 | EUR 74,940 |

**2025 Salary Thresholds (gross monthly, excluding 8% holiday allowance):**

| Category | Monthly Gross | Annual Gross (approx.) |
|----------|--------------|----------------------|
| Standard (age 30+) | EUR 5,493 (indexed) | EUR 65,916 |
| Reduced (age < 30) | EUR 4,028 (indexed) | EUR 48,336 |
| Orientation year graduates | EUR 2,887 (indexed) | EUR 34,644 |
| European Blue Card | EUR 6,435 (indexed) | EUR 77,220 |

**Important salary rules:**
- Salary must be in accordance with the market (marktconform)
- The salary criterion is exclusive of the 8% holiday allowance (vakantiegeld)
- Including holiday allowance: the IND adds 8% to the base salary for threshold comparison
- Variable components (bonuses, commissions) do not count toward the threshold
- Salary reduction below threshold triggers sponsor notification obligation
- The age-30 boundary is assessed at the date of the IND application

### 3. Employment Contract Requirements

- Written employment contract with a Dutch employer (or Dutch branch)
- Contract must specify: gross salary, job title, hours, duration
- Can be fixed-term or indefinite (onbepaalde tijd)
- The employer must be registered with the KVK (Kamer van Koophandel)
- The employment must be genuine and the position must match the qualification level

### 4. No Qualification Requirements

Unlike some countries, the Dutch kennismigrant scheme does **not** require:
- A specific educational qualification or degree
- Professional registration or licensing (unless the profession itself requires it, e.g., medical)
- Language proficiency
- Points-based assessment

The salary threshold serves as the sole proxy for skill level.

## Application Procedure

### Step 1: MVV + VVB (TEV Procedure)
For nationals requiring an MVV:
1. Recognized sponsor files application at IND
2. Processing time: approximately 2 weeks (erkend referent fast-track)
3. Upon approval: MVV sticker collected at Dutch embassy abroad
4. Entry to NL within 90 days of MVV issuance
5. VVB (residence permit) issued automatically upon arrival (pick up at IND desk)

### Step 2: VVB Only
For MVV-exempt nationals (e.g., US, Canadian, Japanese, Australian):
1. Enter NL on national passport (no visa required for 90 days)
2. Recognized sponsor files VVB application at IND
3. Processing time: approximately 2 weeks
4. Collect residence permit at IND desk appointment

### Required Documents
**From the sponsor:**
- Signed application form
- Copy of employment contract
- Employer statement (annexes)
- Proof of recognized sponsor status

**From the migrant:**
- Valid passport (minimum 6 months validity beyond intended stay)
- Passport photo
- Proof of previous qualifications (if applicable, not mandatory)
- Antecedents certificate (may be required depending on nationality)
- TB test certificate (for certain nationalities)

## Duration and Renewal

**Initial permit:**
- Valid for the duration of the employment contract, maximum 5 years
- Indefinite contracts: permit for 5 years

**Extension (verlenging):**
- File before expiry
- Same salary threshold must still be met (at the level applicable at time of extension)
- Same employer or new employer (if changed, new application required)
- Processing time: approximately 2 weeks for recognized sponsors

**Change of employer:**
- New employer must also be a recognized sponsor
- New application required (not an extension)
- Can work for new employer pending decision if filed on time
- Important: salary threshold at time of new application applies

## Connection to the 30% Ruling (30%-Regeling)

### What is the 30% Ruling?
A tax facility under **Art. 31a lid 2 sub e Wet LB** allowing employers to reimburse
up to 30% of the employee's salary tax-free as a deemed allowance for extraterritorial
costs (extraterritoriale kosten).

### Conditions (since 1 January 2024):
1. Employee must be recruited from abroad (or within 150 km of Dutch border)
2. Employee must have specific expertise not available (or scarce) in NL labor market
3. Salary threshold (separate from kennismigrant): approximately EUR 46,107 (2024) for
   standard; EUR 35,048 for under-30 with a Master's degree
4. Employee must not have lived within 150 km of the Dutch border for more than 8 of the
   24 months prior to first day of employment in NL

### Duration:
- Maximum 5 years (60 months)
- Reduced from the original 8 years (2012 reform)
- **Current law (Belastingplan 2025)**: Flat 30% for the full 60-month duration through 2026
- **From 1 January 2027**: Flat 27% for the full 60-month duration
- The previously enacted step-down (30%/20%/10%) was REVERSED before taking effect
- Partieel buitenlands belastingplichtige status abolished for new rulings from 1 Jan 2025

### Relationship with kennismigrant permit:
- The 30% ruling is a **tax measure** (Belastingdienst), not an immigration measure (IND)
- A kennismigrant permit does not automatically grant the 30% ruling, and vice versa
- Many kennismigranten qualify for both, but the criteria and administering bodies differ
- If 30% ruling is applied: for IND salary threshold purposes, the **full gross salary
  before 30% deduction** is used (not the taxable portion)

## Orientation Year (Zoekjaar - Art. 3.42 Vb 2000)

### For Graduates of Dutch Institutions
**Eligibility:**
- Completed a Bachelor's, Master's, or PhD at a Dutch accredited higher education institution
- Application within 3 years of graduation
- No income or employment requirement during the orientation year

**During the orientation year:**
- Free access to the labor market (no TWV required, Art. 1h BuWav)
- May work in any capacity without restrictions
- Duration: 1 year (not extendable)
- Can switch to kennismigrant permit if employment is found meeting the reduced salary
  threshold (approximately EUR 2,801/month in 2024)

### For Graduates of Top-200 Universities
**Eligibility:**
- Graduated with a Master's or PhD from a university ranked in the top 200 of the
  Times Higher Education World University Rankings, QS World University Rankings, or
  Academic Ranking of World Universities (Shanghai ranking)
- Application within 3 years of graduation
- Must apply from abroad and obtain an MVV (unless MVV-exempt)
- Same conditions and benefits as Dutch institution graduates

### Transition to Kennismigrant
- Reduced salary threshold applies (approximately EUR 2,801/month in 2024)
- The reduced threshold applies for **3 years** after the end of the search year
- After 3 years: standard age-based threshold applies

## European Blue Card (Europese Blauwe Kaart)

### Legal Basis
- **EU Directive 2009/50/EC** (original), revised by **Directive 2021/1883/EU**
- Implemented in NL via **Art. 3.30b Vb 2000**

### Conditions
- Higher professional education (HBO) or university degree, or 5 years relevant work experience
- Employment contract of at least 1 year (revised directive: 6 months)
- Salary threshold: EUR 6,245/month gross (2024), higher than standard kennismigrant
- Genuine and adequate health insurance

### Advantages over Standard Kennismigrant
- After 12-18 months: right to move to another EU Member State under facilitated conditions
- Cumulation of residence periods across EU for long-term residence
- More generous family reunification conditions
- Path to EU long-term resident status

### Disadvantages Compared to Kennismigrant
- Higher salary threshold
- Educational qualification requirement (kennismigrant has none)
- Less commonly used in NL due to the attractive kennismigrant scheme

## ICT Directive (Intra-Corporate Transfer)

### Legal Basis
- **EU Directive 2014/66/EU** - ICT Directive
- Implemented in NL via **Art. 3.30c Vb 2000**

### Scope
Applies to third-country nationals transferred within a multinational company to a Dutch
branch/subsidiary as:
- **Manager** (leidinggevende)
- **Specialist** (specialist)
- **Trainee employee** (trainee-werknemer)

### Conditions
- Employment with the company for at least 3-6 months (depending on category)
- Written transfer agreement or assignment letter
- Salary in accordance with Dutch standards (marktconform)
- Professional qualifications for the position
- The host entity need not be a recognized sponsor for ICT short-term mobility

### Duration
- Managers and specialists: maximum 3 years
- Trainees: maximum 1 year
- Short-term mobility to other EU Member States possible after 90 days in first MS

## Scientific Researcher (Onderzoeker)

### Legal Basis
- **EU Directive 2016/801** (recast Research and Students Directive)
- **Art. 3.30d Vb 2000**

### Conditions
- Hosting agreement with a recognized research institution (erkend referent)
- Adequate resources
- Health insurance
- No specific salary threshold (but hosting agreement must specify remuneration)

### Benefits
- Fast-track procedure (2 weeks via recognized sponsor)
- Free labor market access
- Intra-EU mobility rights
- Family members receive same-duration permits

## Family Members (Gezinsleden)

### Simultaneous Application
- Family members can be included in the kennismigrant TEV application
- Each family member needs a separate application form but can be submitted together
- No separate income requirement beyond the kennismigrant salary threshold
- Family members: spouse/registered partner, minor children (under 18)

### Rights of Family Members
- Spouse/partner: free access to the labor market (no separate work permit needed)
- Minor children: access to Dutch education
- Family members receive a residence permit for the same duration as the kennismigrant
- After 5 years: independent residence right possible

### Adult Children and Parents
- Adult children (18+) and parents do NOT qualify under kennismigrant family reunification
- Must apply under regular family reunification rules (stricter income requirements)

## Common Issues and Pitfalls

### Salary Below Threshold
- If salary drops below threshold (e.g., due to part-time, salary reduction, sick leave):
  sponsor must notify IND within 4 weeks
- Long-term salary below threshold: grounds for withdrawal (intrekking) of the permit
- Short-term illness: IND generally accepts temporary salary reduction if employer continues
  to pay at least 70% of salary (in line with Art. 7:629 BW)

### Annual Threshold Indexation Risk
- Salary thresholds are adjusted annually on 1 January based on CBS wage indices (typically ~3-4% increase)
- **Renewal risk**: An employee whose salary met the threshold at the time of the initial application may fall below the new threshold at renewal time
- Example: 2024 standard threshold EUR 5,331/mo → 2025 threshold EUR 5,493/mo (increase of EUR 162/mo)
- Employers should build annual salary increases into contracts to account for threshold indexation
- If salary falls below the **new** threshold at renewal: the extension may be denied
- **Cross-reference**: Tax thresholds (30% ruling) are also indexed annually but by different amounts — meeting one does not guarantee meeting the other

### Job Change (Change of Employer)
- Kennismigrant may only work for the sponsoring employer
- Changing employers requires a **new application** by the new recognized sponsor
- The new employer must **also be an erkend referent** (or apply first)
- Gap between employers: if no application is pending, the migrant's work rights lapse
- Recommend: file new application **before** start date with new employer
- While the new application is pending, work for the new employer is permitted (Art. 3.31 Vb 2000)
- The salary threshold applicable is the threshold **at the time of the new application** (not the original application) — thresholds increase annually, creating renewal risk
- **30% ruling impact**: A new 30% ruling application must also be filed within 3 months of the end of the previous employment to maintain continuity (see dutch-tax-law references)

### Job Loss Grace Period (Art. 3.31a Vb 2000)
- Upon termination of employment, the kennismigrant has a **3-month search period** (zoekperiode)
- During this 3-month period:
  - The residence permit remains valid
  - The migrant may seek new employment
  - No work is permitted unless a new employer has filed a pending application
- After 3 months without new qualifying employment: IND may withdraw the permit
- The former employer must notify IND of the termination within 4 weeks (informatieplicht, Art. 2e Vw 2000)
- **Freelancing/ZZP is NOT permitted** on a kennismigrant permit
- **Starting a BV and self-employing does NOT maintain the permit** (see self-sponsorship limitations in expat-cross-domain-workflow.md)

### Relationship Breakdown
- Family members hold dependent permits tied to the kennismigrant
- If the relationship ends: family member must independently qualify for a permit
- After 5 years on dependent permit: eligible for independent permanent residence

### Public Order (Openbare Orde)
- Criminal convictions can lead to refusal or withdrawal
- IND applies a sliding scale (glijdende schaal, Art. 3.86 Vb): longer residence = higher
  threshold for refusal on public order grounds
- Serious crimes: permit can be withdrawn regardless of duration of stay
