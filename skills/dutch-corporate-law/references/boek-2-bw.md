# Key Articles from BW Book 2 (Legal Persons / Rechtspersonen)

## Title 1: General Provisions (Algemene bepalingen)

### Art. 2:1 - Public Law Legal Entities
- The State, provinces, municipalities, water boards, and other bodies with regulatory powers are legal entities
- Other bodies designated by law are also legal entities

### Art. 2:2 - Religious Organizations
- Churches and their independent parts and organizations are governed by their own statutes insofar as not in conflict with the law

### Art. 2:3 - Types of Private Legal Entities
- Associations (verenigingen), cooperatives (cooperaties), mutual insurance associations (onderlinge waarborgmaatschappijen), NVs, BVs, and foundations (stichtingen) have legal personality

### Art. 2:4 - Legal Capacity
- A legal entity has the same rights as a natural person under private law, unless the law provides otherwise

### Art. 2:5 - Separate Patrimony
- A legal entity is regarded as a person in matters of property law (vermogensrechtelijk verkeer)

### Art. 2:6 - Registered Office
- A legal entity's registered office (statutaire zetel) must be in the Netherlands

### Art. 2:7 - Incorporation Procedure
- Legal entity is incorporated by notarial deed (notariele akte), except for associations with limited legal capacity

### Art. 2:8 - Reasonableness and Fairness
- A legal entity and those involved in its organization must behave towards each other in accordance with reasonableness and fairness (redelijkheid en billijkheid)
- A rule applicable between them does not apply insofar as this would be unacceptable under these standards

### Art. 2:9 - Director's Duty of Proper Management
- Each director is responsible for the general course of affairs (algemene gang van zaken)
- A director is liable towards the legal entity for improper management (onbehoorlijk bestuur), unless:
  - The failure cannot be attributed to them personally, AND
  - They were not negligent in taking measures to avert the consequences

### Art. 2:10 - Bookkeeping Obligation
- The board must keep records of the financial position of the legal entity and everything relating to its activities
- Must retain records for 7 years

### Art. 2:14 - Nullity of Decisions
- Decisions contrary to the law or articles of association are null (nietig) or voidable (vernietigbaar)
- Art. 2:15: Decisions are voidable if:
  - In violation of procedural requirements in the articles of association
  - Contrary to reasonableness and fairness (Art. 2:8)
  - Contrary to internal regulations

## Title 4: BV (Besloten Vennootschap) - Art. 2:175-2:284a

### Formation
- **Art. 2:175**: BV is formed by one or more persons by notarial deed
- **Art. 2:177**: Articles of association must contain: name, registered office, objects, shares, appointment of directors
- **Art. 2:178**: Notarial deed must be in Dutch language

### Shares
- **Art. 2:190**: Share register must be kept by the board
- **Art. 2:191-191a**: Share issuance requires resolution by the general meeting (or delegated body)
- **Art. 2:192**: Specific obligations or restrictions can be attached to shares in the articles
- **Art. 2:194**: Transfer of shares requires notarial deed (akte van levering)
- **Art. 2:195**: Blokkeringsregeling (optional since Flex-BV): articles may require approval or offer-first-to-co-shareholders
- **Art. 2:196**: Share transfer requires notarial deed; deviating agreements are null
- **Art. 2:198**: Right of pledge on shares (pandrecht)
- **Art. 2:199**: Usufruct on shares (vruchtgebruik)
- **Art. 2:201**: Own shares: BV may acquire its own shares subject to conditions

### Capital and Distributions
- **Art. 2:207**: Share repurchase: permitted if equity exceeds any statutory reserves
- **Art. 2:216**: Distribution of profits:
  - Lid 1: Profits to shareholders unless articles provide otherwise
  - Lid 2: Distribution requires board approval (uitkeringstest - can the BV continue to pay its debts?)
  - Lid 3: Directors who approved a distribution knowing it would harm creditors are personally liable for the shortfall
  - Lid 4: Shareholders who received a distribution knowing it would harm creditors must repay

### Board (Bestuur)
- **Art. 2:239**: The board is charged with managing the BV
- **Art. 2:240**: The board represents the BV (vertegenwoordigingsbevoegdheid)
- **Art. 2:241**: Articles may provide for one or more managing directors (gedelegeerd bestuurders)
- **Art. 2:242**: Directors appointed by the general meeting (AVA) unless articles provide for appointment by a meeting of holders of a specific class of shares or by the supervisory board
- **Art. 2:244**: Suspension and dismissal of directors by the body that appointed them
- **Art. 2:245**: Remuneration determined by the general meeting unless delegated

### Supervisory Board (Raad van Commissarissen)
- **Art. 2:250**: Articles may provide for a supervisory board
- **Art. 2:252**: Supervisory board members appointed by the general meeting
- **Art. 2:253**: Supervisory board oversees the management and general affairs

### One-Tier Board (Monistisch bestuursmodel)
- **Art. 2:239a**: Articles may provide for a one-tier board with executive and non-executive directors
- Non-executive directors have supervisory duties

### General Meeting (Algemene Vergadering)
- **Art. 2:217**: Each shareholder has at least one vote (articles may deviate)
- **Art. 2:218-225**: Calling, conducting, and decision-making at general meetings
- **Art. 2:224**: Decisions of the general meeting require absolute majority unless articles provide otherwise

### Annual Accounts
- **Art. 2:210**: Board must prepare annual accounts within 5 months of financial year end (extendable by 5 months)
- **Art. 2:210 lid 3**: Annual accounts adopted by general meeting
- **Art. 2:394**: Filing at KVK

## Title 5: NV (Naamloze Vennootschap) - Art. 2:64-2:174a
(Parallel structure to BV with key differences noted in bv-nv-structure.md)

### Key NV-specific provisions:
- **Art. 2:67**: Minimum capital EUR 45,000
- **Art. 2:80**: Shares must have a nominal value
- **Art. 2:86c**: Bearer shares (toonderstukken) - largely abolished for new issuances
- **Art. 2:92**: Share issuance: pre-emptive rights (voorkeursrecht) for existing shareholders
- **Art. 2:105**: Distribution: board must refuse if it knows or should reasonably foresee the BV cannot continue paying debts

## Title 8: Enterprise Chamber and Inquiry Procedure (Enqueterecht)

### Art. 2:344-2:359 - Inquiry Procedure (Enqueteprocedure)
- **Art. 2:344**: Shareholders holding at least 10% of issued capital (or EUR 225,000 nominal value for NV) may file a request
- **Art. 2:345**: The Enterprise Chamber (Ondernemingskamer) of the Amsterdam Court of Appeal may:
  - Order an inquiry (enquete) into the policy and affairs of the company
  - Appoint investigators
- **Art. 2:349a**: Immediate provisional measures (onmiddellijke voorzieningen):
  - Suspension of directors
  - Temporary appointment of directors
  - Temporary deviation from articles of association
  - Temporary transfer of shares to a custodian
- **Art. 2:355**: If wanbeleid (mismanagement) is found, the Enterprise Chamber may:
  - Annul resolutions
  - Dismiss directors or supervisory board members
  - Temporarily appoint directors
  - Temporarily deviate from articles of association
  - Dissolve the legal entity

## Liability Provisions (Key Articles)

### Internal Liability
- **Art. 2:9**: Director liability towards the company for improper management
- **Art. 2:138/248**: Liability in bankruptcy for the deficit (boedeltekort)
  - Lid 1: Joint and several liability for the deficit if there was manifestly improper management (kennelijk onbehoorlijke taakvervulling) that was an important cause of the bankruptcy
  - Lid 2: Irrebuttable presumption of improper management if Art. 2:10 (bookkeeping) or Art. 2:394 (annual accounts filing) obligations were not met

### External Liability
- **Art. 6:162 BW**: Directors can be personally liable to third parties under tort (onrechtmatige daad)
- **Beklamel doctrine**: Personal liability when director enters into obligations knowing the company cannot perform
- **New Holland Belgium / Ontvanger/Roelofsen**: Personal liability for selective payment of creditors
