# BV vs NV: Comparison, Formation Requirements, and Capital Structure

## Overview

| Feature | BV (Besloten Vennootschap) | NV (Naamloze Vennootschap) |
|---------|---------------------------|---------------------------|
| English equivalent | Private Limited Company | Public Limited Company |
| BW Book 2 | Titel 4 (Art. 2:175-2:284a) | Titel 5 (Art. 2:64-2:174a) |
| Minimum capital | EUR 0.01 (since Flex-BV, 2012) | EUR 45,000 |
| Share transferability | Restricted (blokkeringsregeling optional since Flex-BV) | Freely transferable |
| Share certificates | Not possible | Possible (bearer/registered) |
| Listing possible | No | Yes |
| Common use | SMEs, subsidiaries, holding companies | Listed companies, large enterprises |

---

## BV Formation (Oprichting BV)

### Since the Flex-BV Act (1 October 2012)

#### Requirements:
1. **Notarial deed of incorporation** (notariele akte van oprichting) - MANDATORY
   - Must contain the articles of association (statuten)
   - Can be executed by a proxy (volmacht) but the deed itself must be notarial
2. **Articles of association** (statuten) must contain at minimum:
   - Name of the BV (must include "B.V." or "Besloten Vennootschap")
   - Registered office (statutaire zetel) - must be in the Netherlands
   - Objects/purpose (doel) of the company
   - Shares: nominal value, types, and number authorized
   - Appointment of initial directors (bestuurders)
3. **Share capital**:
   - Minimum: EUR 0.01 per share (no statutory minimum capital)
   - Shares can be denominated in any currency
   - Multiple classes of shares permitted (e.g., ordinary, preference, priority)
   - Shares without voting rights or profit rights permitted (but not both)
4. **Bank statement or auditor's certificate** (if contribution in kind - inbreng in natura)
5. **Registration at KVK** (Handelsregister) within 8 days of incorporation

#### Post Flex-BV Changes:
- No minimum capital requirement (was EUR 18,000 before 2012)
- No mandatory bank statement for cash contributions
- No mandatory blokkeringsregeling (share transfer restriction) - but can be included in articles
- Board approval for share transfers possible as alternative to traditional blokkeringsregeling
- Shares without voting rights possible
- Profit rights can be separated from shares (stemrechtloze/winstrechtloze aandelen)
- Uitkeringstest (distribution test) required for dividends (Art. 2:216 BW)

#### Uitkeringstest (Art. 2:216 BW):
Before any distribution, the board must verify:
1. **Balanstest**: Distribution does not exceed distributable reserves
2. **Uitkeringstest**: Distribution will not cause the BV to be unable to pay its debts as they fall due (directors can be personally liable if they approve a distribution knowing this)

## NV Formation (Oprichting NV)

#### Requirements:
1. **Notarial deed of incorporation** - MANDATORY
2. **Articles of association** must contain:
   - Name (must include "N.V." or "Naamloze Vennootschap")
   - Registered office in the Netherlands
   - Objects/purpose
   - Authorized capital, issued capital, and par value of shares
3. **Minimum capital**: EUR 45,000 (must be fully issued and at least 25% paid up)
4. **Ministerial declaration of no objection** - abolished in 2011
5. **Bank statement** for cash contributions
6. **Auditor's statement** for contributions in kind
7. **KVK registration**

## Corporate Governance Structure

### Two-tier Board (Standard Dutch model)
```
┌─────────────────────────────────────┐
│  Algemene Vergadering (General       │
│  Meeting of Shareholders - AVA)      │
└──────────────┬──────────────────────┘
               │ appoints/dismisses
┌──────────────┴──────────────────────┐
│  Raad van Commissarissen (RvC)       │
│  (Supervisory Board)                 │
│  - Supervisory role                  │
│  - Advisory role                     │
│  - Specific approval rights          │
└──────────────┬──────────────────────┘
               │ supervises
┌──────────────┴──────────────────────┐
│  Bestuur (Management Board)          │
│  - Day-to-day management             │
│  - Strategy and policy               │
│  - Represents the company            │
└─────────────────────────────────────┘
```

### One-tier Board (Monistisch bestuur - since 2013)
```
┌─────────────────────────────────────┐
│  Algemene Vergadering (AVA)          │
└──────────────┬──────────────────────┘
               │
┌──────────────┴──────────────────────┐
│  Board of Directors                  │
│  - Executive directors               │
│    (uitvoerend bestuurders)          │
│  - Non-executive directors           │
│    (niet-uitvoerend bestuurders)     │
└─────────────────────────────────────┘
```

### Structuurregime (Large Company Regime)
Mandatory for companies meeting 2 of 3 criteria for 3 consecutive years:
1. Issued capital + reserves >= EUR 16 million
2. Company or dependent company has a works council (ondernemingsraad)
3. Company + dependent companies employ >= 100 employees in the Netherlands

**Effect**: RvC members are appointed by co-optation (not by AVA), with nomination rights for the works council and AVA. The RvC appoints the bestuur. This limits shareholder control.

## Director Duties and Liability

### Standard of Care (Art. 2:9 BW)
- Each director must perform their duties properly (behoorlijke taakvervulling)
- Liability arises for serious culpable mismanagement (ernstig verwijtbaar - onbehoorlijk bestuur)
- Individual directors can escape liability if the failure is not attributable to them and they were not negligent in taking remedial action

### External Liability (Art. 6:162 BW - Tort)
Directors can be personally liable to third parties under tort law if:
- They acted in violation of an unwritten standard of care (Beklamel doctrine)
- They caused the company to breach its obligations while knowing it could not perform (selectieve betaling / New Holland Belgium)
- They committed fraud or misrepresentation

### Liability in Insolvency (Art. 2:138/248 BW)
- In bankruptcy, directors are jointly and severally liable for the deficit if:
  - They manifestly improperly managed the company (kennelijk onbehoorlijke taakvervulling)
  - This was an important cause of the bankruptcy
- **Presumption of improper management** if:
  - Annual accounts were not filed timely at KVK (Art. 2:394 BW)
  - Bookkeeping obligations were not met (Art. 2:10 BW)

## Share Classes and Rights

### Types of Shares (Flex-BV)
| Share Type | Voting Rights | Profit Rights | Notes |
|-----------|--------------|---------------|-------|
| Ordinary (gewone aandelen) | Yes | Yes | Default |
| Preference (preferente aandelen) | Yes | Priority dividend | Common in financing |
| Priority (prioriteitsaandelen) | Yes + special rights | Yes | Governance rights |
| Stemrechtloos (non-voting) | No | Yes | Since Flex-BV 2012 |
| Winstrechtloos (non-profit) | Yes | No | Since Flex-BV 2012 |

### Share Transfer
- **BV**: Transfer requires notarial deed (Art. 2:196 BW). Blokkeringsregeling optional.
- **NV**: Transfer of registered shares by deed or entry in shareholder register. Bearer shares freely transferable.
