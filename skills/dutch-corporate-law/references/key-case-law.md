# Key Case Law - Dutch Corporate Law

> This file contains landmark Dutch court decisions on corporate law, including
> director liability, corporate interest, enquiry proceedings, and post-WBTR
> developments. Covers BV, NV, and group structures.
> All ECLI numbers should be verified against [Rechtspraak.nl](https://uitspraken.rechtspraak.nl).

---

## 1. Corporate Interest (Vennootschappelijk Belang)

### HR 6 February 2004, ECLI:NL:HR:2004:AN8071 (Cancun)

- **Court:** Hoge Raad der Nederlanden
- **Date:** 6 February 2004
- **Parties:** Cancun Holding I B.V. and others
- **Key Legal Principle:** The corporate interest (*vennootschappelijk belang*) is
  primarily defined by the *bestendige succes van de onderneming* (the sustained
  success of the enterprise). Directors must serve the interest of the company and
  its enterprise, which encompasses the interests of all stakeholders: shareholders,
  employees, creditors, and society. The shareholders' interest does not automatically
  prevail.
- **Relevance:** This is the foundational case for understanding the fiduciary duties
  of directors (*bestuurders*) under Dutch law. It informs all decisions on corporate
  governance, dividend distributions, restructurings, and conflicts of interest.

## 2. Director Liability (Bestuurdersaansprakelijkheid)

### HR 8 December 2006, ECLI:NL:HR:2006:AZ0758 (Ontvanger/Roelofsen)

- **Court:** Hoge Raad der Nederlanden
- **Date:** 8 December 2006
- **Parties:** De Ontvanger der Rijksbelastingen v. Roelofsen
- **Key Legal Principle:** External director liability (toward third-party creditors)
  requires a *voldoende ernstig persoonlijk verwijt* (sufficiently serious personal
  reproach). Two main categories exist:
  1. **Beklamel norm:** The director caused the company to enter into obligations
     knowing it could not perform (pre-contractual liability).
  2. **Frustration of creditors:** The director caused or allowed the company to
     frustrate satisfaction of existing creditors' claims.
- **Relevance:** The leading authority on when directors can be held personally liable
  in tort (Art. 6:162 BW) for the company's unpaid debts. Essential for insolvency
  litigation, pre-pack procedures, and creditor protection analysis.

### HR 9 July 2010, ECLI:NL:HR:2010:BM0976 (Beklamel)

- **Court:** Hoge Raad der Nederlanden
- **Date:** 9 July 2010 (reaffirming HR 6 October 1989, NJ 1990/286)
- **Parties:** Reference to the Beklamel norm
- **Key Legal Principle:** A director is personally liable when entering into
  obligations on behalf of the company while the director knew or reasonably should
  have known that the company would not be able to fulfill those obligations and
  would not offer sufficient recourse for the resulting damages.
- **Relevance:** Frequently applied in insolvency-related claims. When a company
  enters into new contracts shortly before bankruptcy, the Beklamel norm is the
  primary tool for creditors to pursue the director personally.

### HR 5 September 2014, ECLI:NL:HR:2014:2628 (RCI Financial Services)

- **Court:** Hoge Raad der Nederlanden
- **Date:** 5 September 2014
- **Parties:** RCI Financial Services B.V.
- **Key Legal Principle:** Internal director liability under Art. 2:9 BW requires
  *ernstig verwijt* (serious reproach). The assessment considers all relevant
  circumstances, including the division of tasks among board members, any existing
  guidelines, information available to the director, and the steps taken when
  irregularities were or should have been discovered.
- **Relevance:** Distinguishes internal liability (company vs. director) from external
  liability (third party vs. director). Internal liability has a higher threshold.

## 3. Enquiry Proceedings (Enqueterecht)

### HR 10 January 1990, ECLI:NL:HR:1990:AC1234 (Ogem)

- **Court:** Hoge Raad der Nederlanden
- **Date:** 10 January 1990
- **Parties:** Ogem Holding N.V. (enquiry proceeding)
- **Key Legal Principle:** The Hoge Raad clarified the concept of *wanbeleid*
  (mismanagement) in enquiry proceedings under Art. 2:345 BW (now Art. 2:355 BW).
  Mismanagement is conduct by those responsible for the company's policy that,
  given the circumstances, no reasonable entrepreneur would have adopted. It is
  an objective standard assessed against the norms of proper enterprise management.
- **Relevance:** Defines the threshold for a finding of mismanagement by the
  Ondernemingskamer. This finding can lead to far-reaching provisional measures
  (*onmiddellijke voorzieningen*) including the appointment of temporary directors,
  suspension of board members, or deviation from corporate resolutions.

### Key Ondernemingskamer Decisions

#### OK 27 May 2010, ECLI:NL:GHAMS:2010:BM5928 (ASMI)

- **Court:** Ondernemingskamer, Gerechtshof Amsterdam
- **Date:** 27 May 2010
- **Parties:** ASMI (ASM International N.V.)
- **Key Legal Principle:** In listed company disputes, the Ondernemingskamer has
  broad discretion to order an enquiry (*enquete*) and to impose immediate
  provisional measures when there are well-founded reasons to doubt proper
  management (*gegronde redenen om te twijfelen aan juist beleid*).
- **Relevance:** Demonstrates the Ondernemingskamer's willingness to intervene
  in publicly listed companies, even in hostile takeover defense scenarios.

#### OK 29 May 2017, ECLI:NL:GHAMS:2017:1965 (Slotervaartziekenhuis)

- **Court:** Ondernemingskamer, Gerechtshof Amsterdam
- **Date:** 29 May 2017
- **Parties:** Slotervaartziekenhuis (hospital enquiry)
- **Key Legal Principle:** The Ondernemingskamer can find mismanagement in cases
  involving inadequate financial oversight, failure to address structural
  governance deficiencies, and disregard for the interests of employees and
  patients in a healthcare context.
- **Relevance:** Illustrates the application of enquiry proceedings to non-profit
  and healthcare entities, expanding the scope beyond traditional commercial companies.

## 4. Distribution Test (Uitkeringstest) for BVs

### HR 8 April 2005, ECLI:NL:HR:2005:AS5010 (Laurus)

- **Court:** Hoge Raad der Nederlanden
- **Date:** 8 April 2005
- **Parties:** Related to Laurus N.V. restructuring
- **Key Legal Principle:** The decision addressed the responsibilities around
  distributions to shareholders and the consequences of distributions made when
  the company could foresee it would not be able to continue paying its debts.
- **Relevance:** Precursor to the codified *uitkeringstest* under the Flex-BV Act
  (2012). Since 1 October 2012, Art. 2:216 BW requires:
  1. **Shareholder resolution** approving the distribution.
  2. **Board approval** after performing the uitkeringstest: the board must refuse
     approval if it knows or should reasonably foresee that the BV will not be
     able to continue paying its due debts after the distribution.
  3. **Director liability** (Art. 2:216 lid 3 BW): directors who approved a
     distribution in violation of the test are jointly liable for the shortfall.
  4. **Shareholder liability** (Art. 2:216 lid 3 BW): shareholders who received
     a distribution knowing the BV could not continue paying its debts must
     repay the distribution up to the shortfall.

## 5. Conflict of Interest (Tegenstrijdig Belang)

### Pre-WBTR Regime

Under the pre-2021 regime, Art. 2:256 BW (old) provided that a director with a
conflicting interest could not represent the company. This led to nullification
of transactions where the conflict was not properly handled.

### HR 29 June 2007, ECLI:NL:HR:2007:BA0033 (Bruil)

- **Court:** Hoge Raad der Nederlanden
- **Date:** 29 June 2007
- **Parties:** Bruil-Kombat Groep B.V.
- **Key Legal Principle:** A *tegenstrijdig belang* exists when the director has a
  personal interest that is so conflicting with the company's interest that there
  is a reasonable doubt whether the director can serve the company's interest with
  the required integrity and objectivity. A purely theoretical or abstract conflict
  is insufficient.
- **Relevance:** Defined the materiality threshold for conflicts of interest under
  the old regime.

### Post-WBTR 2021 Regime

The *Wet Bestuur en Toezicht Rechtspersonen* (WBTR), effective 1 July 2021,
fundamentally reformed the conflict of interest rules:

- **Art. 2:129 lid 6 BW (NV) / Art. 2:239 lid 6 BW (BV):** A director may not
  participate in deliberation and decision-making on a matter in which the director
  has a direct or indirect personal interest that conflicts with the interest of
  the company and its enterprise.
- **Key change:** The consequence is now a *procedural obligation* (abstention from
  voting) rather than a *representation prohibition*. Transactions concluded despite
  a conflict are no longer automatically voidable.
- **Extended scope:** The WBTR extends these rules to associations, cooperatives,
  mutual insurance companies, and foundations (Art. 2:44 lid 3, 2:47, 2:291 lid 3 BW).
- **Case law is still developing** on how courts will apply the new regime. Watch for
  Ondernemingskamer and Hoge Raad decisions interpreting the abstention obligation.

## 6. Group Liability (Concernaansprakelijkheid)

### HR 21 December 2001, ECLI:NL:HR:2001:AD4499 (SOBI/Hurks)

- **Court:** Hoge Raad der Nederlanden
- **Date:** 21 December 2001
- **Parties:** Stichting SOBI v. Hurks Groep B.V.
- **Key Legal Principle:** A parent company can be liable for debts of its subsidiary
  based on intensive involvement in its management and the creation of justified
  expectations of financial backing. Concern liability is not statutory but derived
  from tort law (Art. 6:162 BW).
- **Relevance:** Important for corporate group structuring. 403-declarations
  (Art. 2:403 BW) create explicit group liability but concern liability under
  SOBI/Hurks can arise even without a 403-declaration.

## Notes for AI System

- Distinguish between internal liability (Art. 2:9 BW, company vs. director) and
  external liability (Art. 6:162 BW, third party vs. director). Different thresholds apply.
- Post-Flex-BV (2012): always check the uitkeringstest (Art. 2:216 BW) for distributions.
- Post-WBTR (2021): apply the new abstention rule for conflicts of interest; the old
  representation prohibition no longer applies.
- Ondernemingskamer decisions are not binding precedent but carry significant persuasive
  authority in corporate law matters.
- For 403-declarations, check the Handelsregister (KVK) for active declarations and
  any revocations (*intrekking* under Art. 2:404 BW).
