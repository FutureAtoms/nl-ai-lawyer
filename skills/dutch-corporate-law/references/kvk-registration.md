# Chamber of Commerce (KVK) Registration Process

## Legal Basis
- **Handelsregisterwet 2007** (Trade Register Act)
- **Handelsregisterbesluit 2008** (Trade Register Decree)

---

## What Must Be Registered

### Mandatory Registration
All of the following must register at the KVK Handelsregister:
- **Legal entities** (rechtspersonen): BV, NV, stichting, vereniging, cooperatie
- **Partnerships**: VOF (vennootschap onder firma), CV (commanditaire vennootschap), maatschap
- **Sole traders**: Eenmanszaak
- **Branches of foreign companies** (bijkantoren)
- **European entities**: SE (Societas Europaea), SCE (European Cooperative)

### Registration Deadline
- Legal entities: Within 8 days of incorporation
- Other enterprises: Before starting business activities

---

## Registration Process for a BV

### Step 1: Pre-incorporation
1. Reserve a company name (optional but recommended)
2. Engage a notaris to prepare the deed of incorporation and articles of association
3. Prepare required documents:
   - Identification of founders/shareholders (passport/ID)
   - Description of share capital structure
   - Appointment of directors (and supervisory board members if applicable)
   - Proof of registered address

### Step 2: Notarial Deed
- Notaris executes the deed of incorporation (akte van oprichting)
- Articles of association (statuten) are included in or attached to the deed
- The BV legally exists from the moment of execution of the notarial deed

### Step 3: KVK Registration
- **Who registers**: Usually the notaris, but directors can also register
- **Where**: Online via KVK or at a KVK office
- **Required information**:
  - Company name and any trade names (handelsnamen)
  - Legal form (BV)
  - Registered office (statutaire zetel) and visiting address
  - SBI code (Standaard Bedrijfsindeling - activity classification)
  - Details of directors and authorized signatories (tekenbevoegdheid)
  - Share capital information
  - UBO (Ultimate Beneficial Owner) information
- **KVK number**: Assigned upon registration (8-digit number)
- **RSIN**: Also assigned (legal entity identification number for government use)

### Step 4: Post-Registration
- Obtain a btw-nummer (VAT number) from the Belastingdienst
- Register for payroll tax if employing staff
- Open a business bank account
- Register at the UBO register (UBO-register)

## UBO Register (UBO-register)

### Legal Basis
- Implemented under the 4th/5th Anti-Money Laundering Directives
- Wet registratie uiteindelijk belanghebbenden van vennootschappen en andere juridische entiteiten (implemented via Handelsregisterwet)

### Who is a UBO?
A natural person who directly or indirectly:
- Holds more than 25% of shares
- Can exercise more than 25% of voting rights
- Has more than 25% beneficial interest (economisch belang)
- Has actual control (feitelijke zeggenschap)

If no UBO can be identified: all statutory directors (bestuurders) are registered as "pseudo-UBO"

### Registration
- Mandatory for all Dutch legal entities
- Information includes: name, date of birth, nationality, country of residence, nature and extent of interest
- Partially public (name, month/year of birth, nationality, country of residence, nature of interest are public; exact date of birth, address, BSN, and supporting documents are restricted)

### Note on CJEU Ruling
- CJEU ruling C-37/20 and C-601/20 (November 2022) declared public access to UBO registers invalid under EU law
- Netherlands implemented restricted access - only competent authorities and obliged entities have full access

## Key Filings at KVK

### Annual Filings
| Filing | Deadline | Applicable to |
|--------|----------|---------------|
| Jaarrekening (Annual accounts) | Within 12 months of financial year end (8 months preparation + 2 months adoption + 2 months filing) | All BVs and NVs |
| UBO changes | Within 7 days of change | All registered entities |
| Director/officer changes | Within 8 days | All registered entities |

### Annual Accounts (Jaarrekening) - Art. 2:394 BW
Filing size regimes (determines disclosure requirements):

| Category | Assets | Revenue | Employees |
|----------|--------|---------|-----------|
| Micro | <= EUR 450,000 | <= EUR 900,000 | <= 10 |
| Small (klein) | <= EUR 7,500,000 | <= EUR 15,000,000 | <= 50 |
| Medium (middelgroot) | <= EUR 25,000,000 | <= EUR 50,000,000 | <= 250 |
| Large (groot) | > EUR 25,000,000 | > EUR 50,000,000 | > 250 |

- **Micro**: May file abbreviated balance sheet only
- **Small**: Abbreviated balance sheet and limited notes; no profit and loss statement required
- **Medium**: Full accounts but certain exemptions; no audit required below certain thresholds
- **Large**: Full accounts with audit report

### Penalty for Late Filing
- Failure to file annual accounts timely creates a rebuttable presumption of improper management in bankruptcy (Art. 2:248 lid 2 BW)
- Possible economic offense (overtreding) under the WED

## KVK Extract (Uittreksel)

### Types
- **Uittreksel Handelsregister**: Basic registration data
- **Uittreksel met rechtstostand**: Including legal status, filed documents
- **Digitaal uittreksel**: Available online via kvk.nl

### Information Available
- Company name, KVK number, RSIN
- Legal form
- Registered office and address
- Directors and their signing authority (tekenbevoegdheid)
- Date of incorporation
- SBI activity codes
- Share capital (for BV/NV)
- Whether annual accounts have been filed

### Tekenbevoegdheid (Signing Authority)
Registered at KVK:
- **Zelfstandig bevoegd**: Authorized to act alone
- **Gezamenlijk bevoegd**: Must act together with another director
- **Beperkt bevoegd**: Limited authority (e.g., up to a certain amount)
- **Gevolmachtigde**: Authorized representative (procuratiehouder)
