# Dutch Corporate Governance Code

## Background
- Originally the **Code Tabaksblat** (2003), named after its chairman Morris Tabaksblat
- Updated in 2008 (Code Frijns), 2016 (Code Van Manen), and most recently in **2022**
- Applies to all companies listed on a Dutch regulated market (Euronext Amsterdam)
- "Comply or explain" principle (pas toe of leg uit): companies must either comply or explain deviations in their annual report

## Legal Basis
- Art. 2:391 lid 5 BW: Annual report must include a statement on compliance with the corporate governance code
- Besluit corporate governance code: Government decree designating the applicable code

---

## Key Principles (2022 Code)

### Chapter 1: Long-Term Value Creation
- The management board is responsible for the company's long-term value creation strategy
- Strategy should consider the interests of all stakeholders (shareholders, employees, customers, suppliers, society)
- ESG (Environmental, Social, Governance) considerations must be integrated into strategy
- The company should define its purpose (purpose statement)
- **Culture**: The board is responsible for embedding a culture of openness, accountability, and integrity

### Chapter 2: Management Board (Bestuur)
- **Composition**: Board should be diverse in gender, age, nationality, expertise, and background
- **Term**: Maximum 4 years per appointment, reappointment possible
- **Conflicts of interest**: Directors must report conflicts; conflicted directors may not participate in deliberation or decision-making
- **Remuneration**: Must be designed to promote long-term value creation; should not encourage excessive risk-taking
- **Severance**: Maximum 1 year's base salary

### Chapter 3: Supervisory Board (Raad van Commissarissen)
- **Independence**: Majority of supervisory board members must be independent
- **Independence criteria**: Not independent if, in the 5 years preceding appointment:
  - Employee of the company or group
  - Management board member of a company where a board member of this company is a supervisory board member
  - Significant business relationship with the company
  - Significant shareholder (>10%) or representative thereof
  - Close family member of any of the above
- **Chairman**: Must be independent
- **Committees**: Audit committee, remuneration committee, and selection and appointment committee required
- **Evaluation**: Annual self-evaluation; every 3 years with external facilitation
- **Term**: Maximum 2 terms of 4 years (8 years total)

### Chapter 4: Shareholders and General Meeting
- **Shareholder engagement**: Companies should engage actively with shareholders
- **Response time** (responstijd/reflectieperiode): Management board may invoke a reflection period of up to 180 days when shareholders propose matters that may fundamentally change the company's strategy (Art. 2:114b BW since 2021)
- **Anti-takeover measures**: Should be used sparingly and transparently; must be in the interest of all stakeholders
- **Related party transactions**: Must be announced and subject to supervisory board approval above the materiality threshold

### Chapter 5: One-Tier Board
- Specific provisions for companies with a one-tier board structure
- Non-executive directors fulfill the role equivalent to the supervisory board
- At least one non-executive director must be independent

## Comply or Explain

### How it Works
- Companies must include a corporate governance statement in their annual report
- For each provision not complied with: explain WHY and HOW the company's actual practice serves the same purpose
- The explanation must be substantive (boilerplate explanations are not acceptable)
- Shareholders can hold the board accountable for non-compliance at the AGM

### Enforcement
- No direct legal sanctions for non-compliance
- However:
  - AFM (Autoriteit Financiele Markten) monitors compliance
  - Proxy advisors and institutional investors assess compliance
  - Non-compliance may be considered in inquiry procedures (enqueteprocedure)
  - Art. 2:391 BW violation (failure to include CG statement) is an economic offense

## Key Governance Mechanisms in Dutch Law

### Protective Measures (Beschermingsmaatregelen)
Common anti-takeover defenses for Dutch listed companies:
1. **Stichting Continuiteit** (Continuity Foundation): Foundation that can acquire preference shares to dilute a hostile bidder
2. **Priority shares** (prioriteitsaandelen): Special shares with enhanced governance rights
3. **Certification of shares** (certificering): Shares held by an administrative office (administratiekantoor) that issues depository receipts; separates economic and voting rights
4. **Binding nomination rights**: Right to make binding nominations for board appointments
5. **Structuurregime**: Large company regime limits shareholder power

### Ondernemingsraad (Works Council)
- Required for enterprises with 50+ employees (WOR)
- Has advice rights (adviesrecht) on major business decisions (Art. 25 WOR):
  - Transfer of control
  - Major investments
  - Mass dismissals
  - Closure of operations
- Has consent rights (instemmingsrecht) on social policy matters (Art. 27 WOR)
- Has nomination rights for 1/3 of supervisory board members under structuurregime

## Diversity Requirements

### Wet bestuur en toezicht rechtspersonen (WBTR - since 2021)
Applies to all legal entities (not just listed):
- Codifies director duties for all legal entities
- Conflict of interest rules
- Applies to stichtingen and verenigingen as well

### Wet evenwichtiger verhouding (since 2022) - Diversity quotas
For listed companies (NV with listing):
- Supervisory board must be at least 1/3 men and 1/3 women ("ingroeiquotum")
- New appointments that do not contribute to meeting this quota are null (nietig)

For large companies (BV and NV meeting 2 of 3 criteria):
- Must set appropriate and ambitious diversity targets for management board, supervisory board, and senior management (subtop)
- Must report on targets and progress in annual report
