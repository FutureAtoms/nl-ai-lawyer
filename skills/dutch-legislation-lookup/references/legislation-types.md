# Types of Dutch Legislation (Soorten regelgeving)

## Hierarchy of Dutch Legislation

### 1. Grondwet (Constitution)
- The fundamental law of the Netherlands
- Amended by special procedure (requires two readings by Parliament with intervening elections)
- **Important limitation**: Art. 120 Grondwet - courts cannot review Acts of Parliament against the Constitution (toetsingsverbod)
- Courts CAN review against directly effective treaty provisions (Art. 93-94 Grondwet), including ECHR and EU law

### 2. Rijkswet (Kingdom Act)
- Applies to the entire Kingdom of the Netherlands (NL, Curacao, Aruba, Sint Maarten)
- Requires approval from parliaments of all constituent countries
- Examples: Rijkswet op het Nederlanderschap (nationality), Statuut voor het Koninkrijk

### 3. Wet in formele zin (Act of Parliament / Formal Act)
- Adopted jointly by the government and the States-General (Tweede Kamer + Eerste Kamer)
- The primary form of Dutch legislation
- Published in the Staatsblad (Stb.)
- Examples: Burgerlijk Wetboek, Wetboek van Strafrecht, Algemene wet bestuursrecht

### 4. Algemene Maatregel van Bestuur (AMvB / Government Decree)
- Adopted by the Crown (King + Council of Ministers / Kroon)
- Requires legal basis in a formal Act (delegation)
- Published in the Staatsblad
- Also called "Koninklijk Besluit" (Royal Decree) for certain matters
- Often contains detailed implementation rules
- Examples: Besluit algemene rechtspositie politie, various implementation decrees

### 5. Ministeriële regeling (Ministerial Regulation)
- Adopted by a Minister
- Requires legal basis in a formal Act or AMvB
- Published in the Staatscourant (Stcrt.)
- Contains the most detailed technical rules
- Examples: Regeling specifiek, technical standards, forms

### 6. Beleidsregels (Policy Rules)
- Adopted by administrative bodies under Art. 1:3 lid 4 Awb
- Not binding on citizens but self-binding on the administrative body
- Published in Staatscourant
- Can be invoked by citizens based on legitimate expectations (vertrouwensbeginsel)
- Examples: Tax authority guidelines, immigration policy rules (IND)

### 7. Decentrale regelgeving (Decentralized legislation)

#### Provinciale verordening (Provincial Regulation)
- Adopted by Provinciale Staten (provincial parliament)
- Examples: Environmental regulations, spatial planning rules

#### Gemeentelijke verordening (Municipal Regulation / Gemeenteverordening)
- Adopted by Gemeenteraad (municipal council)
- Examples: APV (Algemene Plaatselijke Verordening - general local ordinance), parking regulations, land use plans (bestemmingsplan, now omgevingsplan under Omgevingswet)

#### Waterschapsverordening (Water Board Regulation)
- Adopted by the general board of a waterschap
- Examples: Water management rules, levee maintenance

## Special Categories

### Verdragen (Treaties)
- International agreements binding the Netherlands
- Published in the Tractatenblad (Trb.)
- Directly effective provisions prevail over Dutch law (Art. 93-94 Grondwet)
- Key examples: ECHR, EU Treaties, bilateral tax treaties, Vienna Convention

### EU Legislation
| Type | Dutch term | Direct effect | Implementation needed |
|------|-----------|--------------|----------------------|
| Treaty | Verdrag | Yes | No |
| Regulation | Verordening | Yes | No |
| Directive | Richtlijn | No (generally) | Yes, by national law |
| Decision | Besluit | Yes (for addressees) | Depends |

### Noodwetgeving (Emergency Legislation)
- Special laws that can be activated in times of crisis
- Wet buitengewone bevoegdheden burgerlijk gezag
- Coördinatiewet uitzonderingstoestanden
- Used recently during COVID-19 pandemic (Tijdelijke wet maatregelen covid-19)

## Key Characteristics by Type

| Type | Adopted by | Published in | Legal basis needed | Court reviewable |
|------|-----------|-------------|-------------------|-----------------|
| Grondwet | Parliament (2x) + Crown | Stb. | N/A | No (Art. 120) |
| Rijkswet | Kingdom legislature | Stb. | Statuut | No (Art. 120) |
| Wet | Parliament + Crown | Stb. | Grondwet (if required) | No (Art. 120) |
| AMvB | Crown | Stb. | Wet | Yes |
| Min. regeling | Minister | Stcrt. | Wet/AMvB | Yes |
| Beleidsregel | Admin body | Stcrt. | Art. 4:81 Awb | Limited |
| Prov. verordening | Prov. Staten | Prov. blad | Provinciewet | Yes |
| Gem. verordening | Gemeenteraad | Gem. blad | Gemeentewet | Yes |

## Finding Legislation

### By Common Name
Use `search_legislation` with the common name or abbreviation.

### By BWB Identifier
Use `get_legislation` with the BWB number.

### By Subject Matter
Search using Dutch legal terms related to the subject.

### By Article Number
BW articles follow the format: `[Book number]:[Article number]`
- Example: Art. 6:74 BW = Book 6, Article 74 of the Civil Code

### Recent Changes
Check the Staatsblad for recent publications and the Eerste/Tweede Kamer websites for pending bills (wetsvoorstellen).
