# Dutch Collective Labor Agreement System (CAO-stelsel)

## What is a CAO?
A **Collectieve Arbeidsovereenkomst (CAO)** is a collective labor agreement between one or more employers (or employer organizations) and one or more trade unions (vakbonden), setting terms and conditions of employment for a specific sector or company.

## Legal Framework
- **Wet op de collectieve arbeidsovereenkomst (Wet CAO)** - 1927, governs formation and effects
- **Wet op het algemeen verbindend en het onverbindend verklaren van bepalingen van collectieve arbeidsovereenkomsten (Wet AVV)** - 1937, governs extension

---

## Types of CAOs

### 1. Bedrijfstak-CAO (Sector CAO)
- Covers an entire sector/industry (e.g., metalworking, healthcare, retail)
- Negotiated between sector employer organizations and trade unions
- Examples: CAO Metaal en Techniek, CAO Zorg en Welzijn, CAO Retail Non-Food

### 2. Ondernemings-CAO (Company CAO)
- Covers a single company or group of companies
- Negotiated between the specific employer and trade unions
- Examples: CAO KLM, CAO Philips, CAO Rijksoverheid

### 3. Raam-CAO (Framework CAO)
- Sets broad framework; details filled in at company level
- Common in sectors with diverse companies

---

## How a CAO Becomes Binding

### Direct Binding (Wet CAO)
A CAO directly binds:
1. The parties to the CAO (employers and unions who signed)
2. Members of the signatory organizations at the time the CAO is in force
3. The employment contracts of bound employees are automatically supplemented/overridden by the CAO (Art. 12-13 Wet CAO)

### Incorporatiebeding (Incorporation Clause)
- Even non-union employees can be bound if their individual contract refers to the applicable CAO
- Very common in practice: most employment contracts contain a clause like "de CAO [X] is van toepassing"

### Algemeen Verbindend Verklaring (AVV - General Binding Declaration)
- The Minister of Social Affairs can declare a sector CAO **generally binding** (algemeen verbindend)
- This extends the CAO to ALL employers and employees in the sector, regardless of union membership
- Requested by one or more CAO parties
- Requirement: CAO must apply to a "significant majority" (belangrijke meerderheid) of the sector (in practice: 55%+ representativeness)
- Duration: same as the CAO term (max 2 years per declaration)
- Published in Staatscourant
- Check status at uitvoeringsinstituut or SZW website

## Key CAO Provisions

Typical subjects covered by a CAO:

| Subject | Description |
|---------|-------------|
| Loonschalen | Salary scales and annual increases |
| Arbeidsduur | Working hours per week/year |
| Vakantiedagen | Vacation days (above statutory minimum) |
| Pensioen | Pension scheme (often at sector pension fund) |
| Ontslagvergoeding | Additional severance arrangements |
| Scholing | Training and development |
| Ziekteverzuim | Sick pay supplements (above 70% statutory minimum) |
| Toeslagen | Allowances (overtime, shift work, hazardous conditions) |
| Opzegtermijnen | Notice periods (may deviate from BW) |
| Proeftijd | Probationary period arrangements |
| Functie-indeling | Job classification system |
| Verlof | Additional leave types (care leave, bereavement, etc.) |

## Derogation from Statutory Law by CAO

Dutch employment law distinguishes different levels of mandatory protection:

### Dwingend recht (Fully mandatory)
- Cannot be deviated from by anyone
- Example: Art. 7:610 BW definition of employment contract

### Driekwart-dwingend recht (Three-quarter mandatory)
- Can only be deviated from by CAO (not by individual contract)
- Example: Art. 7:668a BW chain rule (ketenregeling) - CAO can extend to 6 contracts / 4 years

### Semi-dwingend recht (Semi-mandatory)
- Can be deviated from by written individual agreement
- Example: Art. 7:628 BW (risk of work allocation in first 6 months)

### Regelend recht (Default)
- Can be deviated from by any agreement
- Example: Art. 7:665 BW (effect of transfer of undertaking on pension)

## Relationship Between CAO and Individual Contract

### Hierarchy
1. Mandatory statutory law
2. CAO provisions
3. Individual employment contract
4. Employer regulations (arbeidsvoorwaardenregeling / personeelshandboek)

### Favorable Deviation (Gunstigere afwijking)
- Generally, individual contracts may provide more favorable terms than the CAO
- But CAO can be a "maximum CAO" (ceiling) or "standard CAO" (no deviation allowed) - check the specific CAO

### Nawerking (Aftereffect)
- When a CAO expires, its provisions continue to apply to existing employment contracts until replaced
- New employees are not automatically bound by expired CAO terms (unless via incorporatiebeding)

## Trade Unions in the Netherlands

### Major Unions
- **FNV** (Federatie Nederlandse Vakbeweging) - largest, ~1 million members
- **CNV** (Christelijk Nationaal Vakverbond) - Christian-democratic tradition, ~260,000 members
- **VCP** (Vakcentrale voor Professionals) - professionals and managers

### Employer Organizations
- **VNO-NCW** (Verbond van Nederlandse Ondernemingen - Nederlands Christelijk Werkgeversverbond) - largest employer organization
- **MKB-Nederland** - SME employers
- **AWVN** (Algemene Werkgeversvereniging Nederland) - provides CAO negotiation support
- Various sector-specific employer organizations

### Union Membership and Rights
- No mandatory union membership (vakverenigingsvrijheid)
- Employees cannot be discriminated against for union membership or activity
- Trade unions have standing to negotiate CAOs, participate in SER, and initiate collective actions (stakingsrecht)
