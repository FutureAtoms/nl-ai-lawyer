# Employment Contract (Arbeidsovereenkomst) - Requirements and Types

## Legal Definition (Art. 7:610 BW)
An employment contract is an agreement whereby one party (the employee / werknemer) undertakes to perform work in the service of the other party (the employer / werkgever) for a period of time in exchange for wages (loon).

### Three Essential Elements:
1. **Arbeid** (Work) - personal obligation to perform work
2. **Loon** (Wages) - remuneration for the work
3. **Gezag** (Authority/subordination) - employer has the right to give instructions

If all three elements are present, an employment contract exists regardless of how the parties label the relationship (Art. 7:610a BW - rechtsvermoeden: if someone works weekly or for 20+ hours per month for 3+ months, an employment relationship is presumed).

## Types of Employment Contracts

### 1. Indefinite Term (Onbepaalde tijd)
- Default type; no end date
- Can only be terminated by:
  - Mutual consent (vaststellingsovereenkomst)
  - UWV permit (ontslagvergunning)
  - Kantonrechter dissolution (ontbinding)
  - Immediate dismissal (ontslag op staande voet)
  - During probationary period

### 2. Fixed Term (Bepaalde tijd)
- Has a specific end date
- Ends by operation of law (van rechtswege) on the end date
- **Aanzegverplichting** (Art. 7:668 BW): For contracts of 6+ months, employer must notify the employee at least 1 month before the end date whether the contract will be renewed. Failure: penalty of up to 1 month's salary.

### 3. Chain Rule (Ketenregeling - Art. 7:668a BW)
**After WAB (2020)**:
- Maximum **3 consecutive fixed-term contracts** within a period of **36 months** (3 years)
- The 4th contract or a contract exceeding 36 months automatically becomes indefinite
- Chain is broken by an interval of **more than 6 months** (can be reduced to 3 months by CAO for seasonal work)
- CAO can derogate: up to 6 contracts in 4 years for certain functions

### 4. On-Call Contracts (Oproepovereenkomst - Art. 7:628a BW)
Types:
- **Nuluren-contract** (zero-hours contract): no guaranteed hours
- **Min-max contract**: minimum and maximum hours guaranteed
- **Voorovereenkomst**: framework agreement; new contract each call

**WAB rules (since 2020)**:
- After 12 months, employer must offer a contract with fixed hours equal to average hours worked in preceding 12 months
- Employee must be called at least 4 days in advance (can be reduced to 24 hours by CAO)
- If call is withdrawn within 4 days, employee is still entitled to wages for the planned hours
- Minimum per call: 3 hours' wages (even if less work is performed)

### 5. Temporary Agency Work (Uitzendovereenkomst - Art. 7:690 BW)
- Triangular relationship: uitzendbureau (agency), inlener (client), uitzendkracht (temp worker)
- Phase system (since WAB):
  - **Phase A**: First 78 worked weeks; uitzendbeding (agency clause) applies - contract ends when assignment ends
  - **Phase B**: Next 4 years; max 6 fixed-term contracts
  - **Phase C**: Indefinite contract
- Inlenersbeloning (client-company remuneration): temp workers entitled to at least the same pay as comparable employees of the client (since Waadi, reinforced by WAB)

## Mandatory Contract Terms

### Written Statement (Art. 7:655 BW - updated 2022 for EU Directive 2019/1152)
Employer must provide written information on:
1. Name and address of parties
2. Place of work
3. Function or nature of work
4. Date of commencement
5. Duration (if fixed-term)
6. Salary amount, components, and payment frequency
7. Working hours (daily/weekly)
8. Vacation entitlement
9. Notice period
10. Applicable CAO (if any)
11. Pension scheme (if any)
12. Whether the contract is with a temporary employment agency
13. **New since 2022**: Training entitlements, identity of social security institutions, procedure for termination

### Must be provided within 1 month of commencement (some elements within 1 week).

## Key Contract Clauses

### Probationary Period (Proeftijd - Art. 7:652 BW)
| Contract Duration | Maximum Proeftijd |
|-------------------|-------------------|
| < 6 months | Not permitted |
| 6 months - 2 years | 1 month |
| >= 2 years or indefinite | 2 months |

- Must be agreed in writing
- Must be equal for both parties
- During proeftijd: either party can terminate immediately without reason or notice
- Not allowed in successive contract (second/third contract) for same work

### Non-Compete Clause (Concurrentiebeding - Art. 7:653 BW)
- Must be in writing
- Employee must be 18+ (adult)
- **Fixed-term contracts**: Non-compete clause only valid if employer provides written justification of substantial business interests (zwaarwegende bedrijfs- of dienstbelangen) in the clause itself
- Court may partially or fully set aside the non-compete if:
  - Employee is unreasonably disadvantaged in proportion to employer's interest
  - Changed circumstances since execution
- Court may award compensation to employee if non-compete is upheld (vergoeding, Art. 7:653 lid 5)

### Non-Solicitation Clause (Relatiebeding)
- Treated as a form of concurrentiebeding by case law
- Same requirements as non-compete
- Must be specific about which relations are covered

### Penalty Clause (Boetebeding - Art. 7:650 BW)
- Special employment law rules (in addition to general Art. 6:91-6:94 BW)
- Must specify the penalty in the clause
- For the same act, both penalty AND damages cannot be claimed (unlike general contract law)
- Court can moderate (Art. 7:650 lid 6)

## Wages and Benefits

### Minimum Wage (WML)
- Statutory minimum wage adjusted twice yearly (January and July)
- As of 2024: minimum hourly wage (since WAB amendment, previously monthly)
- Applies to employees aged 21+ (youth rates for 15-20)
- 8% vacation allowance (vakantiebijslag) on top of gross salary (Art. 15 WML)

### Vacation Days (Art. 7:634 BW)
- Statutory minimum: 4x weekly working hours per year (e.g., 20 days for full-time 5-day week)
- Many CAOs provide additional (bovenwettelijke) days
- Statutory days expire 6 months after the year of accrual
- Bovenwettelijke days: 5-year prescription period
- Employer must actively encourage employees to take vacation (EU requirement per Max-Planck ruling, CJEU)

### Sick Pay (Art. 7:629 BW)
- Employer must pay at least 70% of salary during first 2 years of illness
- First year: at least 70% (many CAOs: 100% in year 1, 70% in year 2)
- During sick leave: employer cannot terminate (opzegverbod bij ziekte, Art. 7:670 lid 1)
- After 2 years: WIA (disability insurance) assessment by UWV

### Transition Payment (Transitievergoeding - Art. 7:673 BW)
- Due upon termination by employer or non-renewal of fixed-term contract (since WAB: from day 1)
- Calculation: 1/3 monthly salary per year of service (pro rata)
- Maximum: EUR 94,000 or one year's salary if higher (2024 figure, adjusted annually)
- Not due if: employee's serious culpable conduct, employee under 18 working <12 hours/week, termination by mutual consent (unless agreed), bankruptcy

## Kennismigrant (Highly Skilled Migrant) Employment Considerations

### Kennismigrant-Specific Contract Requirements
Employment contracts for kennismigrant employees must address additional requirements beyond standard BW 7:610 obligations:

1. **Minimum salary clause**: The contract must specify a gross salary meeting or exceeding the IND kennismigrant threshold:
   - Standard (30+): EUR 5,493/month (2025, excl. 8% vakantiegeld)
   - Under-30: EUR 4,028/month (2025, excl. 8% vakantiegeld)
   - Thresholds are indexed annually — contract should include annual salary review mechanism
2. **30% ruling clause** (if applicable): Include a provision specifying whether the 30% ruling applies and how gross/net salary is structured:
   - Belastingdienst taxable salary threshold: EUR 46,107 (standard) / EUR 35,048 (under-30 with Master's)
   - The 30% ruling application must be filed within 4 months of the start of employment
   - Current law: flat 30% through 2026, then 27% from 2027 (Belastingplan 2025)
3. **Salary reduction safeguard**: If salary drops below the IND threshold, the employer must notify IND within 4 weeks (Art. 2e Vw 2000 informatieplicht). Build in contractual protection against salary reductions that would breach immigration thresholds.

### Notice Period Implications for Permit Holders
- **Standard Dutch notice periods apply** (Art. 7:672 BW), but the consequences are more severe for kennismigrant employees:
  - Upon termination: the employee has only a **3-month grace period** (Art. 3.31a Vb 2000) to find new qualifying employment
  - If no new employer files a kennismigrant application within 3 months: IND may withdraw the residence permit
  - The employee must then leave the Netherlands (or apply for a different permit type)
- **Vaststellingsovereenkomst (settlement agreement)**: When negotiating termination with a kennismigrant:
  - Consider extending the notice period / gardening leave to maximize job search time
  - The WW (unemployment benefit) waiting period interacts with the 3-month immigration grace period
  - Include a clause addressing 30% ruling continuity (new employer must apply within 3 months)
- **Collective dismissal (WMCO)**: Kennismigrant employees are subject to the same rules, but their immigration status adds urgency

### Probationary Period (Proeftijd) Risks for Kennismigrant Employees
- **During proeftijd**: either party can terminate immediately without reason (Art. 7:652 BW)
- **Immigration impact of proeftijd dismissal**:
  - The 3-month grace period applies from the date of termination
  - The employee has already relocated to the Netherlands, arranged housing, possibly brought family
  - Proeftijd dismissal in the first month of employment creates an extremely short total window (1 month worked + 3 months grace = leaving NL within ~4 months of arrival)
- **30% ruling impact**: If terminated during proeftijd, the 30% ruling application may not yet be approved; a new employer must file a fresh application
- **Practical recommendation**: Employers should exercise caution with proeftijd for kennismigrant employees given the disproportionate personal impact. Consider shorter proeftijd (1 month instead of 2) or providing additional contractual notice/severance.
