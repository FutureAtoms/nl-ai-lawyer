# Key Case Law - Dutch Employment Law

> This file contains landmark Dutch court decisions on employment law, covering
> employer liability, unilateral changes, dismissal law, and post-WWZ/WAB developments.
> All ECLI numbers should be verified against [Rechtspraak.nl](https://uitspraken.rechtspraak.nl).

---

## 1. Employer Liability (Werkgeversaansprakelijkheid)

### HR 27 November 2009, ECLI:NL:HR:2009:BJ6596 (Van der Grijp/Stam)

- **Court:** Hoge Raad der Nederlanden
- **Date:** 27 November 2009
- **Parties:** Van der Grijp v. Stam
- **Key Legal Principle:** Under Art. 7:658 BW, the employer has a far-reaching duty
  of care (*zorgplicht*) to ensure the safety of the work environment. The employer
  must demonstrate that it took all reasonably necessary measures to prevent the
  employee from suffering damages in the exercise of their duties. The threshold for
  employer liability is high: the employer is liable unless it proves it fulfilled
  its duty of care or the damages were caused by the employee's willful intent or
  deliberate recklessness (*opzet of bewuste roekeloosheid*).
- **Relevance:** Establishes that the employer bears the burden of proof regarding
  its compliance with the duty of care. In practice, this creates near-strict
  liability for workplace accidents. Employees need only show that they suffered
  damage in the exercise of their duties.

### HR 11 November 2011, ECLI:NL:HR:2011:BR5215 (TNT/Wijenberg)

- **Court:** Hoge Raad der Nederlanden
- **Date:** 11 November 2011
- **Parties:** TNT Post Productie B.V. v. Wijenberg
- **Key Legal Principle:** Art. 7:658 BW also applies to non-traditional work
  situations, including accidents during work-related travel and activities that
  are closely connected to the employment (*in de uitoefening van de werkzaamheden*).
  The employer's duty of care extends beyond the physical workplace.
- **Relevance:** Important for assessing employer liability in remote work, travel,
  and off-site work situations.

## 2. Unilateral Change of Employment Conditions (Eenzijdige Wijziging)

### HR 11 July 2008, ECLI:NL:HR:2008:BD1847 (Stoof/Mammoet)

- **Court:** Hoge Raad der Nederlanden
- **Date:** 11 July 2008
- **Parties:** Stoof v. Mammoet Transport B.V.
- **Key Legal Principle:** Even without a written unilateral change clause
  (*eenzijdig wijzigingsbeding*, Art. 7:613 BW), an employer may propose changes
  to employment conditions based on *goed werkgeverschap* (good employer practice)
  and *goed werknemerschap* (good employee practice) under Art. 7:611 BW. The test
  involves three steps:
  1. Has the employer, as a good employer, had sufficient cause (*aanleiding*) to
     make a proposal for change?
  2. Is the proposal reasonable (*redelijk*)?
  3. Can acceptance of the proposal reasonably be expected from the employee
     (*in redelijkheid te aanvaarden*)?
- **Relevance:** The Stoof/Mammoet test is the standard for unilateral changes when
  there is no Art. 7:613 BW clause. It balances employer flexibility against employee
  protection and is frequently applied in reorganization and cost-reduction scenarios.

### HR 29 November 2019, ECLI:NL:HR:2019:1864 (FairWork)

- **Court:** Hoge Raad der Nederlanden
- **Date:** 29 November 2019
- **Parties:** FairWork v. various parties
- **Key Legal Principle:** When an Art. 7:613 BW clause exists, the employer may
  invoke it only if it has a *zwaarwichtig belang* (weighty interest) that outweighs
  the employee's interest in maintaining the existing conditions. The interests must
  be weighed on a case-by-case basis.
- **Relevance:** Clarifies the relationship between Art. 7:613 BW (contractual
  change clause) and Art. 7:611 BW (Stoof/Mammoet reasonableness test). The two
  routes have different requirements and thresholds.

## 3. Dismissal Law (Ontslagrecht)

### Transitievergoeding (Transition Payment)

### HR 14 September 2007, ECLI:NL:HR:2007:BA4117

- **Court:** Hoge Raad der Nederlanden
- **Date:** 14 September 2007
- **Parties:** Not commonly cited by party name
- **Key Legal Principle:** This pre-WWZ decision established principles regarding
  the calculation and fairness of severance payments. Under the current WWZ/WAB
  regime (since 2015/2020), the *transitievergoeding* is calculated under
  Art. 7:673 BW at 1/3 monthly salary per year of service, from day one.
- **Relevance:** While the old kantonrechtersformule is no longer applicable, the
  principles of proportionality and fairness in severance remain relevant. The
  *billijke vergoeding* (fair compensation, Art. 7:681/7:671b lid 9 BW) is an
  additional award for cases of serious culpable conduct (*ernstig verwijtbaar handelen*)
  by the employer.

### HR 30 June 2017, ECLI:NL:HR:2017:1187 (New Hairstyle)

- **Court:** Hoge Raad der Nederlanden
- **Date:** 30 June 2017
- **Parties:** New Hairstyle B.V. v. employee
- **Key Legal Principle:** Clarified how the *billijke vergoeding* under Art. 7:681 BW
  should be calculated. The court must consider all circumstances of the case,
  including: the extent to which the employer is at fault, the duration of employment,
  the employee's income and job prospects, and the consequences of the dismissal.
  It is not a punitive measure but compensatory in nature.
- **Relevance:** The leading case on billijke vergoeding calculation. Replaced the
  earlier uncertainty about whether the billijke vergoeding should be modest or
  fully compensatory.

## 4. Disfunctioneren (Underperformance) - Dossier Requirements

### Key Kantonrechter Decisions

The following principles emerge from consistent kantonrechter case law on dismissal
for underperformance (*disfunctioneren*, Art. 7:669 lid 3 sub d BW):

#### Rb. Amsterdam 7 March 2016, ECLI:NL:RBAMS:2016:1357

- **Court:** Rechtbank Amsterdam (kantonrechter)
- **Key Legal Principle:** The employer must demonstrate:
  1. The employee has been clearly notified of the underperformance.
  2. The employee has been given a reasonable opportunity to improve (*verbetertraject*).
  3. The improvement trajectory was sufficiently concrete with measurable goals.
  4. The employer provided adequate coaching, guidance, and support.
  5. The underperformance is not caused by inadequate working conditions, illness,
     or insufficient training.
- **Relevance:** The dossier requirements for disfunctioneren are exacting. Dismissal
  requests are frequently denied due to insufficient dossier documentation.

#### Rb. Midden-Nederland 4 October 2017, ECLI:NL:RBMNE:2017:4993

- **Court:** Rechtbank Midden-Nederland (kantonrechter)
- **Key Legal Principle:** A performance improvement plan (PIP / verbeterplan) must
  be genuine and proportionate. A PIP that is designed to fail or that sets
  unrealistic targets may result in denial of the dismissal request and a billijke
  vergoeding award against the employer.
- **Relevance:** Employers must approach verbetertrajecten in good faith. Sham PIPs
  are a significant risk factor in dismissal proceedings.

## 5. Non-Competition Clause (Concurrentiebeding)

### Post-WWZ Regime for Fixed-Term Contracts

Since 1 January 2015 (WWZ), Art. 7:653 lid 2 BW provides that a non-competition
clause in a fixed-term employment contract (*arbeidsovereenkomst voor bepaalde tijd*)
is in principle not permitted, unless the employer provides a written justification
(*schriftelijke motivering*) that the clause is necessary due to weighty business
or service interests (*zwaarwegende bedrijfs- of dienstbelangen*).

#### Hof Arnhem-Leeuwarden 24 September 2019, ECLI:NL:GHARL:2019:7739

- **Court:** Gerechtshof Arnhem-Leeuwarden
- **Date:** 24 September 2019
- **Key Legal Principle:** The written justification must be provided at the time
  of concluding the contract (not retrospectively) and must be sufficiently specific.
  A generic motivation that merely recites the statutory text is insufficient.
  The justification must explain why this specific employee in this specific role
  requires a non-competition clause.
- **Relevance:** Many non-competition clauses in fixed-term contracts are unenforceable
  due to inadequate motivation. This is a key review point for employment contracts.

#### Rb. Amsterdam 23 July 2015, ECLI:NL:RBAMS:2015:4864

- **Court:** Rechtbank Amsterdam (kantonrechter)
- **Date:** 23 July 2015
- **Key Legal Principle:** The court may set aside (*geheel of gedeeltelijk vernietigen*)
  a non-competition clause if the employee is unfairly disadvantaged in proportion
  to the employer's legitimate interest. Under Art. 7:653 lid 3 BW, the court
  weighs the interests of both parties.
- **Relevance:** Even valid non-competition clauses may be moderated or nullified
  in individual cases, particularly where the employee faces significant career
  limitations.

## 6. Key UWV Dismissal Decisions

The UWV (Uitvoeringsinstituut Werknemersverzekeringen) handles dismissal permits
for redundancy (*bedrijfseconomische redenen*, Art. 7:669 lid 3 sub a BW) and
long-term incapacity (*langdurige arbeidsongeschiktheid*, Art. 7:669 lid 3 sub b BW).

### Key Principles from UWV Practice

1. **Afspiegelingsbeginsel** (reflection principle): For redundancy dismissals,
   the employer must apply the reflection principle (Art. 11 Ontslagregeling),
   selecting employees for dismissal within interchangeable function groups
   based on age cohorts. LIFO applies within each cohort.

2. **Herplaatsingsplicht** (redeployment obligation): Before dismissal, the
   employer must investigate whether the employee can be redeployed in a suitable
   alternative position within the company or group (Art. 7:669 lid 1 BW).

3. **Opzegverboden** (dismissal prohibitions): UWV must refuse a permit if a
   dismissal prohibition applies (Art. 7:670 BW), including during illness (first
   two years), pregnancy, maternity leave, and works council membership.

### UWV Uitvoeringsregels

- *Uitvoeringsregels Ontslag om bedrijfseconomische redenen* (current version)
- *Uitvoeringsregels Ontslag wegens langdurige arbeidsongeschiktheid* (current version)
- Available at [uwv.nl](https://www.uwv.nl)

## Notes for AI System

- Post-WWZ (2015) and post-WAB (2020) fundamentally reformed Dutch dismissal law.
  Always verify which regime applies based on the date of the employment contract
  and the dismissal action.
- The transitievergoeding is now due from day one (post-WAB), calculated at 1/3
  monthly salary per year of service (Art. 7:673 BW).
- The cumulatiegrond (i-ground, Art. 7:669 lid 3 sub i BW, post-WAB) allows courts
  to combine insufficient dismissal grounds, with a potential additional 50%
  transitievergoeding surcharge.
- Non-competition clauses in bepaalde tijd contracts require specific written
  motivation at the time of contract conclusion.
- Disfunctioneren dismissals require comprehensive dossiers with documented
  improvement trajectories. Flag inadequate dossiers as a risk.
