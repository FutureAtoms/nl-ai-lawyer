# Key Employment Legislation: WWZ and WAB Overview

## Wet Werk en Zekerheid (WWZ) - Work and Security Act (2015)

### Purpose
Major reform of Dutch employment law aimed at:
1. Making flexible employment less flexible (strengthening position of flex workers)
2. Making permanent employment less permanent (simplifying dismissal law)
3. Reducing unemployment duration

### Key Changes Introduced

#### 1. Dismissal Law Reform (1 July 2015)
- **Closed system of dismissal grounds** (gesloten ontslagstelsel): employer must prove one of the statutory grounds (a through h)
- **Route assignment**: economic redundancy and long-term illness to UWV; all other grounds to kantonrechter
- **Transitievergoeding**: statutory transition payment replacing the old kantonrechtersformule
- **Appeal rights**: right to appeal kantonrechter decisions to Gerechtshof (previously no appeal from kantonrechter ontbinding)

#### 2. Chain Rule Tightening (1 July 2015)
- Maximum **3 contracts in 24 months** (reduced from 36 months)
- Break period to reset chain: 6 months (increased from 3 months)
- *Note: WAB later reversed this back to 36 months*

#### 3. Proeftijd and Concurrentiebeding (1 January 2015)
- No proeftijd for contracts of 6 months or less
- Concurrentiebeding in fixed-term contracts only valid with written motivation of substantial business interest

#### 4. Aanzegverplichting (1 January 2015)
- Employer must notify employee 1 month before end of fixed-term contract (6+ months) whether contract will be renewed
- Penalty: up to 1 month's salary for non-compliance

#### 5. Transitievergoeding (1 July 2015)
- Statutory formula: 1/3 monthly salary per year of service
- Maximum: EUR 75,000 or 1 year's salary (adjusted annually)
- Originally: only after 2 years of service
- Replaced the kantonrechtersformule (judges' formula) which was more generous in many cases

## Wet Arbeidsmarkt in Balans (WAB) - Balanced Labor Market Act (2020)

### Purpose
Address unintended consequences of the WWZ and further balance the labor market between flex and permanent workers.

### Key Changes Introduced (1 January 2020)

#### 1. Cumulation Ground (i-grond)
- **New dismissal ground**: allows combining two or more partial grounds (c through h)
- If dismissal granted on i-grond: kantonrechter may award up to **50% extra** transitievergoeding
- Addresses the problem that strict WWZ grounds made it very difficult to dismiss

#### 2. Chain Rule Relaxation
- **Back to 36 months** (3 years) instead of 24 months
- Still maximum 3 contracts
- CAO derogation: up to 6 contracts in 4 years for specific functions
- Seasonal workers: CAO can reduce break period to 3 months

#### 3. Transitievergoeding from Day 1
- Transition payment accrues from the first day of employment (not after 2 years as under WWZ)
- Rate remains 1/3 monthly salary per year of service (pro rata)
- Removed the higher rate for employees 50+ with 10+ years of service

#### 4. On-Call Workers (Oproepkrachten)
- **12-month offer obligation**: after 12 months, employer must offer contract with fixed hours equal to average worked hours
- **4-day call notice**: employee must be called at least 4 days in advance
- **3-hour minimum per call**: even if less work is available
- **Withdrawal penalty**: if employer withdraws the call within 4 days, wages for planned hours still owed

#### 5. Payroll Workers (Payrollwerknemers)
- New definition of payroll employment (Art. 7:692 BW)
- Payroll workers entitled to same employment conditions as direct employees of the client (adequate pensioenregeling)
- Payroll companies cannot use the lighter temporary agency work regime

#### 6. WW Premium Differentiation
- Employers pay **lower WW premium** for permanent contracts (vast) and **higher premium** for flexible contracts (flex)
- Incentivizes permanent employment
- Low premium: approximately 2.64%; High premium: approximately 7.64% (rates vary)

#### 7. Proeftijd
- Original WAB proposal to allow 5-month proeftijd for indefinite contracts was withdrawn during parliamentary debate
- Current rules remain: max 2 months for indefinite, max 1 month for 6-month to 2-year contracts

## Other Key Employment Legislation

### Wet transparante en voorspelbare arbeidsvoorwaarden (2022)
Implementation of EU Directive 2019/1152 on transparent and predictable working conditions:
- Expanded information obligations for employers
- Restrictions on exclusivity clauses (nevenwerkzaamhedenbeding): employer can only prohibit side activities if objectively justified
- Mandatory training must be free and count as working time
- Employees with 26+ weeks of service can request more predictable working conditions

### Wet flexibel werken (WFW) (2016)
- Right to request changes to working hours, working times, and place of work
- Employer must approve unless there are compelling business reasons
- Applies to employers with 10+ employees
- Employee must have been employed for at least 26 weeks

### Wet gelijke behandeling (Equal Treatment Act)
- Prohibition of discrimination on grounds of religion, belief, political opinion, race, sex, nationality, sexual orientation, civil status
- Applies to hiring, terms of employment, promotion, training, and dismissal
- Enforcement: College voor de Rechten van de Mens (Netherlands Institute for Human Rights)

### Wet aanpak schijnconstructies (WAS) (2015)
- Combating sham employment constructions
- Chain liability (ketenaansprakelijkheid) for underpayment of minimum wage in subcontracting chains
- Mandatory specification of salary components on pay slip
- Minimum wage must be paid via bank transfer (no cash payment)

### Wet betaald ouderschapsverlof (2022)
- 9 weeks of paid parental leave (70% of salary, max dagloon) via UWV
- Must be taken in first year of child's life
- In addition to existing unpaid parental leave entitlement (26 weeks total)

## Timeline of Major Employment Law Changes

| Year | Legislation | Key Change |
|------|-----------|------------|
| 2015 | WWZ Phase 1 | Aanzegverplichting, proeftijd/concurrentiebeding restrictions |
| 2015 | WWZ Phase 2 | New dismissal system, transitievergoeding, chain rule to 24 months |
| 2015 | WAS | Anti-sham constructions |
| 2016 | WFW | Right to flexible working |
| 2020 | WAB | Cumulation ground, chain rule back to 36 months, on-call reforms, payroll reforms, WW premium differentiation |
| 2022 | WTVA | Transparent and predictable working conditions |
| 2022 | Betaald ouderschapsverlof | Paid parental leave |
