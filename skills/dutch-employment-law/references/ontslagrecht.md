# Dutch Dismissal Law (Ontslagrecht)

## Overview of Dismissal Routes

Since the WWZ (2015) and WAB (2020), Dutch dismissal law follows a dual-track system with specific grounds (ontslaggronden) assigned to specific routes.

---

## Route 1: UWV Ontslagvergunning (Administrative Route)

### Applicable Grounds
- **a-grond**: Bedrijfseconomische redenen (business economic reasons / redundancy)
- **b-grond**: Langdurige arbeidsongeschiktheid (prolonged incapacity for work - 2+ years)

### Procedure
1. Employer files application at UWV (Uitvoeringsinstituut Werknemersverzekeringen)
2. Employee has 14 days to respond (verweer)
3. Possible second round of written submissions
4. UWV decides (typically within 4-8 weeks)
5. If granted: employer may terminate with notice (opzegtermijn), minus the UWV procedure time (minimum 1 month notice remains)
6. If denied: employer may apply to kantonrechter for dissolution

### A-grond Requirements (Redundancy)
- Genuine business economic reason (e.g., poor financial results, reorganization, outsourcing, closure)
- Proper selection of employees for redundancy using **afspiegelingsbeginsel** (reflection principle):
  - Divide employees into interchangeable function groups (uitwisselbare functies)
  - Within each group, divide by age brackets (15-25, 25-35, 35-45, 45-55, 55+)
  - Last-in-first-out within each age bracket, proportionally
- No suitable alternative position (herplaatsingsmogelijkheden) available
- Works council advice (if applicable - Art. 25 WOR)

### B-grond Requirements (Long-term Illness)
- Employee has been ill for at least 104 weeks (2 years)
- No prospect of recovery within 26 weeks
- Employee cannot perform adapted work (passende arbeid)
- Employer has fulfilled all re-integration obligations (Wet verbetering poortwachter)
- UWV has issued a positive WIA assessment or the waiting period has expired

## Route 2: Kantonrechter Ontbinding (Judicial Route)

### Applicable Grounds (Art. 7:669 lid 3 BW)
- **c-grond**: Frequent short-term absenteeism (regelmatig ziekteverzuim) with unacceptable consequences for the business
- **d-grond**: Dysfunctional performance (disfunctioneren) - employee does not meet job requirements
- **e-grond**: Culpable acts or omissions (verwijtbaar handelen of nalaten)
- **f-grond**: Refusal to perform work on grounds of serious conscientious objection (gewetensbezwaren)
- **g-grond**: Disrupted working relationship (verstoorde arbeidsverhouding)
- **h-grond**: Residual ground (andere omstandigheden) - catch-all for situations not fitting other grounds
- **i-grond (WAB, since 2020)**: Cumulation ground (cumulatiegrond) - combination of two or more of c through h grounds, each partially but not fully met

### Procedure
1. Employer files petition (verzoekschrift) at the kantonrechter
2. Hearing (mondelinge behandeling) - typically within 4-6 weeks
3. Decision (beschikking)
4. If granted: kantonrechter determines termination date (typically 1 month notice equivalent)
5. Appeal to Gerechtshof possible within 3 months

### Key Requirements per Ground:

#### D-grond (Dysfunctional Performance)
- Employee must have been informed of performance issues
- Employee must have been given opportunity and support to improve (verbetertraject)
- Dysfunctioning must not be caused by insufficient training or working conditions
- Sufficient dossier required

#### E-grond (Culpable Acts)
- Acts must be serious enough to justify dismissal
- Less serious than required for summary dismissal (ontslag op staande voet)
- Examples: repeated policy violations, misconduct, harassment (if not rising to dringende reden level)

#### G-grond (Disrupted Relationship)
- Disruption must be serious and lasting
- Both parties' contributions to the disruption are considered
- Employer must have made reasonable efforts to resolve the situation (mediation)
- No prospect of recovery

#### I-grond (Cumulation - since WAB 2020)
- Allows combining partial grounds (e.g., partial disfunctioneren + partial verstoorde arbeidsverhouding)
- If granted on i-grond: kantonrechter may award up to 50% extra transitievergoeding on top of the regular amount

## Route 3: Ontslag op Staande Voet (Summary Dismissal - Art. 7:677-7:678 BW)

### Requirements
1. **Dringende reden** (urgent reason): facts or circumstances making it unreasonable to continue the employment
2. **Onverwijld** (immediate): dismissal must follow immediately after the employer becomes aware of the reason
3. **Mededeling** (communication): employer must communicate the reason to the employee at the time of dismissal

### Examples of Urgent Reasons (Art. 7:678 BW)
- Theft, fraud, embezzlement
- Serious threats or violence
- Willful damage to employer's property
- Persistent refusal to follow reasonable instructions
- Gross breach of confidentiality
- Providing false information upon hiring (if material)

### Risk
- If the summary dismissal is challenged and the kantonrechter finds it was not justified:
  - Employee may claim reinstatement (herstel dienstverband) or billijke vergoeding (fair compensation)
  - No cap on billijke vergoeding - can be very high (New Hairstyle ruling, ECLI:NL:HR:2017:1187)

---

## Route 4: Mutual Consent (Vaststellingsovereenkomst - Art. 7:900 BW)

### Key Points
- Written settlement agreement (beeindigingsovereenkomst / vaststellingsovereenkomst)
- Employee has 14-day cooling-off period (bedenktijd) to revoke (Art. 7:670b lid 2 BW)
- If employer fails to mention this right: cooling-off extends to 21 days
- WW (unemployment benefit) eligibility: employee must not have taken initiative for termination
- Standard elements: termination date, transition payment, final settlement, references, release of non-compete

---

## Notice Periods (Opzegtermijnen - Art. 7:672 BW)

### Statutory Notice Period (Employer)
| Duration of Employment | Notice Period |
|----------------------|---------------|
| < 5 years | 1 month |
| 5-10 years | 2 months |
| 10-15 years | 3 months |
| 15+ years | 4 months |

### Statutory Notice Period (Employee)
- 1 month (unless otherwise agreed; employer's notice period must be at least double the employee's)

### CAO may deviate from statutory notice periods

---

## Prohibited Dismissals (Opzegverboden - Art. 7:670 BW)

Employer cannot terminate during:
- Illness (first 2 years)
- Pregnancy and maternity/paternity leave
- Military service
- Works council membership (or up to 2 years after)
- Union activity
- Compliance with compulsory school attendance (for young workers)

---

## Transition Payment (Transitievergoeding - Art. 7:673 BW)

### Since WAB (2020):
- Accrues from **day 1** of employment (previously: after 2 years)
- Rate: **1/3 monthly salary per year of service** (pro rata for partial years)
- Includes: base salary + fixed allowances + average variable pay + vacation allowance (8%)
- **Maximum (2024)**: EUR 94,000 or 1 year's salary if higher
- Due in all termination scenarios except:
  - Employee seriously culpable (ernstig verwijtbaar)
  - Employee under 18 working <12 hours/week
  - Termination at pension age
  - Bankruptcy
- Can be increased by court (billijke vergoeding) if employer seriously culpable

### Compensatieregeling (Reimbursement for Employers)
- Employers can claim reimbursement from UWV for transition payments paid after 2-year illness (Regeling compensatie transitievergoeding)
