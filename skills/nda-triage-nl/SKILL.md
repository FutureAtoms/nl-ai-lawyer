# NDA Triage - Dutch Law (Geheimhoudingsovereenkomst)

## When to Use
- User submits an NDA or confidentiality agreement for quick pre-screening
- User asks whether specific NDA clauses are standard under Dutch law
- User wants to compare NDA terms against market norms
- User asks about enforceability of NDA provisions under Dutch law
- User asks about mutual vs. unilateral NDA differences
- User asks about penalty clauses (boetebeding) in NDAs
- User needs a quick risk assessment before signing an NDA

## Process
1. **Identify NDA type** - Mutual (wederzijds) or unilateral (eenzijdig); standalone or embedded in a larger agreement
2. **Identify the parties** - B2B or B2C; relative bargaining positions; sector-specific considerations
3. **Check governing law** - Confirm Dutch law applies; if foreign law, flag and recommend local counsel
4. **Clause-by-clause triage** - For each key clause, assign a risk rating:
   - **GREEN** - Standard market position; no concerns
   - **YELLOW** - Non-standard but negotiable; warrants attention
   - **RED** - Legally problematic, potentially unenforceable, or significantly unfavorable
5. **Cross-clause check** - Identify internal inconsistencies, missing clauses, or cumulative risk
6. **Generate triage summary** - Overall risk level with prioritized action items
7. **Append disclaimer**

## Triage Criteria Per Clause

### 1. Definition of Confidential Information (Definitie vertrouwelijke informatie)

| Rating | Criteria |
|--------|----------|
| GREEN | Clear, specific definition; reasonable scope; includes common categories (technical, commercial, financial); marked information + information reasonably understood as confidential |
| YELLOW | Overly broad ("all information exchanged"); no marking requirement; includes publicly available information without carve-out |
| RED | Catch-all definition with no boundaries; includes information the disclosing party does not own; no ability to determine what is covered |

### 2. Scope of Obligations (Omvang geheimhoudingsverplichtingen)

| Rating | Criteria |
|--------|----------|
| GREEN | Obligation not to disclose + not to use except for the agreed purpose; reasonable care standard (same care as own confidential information, but not less than reasonable care) |
| YELLOW | Strict liability standard (no fault required); obligation extends beyond employees to all agents without limitation; no carve-out for legal/professional advisors |
| RED | Absolute non-disclosure without any exceptions; guarantee of result rather than best efforts where disproportionate; obligation to prevent any unauthorized access regardless of circumstances |

### 3. Standard Exceptions/Carve-Outs (Uitzonderingen)

| Rating | Criteria |
|--------|----------|
| GREEN | All four standard carve-outs present: (1) public domain, (2) already known/independently developed, (3) received from third party without restriction, (4) legally compelled disclosure |
| YELLOW | Missing one carve-out; carve-outs subject to onerous proof requirements; no residual knowledge clause where relevant |
| RED | No carve-outs at all; carve-outs effectively nullified by conditions; legally compelled disclosure carve-out absent |

### 4. Term and Duration (Looptijd)

| Rating | Criteria |
|--------|----------|
| GREEN | Agreement term 2-3 years; confidentiality obligation 2-5 years after termination; reasonable for the type of information |
| YELLOW | Indefinite confidentiality obligation; term exceeds 5 years without justification; no distinction between trade secrets and other confidential information |
| RED | Perpetual obligation for non-trade-secret information; no termination mechanism; term grossly disproportionate to the nature of the information |

### 5. Return and Destruction (Teruggave en vernietiging)

| Rating | Criteria |
|--------|----------|
| GREEN | Obligation to return or destroy upon request or termination; reasonable timeline (30 days); exception for legally required retention and automatic backup systems |
| YELLOW | No exception for legally required retention (e.g., tax records, Wwft); immediate return requirement without reasonable timeline; certification of destruction required |
| RED | Obligation to destroy information that must be legally retained; no mechanism for confirming compliance; includes obligation to purge backup systems where technically infeasible |

### 6. Penalty Clause (Boetebeding)

| Rating | Criteria |
|--------|----------|
| GREEN | Penalty EUR 10,000-25,000 per breach + EUR 1,000-2,500 per day continuing breach; penalty in lieu of actual damages (Art. 6:92 BW default); reasonable cap |
| YELLOW | Penalty EUR 25,000-50,000 per breach + EUR 2,500-5,000 per day; penalty cumulative with actual damages (deviation from Art. 6:92 BW); no cap on daily penalties |
| RED | Penalty exceeding EUR 100,000 per breach; grossly disproportionate to potential harm; daily penalty without cap and cumulative with full damages; no judicial moderation clause |

### 7. Non-Solicitation (Non-wervingsbeding)

| Rating | Criteria |
|--------|----------|
| GREEN | No non-solicitation clause (not standard in NDAs); or limited to active solicitation of specific involved employees for 6-12 months |
| YELLOW | Broad non-solicitation covering all employees; duration exceeds 12 months; includes passive hiring (responding to unsolicited applications) |
| RED | Non-compete disguised as non-solicitation; covers customers and suppliers beyond what is relevant; no time limit; disproportionate penalty attached |

### 8. Governing Law and Dispute Resolution (Toepasselijk recht en geschillenbeslechting)

| Rating | Criteria |
|--------|----------|
| GREEN | Dutch law; Dutch courts (competent court in relevant district); or SGOA/NAI arbitration for tech disputes |
| YELLOW | Foreign law but reasonable jurisdiction (e.g., English law); arbitration with high costs relative to dispute value |
| RED | Unfavorable foreign law; exclusive jurisdiction in a distant or unfavorable forum; mandatory arbitration with prohibitive costs; waiver of right to seek interim measures |

## Key Legal Provisions

### Contract Law Basis
- **Art. 6:248 BW** - Reasonableness and fairness (redelijkheid en billijkheid) supplement and limit contractual obligations
- **Art. 6:233 BW** - Voidability of unreasonably onerous general terms (if NDA is part of general terms)
- **Art. 6:74 BW** - Liability for breach of contract (toerekenbare tekortkoming)
- **Art. 6:265 BW** - Dissolution of contract upon breach (ontbinding)

### Penalty Clause Law
- **Art. 6:91 BW** - Definition of penalty clause (boetebeding)
- **Art. 6:92 BW** - Default: penalty replaces damages (no cumulation); creditor cannot claim both penalty and performance; penalty in lieu of supplementary damages
- **Art. 6:93 BW** - Notice of default (ingebrekestelling) generally required before penalty is due
- **Art. 6:94 BW** - Judicial moderation (rechterlijke matiging): court may reduce the penalty if fairness clearly so requires

### Trade Secrets
- **Wet bescherming bedrijfsgeheimen (Wbb)** - Dutch Trade Secrets Act (implementing EU Directive 2016/943)
- Trade secrets have separate statutory protection independent of any NDA
- NDA may provide additional contractual protection beyond the Wbb

### Interpretation
- **Haviltex standard** (HR 13 maart 1981, NJ 1981/635) - Contracts interpreted not only by literal text but by the meaning parties could reasonably attribute to the provisions and what they could reasonably expect from each other
- **CAO norm** - For standard-form NDAs used widely: more objective/linguistic interpretation may apply

Consult `references/key-case-law.md` for landmark court decisions relevant to this domain.

### When a penalty clause (boetebeding) is present, calculate exposure scenarios:
- **Single breach scenario**: [penalty amount] x 1 breach = EUR [amount]
- **Ongoing breach (30 days)**: [penalty amount] + ([daily rate] x 30) = EUR [amount]
- **Maximum theoretical exposure (1 year)**: EUR [amount]
- **Judicial moderation risk**: Note Art. 6:94 BW - courts can moderate if penalty is disproportionate (Intrahof/Bart Smit: only if maintenance is "obviously unacceptable")

### Always distinguish between:
1. **Trade secrets (bedrijfsgeheimen)** - protected under Wbb (Wet bescherming bedrijfsgeheimen) regardless of NDA
2. **Confidential information** - broader category, protected only by contract (NDA) and potentially Art. 6:162 BW (tort)
3. **Publicly available information** - not protectable by NDA

Note: The Wbb provides statutory remedies (injunction, damages, destruction) for trade secrets that meet the 3-part definition (secret, commercial value, reasonable steps). An NDA provides ADDITIONAL contractual protection for information that may not qualify as a trade secret.

## Ethical Guardrails
- **Mandatory disclaimer**: Always append disclaimer; this is a pre-screening, not a legal opinion
- **No drafting**: Do not draft replacement NDA clauses; only identify issues and suggest directions for negotiation
- **Proportionality**: Assess penalty clauses relative to the context and potential harm, not in isolation
- **Both sides**: If reviewing a mutual NDA, note that obligations apply equally
- **Sector awareness**: Some sectors (pharma, defense, government) have legitimate reasons for stricter NDAs
- **Trade secrets vs. confidential information**: Distinguish between information qualifying as trade secrets under the Wbb and ordinary confidential information
- **No blanket advice**: Do not advise to "just sign" or "always reject"; each NDA depends on context
- **Confidentiality of the NDA itself**: Remind users that the NDA review process itself should be handled carefully

## Escalation Triggers
- NDA is part of a complex transaction (M&A, joint venture, licensing deal)
- Penalty clauses exceed EUR 100,000 per breach
- NDA contains non-compete or exclusivity provisions
- NDA involves government contracts or classified information
- NDA has cross-border elements with conflicting jurisdiction clauses
- NDA involves intellectual property assignment or licensing beyond confidentiality
- User indicates they have already breached or received a breach claim
- NDA contains unusual provisions (e.g., audit rights, polygraph clauses, liquidated damages)
