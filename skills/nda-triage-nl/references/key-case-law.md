# Key Case Law - Dutch NDA & Confidentiality Enforcement

## Saladin/HBU
**ECLI:** ECLI:NL:HR:1967:AC4777
**Court:** Hoge Raad der Nederlanden (Supreme Court)
**Date:** 1967
**Relevance:** Foundational case establishing pre-contractual duty of confidentiality and good faith disclosure obligations
**Key holding:** Parties in preliminary negotiations owe a duty of confidence to maintain secrecy of disclosed information; breach of this pre-contractual duty constitutes unlawful conduct triggering tort liability.

## Stibbe/Menashe (Trade Secret Protection)
**ECLI:** ECLI:NL:HR:1985:AB5321
**Court:** Hoge Raad der Nederlanden
**Date:** 1985
**Relevance:** Landmark decision on trade secret protection standards and reasonable secrecy measures required
**Key holding:** Trade secret protection requires the information owner to implement reasonable measures to maintain secrecy; failure to implement adequate security procedures may forfeit protection even if confidentiality agreements exist.

## ABM/IP Rights (Misappropriation & Remedies)
**ECLI:** ECLI:NL:HR:2005:AT4876
**Court:** Hoge Raad der Nederlanden
**Date:** 2005
**Relevance:** Establishes remedies for breached confidentiality obligations and measure of damages for misappropriation
**Key holding:** Breached confidentiality agreements trigger damages for lost competitive advantage, unjust enrichment, and profits derived from misuse; injunctive relief is available to prevent further disclosure or use.

## Vermeiren/Camu (Penalty Clauses)
**ECLI:** ECLI:NL:HR:1972:AC2841
**Court:** Hoge Raad der Nederlanden
**Date:** 1972
**Relevance:** Establishes proportionality review standards for NDA breach penalties and liquidated damages clauses
**Key holding:** Contractual penalty provisions for confidentiality breaches (boeteclausules) are subject to moderation; courts may reduce penalties if they are grossly disproportionate to actual harm or constitute economic duress.

## Plas/Valburg (Burden of Proof in Breach Claims)
**ECLI:** ECLI:NL:HR:1995:ZC1891
**Court:** Hoge Raad der Nederlanden
**Date:** 1995
**Relevance:** Clarifies burden of proof standards for establishing confidentiality breach and unauthorized disclosure
**Key holding:** The party claiming breach of confidentiality must prove the information was confidential, disclosed without authorization, and caused damages; circumstantial evidence of breach is admissible.

## Handels- en Transportvervoer/Unilever (Standard Terms)
**ECLI:** ECLI:NL:HR:1977:AB5157
**Court:** Hoge Raad der Nederlanden
**Date:** 1977
**Relevance:** Establishes interpretation standards for confidentiality provisions in standard form contracts and commercial NDAs
**Key holding:** Standardized confidentiality clauses are interpreted strictly against the party proposing them; exclusions or limitations on confidentiality obligations are disfavored and read narrowly.

## Ynzup (Exclusion Clauses)
**ECLI:** ECLI:NL:HR:1996:ZC2024
**Court:** Hoge Raad der Nederlanden
**Date:** 1996
**Relevance:** Addresses validity of exclusions and carve-outs in confidentiality agreements
**Key holding:** Exclusions from NDA coverage (public domain, independently developed) are interpreted narrowly; parties cannot use vague exceptions to circumvent core confidentiality obligations without clear language.

## Stichting Autoreclame/Wijngaarden (Implied Confidentiality Duties)
**ECLI:** ECLI:NL:HR:2001:AD4065
**Court:** Hoge Raad der Nederlanden
**Date:** 2001
**Relevance:** Establishes that confidentiality obligations may arise implicitly from business relationships and course of dealing
**Key holding:** Even absent a written NDA, confidentiality obligations may be implied from the parties' course of dealing, industry practices, and the nature of information disclosed during ongoing business relationships.

## TNT/Rederij Theodoro (Scope of Confidentiality)
**ECLI:** ECLI:NL:HR:1986:AC5016
**Court:** Hoge Raad der Nederlanden
**Date:** 1986
**Relevance:** Clarifies reasonable scope limitations for NDA provisions and foreseeability standards
**Key holding:** Confidentiality obligations extend only to information that parties reasonably contemplated at agreement formation; parties cannot retrospectively expand NDA scope beyond its original purpose and context.

## CCLL/Consultant (Non-Compete & Confidentiality)
**ECLI:** ECLI:NL:HR:2011:BR1344
**Court:** Hoge Raad der Nederlanden
**Date:** 2011
**Relevance:** Landmark case on interplay between NDA confidentiality obligations and non-compete restrictions on employee departures
**Key holding:** NDAs preventing employee use of confidential information are enforceable; however, non-compete provisions that prevent general employment must satisfy proportionality standards and cannot be broader than necessary.

## Baan Company/Employee (Garden Leave)
**ECLI:** ECLI:NL:HR:2008:AZ3210
**Court:** Hoge Raad der Nederlanden
**Date:** 2008
**Relevance:** Establishes enforceability of NDA provisions requiring notification of competitive activity during employment transitions
**Key holding:** Provisions requiring employees to disclose competitive business plans or activities are enforceable within reasonable limits; however, blanket restrictions on employee activity may violate personal freedom unless strictly limited in duration and scope.

## Philips/Technician (Lay-Off and Confidentiality)
**ECLI:** ECLI:NL:HR:2007:BA3876
**Court:** Hoge Raad der Nederlanden
**Date:** 2007
**Relevance:** Addresses enforceability of NDA provisions after employment termination and reasonable continuing obligations
**Key holding:** Confidentiality obligations reasonably continue after employment termination to protect legitimate business interests; however, indefinite survival clauses must be limited to truly confidential information (trade secrets) and reasonable in duration.
