# Dutch General Terms and Conditions Framework (Algemene Voorwaarden)

## Legal Basis: Afdeling 6.5.3 BW (Articles 6:231-6:247)

---

## 1. Definition (Art. 6:231 BW)

**Algemene voorwaarden** are one or more written clauses that are drafted to be included in a number of contracts, with the exception of clauses that reflect the core performance obligations (kernbedingen) of the contract.

Key definitions:
- **Gebruiker**: The party that uses the general terms in a contract
- **Wederpartij**: The counterparty that accepts the general terms
- **Kernbeding**: Core clause defining the primary obligations - these are excluded from the unfair terms regime

### What are NOT general terms:
- Individually negotiated clauses (uitonderhandelde bedingen)
- Core performance obligations (kernbedingen) - e.g., price, subject matter of the contract
- Clauses imposed by law or treaty

---

## 2. Applicability (Toepasselijkheid)

### How general terms become part of a contract:
1. **Offer and acceptance**: The user must refer to the general terms before or at the time of contracting (Art. 6:217 BW)
2. **Battle of forms** (Art. 6:225 lid 3 BW): When both parties refer to their own general terms, the first set referenced prevails UNLESS the second party explicitly rejects the first set
3. **Mere reference suffices**: No need for the counterparty to have read or understood the terms - but the reasonable opportunity requirement (see below) must be met

---

## 3. Reasonable Opportunity to Take Note (Redelijke mogelijkheid tot kennisneming - Art. 6:233 sub b jo. 6:234 BW)

The user MUST provide the counterparty a reasonable opportunity to take note of the general terms. Methods:

### Physical contracts:
- Hand over the terms before or at signing
- If not reasonably possible: inform where the terms can be inspected (e.g., KvK depot, court registry)

### Electronic contracts (Art. 6:234 lid 2 BW):
- Make terms available electronically such that they can be stored and are accessible for later review
- Provide terms via email or download link
- Reference to a website is acceptable if the terms can be saved

### KvK/Court Deposit:
- Terms can be deposited at the Chamber of Commerce (Kamer van Koophandel) or court registry
- This serves as a fallback if physical provision is not reasonably possible

### Consequence of failure:
- If the reasonable opportunity is not provided, the counterparty can invoke **vernietiging** (voidability) of specific clauses under Art. 6:233 sub b BW

---

## 4. Unfair Terms - Content Control (Onredelijk bezwarend - Art. 6:233 sub a BW)

A clause in general terms is voidable if it is **unreasonably onerous** (onredelijk bezwarend) for the counterparty, considering:
- The nature and content of the contract
- The manner in which the terms were formed
- The mutually known interests of the parties
- Other circumstances of the case

### Black List (Zwarte lijst - Art. 6:236 BW) - B2C only
Clauses that are **always** considered unreasonably onerous in consumer contracts:

| Letter | Prohibited Clause |
|--------|------------------|
| a | Depriving the consumer of the right to demand performance |
| b | Limiting or excluding the user's obligation to perform |
| c | Giving the user the right to terminate without same right for consumer |
| d | Binding consumer without reasonable termination right |
| e | Unreasonably long notice period for consumer |
| f | Binding consumer's heirs/successors to the contract |
| g | Limiting consumer's right to seek court resolution |
| h | Limiting consumer's right to evidence |
| i | Granting user irrevocable power of attorney over consumer's assets |
| j | Allowing transfer of obligations without consumer's termination right |
| k | Depriving consumer of performance for more than 3 months without termination right |
| l | Giving user right to increase price within 3 months without consumer's termination right |
| m | Giving user right to deliver something substantially different |
| n | Deemed declarations of receipt or approval |

### Grey List (Grijze lijst - Art. 6:237 BW) - B2C only
Clauses that are **presumed** unreasonably onerous (rebuttable presumption) in consumer contracts:

| Letter | Suspect Clause |
|--------|---------------|
| a | Essentially longer performance period than necessary |
| b | Limiting user's obligation to compensate |
| c | Allowing user to perform substantially differently than agreed |
| d | Allowing user to increase price without consumer termination right |
| e | Limiting consumer's right to dissolve (ontbinden) the contract |
| f | Requiring consumer to contract with user for unreasonably long period |
| g | Requiring unreasonably long renewal period for fixed-term contracts |
| h | Limiting consumer's right to suspend performance |
| i | Limiting consumer's right to set off (verrekening) |
| j | Requiring specific form for declarations or requiring receipt of declarations |
| k | Penalty clause without possibility of demanding performance or damages instead |
| l | Requiring unreasonably high compensation for termination |
| m | Obligating consumer to enter into supplementary agreements |
| n | Requiring unreasonably long notice period for fixed-term contracts |

---

## 5. Large Enterprise Exception (Art. 6:235 BW)

The following parties CANNOT invoke the content control (Art. 6:233) or reasonable opportunity (Art. 6:234) protections:
- **Legal persons** that have published annual accounts at the time of contracting, OR
- **Entities** with 50 or more employees at the time of contracting, OR
- **Public bodies** (publiekrechtelijke rechtspersonen)

**Important**: These parties CAN still invoke Art. 6:248 lid 2 BW (derogating effect of reasonableness and fairness) as a general fallback.

---

## 6. Reflexwerking (Indirect Effect)

Small businesses and professionals that do not meet the Art. 6:235 thresholds can:
- Invoke Art. 6:233 BW directly
- Courts may apply the Black and Grey lists by analogy (**reflexwerking**) to small B2B contracts, though this is not automatic

---

## 7. Sector-Specific General Terms

Many Dutch industries have standardized general terms:
- **ICT**: Nederland ICT Voorwaarden (now NLdigital Voorwaarden)
- **Construction**: UAV 2012, UAV-GC 2005, DNR 2011
- **Transport**: AVC (Algemene Vervoercondities), CMR
- **Advertising**: ROTA Voorwaarden
- **Legal services**: Uniforme Voorwaarden advocatuur
- **Wholesale/Retail**: Various sector-specific sets registered at KvK

---

## 8. International Aspects

- For B2B international contracts: Rome I Regulation determines applicable law
- Even if Dutch general terms are used, the law of the counterparty's habitual residence may provide mandatory consumer protections (Art. 6 Rome I)
- Vienna Sales Convention (CISG) applies by default to international sales unless excluded - many Dutch general terms explicitly exclude CISG
- EU Unfair Contract Terms Directive (93/13/EEC) sets minimum B2C protection standards

---

## 9. Practical Checklist for Review

When reviewing general terms (algemene voorwaarden), check:

- [ ] Were the terms properly provided/made available? (Art. 6:234)
- [ ] Is this B2C or B2B? (determines Black/Grey list applicability)
- [ ] If B2B: does the counterparty qualify as a "large enterprise" under Art. 6:235?
- [ ] Are the core obligations (kernbedingen) clearly identifiable and excluded from GT?
- [ ] Are there clauses that appear on the Black list (Art. 6:236)?
- [ ] Are there clauses that appear on the Grey list (Art. 6:237)?
- [ ] Is the liability limitation proportionate?
- [ ] Is there a proper dispute resolution clause?
- [ ] Is CISG excluded (for international sales)?
- [ ] Is the language accessible to the target audience?
