# Key Articles from BW Book 6 (Obligations) and Book 7 (Specific Contracts)

## BW Boek 6 - Verbintenissenrecht (Law of Obligations)

### Title 1: Obligations in General

#### Section 1.1 - Performance (Nakoming)
- **Art. 6:1** - An obligation is a legal relationship whereby one party (the debtor) is obligated to perform towards another party (the creditor)
- **Art. 6:2** - Creditor and debtor must behave in accordance with the requirements of reasonableness and fairness (redelijkheid en billijkheid)
- **Art. 6:6-6:7** - Joint and several liability (hoofdelijke verbondenheid): each debtor can be held liable for the entire debt

#### Section 1.6 - Set-Off (Verrekening)
- **Art. 6:127** - A debtor who has a counter-claim against the creditor may set off against the creditor's claim, provided both claims concern money or things of the same kind and quality
- **Art. 6:130** - Set-off can be invoked even if the counter-claim is contested, provided the court finds it well-founded

#### Section 1.8 - Right of Suspension (Opschortingsrechten)
- **Art. 6:52** - Exceptio non adimpleti contractus: a party may suspend performance if the other party fails to perform a related obligation
- **Art. 6:54** - Suspension right is excluded in certain cases (e.g., when suspension would be contrary to good faith)

#### Section 1.9 - Non-Performance (Niet-nakoming)
- **Art. 6:74** - Every attributable failure (toerekenbare tekortkoming) in performance obliges the debtor to compensate the resulting damages
- **Art. 6:75** - Overmacht (Force majeure): failure cannot be attributed if not due to debtor's fault nor by law, legal act, or generally accepted views (verkeersopvattingen) for the debtor's account
- **Art. 6:76** - Liability for auxiliaries (hulppersonen): debtor is liable for failures of persons engaged to perform the obligation
- **Art. 6:77** - Liability for defective things used in performance
- **Art. 6:80** - Anticipatory breach: creditor may take measures if clear the debtor will not perform
- **Art. 6:81-6:83** - Verzuim (default): debtor is in default after a written notice of default (ingebrekestelling) with a reasonable time for performance, except when:
  - Performance is permanently impossible (Art. 6:81)
  - A deadline has been set and passed (Art. 6:83 sub a - fatale termijn)
  - Debtor communicates they will not perform (Art. 6:83 sub c)
- **Art. 6:87** - Omzetting in schadevergoeding: creditor may convert the obligation into damages after default
- **Art. 6:89** - Klachtplicht (duty to complain): creditor loses rights if they do not complain within a reasonable time after discovering a defect

#### Section 1.10 - Damages (Schadevergoeding)
- **Art. 6:95** - Damages consist of patrimonial loss (vermogensschade) and other disadvantages (ander nadeel/immaterieel)
- **Art. 6:96** - Vermogensschade includes: suffered loss (geleden verlies), lost profits (gederfde winst), and reasonable costs
- **Art. 6:98** - Causation and attribution (toerekening naar redelijkheid): only damages that can be attributed to the event as a reasonable consequence are compensable
- **Art. 6:100** - Voordeelstoerekening (set-off of benefits): advantages accruing from the same event may be deducted
- **Art. 6:101** - Eigen schuld (contributory negligence): damages may be reduced in proportion to the creditor's own fault
- **Art. 6:104** - Court may estimate damages if exact amount cannot be determined
- **Art. 6:109** - Rechterlijke matiging: court may moderate damages if full compensation would lead to manifestly unacceptable results

### Title 3 - Tort (Onrechtmatige daad)
- **Art. 6:162** - Onrechtmatige daad: unlawful act liability requires (1) unlawful act, (2) attributability, (3) damage, (4) causation, (5) relativity (Schutznorm)
- **Art. 6:163** - Relativiteitsvereiste: no obligation to compensate if the violated norm does not serve to protect against the damage suffered
- **Art. 6:170** - Employer liability for employees (werkgeversaansprakelijkheid)
- **Art. 6:171** - Liability for non-subordinate contractors (niet-ondergeschikten)

### Title 5 - Contracts (Overeenkomsten)

#### Section 5.2 - Formation (Totstandkoming)
- **Art. 6:217** - Contract is formed by offer (aanbod) and acceptance (aanvaarding)
- **Art. 6:219** - Offer is irrevocable if it contains a term for acceptance or irrevocability follows from the offer
- **Art. 6:225** - Acceptance that deviates from the offer constitutes a counter-offer, UNLESS the deviations are immaterial; Battle of forms rule in lid 3
- **Art. 6:226-227** - Formal requirements and evidence

#### Section 5.2A - Defects of Will (Wilsgebreken)
- **Art. 6:228** - Dwaling (mistake): contract voidable if entered into under influence of a wrong assumption, unless:
  - The mistake concerns future circumstances
  - The mistake should have been recognized by the party invoking it
  - The other party could not have known about the mistake
- **Art. 6:229** - Imprévision (unforeseen circumstances, maar NB: dit is Art. 6:258)
- **Art. 6:228 lid 2** - Mistake cannot concern exclusively future circumstances

#### Section 5.3 - General Terms and Conditions (Algemene voorwaarden)
- **Art. 6:231** - Definitions (see algemene-voorwaarden.md)
- **Art. 6:232** - Binding effect even if counterparty has not read the terms
- **Art. 6:233** - Grounds for voidability: (a) unreasonably onerous, (b) reasonable opportunity not provided
- **Art. 6:234** - Methods of providing reasonable opportunity
- **Art. 6:235** - Large enterprise exception
- **Art. 6:236** - Black list (zwarte lijst) - always unfair in B2C
- **Art. 6:237** - Grey list (grijze lijst) - presumed unfair in B2C

#### Section 5.4 - Legal Effects (Rechtsgevolgen)
- **Art. 6:248** - Contract effects:
  - Lid 1: Contract has effects agreed between parties and those arising from law, custom, or reasonableness and fairness (aanvullende werking)
  - Lid 2: A provision is not applicable insofar as this would be unacceptable according to standards of reasonableness and fairness (beperkende werking / derogating effect)
- **Art. 6:258** - Imprévision (onvoorziene omstandigheden): court may modify or dissolve a contract on the ground of unforeseen circumstances
- **Art. 6:259** - Voortdurende overeenkomsten (continuing performance contracts): may be dissolved by court

#### Section 5.5 - Dissolution (Ontbinding)
- **Art. 6:265** - Right to dissolve upon attributable failure (tekortkoming), unless the failure does not justify dissolution given its special nature or minor importance (tenzij-clausule)
- **Art. 6:267** - Dissolution by written notice or court order
- **Art. 6:269-6:272** - Effects of dissolution: obligation to restore (ongedaanmakingsverbintenissen)

### Title 4 - Penalty Clauses (Boetebeding)
- **Art. 6:91** - Boetebeding: clause providing that debtor must pay a sum or perform differently upon failure
- **Art. 6:92** - Penalty replaces damages unless otherwise agreed
- **Art. 6:94** - Rechterlijke matiging: court may moderate the penalty if fairness clearly requires it

### Interest (Rente)
- **Art. 6:119** - Wettelijke rente (statutory interest): applicable on monetary obligations in default
- **Art. 6:119a** - Wettelijke handelsrente (statutory commercial interest): higher rate applicable to B2B commercial transactions

## BW Boek 7 - Bijzondere Overeenkomsten (Specific Contracts)

### Title 1: Sale (Koop) - Art. 7:1-7:50a

- **Art. 7:1** - Definition: agreement where seller delivers a thing and buyer pays the price
- **Art. 7:6** - Consumer sale provisions are mandatory (dwingend recht)
- **Art. 7:17** - Conformiteit (conformity): delivered thing must conform to the agreement; factors include nature, statements by seller, price
- **Art. 7:18** - Presumption of non-conformity within 6 months of delivery (B2C) - now 1 year after implementation of Sale of Goods Directive
- **Art. 7:21-7:22** - Remedies for buyer: repair, replacement, price reduction, dissolution
- **Art. 7:23** - Klachtplicht koop: buyer must complain within a reasonable time after discovery of non-conformity (bekwame tijd - generally 2 months for consumers)
- **Art. 7:24** - Producer's liability for consumer products
- **Art. 7:46a-7:46j** - Distance selling (koop op afstand) - consumer right of withdrawal (herroepingsrecht, 14 days)

### Title 7: Services Agreement (Opdracht) - Art. 7:400-7:413

- **Art. 7:400** - Definition: agreement where one party (opdrachtnemer) undertakes to perform work other than under an employment contract or contract for work
- **Art. 7:401** - Duty of care: opdrachtnemer must exercise the care of a good contractor (goed opdrachtnemer - zorgplicht)
- **Art. 7:402** - Must follow client's instructions within reason
- **Art. 7:403** - Duty to inform and account (informatieplicht en rekenschap)
- **Art. 7:404** - Personal performance: if the agreement was entered into with a view to a specific person
- **Art. 7:405** - Remuneration: if no price agreed, a reasonable price is owed (Art. 7:405 lid 2)
- **Art. 7:408** - Client may always terminate the agreement (opzeggen)
- **Art. 7:409** - Death of the opdrachtnemer does not automatically end the agreement
- **Art. 7:411** - Remuneration upon premature termination
- **Art. 7:412** - Provisions of titel 7.7 do not apply to employment contracts (arbeidsovereenkomsten)

### Title 10: Employment Contract (Arbeidsovereenkomst) - Art. 7:610-7:691
(See dutch-employment-law skill for details)

- **Art. 7:610** - Definition of employment contract: work, wage, authority (gezag)
- **Art. 7:611** - Goed werkgeverschap / goed werknemerschap (duty of good employer/employee conduct)

### Title 12: Contract for Work (Aanneming van werk) - Art. 7:750-7:769

- **Art. 7:750** - Definition: agreement to construct a physical work (stoffelijk werk) for a price
- **Art. 7:751** - Contractor may engage subcontractors unless personal performance agreed
- **Art. 7:752** - If no price agreed, a reasonable price is owed
- **Art. 7:754** - Contractor's warning obligation (waarschuwingsplicht) regarding errors in plans, specifications, or materials provided by the client
- **Art. 7:756** - Price increases (meerwerk): only if client has been informed in advance of the price consequence, unless client should have understood the implication
- **Art. 7:758** - Oplevering (delivery/completion): work is deemed delivered if client approves it or should reasonably have approved it. After delivery, contractor is discharged from liability for known defects unless these were concealed
- **Art. 7:759** - Hidden defects (verborgen gebreken) after delivery: contractor must be given opportunity to repair
- **Art. 7:764** - Client may terminate at any time (opzeggen) but must pay full price minus savings
