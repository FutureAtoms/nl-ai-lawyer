# Key Case Law - Dutch Contract Law

> This file contains landmark Dutch court decisions relevant to contract review,
> interpretation, and enforcement under the Burgerlijk Wetboek (BW).
> All ECLI numbers should be verified against [Rechtspraak.nl](https://uitspraken.rechtspraak.nl).

---

## 1. Contract Interpretation

### HR 13 March 1981, ECLI:NL:HR:1981:AG4158 (Haviltex)

- **Court:** Hoge Raad der Nederlanden
- **Date:** 13 March 1981
- **Parties:** Ermes c.s. v. Haviltex
- **Key Legal Principle:** The meaning of a contractual clause is not determined solely
  by its literal text, but by the meaning the parties could reasonably attribute to it
  in the given circumstances, and what they could reasonably expect from each other.
  This is known as the *Haviltex-norm* or *Haviltex-maatstaf*.
- **Relevance:** This is the foundational standard for interpretation of contracts
  between private parties in Dutch law. It applies to virtually every contract
  interpretation dispute and must be considered in every contract review.

### HR 20 February 2004, ECLI:NL:HR:2004:AO1427 (DSM/Fox)

- **Court:** Hoge Raad der Nederlanden
- **Date:** 20 February 2004
- **Parties:** DSM v. Fox
- **Key Legal Principle:** For provisions that apply to a broader group of persons who
  were not involved in the drafting (such as CAO/collective labor agreements, statutes,
  and general terms), an *objective interpretation method* applies. The text and its
  usual linguistic meaning are decisive, read in light of the entire document.
- **Relevance:** Distinguishes the Haviltex subjective standard from the CAO/objective
  standard. Important when reviewing standard-form contracts, CAOs, or any provisions
  that bind third parties.

### HR 5 April 2013, ECLI:NL:HR:2013:BY8101 (Lundiform/Mexx)

- **Court:** Hoge Raad der Nederlanden
- **Date:** 5 April 2013
- **Parties:** Lundiform B.V. v. Mexx Europe B.V.
- **Key Legal Principle:** Even in commercial contracts between professional parties
  with legal counsel, the Haviltex standard applies and not a purely linguistic
  interpretation. The sophistication of the parties is a relevant circumstance but
  does not mandate a textual-only approach.
- **Relevance:** Critical for reviewing B2B commercial agreements. Confirms that
  entire agreement clauses and professional drafting do not override the Haviltex norm.

## 2. Force Majeure (Overmacht)

### HR 19 November 1993, ECLI:NL:HR:1993:ZC1153 (ABP/FGH)

- **Court:** Hoge Raad der Nederlanden
- **Date:** 19 November 1993
- **Parties:** Stichting Pensioenfonds ABP v. FGH Bank N.V.
- **Key Legal Principle:** Force majeure under Art. 6:75 BW requires that the failure
  to perform cannot be attributed to the debtor based on fault, law, legal act, or
  prevailing views in commerce (*verkeersopvattingen*). The burden of proof lies
  with the party invoking force majeure.
- **Relevance:** Foundational for assessing force majeure clauses in contracts
  and determining whether contractual force majeure definitions are broader or
  narrower than the statutory default.

## 3. Penalty Clauses (Boetebeding)

### HR 14 June 2002, ECLI:NL:HR:2002:AE0657

- **Court:** Hoge Raad der Nederlanden
- **Date:** 14 June 2002
- **Parties:** Not commonly referred to by party name
- **Key Legal Principle:** Under Art. 6:94 BW, a court may moderate a contractual
  penalty if justice manifestly requires it (*indien de billijkheid dit klaarblijkelijk
  eist*). The court must exercise restraint; the mere disproportion between the
  penalty and actual damages is insufficient for moderation.
- **Relevance:** When reviewing penalty clauses, consider that Dutch courts apply
  a high threshold for moderation. Excessively high penalties may still be enforceable
  unless the result is manifestly unjust.

## 4. Breach and Dissolution (Ontbinding)

### HR 27 November 2009, ECLI:NL:HR:2009:BH2162 (VGC/VGC)

- **Court:** Hoge Raad der Nederlanden
- **Date:** 27 November 2009
- **Parties:** VGC v. VGC (intercompany dispute)
- **Key Legal Principle:** Dissolution under Art. 6:265 BW requires a breach that
  is sufficiently serious to justify dissolution. The debtor bears the burden of
  proving that the breach, given its special nature or minor significance, does not
  justify dissolution (*tenzij-formule*). Every breach in principle justifies
  dissolution unless the exception applies.
- **Relevance:** Essential for reviewing termination clauses. Parties may contractually
  expand or restrict the right to dissolve, but the statutory default provides a
  low threshold for dissolution upon breach.

## 5. General Terms and Conditions (Algemene Voorwaarden)

### HR 29 January 2021, ECLI:NL:HR:2021:153

- **Court:** Hoge Raad der Nederlanden
- **Date:** 29 January 2021
- **Parties:** Not commonly cited by party name
- **Key Legal Principle:** Clarified the interaction between the Dutch rules on
  algemene voorwaarden (Art. 6:231-247 BW) and the EU Unfair Contract Terms
  Directive (93/13/EEC). A term in general conditions can be annulled under
  Art. 6:233 sub a BW if it is unreasonably onerous (*onredelijk bezwarend*),
  and the court must apply the EU Directive standard of unfairness *ex officio*
  in consumer contracts.
- **Relevance:** When reviewing B2C contracts, general terms must be assessed
  against both Dutch and EU unfairness standards. Courts may strike down clauses
  on their own initiative.

## 6. Classification of Key BW Provisions: Dwingend Recht vs. Regelend Recht

Understanding whether a provision is mandatory (*dwingend recht*) or default/supplementary
(*regelend recht*) is critical for contract drafting and review. Mandatory provisions
cannot be deviated from by contract; default provisions apply only in the absence of
a contrary agreement.

### Art. 6:74 BW - Tekortkoming in de nakoming (Default)
- **Classification:** Regelend recht
- **Note:** Parties may contractually define what constitutes a breach, allocate risk,
  and limit or exclude liability for non-performance (subject to Art. 6:248 BW limits).

### Art. 6:89 BW - Klachtplicht (Mandatory)
- **Classification:** Dwingend recht
- **Note:** The obligation to protest a defective performance within a reasonable time
  cannot be contracted away. Failure to complain timely extinguishes all claims.
  See HR 8 February 2013, ECLI:NL:HR:2013:BY4600 for application.

### Art. 6:233-236 BW - Algemene Voorwaarden (Mandatory for consumers)
- **Classification:** Dwingend recht in B2C; more flexible in B2B
- **Note:** The black list (Art. 6:236) and grey list (Art. 6:237) apply only to
  consumer contracts. Large companies (Art. 6:235 BW) cannot invoke these protections.
  The regime cannot be circumvented by choice-of-law clauses in EU consumer contracts.

### Art. 6:248 lid 2 BW - Redelijkheid en billijkheid (Mandatory)
- **Classification:** Dwingend recht
- **Note:** A contractual clause does not apply to the extent that, in the given
  circumstances, this would be unacceptable according to standards of reasonableness
  and fairness. This is a *beperkende werking* and overrides any contractual provision.
  It cannot be excluded by contract.

### Art. 7:5 BW - Consumentenkoop (Semi-mandatory)
- **Classification:** Semi-dwingend recht
- **Note:** In consumer sales, the provisions of Title 7.1 BW cannot be deviated from
  to the detriment of the consumer (Art. 7:6 BW). Deviations in favor of the consumer
  are permitted. In B2B, these provisions are default.

### Art. 7:400-413 BW - Overeenkomst van opdracht (Mostly default)
- **Classification:** Mostly regelend recht
- **Note:** The duty of care (Art. 7:401 BW) is considered mandatory in its core;
  exclusion of all liability for professional service providers is generally not
  permitted under Art. 6:248 BW.

### Art. 6:119a BW - Handelsrente (Semi-mandatory)
- **Classification:** Semi-dwingend recht
- **Note:** The statutory commercial interest rate for late payment in B2B transactions
  implements the EU Late Payment Directive. Deviations that are grossly unfair to
  the creditor may be annulled (Art. 6:119a lid 6 BW).

### Art. 3:40 BW - Nietigheid (Mandatory)
- **Classification:** Dwingend recht
- **Note:** A juridical act that violates mandatory law is null (lid 2) or voidable
  (lid 3). This cannot be contracted around.

## Notes for AI System

- Always check whether a contractual provision deviates from mandatory law.
- When a clause appears to override a *dwingend recht* provision, flag it as potentially
  void or voidable.
- The Haviltex standard applies to interpretation of individually negotiated terms;
  the DSM/Fox objective standard applies to standardized terms binding third parties.
- Penalty clause moderation is possible but courts apply a high threshold.
- In B2C contracts, apply both Dutch BW and EU Directive standards for unfairness.
- Force majeure clauses should be compared against the Art. 6:75 BW default.
