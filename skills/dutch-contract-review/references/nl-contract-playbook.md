# Dutch Contract Playbook - Standard Positions on Common Clauses

## Purpose
This playbook defines standard, market-accepted positions for common contract clauses under Dutch law. Use these as benchmarks when reviewing contracts.

---

## 1. Liability Cap (Beperking van aansprakelijkheid)

### Standard Position
- **Direct damages only** (directe schade): Typically capped at the contract value or the amount paid in the preceding 12 months
- **Exclusion of indirect/consequential damages** (indirecte/gevolgschade): Standard in B2B; includes lost profits (gederfde winst), lost savings (gemiste besparingen), reputational damage
- **Carve-outs from cap**: Willful misconduct (opzet) and gross negligence (grove schuld/bewuste roekeloosheid) by senior management cannot be excluded per Art. 6:248 lid 2 BW

### GREEN Flags
- Liability limited to direct damages, capped at reasonable amount (e.g., 1x annual contract value)
- Mutual limitation (both parties subject to same cap)
- Clear carve-outs for intent/gross negligence

### YELLOW Flags
- Asymmetric liability caps (one party bears more risk)
- Cap set at less than 50% of contract value
- Vague definition of "direct damages"

### RED Flags
- Attempt to exclude liability for opzet/grove schuld (unenforceable under Art. 6:248 lid 2 BW)
- Unlimited liability for one party only
- No liability cap at all in a high-value contract
- Exclusion of liability for personal injury (letselschade) - unenforceable

## 2. Indemnification (Vrijwaring)

### Standard Position
- Indemnities should be specific (e.g., IP infringement, breach of confidentiality, third-party claims)
- Must include duty to mitigate (schadebeperkingsplicht, Art. 6:101 BW)
- Subject to the same overall liability cap

### GREEN Flags
- Reciprocal indemnities for each party's specific risk areas
- Subject to liability cap
- Clear trigger conditions and claims procedure

### YELLOW Flags
- Broad indemnity ("any and all claims arising from...")
- Indemnity sits outside the liability cap
- No procedural requirements for claiming

### RED Flags
- Blanket indemnity with no limitation
- Indemnity for the other party's own negligence
- Penalty clause (boetebeding) without rechterlijke matiging possibility (but note: Art. 6:94 BW allows courts to moderate penalties)

---

## 3. Force Majeure (Overmacht)

### Standard Position
- Art. 6:75 BW: A failure in performance cannot be attributed to the debtor if not due to their fault nor for their account by law, juridical act, or generally accepted principles (verkeersopvattingen)
- Standard force majeure clauses expand on Art. 6:75 with specific enumerated events
- Typical duration trigger: 30-90 days of force majeure before termination right arises

### GREEN Flags
- Specific enumerated events (natural disaster, pandemic, government action, war, sanctions)
- Notice obligation with reasonable timeframe
- Duty to mitigate and use reasonable efforts to overcome
- Termination right after defined period (60-90 days)

### YELLOW Flags
- Very broad catch-all language without enumeration
- No duty to mitigate or resume performance
- No termination right or excessively long waiting period (>6 months)

### RED Flags
- Includes events within the party's control (e.g., "inability to secure financing")
- Force majeure as excuse for payment obligations (generally not accepted under Dutch law)
- Supplier's delivery failure included as force majeure for a reseller (generally allocated to the reseller per verkeersopvattingen)

## 4. Governing Law and Dispute Resolution (Toepasselijk recht en geschillenbeslechting)

### Standard Position
- Dutch law choice is straightforward for NL-based parties
- District court (Rechtbank) as default forum; Amsterdam, Rotterdam, The Hague, and Utrecht are common choices
- Arbitration common in international/complex commercial disputes (NAI, ICC, or UNUM)

### GREEN Flags
- Dutch law with competent Dutch court
- Clear specification of which Rechtbank
- Arbitration clause with recognized institution (NAI) for complex B2B

### YELLOW Flags
- Foreign governing law for a primarily Dutch transaction
- Multi-tier dispute resolution (negotiation > mediation > arbitration) without time limits
- Silent on language of proceedings

### RED Flags
- Governing law of a jurisdiction with no connection to either party or the transaction
- Exclusive jurisdiction in a remote or inconvenient forum
- Waiver of appeal rights without clear understanding
- Absence of governing law clause entirely (leads to uncertainty under Rome I Regulation)

## 5. Intellectual Property Assignment and Licensing (Intellectueel eigendom)

### Standard Position
- Under Dutch law, IP created by employees belongs to employer (Art. 7 Auteurswet for copyright; Art. 12 ROW for patents)
- IP created by contractors does NOT automatically transfer to the client - requires explicit written assignment (akte requirement for copyright per Art. 2 Auteurswet)
- License grants should specify: scope, territory, exclusivity, sublicensing rights, duration

### GREEN Flags
- Explicit assignment of all foreground IP with written deed (akte)
- Clear delineation of background IP with appropriate license-back
- Moral rights waiver where permissible (Art. 25 Auteurswet - limited waivability)
- Pre-existing IP and third-party IP properly carved out

### YELLOW Flags
- Ambiguous ownership language ("all work product belongs to Client")
- No distinction between background and foreground IP
- Perpetual, irrevocable license without clear scope boundaries

### RED Flags
- No IP clause in a development/creative services agreement
- Attempt to assign IP without written deed (akte) - may be invalid
- Assertion of ownership over background IP of the other party
- No license grant for necessary background IP to use the deliverables

## 6. Non-Compete (Concurrentiebeding / Relatiebeding)

### Standard Position
- In commercial contracts (not employment): parties are generally free to agree on non-compete restrictions
- Must be proportionate in scope, duration, and geography
- Penalty clauses (boetebeding) are common enforcement mechanisms
- Courts may moderate excessive non-competes under Art. 6:248 lid 2 BW

### GREEN Flags
- Duration of 1-2 years post-termination
- Limited to the specific market/activity of the contract
- Geographic limitation proportionate to the business relationship
- Reasonable penalty amounts with judicial moderation clause

### YELLOW Flags
- Duration exceeding 2 years
- Broad activity restriction beyond the contract scope
- Very high penalty amounts
- Non-solicitation (relatiebeding) without clear definition of "relations"

### RED Flags
- Unlimited duration or geography
- Restriction prevents a party from conducting its core business
- Disproportionate penalty with no moderation clause
- Non-compete without any consideration or compensation (may be challenged on proportionality grounds)

## 7. Payment Terms (Betalingsvoorwaarden)

### Standard Position
- Default payment term: 30 days after invoice (Art. 6:119a BW for commercial transactions)
- EU Late Payment Directive (Directive 2011/7/EU) implemented via Art. 6:119a and 6:119b BW
- Maximum payment term B2B: 60 days (unless expressly agreed and not grossly unfair)
- Large enterprises to SMEs: effectively 30 days per best practice
- Statutory commercial interest rate (wettelijke handelsrente) applies automatically on late B2B payments

### GREEN Flags
- 30-day payment terms
- Clear invoicing procedure and requirements
- Reference to wettelijke handelsrente for late payment
- EUR 40 minimum collection cost compensation (Art. 6:96 lid 4 BW)

### YELLOW Flags
- 60-day payment terms (permissible but verify fairness)
- Set-off rights (verrekening) without clear conditions
- Payment contingent on end-client payment ("pay when paid")

### RED Flags
- Payment terms exceeding 60 days without objective justification (potentially unfair per Late Payment Directive)
- No interest on late payment (loss of statutory entitlement)
- Unilateral right to withhold or reduce payment
- "Pay when paid" or "pay if paid" in supply chain (may be challenged under Art. 6:248 lid 2 BW)
