# Economic Criminal Law - WED (Wet op de Economische Delicten)

## Overview
The **Wet op de Economische Delicten (WED)** is the framework act for economic criminal offenses in the Netherlands. It does not define offenses itself but rather designates violations of other laws as economic offenses and provides a separate sentencing framework.

---

## Structure of the WED

### Art. 1 WED - Classification of Economic Offenses
Violations of listed laws are classified into categories:

| Category | Severity | When committed as... |
|----------|----------|---------------------|
| Art. 1 sub 1 | Most serious | Always misdrijf |
| Art. 1 sub 2 | Intermediate | Misdrijf if committed intentionally; otherwise overtreding |
| Art. 1 sub 3 | Least serious | Misdrijf if committed intentionally; otherwise overtreding |
| Art. 1 sub 4 | Specific | Overtreding |

### Laws Listed in Art. 1 WED (Selection)
The WED covers violations of hundreds of laws, including:

#### Financial/Corporate
- Wet op het financieel toezicht (Wft) - Financial Supervision Act
- Wet toezicht trustkantoren (Wtt) - Trust Office Supervision Act
- Wet ter voorkoming van witwassen en financieren van terrorisme (Wwft) - Anti-Money Laundering Act (certain provisions)
- Handelsregisterwet - Trade Register Act
- Wet op de jaarrekening (annual accounts obligations via BW Boek 2)

#### Environmental
- Wet milieubeheer (Wm) - Environmental Management Act
- Wet bodembescherming - Soil Protection Act
- Various environmental regulations under the Omgevingswet

#### Health and Safety
- Warenwet - Commodities Act
- Geneesmiddelenwet - Medicines Act
- Arbeidsomstandighedenwet (Arbowet) - Occupational Health and Safety Act

#### Tax
- Certain tax offenses are prosecuted under the AWR but may also involve WED

#### Competition
- Mededingingswet - Competition Act (cartel violations)

## Penalties under WED

### Art. 6 WED - Maximum Penalties

#### For Economic Misdrijven (felonies)
| Category | Imprisonment | Fine |
|----------|-------------|------|
| Art. 1 sub 1 | Max 6 years | Max 5th category (EUR 90,000) or 10% annual turnover |
| Art. 1 sub 2 | Max 6 years | Max 5th category or 10% annual turnover |
| Art. 1 sub 3 | Max 2 years | Max 4th category (EUR 22,500) |

#### For Economic Overtredingen (misdemeanors)
| Category | Detention | Fine |
|----------|----------|------|
| Art. 1 sub 1-2 | Max 1 year | Max 4th category (EUR 22,500) |
| Art. 1 sub 3-4 | Max 6 months | Max 4th category |

### Art. 7 WED - Additional Penalties and Measures
- **Ontzetting van rechten** (deprivation of rights) - prohibition from practicing certain professions
- **Stillegging van de onderneming** (closure of the enterprise) - max 1 year
- **Verbeurdverklaring** (confiscation) of objects related to the offense
- **Onttrekking aan het verkeer** (removal from circulation)
- **Publication of judgment** (openbaarmaking) at the convicted person's expense
- **Placement under supervision** (onderbewindstelling)

## Key Areas of Economic Criminal Law

### 1. Financial Supervision Offenses (Wft violations)
- Operating without required license (vergunningplicht)
- Market manipulation (marktmanipulatie - Art. 5:58 Wft)
- Insider trading (handel met voorwetenschap - Art. 5:56 Wft)
- Violations of prospectus requirements
- Breaches of fit and proper (betrouwbaarheid en geschiktheid) requirements
- Penalties: up to 6 years imprisonment and/or significant fines
- Dual enforcement: AFM/DNB can impose administrative fines; OM can prosecute criminally (una via principle applies)

### 2. Anti-Money Laundering (Wwft violations)
- Failure to conduct customer due diligence (cliententenonderzoek)
- Failure to report unusual transactions (meldplicht ongebruikelijke transacties) to FIU-Nederland
- Lack of adequate compliance policies
- Penalties: significant fines and/or imprisonment
- Gatekeepers: banks, notaries, lawyers, accountants, tax advisors, real estate agents, trust offices

### 3. Environmental Crime
- Illegal waste disposal
- Pollution of soil, water, or air
- Violations of environmental permits
- Increasingly prosecuted and subject to substantial penalties
- Bijzondere opsporingsdiensten (special investigation services) conduct investigations

### 4. Competition Law Violations
- Cartel agreements (Art. 6 Mededingingswet)
- Abuse of dominant position (Art. 24 Mededingingswet)
- ACM (Autoriteit Consument & Markt) has administrative enforcement powers
- Criminal prosecution possible but rare (OM policy focuses on bid-rigging in public procurement)
- Fines: up to 10% of annual turnover (administrative); WED penalties (criminal)

### 5. Tax Fraud (Fiscale fraude)
- **Art. 69 AWR**: Intentionally filing false tax returns - max 6 years imprisonment
- **Art. 68 AWR**: Failure to comply with tax obligations - max 4 years
- Prosecution by Functioneel Parket (Fiscal Criminal Investigation Service - FIOD)
- Una via: if administrative penalty (vergrijpboete) is imposed, criminal prosecution for same facts is barred (and vice versa)

### 6. Subsidy Fraud
- Obtaining subsidies through false statements
- May constitute oplichting (Art. 326 Sr) and/or valsheid in geschrift (Art. 225 Sr)
- EU subsidy fraud: additional EU anti-fraud provisions (OLAF, EPPO)

## Procedural Aspects

### Investigation
- **FIOD** (Fiscale Inlichtingen- en Opsporingsdienst): Tax and financial investigations
- **ISZW** (Inspectie SZW, now Nederlandse Arbeidsinspectie): Labor and social security fraud
- **NVWA** (Nederlandse Voedsel- en Warenautoriteit): Food safety and commodities
- **ILT** (Inspectie Leefomgeving en Transport): Environmental and transport
- **AFM/DNB**: Financial supervision investigation
- **ACM**: Competition and consumer protection
- **Bijzondere opsporingsdiensten**: Various specialized investigation services

### Prosecution
- **Functioneel Parket**: Specialized prosecution service for complex financial, economic, and environmental crime
- **Landelijk Parket**: For organized and international crime
- **Arrondissementsparket**: Regular prosecution for less complex cases

### Corporate Settlement (Hoge transactie / DPA-equivalent)
- Art. 74 Sr and 74a Sr: Transactie (settlement) - OM offers settlement to avoid prosecution
- For large corporate cases: "hoge transacties" (high-value settlements) with publication
- No formal Deferred Prosecution Agreement (DPA) system, but settlements serve similar purpose
- Settlement amount can be very substantial (e.g., ING: EUR 775 million in 2018 for Wwft violations)
- Since 2024: discussion about introducing a formal DPA regime

---

## Una Via Principle (Art. 5:44 Awb / Art. 243 lid 2 Sv)
- If an administrative penalty (bestuurlijke boete) has been imposed for a violation, the same violation cannot also be criminally prosecuted (and vice versa)
- Prevents double punishment (ne bis in idem variant)
- Applies between administrative fines by supervisory authorities (AFM, DNB, ACM, AP) and criminal prosecution by the OM
- Choice of enforcement route must be made before a final decision
