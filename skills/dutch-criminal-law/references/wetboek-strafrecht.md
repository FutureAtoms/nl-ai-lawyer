# Key Provisions of the Dutch Criminal Code (Wetboek van Strafrecht - Sr)

## Book 1: General Provisions (Algemene bepalingen)

### Title I: Scope (Omvang van de werking)
- **Art. 1 Sr**: Nulla poena sine lege - no punishment without prior statutory provision (legaliteitsbeginsel)
- **Art. 1 lid 2**: Most favorable law applies if legislation changes after the offense (lex mitior)
- **Art. 2-8 Sr**: Jurisdiction rules:
  - Art. 2: Offenses committed in the Netherlands (territorialiteitsbeginsel)
  - Art. 3: On Dutch vessels and aircraft
  - Art. 4: Certain offenses by Dutch nationals abroad (active nationality principle)
  - Art. 5: Offenses abroad by Dutch nationals if also criminal in the country of commission
  - Art. 7: Certain offenses against Dutch interests committed abroad

### Title II: Penalties (Straffen)
- **Art. 9 Sr**: Types of hoofdstraffen (principal penalties):
  - Gevangenisstraf (imprisonment) - for misdrijven only
  - Hechtenis (detention) - for overtredingen
  - Taakstraf (community service)
  - Geldboete (fine)
- **Art. 14a-14i Sr**: Voorwaardelijke veroordeling (conditional/suspended sentence) with probation conditions (bijzondere voorwaarden)
- **Art. 22b-22c Sr**: Taakstraf limitations - cannot be sole penalty for serious violent and sexual offenses
- **Art. 23 Sr**: Fine categories:
  | Category | Maximum |
  |----------|---------|
  | 1st | EUR 450 |
  | 2nd | EUR 4,500 |
  | 3rd | EUR 9,000 |
  | 4th | EUR 22,500 |
  | 5th | EUR 90,000 |
  | 6th | EUR 900,000 |
- **Art. 24 Sr**: Fine in lieu of imprisonment; number of fine-days
- **Art. 36f Sr**: Schadevergoedingsmaatregel (compensation order) for victims

### Title III: Exclusion and Reduction of Criminal Liability

#### Rechtvaardigingsgronden (Justifications)
- **Art. 40 Sr**: Overmacht (force majeure / necessity)
- **Art. 41 lid 1 Sr**: Noodweer (self-defense) - proportionate defense against immediate unlawful attack
- **Art. 42 Sr**: Wettelijk voorschrift (statutory authorization) - act performed in compliance with a legal obligation
- **Art. 43 Sr**: Ambtelijk bevel (official order) - act performed on order of a competent authority

#### Schulduitsluitingsgronden (Excuses)
- **Art. 41 lid 2 Sr**: Noodweerexces (excessive self-defense) - exceeding bounds of self-defense due to intense emotional disturbance caused by the attack
- **Art. 39 Sr**: Ontoerekeningsvatbaarheid (insanity/mental disorder) - act not punishable if committed by someone who cannot be held responsible due to mental defect or disorder
- **Art. 40 Sr**: Psychische overmacht (duress) - overwhelming external pressure making compliance with the law unreasonable

### Title V: Participation (Deelneming - Art. 47-51 Sr)
- **Art. 47 Sr**: Daders (perpetrators):
  - Plegen (perpetration)
  - Doen plegen (causing another to commit)
  - Medeplegen (co-perpetration)
  - Uitlokking (incitement/instigation)
- **Art. 48 Sr**: Medeplichtigheid (complicity/aiding and abetting)
- **Art. 49 Sr**: Medeplichtige receives maximum 2/3 of the penalty of the principal offense
- **Art. 51 Sr**: Corporate criminal liability (see below)

### Title VI: Attempt (Poging - Art. 45 Sr)
- Attempt to commit a misdrijf is punishable if the intent of the perpetrator has manifested itself by a beginning of execution (begin van uitvoering)
- Maximum penalty: the principal penalty reduced by 1/3

### Title VII: Concurrence (Samenloop)
- **Art. 55 Sr**: Eendaadse samenloop (one act, multiple offenses) - most severe penalty applies
- **Art. 57 Sr**: Meerdaadse samenloop (multiple acts, multiple offenses) - aggregate penalties with maximum

## Book 2: Felonies (Misdrijven) - Key Offenses

### Offenses Against Public Order (Titel V)
- **Art. 131**: Opruiing (incitement to violence or other offenses)
- **Art. 137c-137e**: Discrimination offenses (groepsbelediging, aanzetten tot haat/discriminatie)
- **Art. 138**: Huisvredebreuk (trespass)
- **Art. 139a-139e**: Secret surveillance/recording

### Offenses Against Public Authority (Titel VIII)
- **Art. 177-177a**: Omkoping (bribery) of public officials (ambtelijke omkoping)
- **Art. 179**: Geweld/bedreiging tegen ambtenaar (violence/threats against public officials)

### Offenses Against Persons (Titels XVIII-XX)
- **Art. 287**: Doodslag (manslaughter) - max 15 years
- **Art. 289**: Moord (murder - with premeditation) - max 30 years or life imprisonment
- **Art. 300-304**: Mishandeling (assault) - max 3 years (simple), 4 years (causing serious harm)
- **Art. 242-250**: Zedenmisdrijven (sexual offenses) - updated by Wet seksuele misdrijven (2024):
  - Art. 242: Verkrachting (rape) - max 12 years
  - Expanded definitions including non-consensual sex acts

### Offenses Against Property (Titels XXII-XXVI)
- **Art. 310**: Diefstal (theft) - max 4 years
- **Art. 311-312**: Qualified theft (gekwalificeerde diefstal) - aggravated circumstances, up to 9 years
- **Art. 317**: Afpersing (extortion) - max 9 years
- **Art. 321**: Verduistering (embezzlement) - max 3 years
- **Art. 326**: Oplichting (fraud/swindling) - max 4 years
- **Art. 350**: Vernieling (criminal damage) - max 2 years

### Financial/Economic Offenses
- **Art. 225-227**: Valsheid in geschrift (forgery/fraud in documents) - max 6 years
- **Art. 420bis-420quater**: Witwassen (money laundering):
  - Art. 420bis: Opzetwitwassen (intentional money laundering) - max 6 years
  - Art. 420bis.1: Eenvoudig witwassen (simple money laundering) - concealing own criminal proceeds
  - Art. 420quater: Schuldwitwassen (negligent money laundering) - max 2 years

## Corporate Criminal Liability (Art. 51 Sr)

### Art. 51 lid 1 Sr
Criminal offenses can be committed by natural persons **and legal entities** (rechtspersonen).

### Art. 51 lid 2 Sr
If a criminal offense is committed by a legal entity, criminal proceedings can be brought against:
1. The legal entity itself, AND/OR
2. Those who ordered the commission (opdracht geven) of the offense, AND/OR
3. Those who had de facto control (feitelijke leiding geven) over the prohibited conduct

### Attribution to the Legal Entity
A criminal offense is attributed to a legal entity if (Drijfmest arrest, HR):
- The conduct falls within the sphere of the legal entity's activities (sfeer van de rechtspersoon)
- Factors include:
  - The person acted as an employee or officer of the entity
  - The conduct served the entity's interests
  - The entity had the power to determine whether the conduct took place (beschikkingsmacht)
  - The entity accepted or tolerated the conduct (aanvaarding/IJzerdraad criteria)

### Penalties for Legal Entities (Art. 23 lid 7 Sr)
- If the statutory maximum fine is insufficient: fine up to the next category, or
- Fine up to 10% of the annual turnover of the legal entity (for some economic offenses via WED)

## Prescription (Verjaring - Art. 70-73 Sr)

| Maximum Penalty | Prescription Period |
|----------------|-------------------|
| Overtredingen (3 years max) | 3 years |
| Misdrijven with max 3 years imprisonment | 6 years |
| Misdrijven with max 3-8 years | 12 years |
| Misdrijven with max 8+ years | 20 years |
| Offenses punishable by life imprisonment | No prescription (since 2006) |
| Sexual offenses against minors | Starts running from the 18th birthday of the victim |
