## 1. Corporate Criminal Liability (Art. 51 Sr)

### 1.1 HR 23 februari 1954, NJ 1954/378 (IJzerdraad-arrest)
- **Court**: Hoge Raad
- **Date**: 23 February 1954
- **Key principle**: The original framework for corporate criminal liability. The HR established two criteria: (1) the natural person acted within the corporation's sphere of operations ("de verboden gedraging vond plaats in de sfeer van de rechtspersoon"), and (2) the corporation had the power to determine whether the act would take place and the act was accepted or typically accepted by the corporation ("beschikken en aanvaarden").
- **Relevance**: Foundational precedent, though substantially refined by later case law. Still cited as the origin of the criteria.

### 1.2 HR 21 oktober 2003, ECLI:NL:HR:2003:AF7938 (Drijfmest-arrest)
- **Court**: Hoge Raad
- **Date**: 21 October 2003
- **Key principle**: Modernized and broadened the criteria for imputing criminal conduct to a legal entity. The HR articulated the "redelijkerwijs toerekenen" (can reasonably be attributed) standard, listing non-exhaustive factors:
  - Did the conduct occur within the normal business operations?
  - Did the entity benefit from the conduct?
  - Did the entity have control (beschikkingsmacht) over whether the conduct occurred?
  - Did the entity accept the conduct or similar conduct (including by failing to take preventive measures)?
- **Relevance**: The leading modern authority on Art. 51 Sr; replaces the rigid IJzerdraad criteria with a flexible attribution test.

### 1.3 HR 26 april 2016, ECLI:NL:HR:2016:733 (Stichting/Corporate liability)
- **Court**: Hoge Raad
- **Date**: 26 April 2016
- **Key principle**: Confirmed that the Drijfmest criteria apply equally to all types of legal entities, including stichtingen (foundations) and verenigingen (associations), not just commercial corporations (BV/NV).
- **Relevance**: Extends corporate criminal liability beyond commercial entities.

## 2. Feitelijk Leidinggeven (Directing / De Facto Management) -- Art. 51 lid 2 sub 2 Sr

### 2.1 HR 16 december 1986, ECLI:NL:HR:1986:AC9607 (Slavenburg II)
- **Court**: Hoge Raad
- **Date**: 16 December 1986
- **Key principle**: The leading criteria for "feitelijk leidinggeven" (de facto directing) of a corporate offense:
  - The feitelijk leidinggever must have been aware of the offending conduct (or at least had reason to be aware);
  - The feitelijk leidinggever must have been in a position to prevent or stop the conduct;
  - The feitelijk leidinggever must have consciously accepted (bewust aanvaarden) the substantial chance that the offense would occur, including by omission (nalaten).
- **Relevance**: The fundamental precedent for personal criminal liability of directors and managers under Art. 51 lid 2 Sr.

### 2.2 HR 1 juni 2021, ECLI:NL:HR:2021:818
- **Court**: Hoge Raad
- **Date**: 1 June 2021
- **Key principle**: The HR refined the Slavenburg II criteria, confirming that "bewust de aanmerkelijke kans aanvaarden" (conscious acceptance of a substantial chance) can include culpable failure to act by someone in a management position, even without active involvement in the criminal conduct.
- **Relevance**: Broadens the reach of feitelijk leidinggeven liability for passive directors.

## 3. Valsheid in Geschrift (Forgery) -- Art. 225 Sr

### 3.1 HR 14 mei 1957, NJ 1957/472 (Oogmerk-arrest)
- **Court**: Hoge Raad
- **Date**: 14 May 1957
- **Key principle**: The element of "oogmerk als echt en onvervalst te gebruiken" (intent to use as genuine and unforged): the forger must have the purpose of using the document to deceive others. This subjective intent is a constitutive element of Art. 225 Sr.
- **Relevance**: Establishes the subjective intent requirement, distinguishing forgery from innocent inaccuracies.

### 3.2 HR 22 april 2014, ECLI:NL:HR:2014:953
- **Court**: Hoge Raad
- **Date**: 22 April 2014
- **Key principle**: Definition of "geschrift" (document) in Art. 225 Sr: a document is any written instrument to which legal or evidentiary significance may be attached in legal dealings. Electronic documents (emails, digital invoices) can qualify as a "geschrift" if they are intended to serve as proof.
- **Relevance**: Adapts the forgery offense to the digital age.

### 3.3 HR 7 februari 2017, ECLI:NL:HR:2017:168
- **Court**: Hoge Raad
- **Date**: 7 February 2017
- **Key principle**: False invoicing (valselijk opmaken van facturen) as a means of committing tax fraud (Art. 69 AWR) in conjunction with Art. 225 Sr. The HR addressed the relationship between the fiscal offense and the general forgery offense when false documents are used to support fraudulent tax returns.
- **Relevance**: Directly relevant to corporate fraud and FIOD investigations.

## 4. Environmental Crime (Wet milieubeheer, WED, and Wet economische delicten)

### 4.1 HR 23 december 2003, ECLI:NL:HR:2003:AN7818 (Middelburg -- milieustrafrecht)
- **Court**: Hoge Raad
- **Date**: 23 December 2003
- **Key principle**: Criminal liability for operating without or in violation of an environmental permit (milieuvergunning). The HR confirmed that operating in breach of permit conditions constitutes a "wirtschaftliches Delikt" under the Wet economische delicten (WED) even where the environmental harm is minimal.
- **Relevance**: Strict liability approach in environmental criminal enforcement.

### 4.2 HR 21 april 2009, ECLI:NL:HR:2009:BH2684 (Chemie-Pack)
- **Court**: Hoge Raad
- **Date**: 21 April 2009
- **Key principle**: The scope of the zorgplicht (duty of care) under Art. 1.1a Wet milieubeheer in criminal proceedings. The HR ruled that the general duty of care provision can serve as an independent basis for criminal prosecution, even when specific permit conditions do not address the particular form of pollution.
- **Relevance**: Broadens the basis for environmental prosecution beyond permit-specific violations.

### 4.3 Rb. Rotterdam 19 november 2013, ECLI:NL:RBROT:2013:9116
- **Court**: Rechtbank Rotterdam
- **Date**: 19 November 2013
- **Key principle**: Sentencing in a large-scale illegal waste dumping case. The court imposed substantial fines on both the corporate defendant and its feitelijk leidinggever, applying the WED sentencing framework (Art. 6 WED) and considering the environmental damage caused.
- **Relevance**: Illustrates the practical application of environmental criminal sentencing.

## 5. Corporate Settlements (Hoge/Buitengerechtelijke Transactie) -- Art. 74 Sr / Art. 257a Sv

### 5.1 HR 3 november 2009, ECLI:NL:HR:2009:BJ6953 (Clickfonds)
- **Court**: Hoge Raad
- **Date**: 3 November 2009
- **Key principle**: The legal effect of a transactie (out-of-court settlement offered by the OM): acceptance extinguishes the right of prosecution (recht tot strafvordering vervalt). The HR confirmed that the transactie is a voluntary arrangement and that its terms must be clearly communicated.
- **Relevance**: Foundational for understanding the corporate settlement framework.

### 5.2 Rb. Den Haag 26 juni 2018, ECLI:NL:RBDHA:2018:7606
- **Court**: Rechtbank Den Haag
- **Date**: 26 June 2018
- **Key principle**: Judicial review of a high-profile hoge transactie (high-value corporate settlement) under Art. 74 Sr. This was one of the first significant rulings addressing the transparency and proportionality of corporate settlements in the Netherlands.
- **Relevance**: Part of the broader discussion on the legitimacy and oversight of negotiated settlements in corporate criminal matters (analogous to US DPAs/NPAs).

### 5.3 Gerechtshof Den Haag 12 maart 2019, ECLI:NL:GHDHA:2019:542
- **Court**: Gerechtshof Den Haag
- **Date**: 12 March 2019
- **Key principle**: Whether a complainant (Art. 12 Sv procedure -- beklagprocedure) can challenge the OM's decision to resolve a corporate fraud case by transactie rather than prosecution. The court held that the Art. 12 Sv procedure is available to interested parties who wish to challenge the OM's settlement decision.
- **Relevance**: Accountability mechanism for corporate settlements.

## 6. FIOD Investigation Procedures

### 6.1 HR 19 februari 2013, ECLI:NL:HR:2013:BY5322
- **Court**: Hoge Raad
- **Date**: 19 February 2013
- **Key principle**: The rights of a suspect during a FIOD investigation, specifically the cautie (right to remain silent, Art. 29 Sv). The HR confirmed that statements obtained without proper cautioning are inadmissible (bewijsuitsluiting), subject to the Schutznorm.
- **Relevance**: Protects the rights of suspects in financial/economic investigations.

### 6.2 HR 1 juli 2014, ECLI:NL:HR:2014:1563
- **Court**: Hoge Raad
- **Date**: 1 July 2014
- **Key principle**: Legal professional privilege (verschoningsrecht, Art. 218 Sv) during FIOD searches. The HR ruled that documents subject to legal privilege may not be seized, and that a claiming lawyer's assertion of privilege must be respected unless there are "zeer uitzonderlijke omstandigheden" (very exceptional circumstances).
- **Relevance**: Governs the FIOD's treatment of privileged materials during dawn raids.

### 6.3 HR 18 juni 2019, ECLI:NL:HR:2019:965
- **Court**: Hoge Raad
- **Date**: 18 June 2019
- **Key principle**: The nemo tenetur principle (privilege against self-incrimination) in the context of FIOD requests for documents and digital data. The HR distinguished between pre-existing documents (which must be produced) and documents that require the suspect to actively create or compile information (which are protected by nemo tenetur).
- **Relevance**: Critical for advising corporate clients facing FIOD dawn raids and document requests.
