## 1. Bezwaar and Beroep Procedures (Awb Chapters 6-8)

### 1.1 ABRvS 19 april 2006, ECLI:NL:RVS:2006:AW2297
- **Court**: Afdeling bestuursrechtspraak van de Raad van State (ABRvS)
- **Date**: 19 April 2006
- **Key principle**: The requirements for a valid bezwaarschrift (notice of objection), including the duty of the administrative body to offer the applicant the opportunity to correct deficiencies (verzuimherstel) under Art. 6:6 Awb before declaring the objection inadmissible.
- **Relevance**: Protects citizens from premature niet-ontvankelijkverklaring of their bezwaar.

### 1.2 ABRvS 2 februari 2011, ECLI:NL:RVS:2011:BP2831
- **Court**: ABRvS
- **Date**: 2 February 2011
- **Key principle**: The scope of the ex nunc heroverweging (full reconsideration) duty in bezwaar proceedings. The ABRvS confirmed that the administrative body must reconsider the decision in full, including facts and circumstances arising after the primary decision, unless the nature of the decision precludes it.
- **Relevance**: Core principle ensuring that bezwaar is a meaningful administrative remedy.

### 1.3 CRvB 14 maart 2003, ECLI:NL:CRVB:2003:AF7228
- **Court**: Centrale Raad van Beroep (CRvB)
- **Date**: 14 March 2003
- **Key principle**: The informele lus (informal loop): administrative courts may informally indicate deficiencies in the challenged decision and give the administrative body the opportunity to repair them during the beroep proceedings, before formal bestuurlijke lus (Art. 8:51a Awb) was codified.
- **Relevance**: Precursor to the formal bestuurlijke lus; illustrates the collaborative nature of Dutch administrative justice.

## 2. Belanghebbende (Interested Party Standing) -- Art. 1:2 Awb

### 2.1 ABRvS 23 augustus 2017, ECLI:NL:RVS:2017:2271 (Stichting Werkgroep Behoud de Peel)
- **Court**: ABRvS
- **Date**: 23 August 2017
- **Key principle**: The criteria for legal entities (rechtspersonen) to qualify as belanghebbende under Art. 1:2 lid 3 Awb: the entity must pursue interests that are demonstrably related to its statutory purpose (doelstelling) and must carry out actual activities ("feitelijke werkzaamheden") to that end.
- **Relevance**: Tightened standing requirements for interest groups challenging environmental and spatial planning decisions.

### 2.2 ABRvS 4 november 2020, ECLI:NL:RVS:2020:2608 (Varkens in Nood)
- **Court**: ABRvS
- **Date**: 4 November 2020
- **Key principle**: Following the CJEU ruling in Varkens in Nood (C-826/18), the ABRvS abandoned the strict application of Art. 6:13 Awb (the requirement that appellants must have participated in the zienswijze procedure to have standing in beroep) for environmental decisions falling under the Aarhus Convention. All members of the public now have access to court in environmental matters.
- **Relevance**: Major expansion of access to justice for environmental organizations and individuals.

## 3. Dwangsom bij Niet Tijdig Beslissen (Art. 4:17 Awb)

### 3.1 ABRvS 6 maart 2013, ECLI:NL:RVS:2013:BZ3382
- **Court**: ABRvS
- **Date**: 6 March 2013
- **Key principle**: The conditions under which a dwangsom (penalty payment) accrues when the administrative body fails to decide within the statutory term. The ABRvS clarified that the ingebrekestelling (formal notice of default) must satisfy clear requirements and that the 14-day grace period of Art. 4:17 lid 3 Awb runs from receipt of the notice.
- **Relevance**: Practical guidance for claimants seeking to enforce timely administrative decisions.

### 3.2 ABRvS 17 juli 2019, ECLI:NL:RVS:2019:2464
- **Court**: ABRvS
- **Date**: 17 July 2019
- **Key principle**: The maximum dwangsom of EUR 1,442 (indexed) applies per application and does not reset when a new ingebrekestelling is sent for the same delayed decision. The ABRvS further clarified that repeated notices do not restart the accrual period.
- **Relevance**: Prevents abuse of the dwangsom mechanism through serial notices.

## 4. Vertrouwensbeginsel (Principle of Legitimate Expectations) -- The Three-Step Test

### 4.1 ABRvS 29 mei 2019, ECLI:NL:RVS:2019:1694 (Amsterdamse dakopbouw)
- **Court**: ABRvS
- **Date**: 29 May 2019
- **Key principle**: The ABRvS introduced a new, citizen-friendly three-step test for the vertrouwensbeginsel:
  1. **Step 1 (Toerekening)**: Can the statement or conduct be attributed to the competent administrative body?
  2. **Step 2 (Gerechtvaardigd vertrouwen)**: Did the citizen reasonably derive a concrete, justified expectation from that statement or conduct?
  3. **Step 3 (Belangenafweging)**: Even if legitimate expectations exist, do overriding public interests justify departure from those expectations? If so, compensation (nadeelcompensatie) may be required.
- **Relevance**: Overturned decades of restrictive case law on the vertrouwensbeginsel. Now the leading framework for all legitimate expectations claims in Dutch administrative law.

### 4.2 ABRvS 11 augustus 2021, ECLI:NL:RVS:2021:1795
- **Court**: ABRvS
- **Date**: 11 August 2021
- **Key principle**: Application and refinement of the three-step test. The ABRvS confirmed that a concrete, unambiguous statement by a competent civil servant (e.g., during a pre-application consultation) can satisfy step 1, even if the civil servant was not the formal decision-maker.
- **Relevance**: Demonstrates the practical reach of the 2019 landmark ruling.

## 5. Evenredigheidsbeginsel (Principle of Proportionality) -- Art. 3:4 lid 2 Awb

### 5.1 ABRvS 2 februari 2022, ECLI:NL:RVS:2022:285 (Harderwijk)
- **Court**: ABRvS (Grote Kamer)
- **Date**: 2 February 2022
- **Key principle**: The ABRvS adopted a more intensive and differentiated proportionality test (evenredigheidstoetsing) for administrative sanctions and other burdensome decisions. The court abandoned the previous deferential "kennelijk onredelijk" standard (inherited from Maxis/Praxis, ABRvS 9 May 1996) in favor of a three-part test:
  1. **Geschiktheid** (suitability): Is the measure suitable to achieve its goal?
  2. **Noodzakelijkheid** (necessity): Is the measure necessary, or could a less intrusive alternative achieve the same goal?
  3. **Evenwichtigheid** (proportionality stricto sensu): Is the measure proportionate in light of the individual's interests?
- **Relevance**: Landmark ruling fundamentally reshaping judicial review of administrative decisions. Applies to all areas of bestuursrecht, including handhaving, intrekking of vergunningen, and terugvordering.

### 5.2 ABRvS 9 mei 1996, ECLI:NL:RVS:1996:ZF2153 (Maxis/Praxis) -- superseded
- **Court**: ABRvS
- **Date**: 9 May 1996
- **Key principle**: The former deferential standard: the court would only overturn a decision for disproportionality if the outcome was "kennelijk onredelijk" (manifestly unreasonable). This gave administrative bodies very wide discretion.
- **Relevance**: Historical reference; superseded by Harderwijk (2022), but remains important to understand how the standard has evolved.

## 6. Omgevingswet Transitional Issues (Post-1 January 2024)

### 6.1 ABRvS 17 januari 2024, ECLI:NL:RVS:2024:108
- **Court**: ABRvS
- **Date**: 17 January 2024
- **Key principle**: One of the first ABRvS decisions addressing the transitional regime (overgangsrecht) under the Omgevingswet (effective 1 January 2024). The court considered whether proceedings initiated under the old Wabo/Wro framework continue under the old rules or must be assessed under the new Omgevingswet.
- **Relevance**: Essential guidance for practitioners navigating the transition from Wabo/Wro to the Omgevingswet.

### 6.2 ABRvS 6 maart 2024, ECLI:NL:RVS:2024:943
- **Court**: ABRvS
- **Date**: 6 March 2024
- **Key principle**: Interpretation of the transitional provisions in Art. 4.3 Invoeringswet Omgevingswet: decisions taken before 1 January 2024 under the Wabo are governed by the old procedural and substantive rules, including bezwaar and beroep, even if the proceedings conclude after that date.
- **Relevance**: Provides legal certainty during the transition period; important for ongoing spatial planning disputes.

## 7. Bestuurlijke Lus (Administrative Loop) -- Art. 8:51a-8:51d Awb

### 7.1 ABRvS 12 december 2012, ECLI:NL:RVS:2012:BY5865
- **Court**: ABRvS
- **Date**: 12 December 2012
- **Key principle**: Guidelines on the scope and limits of the bestuurlijke lus: the administrative court identifies specific deficiencies and gives the administrative body a fixed term to repair them, avoiding unnecessary annulment and retrial. The ABRvS clarified when a bestuurlijke lus is appropriate and when the deficiency is so fundamental that repair is not possible.
- **Relevance**: Key procedural tool to accelerate administrative litigation and reduce the backlog.

## 8. Beginsel van Fair Play and Zorgvuldigheidsbeginsel (Art. 3:2 Awb)

### 8.1 ABRvS 17 mei 2017, ECLI:NL:RVS:2017:1259
- **Court**: ABRvS
- **Date**: 17 May 2017
- **Key principle**: The duty of care (zorgvuldigheidsbeginsel) under Art. 3:2 Awb requires the administrative body to gather all relevant facts and interests before taking a decision. In this case, the ABRvS annulled a permit decision because the administrative body had failed to conduct adequate environmental research before granting the permit.
- **Relevance**: Reinforces the duty of thorough factual investigation as a prerequisite for lawful administrative decision-making.

### 8.2 CRvB 17 januari 2017, ECLI:NL:CRVB:2017:365
- **Court**: Centrale Raad van Beroep (CRvB)
- **Date**: 17 January 2017
- **Key principle**: The fair play principle in social security decisions: the administrative body must give the applicant a genuine opportunity to present their case and respond to adverse evidence before taking a negative decision. Failure to do so constitutes a violation of Art. 3:2 Awb and the broader principle of zorgvuldige voorbereiding.
- **Relevance**: Applies to all areas of bestuursrecht where the administrative body uses adverse information.
