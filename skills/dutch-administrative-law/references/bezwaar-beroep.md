# Objection and Appeal Procedures (Bezwaar en Beroep)

## Flowchart of Administrative Legal Protection

```
Decision (Besluit)
       │
       ▼ (6 weeks)
    Bezwaar (Objection) ─── Art. 7:1 Awb
       │                    to the same administrative body
       ▼ (6 weeks)
    Beroep (Appeal) ─────── Art. 8:1 Awb
       │                    to Rechtbank (bestuursrechter)
       ▼ (6 weeks)
    Hoger beroep ────────── To ABRvS / CRvB / CBb
       │
       │ (parallel option at any stage)
       ├──► Voorlopige voorziening (Provisional measures) ─ Art. 8:81 Awb
       │
       ▼
    Definitief (Final and binding)
```

### Exception: Afdeling 3.4 Preparation (Uniform Public Preparation)
```
Draft Decision (Ontwerp-besluit)
       │
       ▼ (6 weeks)
    Zienswijzen (Views) ─── Submit views on the draft
       │
       ▼
    Final Decision (Besluit)
       │
       ▼ (6 weeks)
    DIRECT Beroep ────────── Art. 7:1 lid 1 sub d Awb
       │                     No bezwaar phase
       ▼ (6 weeks)
    Hoger beroep
```

## Bezwaar Procedure (Detailed)

### Step 1: Receiving the Decision
- Check the date of the decision carefully
- Check the legal remedy clause (rechtsmiddelenclausule) at the end of the decision - it tells you how and where to object
- If no rechtsmiddelenclausule: this does not affect the deadline but may be grounds for verschoonbare termijnoverschrijding

### Step 2: Filing Bezwaar
- **Deadline**: 6 weeks from the day after the decision was published or sent (Art. 6:7-6:8 Awb)
- **Address**: To the administrative body that made the decision (not the court)
- **Method**: Written (or electronic if the body has opened this channel, Art. 2:15 Awb)
- **Content requirements** (Art. 6:5):
  - Name and address
  - Date
  - Description of the decision (attach a copy)
  - Grounds for objection
  - Signature

### Pro Forma Bezwaar
- If time is running out: file a brief bezwaar stating "grounds to follow" (pro forma bezwaarschrift)
- Request a deadline for submitting grounds
- Body must set a reasonable deadline (usually 2-4 weeks)
- If grounds not submitted timely: bezwaar can be declared inadmissible (niet-ontvankelijk)

### Step 3: Hearing (Horen - Art. 7:2 Awb)
- Right to be heard before a decision on bezwaar is made
- May be heard by:
  - The deciding body itself
  - A bezwaarschriftencommissie (advisory committee, Art. 7:13 Awb)
- Objector may bring witnesses and experts
- Objector may be assisted by a lawyer or other representative
- Informal atmosphere (less formal than court)

### Step 4: Decision on Bezwaar (Beslissing op bezwaar)
- Administrative body must decide within:
  - **6 weeks** after the bezwaar period ends (Art. 7:10 lid 1), OR
  - **12 weeks** if an advisory committee is used (Art. 7:10 lid 1)
- Extension of 6 weeks possible (Art. 7:10 lid 3)
- If body fails to decide in time: deemed refusal (fictieve weigering); applicant can appeal to court for failure to decide (beroep tegen niet-tijdig beslissen, Art. 6:2 sub b Awb)

### Possible Outcomes
| Outcome | Description |
|---------|-------------|
| Niet-ontvankelijk | Inadmissible (too late, no standing, no besluit) |
| Ongegrond | Unfounded (decision upheld) |
| Gegrond | Well-founded (decision revoked/modified) |

## Beroep Procedure (Appeal to Rechtbank)

### Step 1: Filing Beroep
- **Deadline**: 6 weeks from decision on bezwaar (or original decision if direct beroep)
- **Address**: To the Rechtbank with jurisdiction (usually the Rechtbank of the district where the administrative body is located, or where the applicant resides - depends on the specific law)
- **Content** (Art. 8:1, 6:5 Awb):
  - Name, address of appellant
  - Date
  - Copy of decision on bezwaar (and original decision)
  - Grounds for appeal (beroepsgronden)
- **Court fee** (griffierecht):
  - Natural persons: EUR 50 (as of 2024)
  - Legal entities: EUR 371 (as of 2024)
  - *Note: reduced or no fee in certain cases (social security, etc.)*

### Step 2: Written Phase
- Court sends copy of beroepschrift to the administrative body
- Body must submit defense (verweerschrift) and all relevant documents (processtukken)
- Appellant may submit additional documents or supplement grounds (up to 10 days before hearing)

### Step 3: Hearing (Zitting)
- Oral hearing at the Rechtbank (usually 30-60 minutes for standard cases)
- Judge(s): single judge (enkelvoudige kamer) for simple cases, three judges (meervoudige kamer) for complex cases
- Both parties present their positions; judge asks questions
- No formal rules of evidence (vrije bewijsleer in administrative law)

### Step 4: Judgment (Uitspraak)
- Usually within 6 weeks after the hearing (Art. 8:66 Awb)
- Court may:
  - Declare appeal inadmissible, unfounded, or well-founded
  - Annul the decision (vernietigen)
  - Order the body to make a new decision (with or without instructions)
  - Resolve the matter itself (zelf voorzien)
  - Award damages
  - Award costs

## Voorlopige Voorziening (Provisional Measures - Art. 8:81 Awb)

### When to Request
- **Urgency** (spoedeisend belang): the decision has immediate effects that cannot await the regular procedure
- Examples: imminent demolition, deportation, revocation of permit with ongoing business

### Procedure
- Request to the voorzieningenrechter (preliminary relief judge)
- Can be filed during bezwaar phase or beroep phase
- Usually heard within 2-4 weeks (or faster in urgent cases)
- Court fee: same as beroep

### Short Circuit (Kortsluiting - Art. 8:86 Awb)
- If the voorzieningenrechter has sufficient information: may decide the main case (beroep) immediately during the voorlopige voorziening hearing
- Efficient resolution of urgent cases

## Special Topics

### Ingebrekestelling bij niet-tijdig beslissen (Art. 4:17 Awb)
If the administrative body fails to decide within the statutory period:
1. Send an ingebrekestelling (formal notice to decide)
2. Body has 2 weeks to decide
3. If still no decision: automatic dwangsom (penalty) of EUR 23 per day (max EUR 1,442)
4. Simultaneously: right to appeal to court for failure to decide (beroep niet-tijdig beslissen)

### Government Liability (Overheidsaansprakelijkheid)
- **Unlawful decision**: If a decision is annulled, there is a strong presumption of unlawfulness (HR Van Hees/Gemeente Oegstgeest)
- Damages for unlawful government decisions can be claimed:
  - During the administrative procedure (Art. 8:88 Awb - verzoek om schadevergoeding)
  - In a separate civil procedure (Art. 6:162 BW - onrechtmatige overheidsdaad)
- Nadeelcompensatie (compensation for lawful government actions causing disproportionate damage): Art. 4:126 Awb

### Costs (Proceskostenvergoeding)
- If appeal is well-founded: body must reimburse the appellant's costs (Art. 8:75 Awb)
- Costs calculated per standardized fee system (Besluit proceskosten bestuursrecht)
- Covers: lawyer fees (at standardized rates, NOT actual costs), expert fees, travel costs, lost wages
- Court fee is also reimbursed
