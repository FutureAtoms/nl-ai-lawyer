# General Administrative Law Act (Awb) - Key Procedures

## Core Concepts

### Besluit (Administrative Decision - Art. 1:3 Awb)
A written decision by an administrative body (bestuursorgaan) containing a public law legal act (publiekrechtelijke rechtshandeling).

Types of besluiten:
- **Beschikking** (individual decision): addressed to one or more specific persons (e.g., permit, subsidy, fine)
- **Besluit van algemene strekking** (general decision): addressed to an open group (e.g., traffic regulation, zoning plan)
- **Beleidsregel** (policy rule): general rule about the exercise of discretionary power

**Not a besluit**: factual actions (feitelijke handelingen), private law acts, internal instructions, preparatory decisions (generally)

### Bestuursorgaan (Administrative Body - Art. 1:1 Awb)
- Organs of legal entities established under public law (e.g., Minister, College van B&W, Gedeputeerde Staten)
- Persons or bodies with public authority powers conferred by law (b-organs)

### Belanghebbende (Interested Party - Art. 1:2 Awb)
- A person whose interest is **directly** affected by a decision
- Natural persons: personal, own, objective, actual, and direct interest
- Legal persons: interest related to their statutory purpose and actual activities
- Public bodies: interest related to the interests they are charged with

## Chapter 3: General Provisions on Decisions

### Preparation of Decisions

#### Standard Preparation (Afdeling 3.2)
- **Art. 3:2**: Administrative body must carefully investigate relevant facts (zorgvuldigheidsbeginsel)
- **Art. 3:4**: Body must weigh all involved interests (belangenafweging)
- **Art. 3:46**: Decision must be supported by proper reasoning (motiveringsbeginsel)
- **Art. 3:47**: Reasoning must be communicated to interested parties

#### Extended Preparation (Uniforme openbare voorbereidingsprocedure - Afdeling 3.4)
- Applies when designated by specific legislation (e.g., environmental permits under Omgevingswet)
- Draft decision (ontwerp-besluit) published for public inspection
- 6-week period for submitting views (zienswijzen)
- All who submitted views are interested parties for appeal purposes
- No separate bezwaar phase - direct beroep to court

### General Principles of Good Governance (ABBB)
Codified in Awb and developed in case law:

| Principle | Dutch | Awb Article | Description |
|-----------|-------|-------------|-------------|
| Duty of care | Zorgvuldigheidsbeginsel | Art. 3:2 | Careful fact-finding |
| Proportionality | Evenredigheidsbeginsel | Art. 3:4 lid 2 | Consequences not disproportionate to purposes |
| Weighing of interests | Belangenafweging | Art. 3:4 lid 1 | All relevant interests must be weighed |
| Reasoning | Motiveringsbeginsel | Art. 3:46-47 | Decisions must be properly reasoned |
| Legal certainty | Rechtszekerheidsbeginsel | Case law | Decisions must be clear and predictable |
| Legitimate expectations | Vertrouwensbeginsel | Case law | Justified expectations must be honored |
| Equality | Gelijkheidsbeginsel | Case law | Equal cases treated equally |
| Fair play | Fair play-beginsel | Case law | Body must act impartially |
| Prohibition of detournement de pouvoir | Verbod van detournement de pouvoir | Art. 3:3 | Power used only for intended purpose |

## Chapter 4: Special Provisions

### Subsidies (Afdeling 4.2)
- **Art. 4:21**: Definition of subsidy (aanspraak op financiele middelen)
- **Art. 4:23**: Subsidy requires legal basis (wettelijke grondslag)
- **Art. 4:29-4:36**: Subsidy procedures (verlening, vaststelling)
- **Art. 4:46-4:57**: Revocation and recovery of subsidies

### Administrative Fines (Bestuurlijke boete - Titel 5.4)
- **Art. 5:40**: Definition of bestuurlijke boete
- **Art. 5:41**: No fine without fault (schuldbeginsel)
- **Art. 5:43**: Ne bis in idem - no second fine for same violation
- **Art. 5:44**: Una via - choice between administrative fine and criminal prosecution
- **Art. 5:46**: Proportionality of the fine amount
- Due process rights: right to be heard, right to legal assistance, privilege against self-incrimination

### Enforcement (Handhaving - Titel 5.3)
- **Last onder bestuursdwang** (Art. 5:21): Administrative body takes action to end violation, at violator's expense
- **Last onder dwangsom** (Art. 5:31d): Penalty payment for non-compliance with an order
- **Beginselplicht tot handhaving**: Principle that administrative bodies must enforce; non-enforcement only in special circumstances

## Chapter 6-7: Objection Procedure (Bezwaar)

### Art. 6:7-6:8 - Time Limit
- **6 weeks** from the day after the decision is published or sent
- Starts on the day after announcement/publication/sending
- If sent by mail: additional consideration for postal delivery

### Art. 6:5 - Requirements for Bezwaarschrift
Must contain:
1. Name and address of the objector
2. Date
3. Description of the decision objected to
4. Grounds for objection (gronden)
- Pro forma bezwaar possible (filing within deadline, grounds supplemented later - but deadline for grounds must be observed)

### Art. 7:2-7:9 - Hearing (Hoorzitting)
- Objector must be given opportunity to be heard
- Hearing by the deciding body or a bezwaarschriftencommissie (advisory committee)
- Parties may be assisted or represented
- Hearing may be omitted if bezwaar is manifestly inadmissible, manifestly unfounded, or if all parties agree

### Art. 7:10 - Decision on Bezwaar
- Must be decided within **6 weeks** after the bezwaar period expires (or 12 weeks if advisory committee used)
- Extension possible for another 6 weeks
- Body reconsiders the entire matter (heroverweging) - may also reconsider aspects not challenged

### Art. 7:11 - Full Reconsideration (Heroverweging)
- The body reconsidering must reconsider the decision on its merits
- May revoke and replace the original decision
- Reformed decision replaces the original (reformatio in peius is limited)

## Chapter 8: Appeal to Court (Beroep bij de Rechtbank)

### Art. 8:1 - Right of Appeal
- Interested party may appeal to the administrative court (bestuursrechter) of the Rechtbank
- Against the decision on bezwaar (or directly against the decision if Afdeling 3.4 preparation applied)

### Time Limit
- **6 weeks** from the decision on bezwaar

### Art. 8:69 - Scope of Review
- Court reviews the decision based on the grounds raised (gronden van beroep)
- Court must supplement legal grounds ex officio (ambtshalve aanvulling van rechtsgronden)
- Court generally limited to the facts as they were known to the administrative body

### Review Standards
The court tests the decision against:
1. **Legality** (rechtmatigheid): was the decision within the law?
2. **Procedural propriety**: were procedures correctly followed?
3. **Factual basis**: is the factual basis adequate?
4. **Proportionality and reasonableness**: especially for discretionary decisions (beleidsruimte)
5. **ABBB**: compliance with general principles of good governance

### Art. 8:72 - Court Remedies
The court may:
- **Declare the appeal well-founded** (gegrond verklaren)
- **Annul** the challenged decision (vernietigen)
- **Instruct** the body to make a new decision
- **Provide final resolution** (zelf in de zaak voorzien) - decide the matter itself
- **Order payment of damages**
- **Award costs** (proceskosten)

## Higher Appeal (Hoger beroep)

### To ABRvS (Afdeling bestuursrechtspraak van de Raad van State)
- General administrative law cases
- Spatial planning, environmental law
- Immigration law

### To CRvB (Centrale Raad van Beroep)
- Social security law (WW, WIA, Participatiewet, AOW)
- Civil servant law (ambtenarenzaken - though largely transferred to civil courts since WNRA 2020)

### To CBb (College van Beroep voor het bedrijfsleven)
- Economic administrative law
- Competition law (ACM decisions)
- Telecom and energy regulation
- Financial regulation (AFM, DNB decisions)

### Time Limit
- **6 weeks** from the Rechtbank judgment
