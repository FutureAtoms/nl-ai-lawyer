## Immigration Law Analysis

**Nationality**: Brazilian (third-country national, not MVV-exempt)
**Purpose of Stay**: Employment – Highly Skilled Migrant (Kennismigrant)
**Applicable Scheme**: Kennismigrantenregeling (Art. 3.30a Vreemdelingenbesluit 2000)

## Overview

A Brazilian national is classified as a **third-country national** under Dutch immigration law. Brazil is not on the list of MVV-exempt nationalities (which includes Australia, Canada, Japan, Monaco, New Zealand, South Korea, UK, USA, and Vatican City). This means the Brazilian software engineer will need to go through the **TEV procedure** (Toegang en Verblijf) – a combined application for both the machtiging tot voorlopig verblijf (MVV) and the residence permit (verblijfsvergunning bepaalde tijd, VVB).

The highly skilled migrant scheme (kennismigrantenregeling) is the fastest and most appropriate route, provided your company is a **recognized sponsor (erkend referent)** and the salary offered meets the applicable threshold.

## Step 1: Become a Recognized Sponsor (Erkend Referent)

**This is the first and most critical step – without recognized sponsor status, you cannot use the kennismigrant route.**

### Legal Basis
- **Art. 2c Vreemdelingenwet 2000 (Vw 2000)**: Recognized sponsor status is mandatory for the kennismigrant scheme.
- **Art. 2e Vw 2000**: Obligations of recognized sponsors.

### How to Become a Recognized Sponsor

If your company is not yet a recognized sponsor (erkend referent), you must apply to the **IND** (Immigratie- en Naturalisatiedienst):

- **Application**: Filed by the employer at the IND (online or by post)
- **Assessment criteria**: The IND assesses:
  - Financial continuity and solvency (financiële continuïteit)
  - Reliability (betrouwbaarheid) – no serious regulatory violations
  - Compliance history with immigration obligations
  - Genuine business activity (registered with KVK – Kamer van Koophandel)
- **Processing time**: Approximately **4–8 weeks**
- **Fee**: EUR 4,466–4,588 (2024/2025 rates; check ind.nl for current fees)
- **Validity**: Indefinite (subject to ongoing compliance obligations)
- **Public register**: Once recognized, your company appears in the IND's openbaar register erkende referenten

### Ongoing Sponsor Obligations (Art. 2e Vw 2000)

As a recognized sponsor, your company will have three ongoing obligations:
1. **Zorgplicht** (duty of care): Ensure the employee can meet the conditions of her residence permit throughout her stay.
2. **Informatieplicht** (duty to inform): Notify the IND within **4 weeks** of relevant changes, including termination of employment, salary falling below the threshold, or the employee's departure from the Netherlands.
3. **Administratieplicht** (duty of administration): Maintain records (employment contracts, payslips, identity copies) for **5 years** after the end of the sponsorship.

**Penalties for non-compliance**: Administrative fines (bestuurlijke boete) up to EUR 6,000 per violation; possible suspension or withdrawal of recognized sponsor status.

## Step 2: Salary Requirements

### Legal Basis
- **Art. 3.30a Vreemdelingenbesluit 2000 (Vb 2000)**: The salary threshold is the primary (and only) proxy for skill level – no degree or qualification is required.

### 2025 Salary Thresholds (gross monthly, excluding 8% holiday allowance)

> **Important**: These thresholds are indexed annually on 1 January. Always verify current values at **ind.nl** before making an offer.

| Orientation year graduates | EUR 2,887 | EUR 34,644 |
| European Blue Card | EUR 6,435 | EUR 77,220 |

**Which threshold applies to your candidate**:
- The age boundary is assessed at the **date of the IND application** (not date of hire).
- If she is under 30 at the time of the IND application: the reduced threshold of EUR 4,028/month applies.
- If she is 30 or older: the standard threshold of EUR 5,493/month applies.

### Important Salary Rules

- The threshold is **exclusive of the 8% holiday allowance (vakantiegeld)**. The IND adds 8% to the base salary when comparing against the threshold, so if you build in holiday allowance separately, factor this in.
- **Variable components** (bonuses, stock options, commissions) **do not count** toward the threshold (ABRvS 25 maart 2013, ECLI:NL:RVS:2013:BZ7441), unless they are contractually guaranteed.
- The salary must be **marktconform** (in line with the market for the role).
- If holiday allowance of 8% is contractually guaranteed and actually paid, it may be included (ABRvS 5 juni 2019, ECLI:NL:RVS:2019:1844).
- The salary must remain at or above the threshold throughout the permit's validity; if it drops, the sponsor must notify the IND within 4 weeks.

**Practical recommendation**: Offer at least EUR 5,500/month (age 30+) or EUR 4,100/month (under 30) in fixed gross salary to ensure a comfortable margin above the threshold.

## Step 3: The TEV Procedure (MVV + Residence Permit)

Since Brazil is **not** an MVV-exempt nationality, your candidate must go through the **TEV procedure** (machtiging tot voorlopig verblijf / Toegang en Verblijf).

### Legal Basis
- **Art. 2a Vw 2000**: The combined TEV procedure.
- **Art. 3.30a Vb 2000**: Conditions for the kennismigrant residence permit.
- **Art. 1d BuWav** (Besluit uitvoering Wav): Exemption from the separate work permit (TWV) requirement – the kennismigrant permit serves as both a residence and work permit.

### How the TEV Procedure Works

The TEV procedure is a **single combined application** that covers both:
1. The **MVV** (machtiging tot voorlopig verblijf) – the entry authorization collected at the Dutch embassy or consulate in Brazil, and
2. The **VVB** (verblijfsvergunning bepaalde tijd) – the residence permit issued upon arrival in the Netherlands.

**Procedure step by step:**

| Step | Action | Who | Timeframe |
|------|--------|-----|-----------|
| 1 | Your company (as erkend referent) files the TEV application online via the IND portal | Employer (recognized sponsor) | Day 1 |
| 2 | IND processes the application | IND | ~**2 weeks** (fast-track for recognized sponsors) |
| 3 | If approved: IND notifies employer; MVV authorization issued | IND | Within 2 weeks of approval |
| 4 | Candidate visits Dutch Embassy or Consulate General in Brazil to collect the MVV sticker in her passport | Candidate | Within **90 days** of MVV issuance |
| 5 | Candidate travels to the Netherlands (MVV is the entry visa) | Candidate | Within 90 days |
| 6 | Upon arrival: candidate collects residence permit (VVB) at IND desk appointment | Candidate | Shortly after arrival |
| 7 | Candidate registers with the gemeente (municipality) and obtains BSN (citizen service number) | Candidate | Shortly after arrival |

**Total realistic timeframe from IND filing to arrival**: approximately **4–8 weeks** (assuming recognized sponsor status is already in place, documents are complete, and the embassy appointment is obtained quickly). If recognized sponsor status needs to be obtained first, add **4–8 weeks** for that process.

## Rights and Obligations Under the Kennismigrant Permit

### What the Permit Allows
- Full-time employment with the sponsoring employer only (the permit is employer-specific)
- No separate work permit (TWV) required (Art. 1d BuWav exemption)
- Access to Dutch public services and healthcare (with health insurance)
- Travel freely within the Schengen Area

### What the Permit Does NOT Allow
- Working for a different employer without a new permit application
- Part-time work below the salary threshold (unless sponsor notifies IND)

### Candidate's Obligations
- Register with the gemeente and obtain a BSN number
- Take out Dutch health insurance (zorgverzekering) within 4 months of arrival
- Maintain the salary threshold throughout the permit's validity
- No civic integration (inburgering) requirement for kennismigranten

## Optional: The 30% Tax Ruling (30%-Regeling)

Many kennismigranten also qualify for the **30% ruling** under **Art. 31a lid 2 sub e Wet LB** – a tax benefit that allows the employer to reimburse up to 30% of the employee's salary tax-free as a deemed allowance for extraterritorial costs.

**Conditions (since 2024)**:
- Recruited from outside the Netherlands (or from more than 150 km from the Dutch border – Brazil clearly qualifies)
- Specific expertise scarce in the Dutch labor market
- Gross salary above the separate 30% ruling threshold (approximately EUR 46,107 in 2024 for standard; EUR 35,048 for under-30 with a Master's degree)
- The candidate must not have lived within 150 km of the Dutch border for more than 8 of the 24 months prior to the first Dutch employment day

**Important**: The 30% ruling is a **tax measure** administered by the Belastingdienst, not an immigration measure. For IND salary threshold purposes, the **full gross salary before the 30% deduction** is used. The ruling is applied separately from the IND process.

## Path to Permanent Residence

After **5 years of continuous lawful residence** in the Netherlands (on a non-temporary purpose, which the kennismigrant permit satisfies), the candidate can apply for:

- **Permanent residence permit (verblijfsvergunning voor onbepaalde tijd)** under Art. 21 Vw 2000, provided she has:
  - Passed the civic integration exam (inburgeringsexamen) or obtained an exemption
  - Sufficient and durable income
  - No public order (openbare orde) objections

- **EU Long-Term Resident status** (Art. 21 lid 1 sub b Vw 2000 / Directive 2003/109/EC), which also grants facilitated access to residence in other EU member states.

- **Dutch citizenship** (naturalization) under the Rijkswet op het Nederlanderschap (RWN) after 5 years, subject to additional conditions including civic integration and renunciation of Brazilian nationality (unless a treaty exemption applies).
