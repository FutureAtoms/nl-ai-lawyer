## Income Requirements

### Legal Basis
**Art. 3.22 Vb 2000** sets the income requirement for family reunification and family formation. The income must be:
- **Independent** (zelfstandig): from employment, business, or equivalent; social assistance (bijstand) does not count
- **Sufficient** (voldoende): meets the applicable threshold
- **Durable** (duurzaam): stable and continuing; employment contract of at least 1 year, or demonstrated stable self-employment income

### Income Threshold

For **family reunification** (gezinshereniging – married/registered partners), the income threshold is **100% of the applicable minimum wage (minimumloon)** (net):

| Reference | Amount (2024) |
|-----------|--------------|
| **Net minimum wage (netto minimumloon)** | Approximately **EUR 1,934.40 per month net** (2024) |
| (Gross equivalent, approximately) | EUR 2,100–2,200/month gross |

> **Important**: This threshold is adjusted periodically when the statutory minimum wage (wettelijk minimumloon) changes. Verify the current amount at **ind.nl** before applying.

For **family formation** (gezinsvorming – unmarried partners), the same 100% of minimumloon threshold applies under Dutch law post-Chakroun. The CJEU ruled in **CJEU 4 March 2010, C-578/08 (Chakroun)** that Member States may not systematically apply a higher threshold for family formation than for family reunification where the difference is not justified. The **ABRvS confirmed** in ABRvS 4 april 2012, ECLI:NL:RVS:2012:BW0791 that the IND must apply 100% of the minimum wage (not 120%) and must assess income on an individual basis.

### What Counts as Income
- Salary from employment (in the Netherlands)
- Self-employment income (if stable and sufficiently demonstrated)
- Social security benefits based on work contributions (e.g., WW – werkloosheidsuitkering), subject to conditions
- Does **not** include: general welfare (bijstand/TOZO/TONK), child benefit (kinderbijslag), income from a new partner's employment in most cases

### Durability Requirement
Your income must be **durable**: typically demonstrated by an employment contract of at least **1 year** or, for self-employed persons, by several years of declared income with a realistic prospect of continuation (ABRvS 26 maart 2014, ECLI:NL:RVS:2014:1145).

## MVV Requirement and Procedure

### Japan is MVV-Exempt

Japanese nationals are on the **MVV-exempt nationalities list** (Art. 17 Vw 2000 jo. Art. 3.1 Voorschrift Vreemdelingen 2000). This means your partner:

- Can **enter the Netherlands without an MVV** on a Japanese passport (for up to 90 days in the Schengen Area)
- Can **apply for the residence permit (VVB) directly from within the Netherlands**, without first returning to Japan

### Procedure Options

**Option A: Apply from within the Netherlands (most practical for Japanese nationals)**

1. Partner travels to the Netherlands on their Japanese passport (visa-free entry up to 90 days Schengen)
2. You (the sponsor/Dutch citizen) file the residence permit application (VVB) at the IND on behalf of your partner, or jointly
3. IND processes the application (standard processing time: **90 days**)
4. If approved: residence permit sticker/card issued
5. Partner can legally remain in the Netherlands during processing if the application is filed within the 90-day Schengen stay (or if they already have legal basis to stay)

**Option B: TEV Procedure from Japan**

1. File the TEV application at the IND (combined MVV + VVB application)
2. IND processes simultaneously: approximately **90 days** (standard; not fast-tracked as this is not a kennismigrant application)
3. If approved: MVV collected at Dutch Embassy/Consulate in Japan
4. Travel to Netherlands within 90 days of MVV issuance
5. VVB residence permit issued upon arrival

For a Japanese national, **Option A is generally simpler and avoids the need to return to Japan** during the process.

## Civic Integration Abroad (Basisexamen Inburgering Buitenland)

### What is the basisexamen inburgering buitenland?

Under the Wet inburgering 2021 (Wi 2021) and Art. 3.71a Vb 2000, most partners from outside the EU applying for family reunification must **pass a basic civic integration exam abroad** (basisexamen inburgering buitenland) before the MVV (or, in the case of MVV-exempt nationals, before certain applications) is approved.

The exam tests:
- Basic Dutch language knowledge (A1 level)
- Basic knowledge of Dutch society (kennis van de Nederlandse samenleving)
- Practical readiness (oriëntatie op de Nederlandse arbeidsmarkt – for some categories)

The exam is taken at a Dutch embassy or consulate abroad (in Japan: Dutch embassy in Tokyo).

### Is Your Japanese Partner Required to Take the Exam?

**This depends on your relationship status and the applicable exemptions.**

Japanese nationals are **not automatically exempt** from the basisexamen. The following exemptions may apply:

| Exemption Category | Basis |
|---|---|
| Holding a recognized diploma in Dutch (e.g., NT2 certificate) | Art. 3.71b Vb |
| Demonstrable medical or psychological inability | Art. 3.71c Vb |
| If the sponsor is a Dutch citizen and the family reunification falls under EU law provisions (often does NOT apply to Dutch citizens sponsoring third-country nationals in purely domestic situations) | Directive 2003/86/EC limitations |
| Orientation year or certain academic qualifications | Policy Vc |

In most cases involving a Japanese partner of a Dutch citizen (in a domestic, non-free movement situation), **the basisexamen inburgering buitenland will be required** before the residence permit is granted, unless an exemption applies.

**Practical impact**: Your partner should start preparing for and booking the basisexamen inburgering at the Dutch embassy in Tokyo early, as appointment availability can vary. Information is available at **ind.nl** and at the Dutch embassy website.

### Post-Arrival Civic Integration (Inburgering)

Upon arrival in the Netherlands, your partner will also be subject to the full **inburgeringsplicht** (civic integration obligation) under the Wet inburgering 2021:

- Must complete one of three learning routes (B1-route, Onderwijsroute, or Zelfredzaamheidsroute)
- Must be completed within **3 years** of the start of the obligation (extendable under certain circumstances)
- Failure to complete within the deadline can result in fines
- Completion of inburgering is required for a permanent residence permit

## Path to Permanent Residence and Dutch Citizenship

### Permanent Residence (Art. 21 Vw 2000)
After 5 years of continuous lawful residence on the family reunification permit:
- Passed inburgeringsexamen (or obtained exemption)
- Sufficient and durable income
- No public order objections

### Dutch Citizenship (Naturalization – Rijkswet op het Nederlanderschap)
- After **5 years** of continuous lawful residence in the Netherlands (the regular route)
- **Shorter route for partners of Dutch citizens**: After **3 years** of lawful residence AND **3 years** of durable and exclusive partnership (geregistreerd or proven) with a Dutch citizen – Art. 8 lid 1 sub b RWN
- Must pass the inburgeringsexamen (or be exempt)
- Standard requirement: renounce Japanese nationality (Japan generally does not allow dual nationality for adults who acquired foreign nationality voluntarily, though exceptions exist)

## Potential Issues and Risks

1. **Income below threshold**: If your income does not consistently meet 100% of the net minimumloon, the application may be refused. Ensure you have at least a 1-year employment contract and stable income at or above the threshold.
2. **Relationship evidence**: For unmarried partners, extensive evidence of a durable and exclusive relationship is required; insufficient evidence is a common cause of refusal.
3. **Civic integration exam failure or delay**: If the basisexamen inburgering is not yet passed (and no exemption applies), the application will not be approved. Plan ahead.
4. **Processing delays at the IND**: The IND frequently faces backlogs; the 90-day statutory decision period is often exceeded. Consider filing a dwangsom (penalty notice) if the IND misses its deadline (Art. 4:17 Awb; ABRvS 30 mei 2018, ECLI:NL:RVS:2018:1778).
5. **Public order**: Any criminal history in Japan or elsewhere can affect eligibility.
