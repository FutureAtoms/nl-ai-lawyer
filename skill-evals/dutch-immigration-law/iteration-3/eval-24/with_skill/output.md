## Overview

As a Dutch citizen, you have the right to bring your Japanese partner to the Netherlands through the family reunification (gezinshereniging) route. Because you are Dutch (not merely a long-term EU resident), the applicable framework is primarily Dutch national immigration law under the Vreemdelingenwet 2000 and Vreemdelingenbesluit 2000, rather than the EU Free Movement Directive 2004/38/EC (which applies to EU citizens exercising free movement rights, not to Dutch citizens living in their own country).

Importantly, Japan is on the **MVV-exempt list** (Art. 17 Vw 2000 jo. Art. 3.1 VV 2000), which means your partner does not need to obtain an MVV before entering the Netherlands. However, for family reunification, the TEV procedure (combined entry and residence permit) is the standard route, and Japanese nationals must still apply for a residence permit — they are simply exempt from the prior entry visa requirement for the MVV itself.

## Income Requirements (Inkomenseis)

### Legal Basis: Art. 3.22 Vb 2000

The Dutch sponsor (you, as a Dutch citizen) must demonstrate sufficient, independent, and durable income (zelfstandig, voldoende, duurzaam inkomen) at the level of **100% of the net minimum wage (netto minimumloon)**.

### 2024 Income Threshold

- **Minimum required**: 100% of the net minimum wage
- **Approximate amount**: EUR 1,934.40 per month net (2024 figure — verify annually at ind.nl or rijksoverheid.nl)
- The income must be **net** (after tax and social security deductions)

### Important Income Rules

**What counts as income:**
- Employment income (loon uit dienstbetrekking) — must be from a permanent contract (vaste arbeidsovereenkomst) or a temporary contract with a minimum 1-year remaining duration at the time of application
- Self-employment income (inkomsten uit zelfstandige arbeid) — the IND assesses self-employed income based on average income over the prior 3 years; the ABRvS has confirmed that projected future income can qualify if stable past income is demonstrated (ABRvS 26 maart 2014, ECLI:NL:RVS:2014:1145)
- Social benefits (uitkeringen) — certain benefits count (e.g., WAO/WIA disability payments) but not unemployment benefits (WW) or social assistance (bijstand)

**What does NOT count:**
- Holiday allowance (vakantiegeld) is generally excluded from the net calculation
- Variable income components (bonuses, commissions) are assessed with caution
- Income from a second job on a zero-hours contract (nulurencontract) may not be considered durable

**Durability requirement:**
- Employment income: contract must have at least 1 year remaining, or be an indefinite contract (arbeidsovereenkomst voor onbepaalde tijd)
- Self-employed: typically assessed over 3 years of demonstrated income

### Impact of Chakroun Ruling (CJEU C-578/08)

In its landmark ruling of 4 March 2010, the CJEU ruled that the Netherlands could not apply a higher income threshold for family formation (gezinsvorming — new relationships) compared to family reunification (gezinshereniging — existing families). This decision directly influenced Dutch practice, as the ABRvS confirmed in ABRvS 4 april 2012 (ECLI:NL:RVS:2012:BW0791): the IND must apply 100% of the minimum wage (not 120%) and must assess income on an individual basis, considering the stability and regularity of income, not just the absolute amount at the time of application.

**Note**: Since you are a Dutch citizen (not an EU citizen exercising free movement), EU Directive 2003/86/EC (the Family Reunification Directive) applies to your situation. The Chakroun precedent remains directly relevant.

## MVV Requirement — Japan Is Exempt

Japan is on the MVV-exempt list under Dutch law. This means your partner does **not** need to collect an MVV sticker at the Dutch embassy in Tokyo before traveling to the Netherlands. However, a residence permit (verblijfsvergunning voor bepaalde tijd — VVB) is still required for stays exceeding 90 days.

### Procedure for MVV-Exempt Nationals Applying for Family Reunification

**Two possible routes:**

**Route A — TEV Application (from abroad)**
1. File the TEV application with the IND in the Netherlands (you, as sponsor, file on behalf of your partner)
2. IND assesses the application (standard processing time: 90 days — family reunification is **not** subject to the 2-week recognized sponsor fast-track)
3. If approved, your partner can travel to the Netherlands and collect the VVB at an IND desk appointment

**Route B — Apply After Arrival (direct VVB application)**
Since Japan is MVV-exempt, your partner can enter the Netherlands as a tourist/visa-free visitor (90 days in the Schengen Area) and apply for the residence permit from within the Netherlands. However, this approach carries risks:
- The 90-day tourist admission must not be exceeded before the permit decision
- If the IND does not decide within 90 days, your partner may need to leave pending a decision
- It is generally safer and more structured to use the TEV route from Japan

**Recommended approach**: File the TEV application from the Netherlands while your partner is still in Japan, so the process is underway before they travel.

## Civic Integration Abroad (Basisexamen Inburgering Buitenland)

**This is a critical point that many applicants are not aware of.**

Under the Dutch civic integration requirement for family migration, certain nationalities must pass a **basisexamen inburgering buitenland** (basic civic integration exam abroad) before a residence permit for family reunification can be granted.

### Does this apply to Japanese nationals?

Japan is **not** on the list of exempted countries (the exemption applies primarily to EU/EEA/Swiss nationals, US, Canada, Australia, New Zealand, Japan, South Korea, and a small number of others). However, **Japan IS on the exemption list**. Japanese nationals are exempt from the basisexamen inburgering buitenland requirement.

This means your partner does **not** need to pass the integration exam abroad before coming to the Netherlands.

### Civic Integration in the Netherlands (Wet inburgering 2021)

After arriving and receiving a residence permit, your partner **will** be subject to the civic integration obligation under the Wet inburgering 2021 (Wi 2021). This requires:
- Completing one of three learning routes: B1-route (language), Onderwijsroute (education route), or Zelfredzaamheidsroute (self-sufficiency route)
- Passing the civic integration exam (inburgeringsexamen) within 3 years of receiving the residence permit (extendable under certain conditions)
- Failure to complete inburgering can result in fines (bestuurlijke boete) and may affect the application for a permanent residence permit

**Practical note**: Your partner will need to register for inburgering with the gemeente and may be eligible for a taallening (language loan) to finance the courses.

## Duration of the Permit and Path to Permanent Residence

### Initial Permit
- The first family reunification permit is valid for **1 year**
- It is then extendable in 5-year increments, provided the relationship continues and the income requirement is still met

### Conditions for Continuation
- The genuine relationship must continue (if the relationship ends, the permit may be affected)
- Your income must remain at or above the threshold
- Your partner must remain in compliance with the residence conditions and civic integration obligations

### Independent Residence Rights
- After **5 years** of lawful residence on a family reunification permit, your partner may qualify for independent residence rights, even if the relationship ends (Art. 3.51 Vb 2000 — verblijf als niet-geprivilegieerde vreemdeling)
- After 5 years: eligible for a permanent residence permit (verblijfsvergunning voor onbepaalde tijd — VVO) under Art. 21 Vw 2000, subject to civic integration exam completion and income requirements

### Citizenship
After 5 years of lawful residence, your partner may apply for Dutch citizenship by naturalization (Rijkswet op het Nederlanderschap), subject to civic integration, income, and public order requirements. Alternatively, if married to you as a Dutch citizen, naturalization may be possible after 3 years of lawful residence if the couple has been living together for the same period.
