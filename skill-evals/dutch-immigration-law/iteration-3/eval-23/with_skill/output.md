# Immigration Law Analysis

**Nationality**: Brazilian (third-country national)
**Purpose of Stay**: Work - Highly Skilled Migrant (Kennismigrant)
**Applicable Scheme**: Kennismigrantenregeling (Art. 3.30a Vb 2000)

---

## Overview

Hiring a Brazilian software engineer as a kennismigrant is a well-established route under Dutch immigration law. Brazil is not on the MVV-exempt list, which means the engineer must go through the full TEV procedure (Toegang en Verblijf — combined entry visa and residence permit procedure). The good news is that for recognized sponsors (erkend referenten), the IND processes kennismigrant applications within approximately two weeks, making this one of the fastest routes for skilled workers from outside the EU/EEA.

---

## Step 1: Become a Recognized Sponsor (Erkend Referent)

**This is the first and most critical step.** Under Art. 2c Vreemdelingenwet 2000 (Vw 2000), the employer must be a recognized sponsor with the IND before any kennismigrant application can be filed.

### How to Apply for Recognized Sponsor Status

- **Where**: File with the IND (Immigratie- en Naturalisatiedienst) — ind.nl
- **Assessment criteria**: The IND evaluates the employer's continuity (financial stability), solvency, reliability, and compliance history
- **Processing time**: Approximately 4–8 weeks
- **Fee**: EUR 4,588 (2024/2025 rates — verify at ind.nl as fees are periodically adjusted)
- **Validity**: Once granted, recognized sponsor status is valid indefinitely, subject to ongoing compliance obligations
- **Public register**: The employer appears in the IND's openbaar register erkende referenten (public register of recognized sponsors)

### Ongoing Obligations of the Recognized Sponsor (Art. 2e Vw 2000)

Once recognized, the employer assumes three statutory duties:

1. **Zorgplicht (duty of care)**: Ensure the migrant can meet the conditions of their residence permit throughout their stay
2. **Informatieplicht (duty to inform)**: Notify the IND within 4 weeks of relevant changes, including:
   - Termination of employment
   - Salary falling below the threshold
   - The employee's departure from the Netherlands
3. **Administratieplicht (duty of administration)**: Maintain records (employment contracts, payslips, identity documents) for 5 years after the sponsorship ends

**Non-compliance penalties**: Up to EUR 6,000 per violation (bestuurlijke boete), plus risk of suspension or withdrawal of recognized sponsor status.

## Step 2: Salary Threshold — What to Offer

The salary criterion is the central requirement of the kennismigrant scheme. The IND does not require a specific degree or qualification — the salary level acts as the sole proxy for "highly skilled" status.

### 2025 Salary Thresholds (gross monthly, excluding 8% holiday allowance)

| Category | Monthly Gross | Annual Gross (approx.) |
|----------|--------------|------------------------|
| Standard (age 30+) | EUR 5,493 | EUR 65,916 |
| Reduced (age under 30) | EUR 4,028 | EUR 48,336 |
| EU Blue Card | EUR 6,435 | EUR 77,220 |

**Important salary rules:**

- The threshold is exclusive of the statutory 8% holiday allowance (vakantiegeld). The IND adds 8% when comparing the employee's total contractual salary to the threshold.
- Variable components (bonuses, commissions, profit sharing) do **not** count toward the threshold unless they are guaranteed in the contract.
- The salary must be **marktconform** (in accordance with market rates for the role).
- The relevant age threshold is assessed at the date of the IND application (ABRvS 18 juli 2018, ECLI:NL:RVS:2018:2410).
- If the salary drops below the threshold after admission, the sponsor must notify the IND within 4 weeks; long-term non-compliance can lead to withdrawal (intrekking) of the permit (ABRvS 11 december 2019, ECLI:NL:RVS:2019:4165).

**Practical recommendation for your case**: Verify the engineer's age. If she is 30 or older, you must offer at least EUR 5,493/month gross (2025 rate). If she is under 30, the reduced threshold of EUR 4,028/month applies. Always check ind.nl for the most current rates, as thresholds are adjusted annually on 1 January.

## Step 3: The TEV Procedure (Toegang en Verblijf — MVV + VVB)

Since Brazil is **not** on the MVV-exempt list (Art. 17 Vw 2000 jo. Art. 3.1 VV 2000), the engineer must obtain a Machtiging tot Voorlopig Verblijf (MVV) — authorization for temporary stay — before entering the Netherlands. The MVV and residence permit (VVB) application are combined into a single TEV procedure under Art. 2a Vw 2000.

### Full Step-by-Step Process

**Phase A — In the Netherlands (employer/sponsor)**

1. **Obtain recognized sponsor status** (if not already): File application with IND; allow 4–8 weeks.
2. **Prepare employment contract**: Signed offer specifying gross monthly salary, job title, working hours, and duration.
3. **File the TEV application at IND**: The recognized sponsor files on behalf of the employee. Required documents include:
   - Signed application form
   - Copy of the employment contract
   - Employer statement/annexes (as prescribed by IND)
   - Proof of recognized sponsor status
4. **IND assessment**: For recognized sponsors, the IND targets a processing time of approximately **2 weeks** (compared to 90 days for non-recognized sponsors).
5. **IND decision**: If approved, the IND issues an MVV authorization and a provisional VVB decision simultaneously.

**Phase B — In Brazil (employee)**

6. **Collect MVV at Dutch embassy/consulate**: The employee must appear in person at a Dutch diplomatic mission in Brazil (typically Brasília or São Paulo). She will need:
   - Valid passport (minimum 6 months validity beyond intended stay)
   - Passport photo(s)
   - Antecedents certificate (VOG from Brazilian authorities, if required)
   - TB test certificate (depending on IND requirements for Brazilian nationals)
   - Confirmation of the IND MVV approval
7. **MVV validity**: The MVV is valid for 90 days. The employee must enter the Netherlands within this period.

**Phase C — Arrival in the Netherlands**

8. **Entry to the Netherlands**: Travel on the MVV sticker.
9. **VVB collection**: Upon arrival, the employee collects the physical verblijfsvergunning (VVB — residence permit card) at an IND desk appointment. The permit is usually issued immediately or within a few days of the appointment.
10. **Gemeente registration (BSN)**: The employee must register with the municipality (gemeente) of residence. This provides a BSN (burgerservicenummer — citizen service number), required for tax, social security, and banking purposes. Registration is free.
11. **Health insurance (zorgverzekering)**: The employee must take out a Dutch health insurance policy from day one of residence. Annual cost is approximately EUR 1,500–2,400 depending on the insurer and coverage.

## Duration and Validity of the Permit

- **Initial permit**: Valid for the duration of the employment contract, up to a maximum of 5 years. For indefinite-duration contracts: 5 years.
- **Extension (verlenging)**: Must be filed before expiry. Same salary threshold must still be met. Processing time via recognized sponsor: approximately 2 weeks.
- **Change of employer**: If the engineer later moves to a different employer, that new employer must also be a recognized sponsor and a new application must be filed. She may continue working pending the new decision if filed on time.

---

## Path to Permanent Residence and Citizenship

- **Permanent residence (VVO)**: After 5 years of continuous lawful residence on a non-temporary purpose (kennismigrant qualifies), subject to passing the civic integration exam (inburgeringsexamen) or obtaining an exemption, and meeting income requirements (Art. 21 Vw 2000).
- **EU Long-Term Resident status**: After 5 years, the engineer may also apply for EU long-term resident status (Art. 21 lid 1 sub b Vw / Directive 2003/109/EC), conferring rights to move to other EU Member States under facilitated conditions.
- **Dutch citizenship (naturalization)**: Possible after 5 years of lawful residence, subject to civic integration, income, and public order requirements (Rijkswet op het Nederlanderschap).

## Additional Considerations

### 30% Tax Ruling (30%-Regeling)
Many kennismigranten qualify for the 30% ruling (Art. 31a lid 2 sub e Wet LB), which allows the employer to reimburse up to 30% of the gross salary tax-free. This is administered by the Belastingdienst (Tax Authority), not the IND, and has separate eligibility criteria. The engineer must have been recruited from abroad and not lived within 150 km of the Dutch border for more than 8 of the prior 24 months. Note that for IND salary threshold purposes, the **full gross salary before the 30% ruling** is used — not the taxable portion.

### Family Members
If the engineer wishes to bring family members (spouse/partner, minor children), they can be included in the same TEV application. Family members receive permits of the same duration and the spouse/partner gains unrestricted access to the Dutch labor market.

### No Work Permit (TWV) Required
Kennismigranten are exempt from the requirement to obtain a separate work permit (tewerkstellingsvergunning — TWV) under Art. 1d Besluit uitvoering Wav (BuWav). The IND kennismigrant procedure covers both residence and work authorization.
