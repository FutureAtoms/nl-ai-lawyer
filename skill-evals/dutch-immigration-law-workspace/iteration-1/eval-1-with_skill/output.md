Your company must be registered as a **recognized sponsor (erkend referent)** with the IND before you can apply for a kennismigrant residence permit. This is a mandatory prerequisite under Art. 2c Vw 2000.

**Sponsor obligations once recognized (Art. 2e Vw 2000):**
- **Zorgplicht** (duty of care): ensure the migrant can meet residence conditions
- **Informatieplicht** (duty to inform): notify IND of relevant changes within 4 weeks (e.g., termination, salary changes below threshold)
- **Administratieplicht** (duty of administration): maintain records for 5 years after end of sponsorship

## Application Procedure: Full Process from Application to Arrival

### Step 1: Become a Recognized Sponsor (if not already)
- File application at IND with supporting documents
- Processing: 4-8 weeks
- Fee: EUR 4,466

### Step 2: File TEV Application (Toegang en Verblijf)

Since Brazil is **not** on the MVV-exempt list, a combined **TEV procedure** applies. This combines the MVV (Machtiging tot Voorlopig Verblijf - entry visa) and VVB (verblijfsvergunning voor bepaalde tijd - temporary residence permit) into a single application (Art. 2a Vw 2000).

- **Who applies**: The recognized sponsor (your company) files the application at the IND in the Netherlands
- **Required documents from sponsor**: Signed application form, copy of employment contract, employer statement, proof of recognized sponsor status
- **Required documents from the migrant**: Valid passport (minimum 6 months validity), passport photo, antecedents certificate (may be required), TB test certificate (may be required for Brazilian nationals)
- **Processing time**: Approximately **2 weeks** for recognized sponsors (fast-track)
- **Application fee**: EUR 210

### Step 3: MVV Collection at Embassy

Upon IND approval:
- The employee collects the **MVV sticker** at the Dutch embassy or consulate in Brazil (e.g., Brasilia, Sao Paulo, or Rio de Janeiro)
- The MVV is a visa sticker placed in the passport
- The employee must enter the Netherlands within **90 days** of MVV issuance

### Step 4: Arrival in the Netherlands

Upon arrival in the Netherlands:
- The employee picks up the **residence permit (VVB)** at an IND desk appointment
- The employee registers at the municipality (gemeente) to obtain a BSN (burgerservicenummer)
- The employee can begin working immediately upon arrival with the MVV

### Step 5: Biometrics and Residence Permit Card

- The employee attends a biometrics appointment at the IND
- The residence permit card (verblijfsdocument) is issued and mailed to the home address

**Obligations:**

## Duration and Renewal

- **Initial permit**: Valid for the duration of the employment contract, maximum 5 years
- **Extension**: File before expiry; same threshold applies at time of extension; ~2 weeks processing
- **Change of employer**: New employer must also be a recognized sponsor; new application required

## Path to Permanent Residence / Citizenship

- **Permanent residence**: After 5 years of continuous lawful residence, subject to passing the civic integration exam (inburgeringsexamen), sufficient income, and no public order objections
- **EU Long-term Resident status**: Also available after 5 years (Directive 2003/109/EC)
- **Dutch citizenship**: Possible after 5 years of uninterrupted residence and passing the naturalization requirements (RWN)

## Additional Consideration: 30% Ruling

Your employee may qualify for the **30% ruling** (30%-regeling), a tax benefit allowing up to 30% of salary to be paid tax-free as a deemed extraterritorial cost allowance. This is a separate application to the Belastingdienst (not IND) with its own conditions, including that the employee was recruited from abroad and lived more than 150 km from the Dutch border for at least 16 of the 24 months prior to employment.
