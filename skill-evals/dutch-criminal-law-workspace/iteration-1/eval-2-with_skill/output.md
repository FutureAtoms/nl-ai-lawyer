# With-Skill Response: Dutch Criminal Law - Test 2

**Prompt:** "An employee reported that our factory might be violating environmental waste disposal regulations. Could this lead to criminal prosecution under Dutch law?"

---

## Criminal Law Analysis

**Topic**: Potential criminal prosecution for environmental waste disposal violations
**Applicable Law**: Wet op de Economische Delicten (WED), Wet milieubeheer (Wm), Omgevingswet, Wetboek van Strafrecht (Sr), Art. 51 Sr (corporate criminal liability)

## Legal Framework

### Environmental Violations as Criminal Offenses

Environmental violations in the Netherlands are primarily prosecuted through the **Wet op de Economische Delicten (WED)** framework. The WED does not define offenses itself but designates violations of specific environmental laws as economic criminal offenses with their own sentencing regime.

### Key Environmental Legislation

1. **Wet milieubeheer (Wm)** - Environmental Management Act:
   - Regulates waste management (afvalbeheer), including the collection, transport, processing, and disposal of waste
   - Chapter 10 (Titel 10.1-10.9): Comprehensive waste regulation
   - Violations are listed in **Art. 1a sub 1 WED** (most serious category)

2. **Omgevingswet** (since 1 January 2024):
   - Replaced parts of the Wm and other environmental legislation
   - Environmental permits (omgevingsvergunningen) for environmentally harmful activities
   - Violations of permit conditions are criminal offenses under the WED framework

3. **Besluit activiteiten leefomgeving (Bal)** - Activities Decree:
   - Detailed rules for specific activities, including waste processing and disposal
   - Violations of the Bal requirements are punishable through the WED

4. **EU Waste Framework Directive (2008/98/EC)** and **EU Directive on the Protection of the Environment through Criminal Law (2008/99/EC)**:
   - Dutch law implements EU environmental criminal law obligations
   - Serious environmental offenses can be subject to harmonized EU standards

### Classification Under WED

Environmental offenses under the Wm and Omgevingswet are typically classified in **Art. 1a sub 1 WED** (most serious economic offenses):

| If committed... | Classification | Maximum Imprisonment | Maximum Fine |
|----------------|---------------|---------------------|-------------|
| Intentionally (opzettelijk) | Misdrijf (felony) | **6 years** | 5th category (EUR 90,000) or **10% annual turnover** |
| Without intent (niet-opzettelijk) | Overtreding (misdemeanor) | **1 year** detention | 4th category (EUR 22,500) |

The distinction between opzet (intent) and schuld (negligence) is critical: intentional violations are prosecuted as felonies with much higher penalties.

### Art. 173a-173b Sr - Environmental Pollution Offenses

In addition to WED offenses, the Wetboek van Strafrecht contains specific environmental pollution offenses:

- **Art. 173a Sr**: Intentionally and unlawfully releasing substances into the soil, water, or air, creating danger to public health or risk of death -- **maximum 12 years imprisonment**
- **Art. 173b Sr**: Negligently (culpoos) causing the same -- **maximum 1 year imprisonment or 4th category fine**

These are direct criminal code offenses (not WED) and apply to the most serious cases of environmental pollution.

### Corporate Criminal Liability (Art. 51 Sr)

Your factory (as a legal entity -- BV, NV, etc.) can be criminally prosecuted for environmental offenses under Art. 51 Sr:

- **The company itself** can be prosecuted as the perpetrator
- **Those who ordered** the violations (opdracht geven) can be prosecuted
- **Those who had de facto control** (feitelijke leiding geven) over the violations can be prosecuted

For environmental offenses at a factory, corporate criminal liability is readily established because:
- The waste disposal activities are core business operations (within the sfeer van de rechtspersoon)
- The company has the power to ensure compliance (beschikkingsmacht)
- If adequate compliance measures were lacking, the company is considered to have "accepted" the violations (aanvaarding / IJzerdraad criteria)

## Can This Lead to Criminal Prosecution?

**Yes, absolutely.** Environmental waste disposal violations can and do lead to criminal prosecution in the Netherlands. The answer depends on the nature and severity of the violation:

### Scenarios and Likelihood of Criminal Prosecution

| Violation Type | Likelihood | Typical Response |
|---------------|-----------|-----------------|
| Minor administrative violations (e.g., incorrect waste registration) | Low | Administrative enforcement (last onder dwangsom/bestuursdwang) by the environmental authority |
| Systematic violations of permit conditions | Medium-High | Criminal prosecution possible, especially if intentional |
| Illegal waste dumping or discharge | High | Criminal prosecution likely |
| Causing soil or water contamination | High | Criminal prosecution + administrative enforcement |
| Serious health risks to employees or the public | Very High | Criminal prosecution almost certain |

### Enforcement Landscape

Environmental enforcement in the Netherlands involves both **administrative** and **criminal** tracks:

**Administrative enforcement** (bestuursrechtelijke handhaving):
- Carried out by the gemeente, province, or Omgevingsdienst (regional environmental service)
- Instruments: last onder dwangsom (penalty order), last onder bestuursdwang (administrative enforcement), intrekking vergunning (permit revocation)
- Applies the **beginselplicht tot handhaving** (principle of mandatory enforcement)

**Criminal enforcement** (strafrechtelijke handhaving):
- Investigated by:
  - **Politie** (police) - for standard cases
  - **ILT** (Inspectie Leefomgeving en Transport) - specialized environmental inspectorate
  - **Bijzondere opsporingsdiensten** (special investigation services)
- Prosecuted by the **Functioneel Parket** (specialized prosecution service) of the OM

**Una via principle** (Art. 5:44 Awb): If the administrative authority imposes an administrative fine (bestuurlijke boete) for a violation, the OM cannot also criminally prosecute for the same violation (and vice versa). However, administrative enforcement measures (last onder dwangsom, bestuursdwang) are NOT administrative fines and do NOT prevent criminal prosecution.

### Sanctions for Environmental Crimes

If your company is convicted:

1. **Fines**: Up to EUR 900,000 (6th category) or **10% of annual turnover** for WED offenses -- whichever is higher
2. **Imprisonment**: Directors or managers with de facto control can face up to **6 years imprisonment** (for intentional WED misdrijven) or up to **12 years** (for Art. 173a Sr offenses)
3. **Stillegging van de onderneming** (Art. 7 WED): The court can order the **closure of the enterprise** for up to 1 year
4. **Confiscation** of objects related to the offense
5. **Publication of the judgment** at the company's expense -- significant reputational damage
6. **Onttrekking aan het verkeer**: Removal from commerce of harmful substances
7. **Remediation obligations**: Administrative orders to clean up contamination (sanering)
8. **Civil liability**: Third parties (including government bodies) can claim damages for environmental damage

## The Employee Report (Whistleblower Considerations)

The employee who reported the potential violation may be protected under:

- **Wet bescherming klokkenluiders (2023)** (Whistleblower Protection Act, implementing EU Directive 2019/1937):
  - Employees who report suspected wrongdoing in good faith are protected against retaliation
  - The company must have an internal reporting procedure (interne meldregeling)
  - Retaliation against the reporting employee (e.g., dismissal, demotion, harassment) is prohibited and can result in additional liability

- **It is important NOT to retaliate** against the reporting employee. Doing so is itself a violation of the Wet bescherming klokkenluiders and could create additional legal exposure.

## Recommendations

1. **Take the report seriously**: Investigate the employee's report promptly and thoroughly through an internal investigation
2. **Engage environmental law counsel**: Consult both an environmental law specialist (milieurecht/omgevingsrecht) and a criminal defense lawyer (strafrechtadvocaat) with experience in environmental criminal law
3. **Assess the scope of violations**: Determine what waste disposal regulations may be violated, the duration and severity of the violations, and any resulting environmental damage
4. **Review permits and compliance**: Check your omgevingsvergunning (environmental permit) and all applicable waste management regulations against actual practices
5. **Consider self-reporting**: In some cases, voluntarily reporting the violation to the competent authority (bevoegd gezag) and taking immediate corrective action can mitigate criminal liability. Discuss this strategy with your lawyer before taking action.
6. **Protect the whistleblower**: Ensure the reporting employee is protected from retaliation in compliance with the Wet bescherming klokkenluiders
7. **Implement corrective measures**: If violations are confirmed, take immediate remedial action (stop the violations, clean up contamination, correct waste management procedures)
8. **Document everything**: Maintain detailed records of the investigation, corrective actions, and communications with authorities
9. **Prepare for potential investigation**: If the violation is serious, be prepared for inspections by the Omgevingsdienst, ILT, or police. Do not destroy evidence.
