# With-Skill Response: Dutch Criminal Law - Test 1

**Prompt:** "Our company's financial director is suspected of submitting false invoices (valsheid in geschrift). The FIOD has raided our offices. What are the company's potential criminal liability risks?"

---

## Criminal Law Analysis

**Topic**: Corporate criminal liability for valsheid in geschrift (forgery/document fraud), FIOD investigation
**Applicable Law**: Wetboek van Strafrecht (Sr), Art. 225 (valsheid in geschrift), Art. 51 (corporate criminal liability); potentially Wet op de Economische Delicten (WED); Wetboek van Strafvordering (Sv)

## Legal Framework

### Art. 225 Sr - Valsheid in Geschrift (Forgery of Documents)

**Art. 225 lid 1 Sr** - Creating a false document or falsifying an authentic document:
- Creating a document that purports to be genuine (valselijk opmaken), OR
- Falsifying an existing authentic document (vervalsen)
- With the intent that the document be used as genuine (met het oogmerk om het als echt en onvervalst te gebruiken of door anderen te doen gebruiken)
- **Maximum penalty**: 6 years imprisonment or a fine of the 5th category (EUR 90,000)

**Art. 225 lid 2 Sr** - Intentional use of a false document:
- Intentionally using a false or falsified document as if it were genuine (opzettelijk gebruik maken van een vals geschrift als ware het echt)
- **Maximum penalty**: 6 years imprisonment or a fine of the 5th category

Submitting false invoices (valse facturen) is a classic example of valsheid in geschrift. The false invoices constitute "geschriften" (documents) within the meaning of Art. 225 Sr, and their submission for payment or accounting purposes constitutes "gebruik maken" (use) of false documents.

### Art. 51 Sr - Corporate Criminal Liability

This is the central provision for your company's criminal exposure:

**Art. 51 lid 1 Sr**: Criminal offenses can be committed by both natural persons **and legal entities** (rechtspersonen). Your company (as a BV, NV, or other legal entity) can itself be a suspect and be prosecuted for criminal offenses.

**Art. 51 lid 2 Sr**: If an offense is committed by a legal entity, criminal proceedings can be brought against:
1. **The legal entity itself** (de rechtspersoon)
2. **Those who ordered** the commission of the offense (degenen die opdracht hebben gegeven), AND/OR
3. **Those who had de facto control** (feitelijke leiding) over the prohibited conduct (degenen die feitelijke leiding hebben gegeven aan de verboden gedraging)

The prosecution can choose to prosecute **any combination** of these three categories simultaneously.

### Attribution of Conduct to the Legal Entity (Drijfmest Criteria)

Under the landmark Drijfmest arrest (HR 21 October 2003, ECLI:NL:HR:2003:AF7938), a criminal offense is attributed to a legal entity if the conduct can be said to have taken place within the **sphere of the legal entity** (in de sfeer van de rechtspersoon). Relevant factors include:

1. **Employee/agent conduct**: The person who committed the offense was an employee, officer, or agent of the company (the financial director qualifies)
2. **Business interest**: The conduct served the company's interests or fell within its business operations (false invoices typically relate to the company's financial operations)
3. **Power and control** (beschikkingsmacht): The company had the power to determine whether the conduct would or would not take place
4. **Acceptance/toleration** (aanvaarding): The company accepted or tolerated the conduct -- this is assessed broadly. Even a failure to take reasonable preventive measures can constitute "acceptance" (IJzerdraad criteria)

### Your Company's Specific Risk Assessment

Given that your **financial director** -- a senior officer with significant authority -- is the suspect, the attribution to the company is likely straightforward:

| Factor | Assessment |
|--------|-----------|
| Employee/officer status | Financial director is a senior officer -- strong nexus to company |
| Business interest | False invoices relate directly to the company's financial operations |
| Power and control | The company had the ability to supervise the financial director |
| Acceptance/toleration | Did the company have adequate internal controls? Were warning signs ignored? |

**Critical question**: Did the company have adequate **compliance measures and internal controls** in place to prevent and detect fraudulent invoices? A lack of adequate controls strongly supports a finding that the company "accepted" the conduct.

## Potential Criminal Charges Against the Company

1. **Art. 225 Sr (valsheid in geschrift)** -- The company itself as perpetrator (pleger) or co-perpetrator (medepleger) of document fraud
2. **Art. 225 lid 2 Sr** -- Use of false documents in the company's administration, tax filings, or dealings with third parties
3. **Art. 69 AWR (tax fraud)** -- If the false invoices were used to file incorrect tax returns (VPB, BTW), the company may face tax fraud charges
4. **Art. 420bis Sr (witwassen)** -- If the false invoices were used to conceal or disguise the proceeds of crime (money laundering)
5. **WED offenses** -- Depending on the industry and regulatory framework, violations of sector-specific regulations may also apply

## The FIOD Investigation

### What the FIOD Is

The FIOD (Fiscale Inlichtingen- en Opsporingsdienst) is the Dutch fiscal intelligence and investigation service. It investigates:
- Tax fraud (fiscale fraude)
- Financial and economic crime
- Money laundering
- Customs violations

The FIOD operates under the authority of the Functioneel Parket (specialized prosecution service) of the Openbaar Ministerie (OM - Public Prosecution Service).

### The Raid (Doorzoeking)

The FIOD raid on your offices (doorzoeking ter inbeslagneming) is authorized under Art. 96c Sv (by a hulpofficier van justitie) or Art. 110 Sv (by a rechter-commissaris, for more intrusive searches). During a raid, the FIOD may:
- Seize documents, computers, servers, and digital storage devices (inbeslagneming)
- Copy digital data (maken van kopieën)
- Interview employees as witnesses or suspects
- Seal areas for later search

### Immediate Implications

1. **The company is likely already a suspect** (verdachte) in the FIOD investigation, alongside the financial director
2. **Everything the company and its employees say to investigators can be used** -- exercise extreme caution
3. **Legal privilege** (verschoningsrecht): Communications with your lawyer are privileged and cannot be seized. If privileged documents were seized, notify your lawyer immediately.

## Rights of the Company as Suspect

- **Right to remain silent** (zwijgrecht, Art. 29 Sv) -- the company (through its representatives) has the right to refuse to answer questions
- **Right to legal counsel** -- the company should immediately engage a criminal defense lawyer (strafrechtadvocaat), preferably one specialized in economic criminal law (bijzonder strafrecht / financieel strafrecht)
- **Cautie**: Investigators must inform the suspect of the right to remain silent before questioning
- **Access to the case file** (recht op kennisneming van processtukken) -- after the investigation reaches a certain stage

## Penalties for the Company

If the company is convicted:
- **Fine**: Up to the 6th category (EUR 900,000), or if that is insufficient, up to 10% of the company's annual turnover (for economic offenses via WED)
- **Publication of the judgment** at the company's expense
- **Confiscation** (verbeurdverklaring) of objects related to the offense
- **Onttrekking aan het verkeer** (removal from commerce)
- **Settlement** (transactie): The OM may offer a settlement (hoge transactie) as an alternative to prosecution, which can involve a substantial payment plus compliance measures

## Practical Recommendations

1. **Engage a strafrechtadvocaat IMMEDIATELY**: This is urgent. The company needs specialized criminal defense counsel (financieel/economisch strafrechtadvocaat) from the moment of the raid. Every statement, document production, and interaction with the FIOD should be coordinated through your lawyer.

2. **Exercise the right to remain silent**: Do not voluntarily provide information to the FIOD without consulting your lawyer. Instruct employees not to make statements without legal guidance.

3. **Preserve documents**: Do NOT destroy, alter, or conceal any documents or data. Doing so could constitute a separate criminal offense (belemmering van de rechtsgang) and would severely damage the company's position.

4. **Review internal controls**: Conduct an internal investigation (under attorney-client privilege) to understand what happened, the scope of the false invoices, and whether other employees were involved.

5. **Separate interests**: The interests of the company, the financial director, and other employees may diverge. Each party should have independent legal representation. The company should not use the same lawyer as the financial director.

6. **Assess cooperation strategy**: Discuss with your lawyer whether and to what extent cooperation with the FIOD may be beneficial (cooperation can be a mitigating factor in sentencing, but must be carefully managed to avoid self-incrimination).

7. **Consider regulatory implications**: Depending on your industry, the investigation may trigger obligations to notify regulatory authorities (e.g., AFM, DNB, professional bodies). Your lawyer can advise on these.

8. **Board/shareholder notification**: Consider governance obligations to notify the board of directors and shareholders about the investigation and its potential impact.
