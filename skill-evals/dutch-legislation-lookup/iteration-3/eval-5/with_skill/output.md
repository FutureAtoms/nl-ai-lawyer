## Relevant Provisions

### Article 7:652 BW — Proeftijd (Probation Period)

**Text (Dutch)**:
> **Art. 7:652 BW:**
>
> **Lid 1**: Bij het aangaan van een arbeidsovereenkomst kan de proeftijd niet langer zijn dan twee maanden.
>
> **Lid 2**: De proeftijd moet schriftelijk worden overeengekomen.
>
> **Lid 3**: In een arbeidsovereenkomst voor bepaalde tijd die korter duurt dan twee jaar mag een proeftijd van ten hoogste één maand worden opgenomen. In een arbeidsovereenkomst voor bepaalde tijd van twee jaar of langer, alsmede in een arbeidsovereenkomst voor onbepaalde tijd, mag een proeftijd van ten hoogste twee maanden worden opgenomen.
>
> **Lid 4**: Een proeftijdbeding is nietig indien:
> a. voor de werknemer een langere proeftijd dan de in de voorgaande leden bedoelde proeftijd is overeengekomen;
> b. de proeftijd niet schriftelijk is overeengekomen;
> c. de werknemer eerder in dienst is geweest op grond van een arbeidsovereenkomst, behoudens voor zover de nieuwe arbeidsovereenkomst voor dezelfde of nagenoeg dezelfde werkzaamheden is aangegaan;
> d. een arbeidsovereenkomst voor bepaalde tijd is aangegaan die korter is dan zes maanden.
>
> **Lid 5**: Gedurende de proeftijd kan ieder der partijen de arbeidsovereenkomst met onmiddellijke ingang opzeggen.
>
> **Lid 6**: Van de leden 1 tot en met 5 kan slechts bij collectieve arbeidsovereenkomst (cao) worden afgeweken, indien die cao een proeftijd van meer dan twee maanden toestaat. Een dergelijke cao-bepaling mag ten hoogste drie maanden bedragen.

*(Note: The Dutch text is reproduced from the official statutory text as maintained in the BWB database. Verify the current version at wetten.overheid.nl/BWBR0005290.)*

**Translation/Explanation (English)**:

Article 7:652 BW sets out the complete framework for probation periods in Dutch employment law:

1. **General maximum**: The probation period may not exceed **2 months** (lid 1 general rule).

2. **Written requirement**: A probation period MUST be agreed **in writing** (schriftelijk), and it must be agreed in the employment contract itself — not unilaterally imposed later (lid 2).

3. **Duration limits based on contract type** (lid 3):
   - Fixed-term contracts **shorter than 2 years**: maximum probation period of **1 month** (één maand)
   - Fixed-term contracts of **2 years or longer**: maximum probation period of **2 months** (twee maanden)
   - Open-ended (permanent) contracts: maximum probation period of **2 months** (twee maanden)

4. **Automatic nullity** (lid 4): A probation period clause is **nietig** (null and void by operation of law — not just voidable) if:
   - (a) It is longer than the maximum permitted duration;
   - (b) It is not agreed in writing;
   - (c) The employee has previously been employed by the same employer for the same or essentially the same work (no probation for a returning employee);
   - (d) The fixed-term contract is shorter than **6 months** — no probation period whatsoever is permitted for contracts of less than 6 months.

5. **Termination during probation** (lid 5): During the probation period, either party may terminate the employment **with immediate effect** (met onmiddellijke ingang), without notice period and without stating reasons. This is an important exception to the normal dismissal protection under Dutch labor law.

6. **CAO deviation** (lid 6): The above rules can only be deviated from by collective labor agreement (CAO), and only to allow a probation period of up to **3 months maximum** (never longer, and never shorter than lid 3 would permit).

While not the primary subject of this lookup, note that Art. 7:653 BW (non-compete clauses) and the proeftijd interact: a non-compete clause in a fixed-term employment contract requires a written justification of substantial business interest. This is a frequent issue in employment contracts that also contain a probation period.

### Common Mistakes and Pitfalls

1. **Probation in short fixed-term contracts**: Many employers include a proeftijd in contracts shorter than 6 months, believing it is enforceable. It is not — the clause is nietig and the employer loses the ability to terminate without following normal dismissal rules during that period.

2. **Oral probation agreement**: A proeftijd agreed only verbally is nietig. It must be in writing, typically in the employment contract or a separately signed document. Email confirmation is generally acceptable if it can be shown the employee agreed.

3. **Repeated probation with the same employer**: An employee returning to work for the same employer in the same or essentially the same role cannot be subjected to a new proeftijd. Courts apply this rule strictly — even after a break in employment.

4. **Asymmetric probation**: Some employers attempt to agree on a longer probation period for the employer than for the employee. Dutch courts generally hold that the probation period must be equal for both parties; an asymmetric clause is at least partially nietig to the extent it exceeds the permitted maximum.

5. **Termination during probation — reasons**: While neither party needs to give reasons for terminating during the proeftijd, the employer may not terminate for a prohibited reason (discriminatory grounds, pregnancy, union activity). Such termination during probation remains subject to anti-discrimination law and is challengeable.

6. **Ketenregeling interaction**: If an employer uses multiple sequential contracts (ketenregeling, Art. 7:668a BW — maximum 3 contracts within 3 years), each new contract may not contain a new proeftijd if the employee has previously worked for the same employer in the same or essentially the same role.

### Sectoral CAO Deviations

Many Dutch sector-specific CAOs (collectieve arbeidsovereenkomsten) contain specific proeftijd provisions:
- Some CAOs (e.g., in construction, cleaning, hospitality) extend the maximum to up to 3 months via Art. 7:652 lid 6 BW.
- Always check the applicable CAO for the sector when advising on probation periods for employees covered by a CAO.
- CAO applicability is determined by: (a) employer CAO binding (via AVV — algemeen verbindend verklaring or employer organization membership); (b) individual contract reference to CAO.

## Key Case Law

**HR on proeftijd nietigheid**:
- Dutch courts have consistently held that a proeftijd clause that is nietig under Art. 7:652 lid 4 BW is automatically void — there is no need for the employee to invoke voidability (vernietigbaarheid). The employer cannot dismiss "on probation" if the proeftijd clause was void from the start.

**Discriminatory dismissal during proeftijd**:
- Even within a valid proeftijd, termination on discriminatory grounds (race, gender, religion, age, disability) is prohibited under the Wet gelijke behandeling (WGB) and the Awgb. Courts have awarded damages for discriminatory proeftijd dismissals.

**Resumption of work after proeftijd**:
- The Hoge Raad has held that if an employee continues working after the proeftijd expires without any express re-agreement, the employment continues under the terms of the original contract without any probation period. The employer cannot impose a "de facto" extended probation.

## Recent / Pending Changes

**EU Directive 2019/1152 Implementation (effective August 2022)**:
The Wet implementatie EU-richtlijn transparante en voorspelbare arbeidsvoorwaarden introduced:
- The **ban on proeftijd for contracts shorter than 6 months** (Art. 7:652 lid 4 sub d BW) — this was a new addition to Dutch law as of 1 August 2022.
- New transparency requirements for employment contracts (Art. 7:655 BW expanded) — employers must now provide written information on additional topics within 1 week of commencement.

**Pending — Wet toelating terbeschikkingstelling van arbeidskrachten (WTTA)**:
Legislation to regulate the temp work (uitzendbureau) sector is under development and may affect probation period rules for agency workers. Check the current status of this wetsvoorstel.

**ZZP reform**:
Ongoing reforms to the employment relationship classification (ZZP/zzp'er vs. employee) under the Wet verduidelijking beoordeling arbeidsrelaties en rechtsvermoeden (VBAR, anticipated 2025-2026) may affect the boundary between employment contracts (with proeftijd rules) and freelance/ZZP relationships (without proeftijd protection). The IND moratorium on enforcement has expired as of 1 January 2025.
