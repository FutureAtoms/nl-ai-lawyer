## Relevant Provisions

### Article 7:652 BW — Proeftijd (Probation Period)

**Text (Dutch)**:

> **Lid 1**: Indien partijen een proeftijd zijn overeengekomen, is ieder van hen gedurende die tijd bevoegd de arbeidsovereenkomst met onmiddellijke ingang op te zeggen.
>
> **Lid 2**: Een proeftijd moet schriftelijk worden overeengekomen.
>
> **Lid 3**: Een proeftijd is slechts geldig indien:
> a. het een arbeidsovereenkomst voor bepaalde tijd van meer dan zes maanden betreft en de proeftijd niet langer is dan één maand; of
> b. het een arbeidsovereenkomst voor onbepaalde tijd betreft en de proeftijd niet langer is dan twee maanden.
>
> **Lid 4**: Een proeftijd is nietig indien:
> a. een tijdelijk contract van zes maanden of korter;
> b. de werknemer tevoren voor dezelfde werkgever heeft gewerkt voor dezelfde werkzaamheden;
> c. de maximale duur wordt overschreden.
>
> **Lid 7**: Een proeftijdbeding in een cao kan afwijken van de maximale duur in lid 3, maar mag niet meer bedragen dan twee maanden voor bepaalde tijd en twee maanden voor onbepaalde tijd, tenzij de cao expliciet een langere proeftijd toestaat (maximaal drie maanden bij bepaalde tijd van meer dan twee jaar in bepaalde cao's — zie lid 8).
>
> **Lid 8**: Een proeftijd van maximaal drie maanden kan alleen worden overeengekomen bij cao voor een tijdelijk contract van langer dan twee jaar.

**Translation/Explanation (English)**:

Article 7:652 BW governs the probation period in Dutch employment contracts. During the agreed probation period, both the employer and the employee may terminate the employment contract with immediate effect, without notice and without requiring a ground for dismissal (Article 7:676 BW). The key rules are:

1. **Written requirement**: The probation period MUST be agreed in writing (schriftelijk). An oral probation agreement is null and void (nietig). This is an absolute requirement.

2. **Maximum duration**:
   - **Fixed-term contracts of more than 6 months**: Maximum probation period of **één maand** (one month).
   - **Fixed-term contracts of 6 months or less**: No probation period is permitted at all.
   - **Indefinite-term (permanent) contracts**: Maximum probation period of **twee maanden** (two months).
   - **By collective labour agreement (cao)**: A cao may extend the maximum to two months for fixed-term contracts and up to three months only for fixed-term contracts exceeding two years (Art. 7:652 lid 8 BW).

3. **Prohibition on successive probation periods**: If the employee has previously worked for the same employer in the same or substantially similar role, a new probation period cannot be agreed (Art. 7:652 lid 4 BW, sub b). This prevents employers from circumventing dismissal protection through repeated probation periods.

4. **Nullity**: Any probation period that violates the statutory requirements is null (nietig) and therefore has no legal effect. The employment contract itself remains valid; only the probation clause is struck.

**Legal Context**:

- **Type**: Dwingend recht (mandatory law) — the statutory maximum durations are mandatory; parties cannot contract out of them to the employee's detriment, except via a collective labour agreement (cao) to the extent the law permits.
- **Part of**: BW Boek 7, Titel 10 (Arbeidsovereenkomst), Afdeling 2 (De verplichtingen van de werkgever)
- **Related provisions**:
  - Art. 7:676 BW: Dismissal during probation period — each party may terminate with immediate effect; no notice period is required
  - Art. 7:677 BW: Liability for early termination outside the probation period
  - Art. 7:691 BW: Special rules for agency workers (uitzendovereenkomst)
  - Art. 7:668a BW: Ketenregeling (chain of fixed-term contracts) — interacts with probation rules
  - Art. 7:610a BW: Presumption of employment (relevant for determining whether a probation clause is valid)
- **EU basis**: Directive 2019/1152 (Transparent and Predictable Working Conditions Directive) sets an outer limit of 6 months for probation periods at EU level (Art. 8). Dutch law is more restrictive. The Directive was implemented in the Netherlands via the Wet implementatie EU-richtlijn transparante en voorspelbare arbeidsvoorwaarden (2022).

## Practical Application

### Key Rules in Practice

| Contract type | Maximum proeftijd |
|---------------|-------------------|
| Bepaalde tijd ≤ 6 maanden | Niet toegestaan (niet geldig) |
| Bepaalde tijd > 6 maanden t/m 2 jaar | Maximaal één maand |
| Bepaalde tijd > 2 jaar (alleen via cao) | Maximaal drie maanden (cao vereist) |
| Onbepaalde tijd | Maximaal twee maanden |

### Written Requirement in Practice

The requirement that the probation clause must be agreed **schriftelijk** (in writing) means:
- The clause must be included in the written employment contract (arbeidsovereenkomst)
- A separate written addendum is also sufficient
- An oral agreement, even if acknowledged by both parties, is legally invalid
- Email confirmation of an oral agreement has been held insufficient by some courts; a signed document is strongly advisable

### Same Employer / Same Work Prohibition

Dutch courts interpret this strictly. If:
- A fixed-term contract expires and the parties enter into a new contract, a new probation period is generally invalid
- The employee worked at the same employer via an agency (uitzendbureau) for the same role and is then directly hired, a probation period may be invalid depending on the circumstances
- There is a change of employer (overgang van onderneming, Art. 7:662 BW), the existing employment continues and a fresh probation period is invalid

### Nullity and Its Effect

If a probation clause is null (nietig):
- The employment contract itself remains valid
- The employer cannot rely on the probation period to terminate without following the normal dismissal procedures (ontslag via UWV or kantonrechter, or otherwise meeting the grounds in Art. 7:669 BW)
- Any notice of dismissal "during probation" on the basis of a void clause can be challenged; the employee may be entitled to compensation or reinstatement

## Key Case Law

### HR 2 April 2004, ECLI:NL:HR:2004:AO1718
The Hoge Raad confirmed that the written requirement for probation periods is absolute. A probation period that was only orally agreed is null and void, and the employer cannot rely on it.

### Kantonrechter (various decisions) on successive probation periods
Lower courts have consistently held that a second probation period for the same employee in the same role is invalid, even where the new contract has a different formal legal basis (e.g., moving from temp to permanent). The relevant test is whether the work is substantially the same.

### HR on termination during probation period
The Hoge Raad has confirmed that termination during a valid probation period requires no substantive ground and cannot be challenged on substantive grounds. However, termination may still be challengeable if motivated by a discriminatory reason (e.g., pregnancy, disability, union membership — Art. 7:646-648 BW) or if the employer acts contrary to good faith (misbruik van recht — Art. 3:13 BW).

## Recent / Pending Changes

### Wet Toelating Terbeschikkingstelling van Arbeidskrachten (WTTA)
Pending legislation affecting agency work may indirectly affect probation periods for workers moving from agency to direct employment.

### EU Directive 2019/1152 — Transparent and Predictable Working Conditions
Implemented in 2022. This Directive caps probation periods at 6 months at EU level and requires proportionality. Dutch law already complies with the maximum but employers should note that any probation period must be proportionate to the expected duration and nature of the work.

### Arbeidsongeschiktheid during Proeftijd
Important practical point: if an employee falls ill during the probation period, the employer retains the right to terminate during the probation period (the illness does not suspend the employer's right). However, the termination prohibition on sick employees (Art. 7:670 lid 1 BW) does NOT apply during a valid probation period.
