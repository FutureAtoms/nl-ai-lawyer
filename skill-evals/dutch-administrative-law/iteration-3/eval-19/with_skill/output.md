## Standing Analysis

As the applicant for the omgevingsvergunning, you are unquestionably an **belanghebbende** (interested party) within the meaning of Art. 1:2 Awb: you have a direct, personal, objective, and actual interest in the outcome of the permit decision. You have full standing to lodge bezwaar and, if necessary, beroep.

A rejected permit application is a **beschikking** (individual administrative decision) within the meaning of Art. 1:3 Awb, which is subject to the full bezwaar and beroep procedure under the Awb.

## Options and Procedures

### Step 1: Bezwaar (Administrative Objection) - Art. 7:1 Awb

**What it is**: A formal objection lodged with the **same administrative body** (the gemeente) that rejected your application. The gemeente must fully reconsider its decision (heroverweging - Art. 7:11 Awb), including aspects not specifically challenged.

**Deadline**: **6 weeks** from the day after the decision was sent (Art. 6:7-6:8 Awb).

**Where to send**: To the College van Burgemeester en Wethouders of the gemeente. The rechtsmiddelenclausule on the decision letter will specify the exact address and method (mail or electronic, if available).

**What must be included** (Art. 6:5 Awb):
- Your name and address
- Date of the bezwaar
- Description of the decision being challenged (attach a copy)
- Your grounds for objection (bezwaargronden)
- Signature

**Process after filing**:
1. The gemeente must give you an opportunity to be heard (hoorzitting - Art. 7:2 Awb), either directly or through a bezwaarschriftencommissie (advisory committee).
2. The gemeente must then decide on your bezwaar within **6 weeks** (or **12 weeks** if an advisory committee is used - Art. 7:10 Awb). A 6-week extension is possible.
3. If the gemeente fails to decide in time: this is treated as a constructive refusal and you can appeal to court for failure to decide (beroep niet-tijdig beslissen - Art. 6:2 sub b Awb).

**Possible outcomes**:
- **Gegrond** (well-founded): Gemeente overturns or modifies the rejection - you may receive the permit.
- **Ongegrond** (unfounded): Gemeente upholds the rejection - you proceed to beroep.
- **Niet-ontvankelijk** (inadmissible): Bezwaar is rejected on procedural grounds (e.g., too late, no standing).

### Step 2: Beroep (Appeal to Rechtbank) - Art. 8:1 Awb

**What it is**: A formal appeal to the **Rechtbank** (administrative law court / bestuursrechter) if the bezwaar is unsuccessful. The court tests the legality of the decision.

**Deadline**: **6 weeks** from the day after the decision on bezwaar is sent.

**Where to file**: The Rechtbank in the district where the administrative body (gemeente) is located, or as specified in the decision. A court fee (griffierecht) applies: approximately EUR 371 for legal entities (2024 rates).

**What the court reviews** (Art. 8:69 Awb):
- **Legality** (rechtmatigheid): Was the rejection lawful?
- **Procedural propriety**: Were the correct procedures followed during the permit application process?
- **Factual basis**: Is the factual basis for the rejection adequate and correctly established (Art. 3:2 Awb - duty of careful investigation)?
- **Reasoning** (Art. 3:46-47 Awb): Is the rejection properly reasoned and motivated?
- **Proportionality** (Art. 3:4 lid 2 Awb): Following ABRvS 2 februari 2022 (Harderwijk), courts now apply a more intensive proportionality review.
- **General principles of good governance (ABBB)**: Legitimate expectations (vertrouwensbeginsel), equal treatment, fair play.

**Court remedies** (Art. 8:72 Awb): The court may annul the decision, instruct the gemeente to make a new decision, or (in some cases) decide the matter itself (zelf voorzien).

### Step 3: Hoger Beroep (Higher Appeal) - ABRvS

**What it is**: Appeal against the Rechtbank's judgment to the **Afdeling bestuursrechtspraak van de Raad van State (ABRvS)**, which is the highest administrative court for environmental and spatial planning matters (under the Omgevingswet).

**Deadline**: **6 weeks** from the date of the Rechtbank judgment.

**Note**: Under the Omgevingswet, for certain categories of complex permits (those subject to the extended procedure under Art. 16.85 Ow), the procedure may differ - direct beroep to the ABRvS may apply in some cases. Verify the applicable procedural route for your specific permit type.

## Grounds for Challenge

The following grounds may support your bezwaar and beroep:

### Procedural Grounds
- Failure by the gemeente to conduct an adequate investigation (schending van Art. 3:2 Awb - zorgvuldigheidsbeginsel)
- Insufficient or unclear reasoning for the rejection (schending van Art. 3:46-47 Awb - motiveringsbeginsel)
- Failure to properly weigh all relevant interests (Art. 3:4 lid 1 Awb)
- Failure to follow the correct procedure under the Omgevingswet (check whether the standard 8-week or extended 26-week procedure applied)
- Procedural errors during the application phase (e.g., failure to give you an opportunity to respond to adverse information)

### Substantive Grounds
- The rejection is disproportionate in light of your interests (Art. 3:4 lid 2 Awb; see ABRvS Harderwijk 2022 for the enhanced proportionality test)
- The rejection is based on incorrect facts or incorrect application of the applicable rules (omgevingsplan, national rules, etc.)
- The rejection conflicts with the omgevingsplan (the local environmental plan) or a legitimate expectation created by the gemeente (vertrouwensbeginsel - ABRvS 29 mei 2019)
- Violation of the equal treatment principle (gelijkheidsbeginsel): if comparable applications were granted
- The conditions of the Omgevingswet or the omgevingsplan for refusal were not met

## Provisional Measures (Voorlopige Voorziening - Art. 8:81 Awb)

If the rejection of the permit has **immediate negative consequences** that cannot await the outcome of the regular bezwaar/beroep procedure (e.g., a construction project that has already commenced and must stop, a business that cannot operate, an investment deadline that will be missed), you can simultaneously request a **voorlopige voorziening** (provisional measure) from the Rechtbank.

- The voorzieningenrechter (preliminary relief judge) can order the gemeente to provisionally grant the permit, or suspend the effect of the rejection, pending the outcome of the bezwaar/beroep.
- This requires demonstrating **urgency** (spoedeisend belang) and at least a reasonable likelihood that the bezwaar/beroep will succeed.
- A voorlopige voorziening can be requested as soon as bezwaar has been filed (or simultaneously with beroep after bezwaar).
- Processing: typically 2-4 weeks (or faster in very urgent cases).
- **Kortsluiting** (Art. 8:86 Awb): If the voorzieningenrechter has sufficient information, they may decide the entire main case at the same hearing.

5. **Analyze the grounds for rejection**: Review the gemeente's reasoning and identify all factual and legal errors.
6. **Gather supporting documents**: Technical reports, architectural drawings, expert opinions, comparable permit approvals (for equal treatment arguments), and any pre-application communications with the gemeente that may support a legitimate expectations argument.
7. **Attend the hoorzitting**: Use the hearing to present your position and respond to the gemeente's reasoning. Bring witnesses or experts if needed.

### If Bezwaar Is Unsuccessful
8. **File beroep within 6 weeks** of the decision on bezwaar, at the Rechtbank.
9. **Consider whether a voorlopige voorziening is needed** at any stage if urgent interim relief is required.
