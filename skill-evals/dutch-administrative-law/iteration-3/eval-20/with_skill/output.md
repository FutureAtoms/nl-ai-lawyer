## The Core Problem: Unlawful Delay

Under Dutch administrative law, administrative bodies are required to decide on applications within the period prescribed by law (or, if no period is prescribed, within a reasonable period - Art. 4:13 Awb). The applicable subsidy regulation prescribed a decision within **8 weeks**. The agency has now taken **4 months (approximately 16 weeks)**, which means it has been in default for approximately 8 weeks.

This delay is a violation of Dutch administrative law. You have several enforceable remedies under the Awb.

## Available Remedies

### Remedy 1: Ingebrekestelling (Notice of Default) - Art. 4:17 Awb

**What it is**: A formal written notice to the administrative body that it has failed to decide within the statutory period, triggering the obligation to pay a dwangsom (penalty payment) for each day the body continues to fail to decide.

**How it works**:
1. Send a written **ingebrekestelling** to the administrative body, stating:
   - Reference to the subsidy application (with date and reference number)
   - Statement that the statutory decision period of 8 weeks has expired
   - Formal notice that the body is in default (in gebreke) within the meaning of Art. 4:17 Awb
   - Demand that the body issue a decision within 2 weeks
2. The administrative body then has a **2-week grace period** (Art. 4:17 lid 3 Awb) to issue a decision.
3. If no decision is issued within those 2 weeks: the body **automatically owes a dwangsom** (penalty payment) for every day it continues to fail to decide, from the first day after the 2-week grace period.

**Critical timing**: The 14-day grace period runs from the day the ingebrekestelling is **received** by the body (ABRvS 6 maart 2013). Send it by registered mail (aangetekende post) or another method that produces proof of receipt.

### Remedy 2: Dwangsom (Penalty Payment for Late Decision) - Art. 4:17 Awb

Once the ingebrekestelling has been sent and the 2-week grace period has passed without a decision, the administrative body owes a **dwangsom** for each additional day of delay:

| Period | Daily Penalty |
|--------|--------------|
| First 14 days after grace period | EUR 23 per day |
| Next 14 days | EUR 35 per day |
| Subsequent days | EUR 45 per day |
| **Maximum total dwangsom** | **EUR 1,442** (indexed) |

**Important limitations** (ABRvS 17 juli 2019, ECLI:NL:RVS:2019:2464):
- The maximum dwangsom of EUR 1,442 applies **per application**, not per ingebrekestelling. Sending multiple notices does not reset or increase the maximum.
- The dwangsom applies automatically by operation of law - the body does not have to agree to pay it.
- The body must formally determine (vaststellen) the amount of the dwangsom (Art. 4:18 Awb); if it fails to do so within 6 weeks of issuing the decision, you can challenge this separately.

**How to claim**: Once the 2-week grace period has passed, the dwangsom accrues automatically. The body should issue a decision on the amount of the dwangsom (dwangsombeschikking). If it does not, you can demand this separately or pursue it through the court.

### Remedy 3: Beroep Niet-Tijdig Beslissen (Court Appeal for Failure to Decide) - Art. 6:2 sub b and Art. 8:55b Awb

Under Art. 6:2 sub b Awb, a failure to decide within the statutory period is treated as a **fictieve weigering** (deemed refusal), which can be appealed to the administrative court (Rechtbank).

**Special expedited procedure** (Art. 8:55b-8:55f Awb):
- For failure-to-decide appeals, a **simplified and expedited procedure** applies.
- The Rechtbank must decide within **8 weeks** of receiving the beroepschrift.
- No hearing is required if the court has sufficient information (though one can be held).
- The court can order the body to make a decision within a set period, on pain of a court-ordered penalty.

**When to use**: This remedy is most useful when the ingebrekestelling/dwangsom route is not producing results, or when you want a court order compelling the body to decide.

**Note**: The ingebrekestelling (Art. 4:17) and the beroep niet-tijdig (Art. 6:2 sub b) can be pursued **simultaneously**. Sending the ingebrekestelling does not prevent you from filing beroep, and vice versa.

**Deadline for beroep niet-tijdig**: There is no fixed deadline, but it must be filed while the body is still in default. Once a decision is issued (even if negative), the failure-to-decide route ends and you would need to challenge the actual decision through bezwaar.

### Remedy 4: Wettelijke Rente (Statutory Interest)

If the delay in the subsidy decision causes you financial damage (e.g., delayed receipt of subsidy funds), you may be entitled to claim **wettelijke rente** (statutory interest) on amounts ultimately awarded as compensation for the period of unlawful delay. This can be claimed during the administrative procedure (Art. 8:88 Awb) or in a separate civil procedure.

### Step 4 - Claim the Dwangsom

- The body should issue a dwangsombeschikking; if it does not, request it formally.

### Step 5 - Address the Decision When It Arrives

When the body finally issues a decision (which is likely after the ingebrekestelling):
- If the subsidy is granted: ensure the full amount owed is paid, including any interest for the period of delay.
- If the subsidy is refused: file bezwaar within **6 weeks** of the decision and consider whether the refusal is lawful or is a continuing attempt to avoid granting the subsidy.
- If the decision is again delayed after beroep: the court order will specify penalties for continued non-compliance.

## Additional Considerations

### Was the Deadline Properly Suspended?

Under Art. 4:15 Awb, the statutory decision period can be **suspended** in certain circumstances:
- If the body requests additional information from the applicant and the applicant has not yet responded (the period is suspended from the request until the response or the response deadline).
- If the applicant consented to an extension.

Check whether the body sent you a request for additional information or notified you of a suspension. If so, the 8-week period may not have started running from the original submission date. However, if no such notice was given, the 8-week period likely expired as calculated.

### Was a Reasonable Deadline Extension Communicated?

Under Art. 4:14 Awb, if the body cannot decide within the statutory period, it must:
- **Before the statutory period expires**: notify the applicant in writing, state the reason for the delay, and give an estimated date for the decision.
- If this was not done, this constitutes an additional violation of the Awb.

If the body did notify you of an extension, assess whether the extended period was reasonable and whether even that extended period has now expired.

### Government Liability for Damages

If the delay has caused you **concrete financial damage** (e.g., interest costs because you had to bridge-finance the subsidized project, loss of business opportunities, penalties incurred because of the delayed grant), you may have a claim for damages against the government body:
- Under **Art. 8:88 Awb**: Claim for damages within the administrative procedure.
- Under **Art. 6:162 BW** (onrechtmatige daad): Separate civil tort claim if the administrative route is insufficient.
- The standard for government liability is whether the delay constitutes an unlawful act (onrechtmatige overheidsdaad). Given that the statutory deadline has been significantly exceeded without evident justification, this threshold is likely met.
