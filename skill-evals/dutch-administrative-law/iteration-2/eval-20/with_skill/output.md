## Remedy 1: Ingebrekestelling (Notice of Default)

### What Is an Ingebrekestelling?

Under Art. 4:17 Awb, before the automatic penalty payment (dwangsom) begins accruing, you must first send an **ingebrekestelling** - a formal written notice to the agency stating that:
- The statutory decision deadline has passed;
- The agency is in default (in gebreke);
- You demand a decision to be issued.

### Procedure (Art. 4:17 Awb)

1. **Send the ingebrekestelling in writing** to the responsible administrative body (by registered mail or in a manner that creates proof of receipt);
2. After receipt of the ingebrekestelling, the agency has **2 weeks** to issue the decision;
3. If after those 2 weeks **still no decision is issued**: the **dwangsom** starts accruing automatically for each day of further delay.

### Practical Note

The ingebrekestelling must clearly:
- Identify your subsidy application (case number, date, subject);
- State that the statutory decision deadline has expired;
- Demand that the agency issue the decision without further delay.

If you have not yet sent an ingebrekestelling, do so immediately. The dwangsom clock does not start until after the 2-week grace period following a valid ingebrekestelling.

## Remedy 2: Dwangsom bij Niet Tijdig Beslissen (Art. 4:17 Awb)

### What Is the Dwangsom?

The dwangsom (penalty payment) is an automatic financial sanction imposed on the administrative body for each day it fails to decide after the ingebrekestelling grace period expires. It is not a punishment in the criminal sense - it is a civil law mechanism to incentivize the agency to act.

### Amount and Maximum (Art. 4:17 lid 2-4 Awb)

| Day | Daily dwangsom |
|-----|----------------|
| Day 1-14 | EUR 23 per day |
| Day 15-42 | EUR 35 per day |
| Day 43 onwards | EUR 45 per day |
| **Maximum total** | **EUR 1,442** |

*Note: These amounts are indexed; verify current amounts under Art. 4:17 Awb.*

The maximum dwangsom per application is **EUR 1,442** (indexed). As confirmed by ABRvS 17 juli 2019 (ECLI:NL:RVS:2019:2464), this maximum applies per application and does not reset if additional ingebrekestellingen are sent.

### How to Collect the Dwangsom

Under Art. 4:18 Awb, the administrative body must fix and pay the dwangsom within 2 weeks of the decision being issued (or upon request if no decision follows). If the agency fails to fix or pay the dwangsom:
- You can file beroep against this refusal (Art. 4:19 Awb);
- Alternatively, you may enforce payment through civil law means.

## Remedy 3: Beroep bij Niet Tijdig Beslissen (Art. 6:2 sub b and Art. 8:55b Awb)

In addition to (or instead of) pursuing the dwangsom, you have the right to **appeal to the administrative court (bestuursrechter at the Rechtbank)** for failure to decide in time.

### Procedure

Under Art. 6:2 sub b Awb, a failure to decide in time is treated as a (fictitious negative) decision, which can be appealed. Under Art. 8:55b-8:55f Awb:
- You file a beroepschrift at the competent Rechtbank;
- The court may impose a new deadline on the agency and order it to issue a decision;
- If the agency still does not comply, the court can impose further measures;
- The procedure is handled expeditiously (spoedprocedure).

This remedy is particularly useful when:
- The dwangsom maximum (EUR 1,442) has been reached and the agency still has not decided;
- You need a binding court order to force the agency to act;
- You wish to escalate beyond the administrative channel.
