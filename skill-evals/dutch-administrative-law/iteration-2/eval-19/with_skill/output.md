## Your Options and the Procedure

### Step 1: Bezwaar (Administrative Objection) - Art. 7:1 Awb

**What it is**: A formal written objection filed with the **same administrative body** (the gemeente) that rejected your application. The gemeente must reconsider its own decision in full (heroverweging - Art. 7:11 Awb).

**Deadline**: **6 weeks** from the day after the date of the refusal decision (Art. 6:7-6:8 Awb).

**Procedure**:
1. File the bezwaarschrift in writing with the gemeente (or electronically if the gemeente has opened this channel);
2. Include: your name and address, the date, a description and copy of the rejected decision, and your grounds for objection;
3. The gemeente may convene a hoorzitting (hearing), often through a bezwaarschriftencommissie, at which you can present your case;
4. The gemeente must decide on the bezwaar within 6 weeks of the expiry of the bezwaar period (or 12 weeks if an advisory committee is used), with a possible 6-week extension.

**Possible outcomes**: the gemeente can uphold the refusal (ongegrond), grant the permit (gegrond), or issue a modified decision.

### Step 2: Beroep (Appeal to the Rechtbank) - Art. 8:1 Awb

If the bezwaar is rejected, you can appeal to the **bestuursrechter** (administrative law judge) at the **Rechtbank**.

**Deadline**: **6 weeks** from the date of the gemeente's decision on bezwaar.

**Procedure**:
1. File a beroepschrift at the competent Rechtbank (usually the district court where the gemeente is located);
2. Include: grounds for appeal, copies of the refusal decision and the bezwaar decision;
3. Pay court fee (griffierecht): EUR 371 for legal entities (2024 - verify current amount);
4. The Rechtbank reviews the decision for legality, procedural compliance, factual adequacy, and compliance with the general principles of good governance (ABBB);
5. The court may annul the decision, order the gemeente to decide again, or resolve the matter itself.

**Higher appeal**: Following the Rechtbank, further appeal (hoger beroep) lies to the **Afdeling bestuursrechtspraak van de Raad van State (ABRvS)** within 6 weeks.

### Important Note: Direct Beroep Route (Afdeling 3.4 Awb)

In some cases (particularly for complex omgevingsvergunningen subject to the extensive 26-week procedure under Art. 16.85 Ow), the preparation follows the **uniforme openbare voorbereidingsprocedure** (Afdeling 3.4 Awb). In that case:
- There is **no bezwaar phase**;
- You proceed directly to **beroep** at the Rechtbank within 6 weeks of the final decision.

Check the decision carefully: it must state which legal remedy is available (rechtsmiddelenclausule). If the decision notes direct beroep (no bezwaar), the 6-week beroep period applies immediately.

## Grounds for Challenge

When assessing whether the refusal can be successfully challenged, consider the following grounds:

### 1. Procedural Grounds (Formele Gebreken)

- Was the decision issued within the statutory deadline? (Standard procedure: 8 + 6 weeks under Art. 16.65 Ow; extensive procedure: 26 weeks under Art. 16.85 Ow);
- Was a public consultation period (4-week inspraakperiode) properly observed?
- Was the duty to give reasons (motiveringsbeginsel, Art. 3:46-3:47 Awb) fulfilled? Is the stated reason for refusal adequate and properly explained?
- Was the duty of careful investigation (zorgvuldigheidsbeginsel, Art. 3:2 Awb) met? Did the gemeente properly investigate all relevant facts?

### 2. Substantive/Legal Grounds (Inhoudelijke Gebreken)

- Does your planned activity actually conflict with the omgevingsplan, or was the municipality's interpretation incorrect?
- Was the municipality's assessment of your application under the correct statutory framework (Omgevingswet or transitional Wabo regime, depending on when you applied)?
- Was proportionality (evenredigheidsbeginsel, Art. 3:4 lid 2 Awb) respected? Following ABRvS 2 februari 2022 (Harderwijk), courts now apply an enhanced three-part proportionality test: suitability, necessity, and proportionality stricto sensu;
- Was the weighing of interests (belangenafweging, Art. 3:4 lid 1 Awb) properly conducted?

### 3. Legitimate Expectations (Vertrouwensbeginsel)

If a municipality official (ambtenaar) gave you concrete indications that the permit would be granted, this may be relevant. Following ABRvS 29 mei 2019 (ECLI:NL:RVS:2019:1694), a three-step test applies: attribution, justified expectation, and whether public interests override those expectations.

### 4. Omgevingswet Transitional Law

If your application was submitted before 1 January 2024 under the former Wabo, the old law applies to the substantive and procedural assessment, pursuant to ABRvS 6 maart 2024 (ECLI:NL:RVS:2024:943). Ensure the gemeente applied the correct legal framework.

## Provisional Measures (Voorlopige Voorziening - Art. 8:81 Awb)

If the refusal has immediate and irreversible consequences - for example, if you cannot lawfully proceed with construction you have already begun, or if you have contractual obligations that depend on obtaining the permit - you may simultaneously request a **voorlopige voorziening** (preliminary injunction/provisional measure) from the **voorzieningenrechter** at the Rechtbank.

A voorlopige voorziening can be requested:
- During the bezwaar phase (request to the voorzieningenrechter simultaneously with filing bezwaar);
- During the beroep phase.

This does not suspend the refusal automatically - you must request and obtain the provisional measure. The voorzieningenrechter weighs the urgency and the interests involved.
