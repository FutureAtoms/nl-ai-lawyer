## Analysis

### 1. Classification: Art. 7:290 BW — Middenstandsbedrijfsruimte

The threshold question is whether your property qualifies as **middenstandsbedrijfsruimte** under **Art. 7:290 BW**. This category covers premises used as:
- Retail space (winkelruimte) accessible to the public for direct supply of goods
- Restaurant, cafe, hotel, or other hospitality (horeca)
- Take-away establishment
- Camp site
- Craft workshop (ambachtsbedrijf) accessible to the public for direct supply of services

The **key criterion** is public accessibility for the direct supply of goods or services to customers. You have confirmed this is a 7:290 property, so this regime — with its relatively strong tenant protections — applies.

This is important because the 7:290 regime gives tenants significantly more protection than, for example, Art. 7:230a commercial space (office space, warehouses), and requires going through the proper judicial procedure even for non-payment.

### 2. The Core Legal Mechanism: Ontbinding (Dissolution) for Breach

Under **Art. 6:265 BW**, every failure in the performance of an obligation entitles the other party to dissolve the agreement (ontbinden), unless the failure, given its special nature or minor significance (bijzondere aard of geringe betekenis), does not justify dissolution.

Non-payment of rent for 3 consecutive months is a serious breach (tekortkoming in de nakoming) that Dutch courts regularly accept as justifying ontbinding of a commercial lease and the accompanying ontruiming (eviction). However, as confirmed by the Hoge Raad in **Eigen Haard (HR 28 September 2018)** and **LCCM/A (HR 30 November 2012)**, Dutch courts must always conduct a proportionality assessment — even in commercial lease terminations.

For a 7:290 lease: 3 months of unpaid rent is generally considered sufficient to justify ontbinding and ontruiming, though courts will look at the full circumstances (e.g., whether the tenant has already partially repaid, whether there is a legitimate dispute about the rent amount, etc.).

### 3. Step-by-Step Procedure

#### Step 1: Ingebrekestelling (Notice of Default) — Art. 6:82 BW

Before you can claim ontbinding, you must formally put the tenant in **default** (ingebrekestelling) by sending a written demand:
- Specify the outstanding rent amounts (with exact months and amounts)
- Set a reasonable deadline (redelijke termijn) for payment — typically 14 days for commercial tenants
- State that failure to pay within this period will result in legal proceedings for ontbinding and ontruiming

**Practical note**: Under Art. 6:83 sub c BW, if the obligation is monetary (geldsom), a special form of ingebrekestelling may not always be required, as the debtor is in default by mere expiry of the agreed payment period. However, a formal ingebrekestelling is strongly recommended in practice as it:
- Creates a clear record of the landlord's claim
- Gives the tenant a final opportunity to cure
- Supports the urgency requirement if you pursue a kort geding

Send the ingebrekestelling by registered letter (aangetekend) with proof of delivery.

#### Step 2: Assess the Payment Default Period

Three months of unpaid rent is the baseline. Before proceeding, verify:
- Exactly how many months are outstanding and the total amount
- Whether the tenant has raised any defense (e.g., gebrek — maintenance defect reducing the rent obligation, or a dispute about the rent amount)
- Whether any payment has been received after the commencement of non-payment (partial payments)

If there is a legitimate maintenance defect (gebrek) that the landlord has failed to remedy, the tenant may have a right to rent reduction under Art. 7:207 BW, which can complicate the non-payment claim. Document your own fulfillment of maintenance obligations.

#### Step 3: Choose the Appropriate Judicial Route

You have two main procedural options:

**Option A: Kort Geding (Summary Proceedings for Ontruiming)**

A **kort geding** (Art. 254 Rv) before the **kantonrechter** can be used to obtain an urgent court order for:
- Payment of the outstanding rent (huurachterstand)
- Ontruiming (eviction) of the premises within a short period (e.g., 14 days)

For this to succeed, you must demonstrate:
1. **Urgency (spoedeisend belang)**: The ongoing non-payment causes ongoing financial harm — this is generally accepted by courts
2. **Aannemelijkheid**: The court must find it sufficiently likely that the main proceedings (bodemprocedure) would result in ontbinding and ontruiming
3. **Proportionality**: The court must weigh the interests of landlord (huurachterstand, economic damage) against tenant (loss of business premises and livelihood)

Three months of rent arrears generally supports urgency. However, if the 7:290 lease is still in the first 5-year or second 5-year period, the tenant's termination protection under Art. 7:292 BW is strong, and the kort geding judge will typically require clear evidence that the breach is serious and ongoing.

**Important**: A kort geding results in a **voorlopige voorziening** (provisional measure) — not a final judgment. If the tenant opposes, you will also need to bring main proceedings within the timeline typically set by the court.

**Option B: Bodemprocedure (Main Proceedings)**

A standard procedure (dagvaarding) before the **kantonrechter** for:
- Ontbinding of the lease agreement (Art. 6:265 BW)
- Ontruiming of the premises
- Payment of all outstanding rent (huurachterstand) and future rent until vacating
- Costs of proceedings

The kantonrechter (part of the Rechtbank) has jurisdiction over commercial lease disputes, including 7:290 properties, under **Art. 93–94 Rv** (competentie kantonrechter in huurzaken).

**Timeline**: A bodemprocedure typically takes 6–12 months to judgment. If you need quicker relief, combine the kort geding with the bodemprocedure.

#### Step 4: The Dagvaarding (Writ of Summons)

To initiate proceedings, your lawyer (or a deurwaarder) must issue a **dagvaarding** (writ of summons), which must:
- Be served on the tenant by a gerechtsdeurwaarder (bailiff) — Art. 45 et seq. Rv
- State the legal grounds for ontbinding (Art. 6:265 BW) and ontruiming
- Specify the total outstanding rent with interest (wettelijke handelsrente — commercial interest rate under Art. 6:119a BW)
- Include a claim for legal costs (proceskostenvergoeding)

#### Step 5: Kantonrechter Proceedings

The kantonrechter will:
1. Schedule a hearing (zitting)
2. Allow the tenant to file a defense (conclusie van antwoord) and potentially raise counterclaims
3. Conduct a proportionality assessment under Art. 6:265 BW:
   - Is the breach (3 months non-payment) of sufficient seriousness to justify ontbinding?
   - Are there mitigating circumstances (e.g., financial hardship, COVID-related issues, disputed rent)?
4. Issue a **vonnis** (judgment):
   - Granting ontbinding and ontruiming (most likely outcome for 3 months non-payment without valid defense)
   - Possibly with a conditional element: e.g., "ontbinding granted unless tenant pays all arrears within 14 days"
   - Ordering payment of the rent arrears and legal costs

#### Step 6: Enforcement — Ontruiming (Eviction)

Once you have an enforceable vonnis (judgment):
1. Serve the vonnis on the tenant via a deurwaarder (gerechtsdeurwaarder)
2. The vonnis will typically include an **ontruimingstermijn** (eviction deadline) — the number of days the tenant has to vacate voluntarily after the vonnis
3. If the tenant fails to vacate voluntarily, the deurwaarder executes the **gedwongen ontruiming** (forced eviction):
   - The deurwaarder issues a formal ontruimingsexploot
   - The tenant is given notice of the eviction date
   - On the eviction date, the deurwaarder (with police assistance if necessary) physically removes the tenant and their belongings
   - Locks are changed; property is returned to the landlord

## Risks and Protections

### Landlord's Risks

- **Delay**: The judicial process takes time, during which rent arrears continue to accumulate
- **Counterclaims**: The tenant may raise a defense based on maintenance defects (gebreken) or dispute the rent amount
- **Suspension/postponement**: In very exceptional circumstances, courts may grant the tenant additional time, particularly if there are mitigating factors
- **Insolvency**: If the tenant is insolvent or bankrupt, the ontruiming may proceed but rent recovery may be difficult

### Landlord's Protections

- **Art. 6:265 BW**: Non-payment for 3 months is well-established grounds for ontbinding
- **Wettelijke handelsrente**: Interest accrues on outstanding rent under Art. 6:119a BW (commercial rate)
- **Retentierecht**: The deurwaarder can, in principle, retain the tenant's goods during ontruiming if necessary to secure payment claims
- **Kantonrechter judgment**: Once obtained, the vonnis is an enforceable title (executoriale titel)

## Recommendations

1. **Send the ingebrekestelling immediately**: Do not delay — every day of non-payment increases the arrears and strengthens your case, but also delays your recovery of possession.

2. **Engage a lawyer (advocaat) with commercial lease experience**: The 7:290 regime has specific statutory protections for tenants, and procedural errors can cause significant delays. A specialist huurrechtadvocaat will navigate this efficiently.

3. **Gather all documentation**:
   - The lease agreement (huurovereenkomst) — confirm it qualifies as 7:290
   - Rent statements and bank records demonstrating 3 months of non-payment
   - Any prior correspondence with the tenant about payment
   - Evidence of your own compliance with maintenance obligations

4. **Consider a kort geding for urgent relief**: If the tenant shows no intention to pay and you need the premises back quickly (e.g., significant financial harm to you), a kort geding is the fastest judicial route. A kantonrechter in kort geding can order ontruiming within weeks, not months.

5. **Simultaneously commence the bodemprocedure**: Dutch courts in kort geding proceedings often require or expect that main proceedings (bodemprocedure) are commenced within a short period. Filing both in parallel is common practice.

6. **Explore a negotiated settlement**: If the tenant is genuinely in temporary financial difficulty and otherwise a good tenant, a negotiated settlement (minnelijke regeling) — e.g., a payment plan with a clear deadline — may be faster and cheaper than full litigation. However, ensure any settlement is documented in writing (vaststellingsovereenkomst).

7. **After eviction — re-letting**: Once you recover the property, document its condition carefully (process-verbaal van ontruiming, photos) to support any subsequent claim against the tenant for damages to the property.
