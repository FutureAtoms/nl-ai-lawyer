## What Is Erfpacht?

**Erfpacht** is a limited right in rem (beperkt zakelijk recht) under **Art. 5:85 BW**, distinct from ordinary rental (huur). It entitles the holder (erfpachter) to hold and use immovable property belonging to another — in this case, the Gemeente Amsterdam. The erfpachter has near-complete enjoyment of the property, similar to ownership, and the right is:
- Registered at the kadaster (and thus fully visible to buyers)
- Transferable (by notarial deed of levering + kadaster registration)
- Inheritable
- Encumberable with a mortgage (hypotheek) — critical for financing

The **canon** is the periodic payment made by the erfpachter to the grondeigenaar (here: the Gemeente Amsterdam) in exchange for the use of the land (Art. 5:89 BW). The municipality retains the bare ownership (bloot eigendom) of the land.

## Analysis

### 1. The Amsterdam Erfpacht System — Critical Context

Amsterdam is unique in the Netherlands: the Gemeente Amsterdam owns approximately 80% of all land within the city, and virtually all residential and commercial property is on erfpacht land. Amsterdam has operated a municipal erfpacht system since 1896.

The applicable conditions depend on **which set of Algemene Bepalingen (AB)** apply to your apartment:

| AB Version | Type | Canon Revision |
|---|---|---|
| AB1915 / AB1934 / AB1966 | Tijdelijk (75 or 50-year terms) | At end of fixed term — large potential shock |
| AB1994 / AB2000 | Voortdurend (perpetual, periodic revision) | Every 50 years based on ground value — large potential shock |
| AB2016 | Eeuwigdurend (perpetual, no revision) | No revision — most favorable for erfpachter |

The fact that the canon has **not been revised in 20 years** is a major risk indicator. It suggests either:
- The property is on **AB2000** conditions (50-year revision cycle) — but the revision date may be approaching; or
- The property is on **older conditions** (AB1966 or earlier) with a fixed term approaching expiry; or
- The erfpachter has **not converted to AB2016** (eeuwigdurende erfpacht)

### 2. The Central Risk: Canonherziening (Canon Revision)

The most significant financial risk for any buyer is the **canonherziening** — the periodic revision of the canon when the applicable revision moment arrives.

Under AB2000 (the most common condition for properties not yet converted), the canon is revised based on the **residuele grondwaarde** (residual ground value) at the time of revision:
1. Market value of the property (marktwaarde) is determined
2. Depreciated replacement value of the building is subtracted
3. The remainder = ground value (grondwaarde)
4. A canon percentage (canonpercentage) is applied to the ground value

Given that Amsterdam property values have increased dramatically over the past 20+ years, a canon that has not been revised in 20 years is almost certainly well below what it would be after revision. The **canonsprong** (canon shock/leap) at the next revision moment can be enormous:

- Example: An erfpachter paying EUR 500/year in canon may face EUR 5,000–10,000/year or more after revision when ground values have risen significantly
- This is precisely why the AB2016 reform was introduced and why many erfpachters have converted

### 3. Which Algemene Bepalingen Apply? — The Most Important Due Diligence Question

Before making any offer, you must establish exactly which AB apply. This determines:
- Whether there will be a canon revision (herziening) and when
- Whether you can still convert to AB2016 (eeuwigdurende erfpacht) and at what cost
- The financial implications for the total cost of ownership

### 4. AB2016 — Eeuwigdurende Erfpacht (The Reformed Amsterdam System)

Since 1 July 2016, the Gemeente Amsterdam issues all new erfpacht under the **AB2016** (eeuwigdurende erfpacht) conditions:
- **Eeuwigdurend** (perpetual, no end date, no canon revision)
- Canon is either:
  - A fixed annual amount (jaarlijkse canon), indexed only with CPI
  - Or fully bought off in a lump sum (eeuwigdurende afkoop) — a one-time payment

If your apartment is already on AB2016, the canon revision risk is eliminated (or minimized to CPI adjustments only). If the apartment is on older conditions, the seller may or may not have applied to convert (overstappen) to AB2016.

### 5. Overstapregeling — Conversion to AB2016

Erfpachters on AB2000 or older conditions can apply to convert (overstappen) to eeuwigdurende erfpacht under AB2016. The cost of conversion depends on:
- The BSQ (buurtstraatquote) for the neighborhood — a ratio of ground value to property value
- The WOZ value (used as a proxy for market value)
- Whether you choose annual canon or full afkoop

If the seller has not yet converted, you as the buyer may be able to convert after purchase, but at the cost applicable at that time (which may be higher if property values have risen further). Alternatively, the conversion cost can be negotiated into the purchase price.

### 6. Financing Implications

**Mortgage lenders** apply strict criteria to erfpacht properties. If the erfpacht conditions are unfavorable (e.g., canon revision imminent, old AB, no conversion), some banks may:
- Refuse to provide a mortgage
- Apply a lower loan-to-value (LTV) ratio
- Require the canon to be fully bought off (afkoop) as a condition of financing

This is a critical practical concern: if your preferred lender does not accept the erfpacht conditions, you may not be able to finance the purchase.

### 7. Notarial Requirements

Transfer of the apartment (the appartementsrecht on erfpacht land) requires:
- A **notariele akte van levering** (notarial deed of transfer) — mandatory under **Art. 3:89 BW**
- Registration at the **kadaster**
- The notaris must verify:
  - The erfpacht conditions (erfpachtvoorwaarden) and applicable AB
  - Any canon arrears (any outstanding canon debt transfers with the right)
  - Whether municipal consent (toestemmingsvereiste) is required for transfer (Art. 5:91 BW)
  - The VvE (Vereniging van Eigenaren) status (for apartments, both VvE obligations and erfpacht conditions apply)

The notaris is your primary legal safeguard in the transfer process — ensure you use a notaris with specific experience in Amsterdam erfpacht transactions.

## Practical Requirements

### Key Due Diligence Steps Before Making an Offer

1. **Request the erfpachtakte and applicable Algemene Bepalingen** from the seller. The seller has a disclosure obligation (mededelingsplicht) — failure to disclose known material erfpacht issues can give rise to non-conformity claims (Art. 7:17 BW).

2. **Establish the applicable AB version**: AB1966, AB2000, AB2016, or earlier? This determines all subsequent risks.

3. **Identify the next revision date** (herziening): When is the next canonherziening due? The closer this date, the higher the risk and the lower the property value should be.

4. **Calculate the expected canon after revision**: Have an advisor or the Gemeente itself provide an indicative canon figure after revision, based on current ground values.

5. **Check if AB2016 conversion is possible** (and at what cost): If not yet converted, request a quote from the Gemeente Amsterdam for conversion (overstapaanbieding). Compare the cost to the benefit of fixing the canon.

6. **Verify with the kadaster**: Do not rely solely on the seller's documents. Verify the erfpacht conditions directly from the kadaster extract (eigendomsinformatie / erfpachtakte).

7. **Check for canon arrears**: Ensure no canon is in arrears — outstanding canon debt transfers with the erfpacht right to the buyer.

8. **Assess VvE position**: For apartments, check both the VvE (splitsingsakte, reservefonds, servicekosten) and the erfpacht conditions.

9. **Consult your mortgage advisor**: Before making an offer, confirm that your bank will provide a mortgage on the property under the applicable erfpacht conditions.

## Risks and Protections

### Key Risks

| Risk | Description | Severity |
|---|---|---|
| **Canonherziening** | If canon has not been revised for 20 years and revision is due, the new canon could be dramatically higher — potentially making the property unaffordable | Very high |
| **Tijdelijk erfpacht expiry** | If on older AB with fixed term, the term may be approaching expiry — renewal terms may be unfavorable | High (if applicable) |
| **Financing refused** | Banks may refuse mortgage on unfavorable erfpacht conditions | High |
| **Conversion cost** | Converting to AB2016 can cost tens of thousands to hundreds of thousands of euros depending on property value and location | Medium to high |
| **VvE + erfpacht interaction** | Both VvE obligations (servicekosten, reservefonds) and erfpacht obligations (canon) add to total housing costs | Medium |
| **Transfer consent** | If toestemmingsvereiste applies (Art. 5:91 BW), gemeente consent is needed for transfer — delays possible | Low to medium |

### Protections Available

- **Art. 5:87 BW**: The grondeigenaar (Gemeente Amsterdam) can only terminate erfpacht for serious breach or 2+ years' canon arrears — as an erfpachter, you have strong security of tenure
- **Redelijkheid en billijkheid** (Art. 6:248 BW): Canon revision clauses can be tested against reasonableness and fairness — as confirmed in HR 26 March 1999 and the 2010 Gerechtshof Amsterdam case
- **AB2016 conversion**: Converting to eeuwigdurende erfpacht eliminates the revision risk
- **Notarial protection**: The notaris is required to disclose all erfpacht conditions and verify their content before transfer

## Recommendations

1. **Make the purchase conditional** on obtaining a satisfactory assessment of the erfpacht conditions (or include an ontbindende voorwaarde relating to erfpacht due diligence).

2. **Request an overstapaanbieding** (conversion quote) from the Gemeente Amsterdam before completing the purchase, so you know the full cost of converting to AB2016.

3. **Negotiate the purchase price** to reflect the canon revision risk, the conversion cost, and any potential financing restrictions. Properties subject to unfavorable erfpacht conditions should trade at a discount to comparable vol eigendom (freehold) properties.

4. **Engage a notaris with Amsterdam erfpacht expertise** — not all notarissen have deep familiarity with the Amsterdam system. Ask specifically about their experience with AB2000/AB2016 transactions.

5. **Consult a real estate lawyer (vastgoedadvocaat) or specialist makelaar** familiar with Amsterdam erfpacht to assess the specific risks of this property before committing.

6. **Get a mortgage offer in principle** (hypotheekofferte) confirming that your lender accepts the property's erfpacht conditions before making a binding offer.

7. **Request an erfpacht waardebepaling**: In Amsterdam, the Gemeentelijk Grondbedrijf can in some cases provide information about the expected canon after revision — ask for this before purchase.
