## Analysis

### 1. What is Erfpacht and Why Does Amsterdam Use It?

**Erfpacht** is a beperkt zakelijk recht (limited right in rem) under **Art. 5:85 BW** entitling the holder (erfpachter) to hold and use immovable property belonging to another person (the grondeigenaar / bloot eigenaar — in Amsterdam, the Gemeente). The erfpachter has near-complete enjoyment of the property, similar to full ownership, but pays a periodic fee (the **canon**) to the landowner.

The **Gemeente Amsterdam** owns approximately **80% of the land** in Amsterdam. Since 1896, Amsterdam has issued erfpacht rights rather than selling the underlying land outright, retaining public control over land use and capturing land value increases for the community.

**Erfpacht is a registered right at the kadaster** — it is transferable and can be encumbered with a mortgage, making home ownership on erfpacht land functionally similar to vol eigendom (full ownership) in many respects.

### 2. Understanding the Amsterdam Erfpacht System — Critical Due Diligence

The most critical factor for this purchase is determining **which set of Algemene Bepalingen (AB / General Conditions)** applies to this specific erfpacht right. Amsterdam has used different systems over time:

#### AB2016 — Eeuwigdurende Erfpacht (Introduced 1 July 2016)
- **No end date** and **no canon revision** (eeuwigdurend = perpetual)
- Canon is either:
  - **Paid annually** (vast jaarlijks canon): fixed amount, indexed only with CPI (consumer price index) — no market-based revision
  - **Bought off as a lump sum** (eeuwigdurende afkoop): one-time payment, no further annual canon
- This is the **most favorable system for buyers** — maximum certainty over future housing costs
- Applies to all new erfpacht issued after 1 July 2016
- Existing erfpachters under older conditions can voluntarily convert (overstappen) to AB2016

#### AB2000 — Voortdurende Erfpacht (The Problematic System)
- **No end date** (perpetual) but canon is subject to **periodic herziening (revision)**
- Under AB2000: canon revised every **50 years** based on the **residuele grondwaarde** (residual ground value) at the time of revision
- The 20-year gap since the last revision is a **MAJOR RED FLAG** — if this property is under AB2000 or similar conditions, a canon revision may be imminent or overdue
- The canon revision is calculated based on current market ground values — in Amsterdam's dramatically rising real estate market, this can result in a **canonsprong (canon shock)**
- Example: A canon of EUR 500/year can increase to EUR 5,000–10,000/year or more after revision, depending on the location and current ground values
- AB2000 erfpachters who did NOT convert to AB2016 still face this revision risk

#### AB1994 — Similar revision mechanism to AB2000 (periodic revision)

#### AB1966 and older (AB1934, AB1915) — Tijdelijk Erfpacht
- Fixed **75-year or 50-year term** (tijdelijk)
- Canon revision at end of term
- If the erfpacht term expires soon, the Gemeente sets entirely new conditions — including potentially much higher canon — which can be devastating for the property value and financing

### 3. The 20-Year Canon Gap — What It Means

The fact that the **canon has not been revised in 20 years** is a critical signal that requires detailed investigation:

**Scenario A: The property is under AB2000 (50-year revision cycle)**
- The 20-year gap means the first revision is approximately 30 years away
- This provides some medium-term certainty but the eventual revision will be based on ground values at that future time
- Buyers should understand and financially plan for this future revision
- Consider converting to AB2016 before or after purchase

**Scenario B: The property is under AB1966 or other older tijdelijk conditions (75-year term)**
- If the 75-year term is approaching expiry, this is very serious
- The Gemeente will set entirely new canon conditions upon renewal, typically based on current ground values
- Example: An AB1966 erfpacht from 1966 would expire around 2041 — 15 years away
- Buyers should assess: when does the term expire? What are the renewal terms?

**Scenario C: The property was on the overstapregeling but the erfpachter did NOT convert**
- Since 2017, AB2000 erfpachters had the opportunity to convert to AB2016 (eeuwigdurend)
- If this opportunity was not taken, the property remains under the old revision-based system
- Verify: has an overstap (conversion) to AB2016 been offered and declined? Or is the overstapperiod still open?

**Scenario D: The property is already under AB2016 (eeuwigdurend)**
- The 20-year gap simply represents 20 years of CPI-indexed canon — no market revision
- This is the most favorable situation

**The 20-year figure means: establish which system applies before proceeding further.**

### 4. The Canonherziening / Canon Revision Risk

Under the older AB systems (AB2000 and earlier), the **canonherziening** is the central financial risk:

**How the canon is calculated in Amsterdam**:
1. Determine the **marktwaarde** (market value) of the full property
2. Subtract the depreciated replacement value of the buildings (afgeschreven herbouwwaarde)
3. The remainder is the **grondwaarde** (ground value)
4. Apply the **canonpercentage** (approximately 2.39% for residential in 2024/2025 — verify annually)
5. Result = annual canon

**The risk for Amsterdam properties**: Amsterdam has seen property value increases of 100–300% over the past 20 years. A canon last set 20 years ago at EUR 1,000/year based on a ground value of, say, EUR 40,000 would, upon revision, be recalculated based on today's much higher property values — potentially resulting in an annual canon of EUR 5,000–15,000 or more, depending on the property.

**This is not a hypothetical risk** — thousands of Amsterdam homeowners have experienced exactly this canonsprong, leading to court proceedings (Gerechtshof Amsterdam 2010, ECLI:NL:GHAMS:2010:BN2643) and collective actions.

### 5. The Notaris — Non-Negotiable Role

The purchase of an apartment in Amsterdam requires involvement of a **notaris (civil law notary)** at multiple stages:

**At purchase (levering)**:
- Transfer of the erfpacht right to the new owner requires a **notariele akte van levering** (notarial transfer deed) and registration at the **kadaster** (Art. 3:89 BW)
- The notaris must verify the erfpacht conditions, outstanding canon, any transfer restrictions (toestemmingsvereiste, Art. 5:91 BW), and whether Gemeente consent is needed
- The notaris performs eigendomsonderzoek (title search) and hypotheekonderzoek (mortgage search) at the kadaster
- If canon arrears exist, these can transfer with the erfpacht right — the notaris must check

**For the mortgage**:
- Banks finance erfpacht properties but the mortgage must be on the erfpacht right (not the land)
- Banks may apply stricter conditions for properties with impending canon revision or short remaining term

**The notaris should specifically advise you on the erfpacht conditions** as part of their legal examination. If your notaris does not specifically address the erfpacht type, revision schedule, and canon risk, insist on this analysis before signing.

### 6. Apartment-Specific Considerations (Appartementsrecht + VvE)

Since this is an apartment (appartementsrecht), there is an additional layer:
- The apartment right (appartementsrecht) is established by a **splitsingsakte** (deed of division)
- A **VvE (Vereniging van Eigenaren)** exists and governs the building
- Both the VvE rules and the erfpacht conditions apply — check for any interaction or conflicts
- The VvE's mortgage lender may have requirements about the erfpacht conditions
- Verify the VvE has adequate reserves (reservefonds) — required for mortgage approval at most banks
- The Gemeente's erfpacht may include provisions about what the erfpachter can do with the apartment — check the bestemming (use restrictions) in the erfpachtvoorwaarden

## Practical Requirements

### Essential Due Diligence Before Purchase

1. **Obtain the complete erfpachtakte (vestigingsakte)** from the kadaster — do not rely on documents provided by the seller
2. **Determine which Algemene Bepalingen apply**: AB1915, AB1934, AB1966, AB1994, AB2000, or AB2016
3. **Check the canon revision schedule**: When was the canon last set? When is the next revision due? What is the methodology?
4. **Calculate the potential canon after revision**: Use the Amsterdam Gemeente's online calculation tool or commission an independent valuation of the ground value
5. **Check whether AB2016 conversion (overstap) is possible**: Contact the Gemeente Amsterdam's erfpacht department (Grondzaken) to verify whether conversion to eeuwigdurend is still available and at what cost
6. **Verify the current canon amount** and confirm it is being paid (no arrears)
7. **Check for transfer restrictions** (toestemmingsvereiste, Art. 5:91 BW) — does the Gemeente need to approve the transfer?
8. **Review the VvE** — financial health, reserve fund, any pending major maintenance
9. **Assess mortgage financing** — confirm your lender will finance this specific erfpacht and check any conditions imposed

### Professionals Required

- **Notaris**: Mandatory for the levering (transfer); must examine erfpacht conditions carefully
- **Makelaar** (real estate agent): Should advise on erfpacht implications for property value
- **Erfpacht specialist**: For complex or unclear erfpacht conditions, an independent juridisch adviseur or notaris specializing in erfpacht is advisable
- **Taxateur** (valuer): Property valuation should explicitly account for erfpacht conditions
- **Hypotheekadviseur** (mortgage advisor): Must assess whether the bank will finance under the specific erfpacht conditions

## Risks and Protections

### Red Flags in This Transaction

| Risk | Description | Severity |
|------|-------------|---------|
| Canon revision imminent | Under AB2000 with 50-year cycle, 20 years in — 30 years to next revision. Under AB1966-tijdelijk approaching end of term — potentially very near canon reset | VERY HIGH if tijdelijk; MEDIUM if voortdurend |
| No AB2016 conversion | Property still under revision-based system; failed or missed overstapperiode | HIGH |

| Transfer restriction | Gemeente consent may be required (Art. 5:91 BW) | MEDIUM |
| Mortgage rejection | Banks may refuse to finance on unfavorable erfpacht conditions | HIGH |
| Property value discount | Erfpacht properties trade at discount vs. vol eigendom; revision risk amplifies discount | MEDIUM |

### Potential Mitigation

- **Convert to AB2016** before or immediately after purchase (if still possible) — eliminating the revision risk entirely
- **Buy off the canon** (eeuwigdurende afkoop) to eliminate future annual payments — calculate net present value vs. upfront cost
- **Include a resolutive condition** in the koopovereenkomst (sale agreement) allowing withdrawal if erfpacht conditions are less favorable than disclosed, or if mortgage cannot be obtained

1. **Prioritize identifying the applicable AB** — this single factor determines your entire risk profile; contact the Gemeente Amsterdam Grondzaken department directly if uncertain
2. **Commission a specialist erfpacht assessment** before signing the koopovereenkomst — understand the actual canon revision risk before committing
