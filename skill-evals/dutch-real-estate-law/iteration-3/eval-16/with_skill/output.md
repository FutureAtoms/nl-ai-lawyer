## Analysis

### 1. The Art. 7:290 Regime — Why It Matters

The **Art. 7:290 BW regime** (middenstandsbedrijfsruimte) applies to premises used for:
- Retail space (winkelruimte) accessible to the public for direct supply of goods/services
- Restaurant, cafe, hotel (horeca)
- Camp site
- Craft workshop (ambachtsbedrijf) accessible to the public

If the property qualifies as **Art. 7:290 bedrijfsruimte**, it is subject to **significant statutory tenant protection**, including:
- Mandatory minimum lease duration of 5+5 years (Art. 7:292 BW)
- Limited grounds for termination by the landlord (Art. 7:293–7:296 BW)
- One-year notice period before end of lease period
- These protections are **semi-dwingend** (semi-mandatory): they cannot be deviated from to the tenant's detriment except by court or in certain specific circumstances

**Key distinction**: If the premises is **not** public-facing retail/hospitality but rather an office, warehouse, or other commercial space, the **Art. 7:230a BW** regime applies — which provides much more limited tenant protection. Confirm which regime applies based on the actual use of the premises.

**For this analysis**: We proceed on the basis that the property qualifies as Art. 7:290 BW middenstandsbedrijfsruimte (as stated in the question).

### 2. The Grounds for Termination: Dissolution for Non-Payment

**3 months of unpaid rent** is a significant breach of the lease. The applicable legal routes are:

#### Route 1: Ontbinding (Dissolution) — Art. 6:265 BW via the Kantonrechter

Under **Art. 6:265 BW**, any significant failure in the performance of an obligation (tekortkoming in de nakoming) entitles the non-defaulting party to dissolve the agreement (ontbinding), unless the failure is of insufficient importance (tenzij de tekortkoming gezien haar bijzondere aard of geringe betekenis, deze ontbinding niet rechtvaardigt).

For a lease, **dissolution requires a court judgment (vonnis) from the kantonrechter** — a landlord cannot self-help evict a tenant from commercial premises.

**Pre-conditions for dissolution (ingebrekestelling)**:

Under **Art. 6:82 BW**, the debtor (tenant) must be in **verzuim (default)** before dissolution can be claimed. For a monetary obligation like rent, the tenant is generally in default after the payment date has passed without payment. However, it is standard and strongly advisable to send a written **ingebrekestelling (notice of default)** providing the tenant a final opportunity to pay.

**Required content of the ingebrekestelling**:
- Clear identification of the breach (non-payment of rent for specified months, with amounts)
- The total amount outstanding
- A **reasonable final payment deadline** — typically 14 days in commercial practice
- Warning that failure to pay will result in legal proceedings (including claim for dissolution and eviction)
- Reference to the lease agreement

The ingebrekestelling should be sent by **registered letter (aangetekende post)** or other means providing proof of delivery (e.g., process server / deurwaarder).

#### Proportionality Requirement — Art. 6:265 BW Tenzij-Clause

Per **HR 30 November 2012, ECLI:NL:HR:2012:BX5585** and **HR 28 September 2018, ECLI:NL:HR:2018:1810**, the court must assess whether the breach (tekortkoming) justifies dissolution given its **nature and significance** (bijzondere aard of geringe betekenis). This is the "tenzij-clause" of Art. 6:265 BW.

**For commercial leases (Art. 7:290) with 3 months' rent arrears**:
- 3 months' arrears is generally considered a serious and significant breach
- Courts typically grant dissolution of commercial leases for rent arrears of 3 or more months
- However, the court will consider mitigating circumstances: Has the tenant paid previously? Is there a genuine dispute about the rent amount? Has COVID-19 or another extraordinary event affected the tenant's ability to pay? Has the landlord contributed to the situation?
- **Practical note**: 3 months' arrears is the typical threshold at which courts grant dissolution of commercial leases in the Netherlands. The tenant may offer to pay the arrears during proceedings — the court has discretion to take this into account

#### Route 2: Termination by Notice (Opzegging) — Less Relevant for Non-Payment

The Art. 7:293–7:296 BW termination grounds (opzegging) include "slecht huurderschap" (bad tenancy) which covers persistent non-payment. However, opzegging requires:
- Minimum 1-year notice before end of the current 5-year period
- Following the specific procedural requirements of Art. 7:294–7:296 BW
- Court confirmation if the tenant contests

This route is **procedurally more cumbersome** for an immediate non-payment situation than seeking dissolution under Art. 6:265 BW. The dissolution route via the kantonrechter is the standard approach for non-payment situations.

### 3. The Court Procedure — Kantonrechter

For the dissolution and eviction of an Art. 7:290 commercial tenant, the competent court is the **kantonrechter** (sector kanton of the Rechtbank).

#### Option A: Regular Proceedings (Dagvaardingsprocedure)

1. Send the ingebrekestelling and allow the deadline to pass
2. Instruct a deurwaarder (process server) to serve a dagvaarding (summons) on the tenant
3. The tenant is summoned to appear before the kantonrechter
4. The kantonrechter hears the case at the rol hearing and sets a date for a full hearing
5. Both parties may submit written statements (conclusies)
6. Oral hearing (zitting) — typically within 3–6 months of the dagvaarding
7. The kantonrechter issues a vonnis (judgment) — typically granting:
   - Dissolution of the lease (ontbinding)
   - Order to vacate (ontruiming) within a specified period (typically 14–30 days from judgment)
   - Payment of rent arrears plus statutory interest (wettelijke handelsrente, Art. 6:119a BW)
   - Legal costs (proceskosten)

**Timeline**: Regular proceedings: approximately 3–6 months to judgment.

#### Option B: Kort Geding (Preliminary Injunction Procedure)

If urgency justifies it (e.g., the property is being damaged or there is an acute financial impact), a **kort geding** (Art. 254 Rv) before the kantonrechter can provide faster interim relief:
- Typically within 4–8 weeks of filing
- The kantonrechter can grant a provisional eviction order (ontruimingsvonnis)
- **Limitation**: The kort geding judgment is provisional; the tenant can challenge it in full proceedings (bodemzaak)
- **Requires**: Demonstration of urgency (spoedeisend belang) beyond the normal urgency of non-payment

For straightforward non-payment cases, the **regular dagvaarding procedure** is more common and ultimately more definitive than kort geding.

### 4. The Eviction (Ontruiming) Procedure

Once the kantonrechter has issued an **ontruimingsvonnis** (eviction judgment):

1. The judgment must be served on the tenant by a **deurwaarder**
2. The deurwaarder gives the tenant a specific deadline to vacate voluntarily
3. If the tenant does not vacate voluntarily: the deurwaarder executes the eviction with, if necessary, assistance from the **politie (police)**
4. The tenant's property found on the premises during forced eviction is typically stored briefly before disposal

**Self-help eviction is illegal**: The landlord cannot change locks, remove the tenant's property, or cut off utilities to force the tenant out — this constitutes onrechtmatige daad (tortious conduct) and can expose the landlord to significant liability.

### 5. Recovery of Rent Arrears

In addition to dissolution and eviction, the landlord can claim:

- **Huurpenningen (rent arrears)**: all unpaid rent at the time of judgment
- **Wettelijke handelsrente (statutory commercial interest, Art. 6:119a BW)**: automatically accrues on commercial debts from the due date
- **Huurderving (lost rent)**: for the period between the end of the lease and re-letting the property (in some cases)
- **Herstelkosten (restoration costs)**: if the tenant has caused damage to the property beyond normal wear and tear

These claims are typically included in the dagvaarding for the dissolution/eviction proceedings.

### 6. Risk Factors and Tenant Defenses

Be aware that the tenant may raise the following defenses:

- **Gebreken (defects)**: If the property has defects (Art. 7:204 BW), the tenant may claim a right to reduce rent (huurprijsvermindering) or withhold rent. Assess whether any pending maintenance disputes exist.
- **Covid or extraordinary circumstances**: While less commonly accepted now, tenants may still raise force majeure arguments
- **Counterclaim**: The tenant may assert claims against the landlord (e.g., for failure to maintain the property, unlawful rent increases)
- **Payment during proceedings**: If the tenant pays the full arrears during the court procedure, the court may not grant dissolution — it retains discretion under the Art. 6:265 tenzij-clause

**Mitigation**: Maintain the property properly, respond to maintenance requests, and document any history of the tenant's payment behavior. A clean landlord record strengthens the dissolution claim.

## Practical Requirements — Step by Step

### Phase 1: Pre-Litigation (Immediate)

1. **Calculate the exact arrears** — identify all unpaid rent months, amounts, and due dates
2. **Review the lease agreement** — confirm the rent amount, payment terms, any dispute mechanism, and any applicable CAO or specific provisions
3. **Send a formal ingebrekestelling** — by registered post, giving the tenant 14 days to pay the full arrears
4. **Document all evidence of non-payment** — bank statements showing absence of payments, payment history, any communication with the tenant

### Phase 2: Legal Proceedings (After ingebrekestelling deadline passes)

5. **Engage a lawyer (advocaat) or deurwaarder** — the dagvaarding must be drafted and served by a deurwaarder; legal representation is strongly advisable
6. **File the dagvaarding** with the kantonrechter — claiming dissolution, eviction, and payment of arrears + interest
7. **Attend the hearing** and respond to any tenant defenses
8. **Obtain the vonnis** — the kantonrechter's judgment

### Phase 3: Execution (After judgment)

9. **Have the vonnis served on the tenant** by a deurwaarder
10. **Allow the voluntary vacation deadline** to pass
11. **Execute forced eviction** via deurwaarder (and police if needed)
12. **Inspect the property** for damage and document restoration obligations
