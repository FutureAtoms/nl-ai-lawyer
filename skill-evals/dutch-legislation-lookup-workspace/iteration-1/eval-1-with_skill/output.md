## Relevant Provisions

### Artikel 7:652 BW -- Proeftijd (Probation Period)

**Text (Dutch)**:
> **Lid 1.** Indien partijen een proeftijd overeenkomen, is deze voor beide partijen gelijk.
>
> **Lid 2.** De proeftijd wordt schriftelijk overeengekomen.
>
> **Lid 3.** Bij het aangaan van een arbeidsovereenkomst voor onbepaalde tijd kan een proeftijd worden overeengekomen van ten hoogste twee maanden.
>
> **Lid 4.** Bij het aangaan van een arbeidsovereenkomst voor bepaalde tijd kan een proeftijd worden overeengekomen van:
> a. ten hoogste een maand, indien de overeenkomst is aangegaan voor korter dan twee jaar;
> b. ten hoogste twee maanden, indien de overeenkomst is aangegaan voor twee jaar of langer.
>
> **Lid 5.** Geen proeftijd kan worden overeengekomen indien de arbeidsovereenkomst is aangegaan voor ten hoogste zes maanden.
>
> **Lid 6.** Er kan geen proeftijd worden overeengekomen indien de werknemer eerder bij dezelfde werkgever in dienst is geweest voor dezelfde of soortgelijke werkzaamheden, tenzij die arbeidsovereenkomst meer dan zes maanden geleden is geeindigd. Dit geldt eveneens indien de werknemer eerder bij een andere werkgever in dienst is geweest, indien die werkgever redelijkerwijs geacht moet worden ten aanzien van de verrichte arbeid de opvolger van de vorige werkgever te zijn.
>
> **Lid 7.** Elk beding waarbij de proeftijd op een langere termijn wordt gesteld dan in de leden 3 en 4 is aangegeven, is nietig. Is een proeftijd overeengekomen die langer is dan de toegestane duur, dan geldt dat de proeftijd in haar geheel nietig is.
>
> **Lid 8.** Van de leden 1 tot en met 7 kan niet ten nadele van de werknemer worden afgeweken.

**Translation/Explanation (English)**:

Article 7:652 BW is the central provision governing probation periods in Dutch employment law. Here is a plain-language summary of each subsection:

- **Lid 1 (Equal duration):** If the parties agree to a probation period, it must be equal in duration for both the employer and the employee. Neither party can have a longer or shorter probation than the other.

- **Lid 2 (Written requirement):** The probation period must be agreed upon **in writing**. An oral probation agreement is not valid. This is a strict formal requirement.

- **Lid 3 (Permanent contracts -- max 2 months):** For an employment contract for an indefinite period (onbepaalde tijd), the maximum probation period is **two months**.

- **Lid 4 (Fixed-term contracts -- duration-dependent):**
  - If the fixed-term contract is for **less than two years**: maximum **one month** probation.
  - If the fixed-term contract is for **two years or longer**: maximum **two months** probation.

- **Lid 5 (Short contracts -- no probation):** No probation period may be agreed if the employment contract is for **six months or shorter**. This was introduced by the Wet Werk en Zekerheid (WWZ) in 2015 to protect short-term workers.

- **Lid 6 (Successive employment prohibition):** A new probation period cannot be agreed if the employee previously worked for the same employer in the same or similar role, unless that previous employment ended more than six months ago. This also applies when a new employer is effectively the successor of the previous employer (opvolgend werkgeverschap).

- **Lid 7 (Nullity / nietigheid):** Any clause setting a probation period longer than the statutory maximum is **void (nietig)** -- and importantly, if the agreed probation exceeds the limit, the **entire probation clause is void**, not just the excess. This is a critical rule: the consequence is not reduction to the maximum, but complete nullity.

- **Lid 8 (Semi-mandatory law):** The provisions of lids 1 through 7 are **semi-dwingend recht** (semi-mandatory law): they cannot be deviated from to the detriment of the employee. A CAO (collective labor agreement) may deviate, but only if this does not disadvantage the employee.

### Artikel 7:676 BW -- Opzegging tijdens proeftijd (Termination during probation)

**Text (Dutch)**:
> **Lid 1.** Gedurende de proeftijd kan ieder der partijen de arbeidsovereenkomst met onmiddellijke ingang opzeggen.
>
> **Lid 2.** Degene die opzegt, is verplicht de wederpartij op diens verzoek de reden van de opzegging schriftelijk mede te delen.

**Translation/Explanation (English)**:
- **Lid 1:** During the probation period, either party may terminate the employment contract with **immediate effect** (met onmiddellijke ingang). No notice period is required. No ground for dismissal (ontslaggrond) needs to be established. No permission from UWV (employment agency) or the cantonal court (kantonrechter) is needed.

- **Lid 2:** The party who terminates is obligated, upon request by the other party, to provide the **reason for termination in writing**. This is important: while no specific ground is legally required, the other party has a right to know the reason.

**Legal Context**:
- Type: Dwingend recht (mandatory law)
- Part of: BW Boek 7, Titel 10, Afdeling 4
- Related provisions: Art. 7:652 BW (probation clause validity), Art. 7:611 BW (good employer conduct)

## Practical Application

### Summary of Maximum Probation Durations

| Contract Type | Maximum Proeftijd |
|---|---|
| Onbepaalde tijd (permanent) | 2 months |
| Bepaalde tijd >= 2 years | 2 months |
| Bepaalde tijd > 6 months but < 2 years | 1 month |
| Bepaalde tijd <= 6 months | No probation allowed |

### Key Practical Rules

1. **Always in writing**: The probation clause must appear in the employment contract (or attached letter). Failure to put it in writing means there is no valid probation period.

2. **Simultaneous with the start of employment**: The probation period must be agreed at the time the contract is entered into. It cannot be added later.

3. **Both parties equal**: The probation must be the same for employer and employee. A clause stating "1 month for the employee, 2 months for the employer" is void.

4. **Exceeding the maximum voids the entire clause**: Unlike many other clauses where an excessive term is reduced to the maximum, an excessive probation clause is void **in its entirety** (Art. 7:652 lid 7 BW). For example, if a 1-year fixed-term contract includes a 2-month probation clause (where the max is 1 month), the **entire probation clause is void**, and the employee is immediately protected by regular dismissal law.

5. **No probation on successive contracts**: If an employee previously worked for the same employer in the same function, a new probation period cannot be included unless more than six months have passed (Art. 7:652 lid 6 BW).

6. **CAO provisions**: A collective labor agreement (CAO) may contain specific probation rules, but these cannot be less favorable to the employee than the statutory rules.

7. **Discrimination prohibition**: Although dismissal during probation does not require a ground, it may **not** be based on discriminatory grounds. The Algemene wet gelijke behandeling (AWGB) and related anti-discrimination legislation still apply. Dismissal during probation on grounds of pregnancy, religion, race, gender, disability, or other protected characteristics is unlawful.

## Key Case Law

- **HR 13 January 1995, NJ 1995/430** -- The Hoge Raad confirmed that even during probation, the employer's freedom to dismiss is not unlimited: the prohibition of discrimination applies, and the principle of good employer conduct (Art. 7:611 BW) imposes boundaries.

- **HR 10 November 2000, JAR 2000/249** -- Clarification that the reason for probation dismissal must be related to the purpose of the probation period (assessing suitability). Dismissal for reasons unrelated to assessing the employee (e.g., economic reasons known before hiring) can be considered abuse of the probation right (misbruik van proeftijd).

- **Kantonrechter jurisprudentie** -- Various lower court decisions have held that dismissal during probation motivated by pregnancy, illness discovered during probation, or other discriminatory grounds is unlawful, and the employee may claim damages (schadevergoeding) or reinstatement.

## Recent / Pending Changes

- The **Wet Werk en Zekerheid (WWZ, 2015)** introduced the prohibition on probation periods in contracts of six months or shorter (Art. 7:652 lid 5 BW).

- **EU Directive 2019/1152** on transparent and predictable working conditions (implemented in the Netherlands on 1 August 2022 via the Wet implementatie EU-richtlijn transparante en voorspelbare arbeidsvoorwaarden) capped probation at six months and introduced requirements that probation must be proportionate. The Dutch implementation largely aligned with existing Art. 7:652 BW rules, which were already more protective than the Directive's minimum.

- There has been ongoing political discussion about potentially extending probation periods for permanent contracts (e.g., allowing up to 5 months for onbepaalde tijd contracts), which appeared in the 2019 coalition agreement but was not legislated. This remains a topic of potential future reform. As of March 2026, no such extension has been enacted.
