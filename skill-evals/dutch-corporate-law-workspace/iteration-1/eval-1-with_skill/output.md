## Corporate Law Analysis

**Entity Type**: BV (Besloten Vennootschap met beperkte aansprakelijkheid)
**Legal Question**: What is the minimum share capital for a Dutch BV, what governance structure is recommended for a two-founder setup (60/40 split), and what should be included in the shareholders' agreement (aandeelhoudersovereenkomst)?

## Applicable Legal Framework

The formation and governance of a Dutch BV are governed by **Burgerlijk Wetboek Boek 2 (BW Boek 2), Titel 4** (Art. 2:175-2:284a), as amended by the **Flex-BV Act** (Wet vereenvoudiging en flexibilisering bv-recht), which entered into force on **1 October 2012**. Additional applicable legislation includes:

- **Handelsregisterwet 2007** - KVK registration requirements
- **Wet op het notarisambt** - Notarial deed requirement for incorporation
- **Wet bestuur en toezicht rechtspersonen (WBTR)** - Since 2021, codifies director duties
- **Dutch Corporate Governance Code (2022)** - Applies to listed companies on a comply-or-explain basis, but its principles serve as best-practice guidance for private BVs as well

## Analysis

### 1. Minimum Share Capital

Since the introduction of the **Flex-BV Act** on 1 October 2012, there is **no statutory minimum share capital** for a BV. Previously, the minimum was EUR 18,000.

Under the current regime:

- The minimum nominal value per share is **EUR 0.01** (Art. 2:178 BW).
- Shares may be denominated in **any currency** (Art. 2:178 lid 2 BW).
- There is no requirement for a bank statement for cash contributions (abolished by Flex-BV).
- Multiple classes of shares are permitted, including ordinary shares, preference shares, priority shares, non-voting shares (stemrechtloze aandelen), and non-profit shares (winstrechtloze aandelen) (Art. 2:192 BW).

**Practical recommendation**: While EUR 0.01 is the legal minimum, it is common practice to incorporate with a share capital between **EUR 100 and EUR 1,000** to demonstrate substance and facilitate banking relationships. For a 60/40 split, you could structure:

- **60 shares** of EUR 1.00 each to Founder A = EUR 60
- **40 shares** of EUR 1.00 each to Founder B = EUR 40
- Total share capital: EUR 100

This creates a clean, proportional structure aligned with the intended shareholding percentages.

**Important**: The board must perform a **uitkeringstest** (distribution test) before any dividend payment or other distribution to shareholders (Art. 2:216 BW). Directors who approve a distribution knowing the BV cannot continue to pay its debts may be **personally liable** (Art. 2:216 lid 3 BW).

### 2. Governance Structure

For a two-founder BV with a 60/40 split, two governance structures are available under Dutch law:

#### Option A: Two-Tier Board (Recommended for this situation)

**Management Board (Bestuur)** - Art. 2:239 BW:
- Both founders appointed as managing directors (bestuurders).
- Art. 2:242 BW: Directors are appointed by the general meeting of shareholders (Algemene Vergadering, AVA), unless the articles of association provide for appointment by a class meeting or supervisory board.
- Art. 2:244 BW: Directors can be suspended and dismissed by the body that appointed them.
- Consider **joint signing authority** (gezamenlijk bevoegd) registered at the KVK to ensure neither founder can unilaterally bind the company.
- Alternatively, each director could be **individually authorized** (zelfstandig bevoegd) up to a threshold amount, with joint authority required above that threshold.

**Advisory Board (Raad van Advies)**:
- An advisory board (raad van advies) has **no statutory basis** in BW Boek 2 -- unlike the formal supervisory board (Raad van Commissarissen, Art. 2:250 BW), which has legally defined powers and duties.
- An advisory board is an **informal body** whose role, composition, and powers are defined entirely by the articles of association or a separate charter (reglement).
- The advisory board typically advises the management board on strategy, market opportunities, and industry expertise, but has **no legally binding decision-making authority** and no formal supervision duties.
- This is a pragmatic choice for a startup BV: it provides external expertise without the formal legal obligations and liabilities attached to a statutory supervisory board (Raad van Commissarissen).

**General Meeting of Shareholders (AVA)** - Art. 2:217 ff. BW:
- The AVA is the ultimate decision-making body for matters reserved to it by law or the articles of association.
- In a 60/40 split, the majority shareholder (60%) will control ordinary resolutions (absolute majority required, Art. 2:224 BW).
- **Critical point**: Consider building in **qualified majority requirements** (e.g., 2/3 or 75%) in the articles of association for key decisions to protect the minority shareholder (40%). This is permissible under Art. 2:224 BW.

#### Option B: One-Tier Board (Monistisch bestuursmodel)

- Art. 2:239a BW: The articles may provide for a one-tier board with both executive and non-executive directors.
- Both founders would serve as **executive directors** (uitvoerend bestuurders).
- Advisory board members could serve as **non-executive directors** with formal supervisory duties.
- This is less common for small BVs but may be suitable if the founders want a more integrated governance model.

**Recommendation**: Option A (two-tier structure with an informal advisory board) is most suitable for a two-founder startup BV. It provides flexibility, avoids the formal liabilities and appointment rules associated with a statutory supervisory board, and allows the advisory board to be tailored to the company's needs.

### 3. Aandeelhoudersovereenkomst (Shareholders' Agreement)

The shareholders' agreement is a **private contract** between the shareholders, separate from the articles of association (statuten). While the statuten are a public document filed at the KVK, the aandeelhoudersovereenkomst remains confidential. A well-drafted shareholders' agreement for a 60/40 BV should address:

#### Essential Provisions:

**a. Share Transfer Restrictions**
- **Tag-along rights** (mee-verkooprecht): If the majority (60%) sells, the minority (40%) can join on the same terms.
- **Drag-along rights** (meesleeprecht): If a certain percentage (e.g., 75% or 80%) wants to sell, they can force the remaining shareholders to join.
- **Right of first refusal** (aanbiedingsplicht): Shares must first be offered to existing shareholders before external sale.
- **Lock-up period**: Period during which founders cannot transfer shares (e.g., 2-5 years).
- Note: Share transfers in a BV always require a **notarial deed** (Art. 2:196 BW). The optional blokkeringsregeling (Art. 2:195 BW) may also be included in the articles of association.

**b. Decision-Making and Deadlock**
- **Reserved matters** requiring unanimous consent or qualified majority (e.g., issuance of new shares, major investments above a threshold, entering into debt above a threshold, hiring/firing key personnel, amendment of articles).
- **Deadlock resolution mechanism**: Step-by-step escalation -- e.g., mediation, then binding advice (bindend advies), then arbitration (e.g., Netherlands Arbitration Institute -- NAI).
- **Anti-deadlock buy-out**: "Russian roulette" or "Texas shoot-out" clauses for irreconcilable deadlock situations.

**c. Non-Compete and Non-Solicitation**
- **Non-compete clause** (concurrentiebeding): Founders may not compete with the BV during their involvement and for a reasonable period after exit (typically 1-2 years, limited geographically).
- **Non-solicitation** (relatiebeding): Restrictions on soliciting clients, employees, or suppliers.

**d. Good Leaver / Bad Leaver**
- Define what constitutes a "good leaver" (e.g., death, disability, termination without cause) vs. "bad leaver" (e.g., gross misconduct, voluntary departure within lock-up).
- Good leaver: shares bought at fair market value.
- Bad leaver: shares bought at a discount (e.g., nominal value or cost price).
- Valuation mechanism: independent appraiser, agreed formula, or binding expert determination.

**e. Intellectual Property (IP)**
- All IP created by founders should be assigned to the BV.
- Non-disclosure obligations regarding the BV's confidential information.

**f. Dividend Policy**
- Agree on dividend policy (e.g., minimum distribution percentage of profits, subject to the uitkeringstest under Art. 2:216 BW).
- Board approval is required for any distribution.

**g. Board Composition and Governance**
- Each founder entitled to appoint one director (or both founders serve as directors).
- Advisory board composition and appointment mechanism.
- Matters requiring board unanimity vs. majority.

**h. Financing**
- Obligations regarding additional capital contributions (bijstortingsplicht).
- Shareholder loans: terms, subordination, and conversion mechanisms.
- Pre-emptive rights (voorkeursrecht) for new share issuances (also see Art. 2:206a BW).

**i. Exit and Termination**
- Circumstances triggering a buy-out obligation.
- Valuation method for exit scenarios.
- Liquidation preferences (if applicable).

## Practical Requirements

- **Mandatory steps**:
  1. Engage a **notaris** (notary) to prepare the deed of incorporation and articles of association -- this is a mandatory requirement (Art. 2:175 BW, Art. 2:178 BW).
  2. Draft the **aandeelhoudersovereenkomst** -- typically handled by a corporate lawyer (advocaat or bedrijfsjurist), not the notaris.
  3. Register the BV at the **KVK** (Kamer van Koophandel / Chamber of Commerce) within 8 days of incorporation (Handelsregisterwet 2007).
  4. Register **UBO** (Ultimate Beneficial Owner) information -- both founders will be UBOs as they each hold more than 25% (the 60% shareholder directly, the 40% shareholder directly).
  5. Obtain a **btw-nummer** (VAT number) from the Belastingdienst.
  6. Open a **business bank account**.

- **Timeline**: Incorporation can typically be completed within **1-3 weeks** once all documents are prepared and the notaris has completed identity verification (including Wwft/anti-money laundering checks).

- **Costs** (indicative):
  - Notaris fees for BV incorporation: EUR 500 - EUR 2,000+ (depending on complexity of articles)
  - KVK registration: EUR 75 (one-time)
  - Shareholders' agreement drafting by a lawyer: EUR 1,500 - EUR 5,000+ (depending on complexity)
  - Advisory board charter: EUR 500 - EUR 1,500

- **Required professionals**:
  - **Notaris** (mandatory for incorporation)
  - **Advocaat / Corporate lawyer** (strongly recommended for the shareholders' agreement)
  - **Belastingadviseur** (tax advisor -- recommended to structure the shareholding tax-efficiently, especially if a holding structure is considered)

## Key Risks

1. **Majority/minority imbalance**: With a 60/40 split, the majority shareholder controls ordinary resolutions. Without contractual protections (qualified majorities, reserved matters), the minority shareholder is vulnerable.
2. **Director liability**: Under Art. 2:9 BW, directors are jointly liable for improper management (onbehoorlijk bestuur). The uitkeringstest (Art. 2:216 BW) creates personal liability risk for distributions.
3. **Deadlock risk**: With two directors and no tie-breaking mechanism, board deadlocks are possible. The shareholders' agreement must address this.
4. **Informal advisory board limitations**: An advisory board has no legal standing under BW Boek 2. Advisory board members have no statutory duties or protections. Their role depends entirely on what is agreed contractually.
5. **Tax structuring**: The direct 60/40 shareholding may have tax implications (e.g., box 2 income tax in the IB, DGA-regeling). A **holding structure** (personal holding BV for each founder) is often recommended for tax optimization and liability ring-fencing. Consult a tax advisor.

## Recommendations

1. **Incorporate the BV via a notaris** with well-drafted articles of association that include qualified majority requirements for key decisions, board composition rules, and share transfer provisions.
2. **Draft a comprehensive aandeelhoudersovereenkomst** with the provisions outlined above, particularly focusing on deadlock resolution, good/bad leaver provisions, and minority protection.
3. **Consider a holding structure**: Each founder incorporates a personal holding BV, which then holds shares in the operating BV. This provides tax benefits and liability protection. Discuss with a belastingadviseur.
4. **Register at the KVK** within 8 days of incorporation and ensure UBO registration is completed.
5. **Establish a clear advisory board charter** defining the role, composition, meeting frequency, and confidentiality obligations of advisory board members.
6. **Ensure joint signing authority** (gezamenlijk bevoegd) is registered at the KVK, at least for material transactions, to protect both founders.
