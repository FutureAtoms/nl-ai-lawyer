## Corporate Law Analysis

**Entity Type**: BV (Besloten Vennootschap met beperkte aansprakelijkheid)
**Legal Question**: What are the legal implications under Dutch corporate law when a BV board approves a related party transaction (EUR 500K with a director's other company) and a minority shareholder (30%) objects?

## Applicable Legal Framework

This analysis involves several intersecting provisions of **Burgerlijk Wetboek Boek 2 (BW Boek 2)** and related governance norms:

- **Art. 2:9 BW** - Director's duty of proper management (behoorlijke taakvervulling)
- **Art. 2:8 BW** - Duty of reasonableness and fairness (redelijkheid en billijkheid) between the legal entity and those involved in its organization

- **Art. 6:162 BW** - Tort liability (onrechtmatige daad) of directors

- **Wet bestuur en toezicht rechtspersonen (WBTR)** - Since 1 July 2021, codifies conflict of interest rules for all legal entities

## Analysis

### 1. Conflict of Interest (Tegenstrijdig Belang)

The director who has another company that is the counterparty to the EUR 500K transaction has a **direct and personal conflict of interest** (tegenstrijdig belang). Under Dutch law, this triggers the following rules:

#### Art. 2:239 lid 6 BW (Conflict of Interest Rule for BV Directors)
- A director who has a **direct or indirect personal interest** that conflicts with the interests of the BV and its enterprise may **not participate in the deliberation and decision-making** on the relevant matter.
- If as a result no board resolution can be taken (e.g., because all directors are conflicted, or there is only one director), the resolution is taken by the **supervisory board** (Raad van Commissarissen).
- If there is no supervisory board, the resolution is taken by the **general meeting of shareholders (AVA)**, unless the articles of association provide otherwise.

**Application to this case**: The conflicted director should have **recused** (zich verschoond) from the board deliberation and vote on the EUR 500K transaction. If they participated in the decision, the decision-making process was tainted.

#### Consequences of Improper Conflict Management

**a. Voidability of the Resolution (Art. 2:15 BW)**
- A board resolution adopted in violation of the conflict of interest rules (Art. 2:239 lid 6 BW) may be **voidable** (vernietigbaar) under Art. 2:15 lid 1 sub a BW (violation of procedural requirements in the law or articles of association).
- A resolution may also be voidable under Art. 2:15 lid 1 sub b BW if it is **contrary to the standards of reasonableness and fairness** (redelijkheid en billijkheid) as set out in Art. 2:8 BW.
- The minority shareholder (30%) may petition the court to **annul** (vernietigen) the resolution within **one year** of learning of the resolution or of it being announced (Art. 2:15 lid 5 BW).

**b. Director Liability (Art. 2:9 BW)**
- The conflicted director may be liable to the BV under Art. 2:9 BW for **improper management** (onbehoorlijk bestuur) if:
  - The transaction was not at arm's length (niet op zakelijke voorwaarden);
  - The director failed to disclose the conflict;
  - The director participated in the decision-making despite the conflict.
- The standard is **serious culpability** (ernstig verwijtbaar): the director must have acted in a manner that no reasonably acting director would have done under the same circumstances.
- The non-conflicted directors may also face liability under Art. 2:9 BW if they knew or should have known about the conflict and failed to take action.

**c. Tort Liability (Art. 6:162 BW)**
- If the transaction caused damage to the BV or its shareholders, the conflicted director may be personally liable to the minority shareholder under **tort law** (onrechtmatige daad).
- This requires the shareholder to demonstrate that the director's conduct violated an unwritten standard of care in society (maatschappelijke betamelijkheid) and that there is sufficient personal culpability (persoonlijk ernstig verwijt).

### 2. Minority Shareholder Protection

Dutch corporate law provides several mechanisms for the objecting minority shareholder (30%):

#### a. Right to Challenge Resolutions (Art. 2:15 BW)
- As noted above, the minority shareholder can seek annulment of the board resolution approving the transaction.
- This must be done within **one year** of becoming aware of the resolution.

#### b. Inquiry Procedure (Enqueteprocedure) - Art. 2:344-2:359 BW
- A shareholder holding at least **10% of issued capital** (or EUR 225,000 in nominal value for an NV) may petition the **Ondernemingskamer** (Enterprise Chamber of the Amsterdam Court of Appeal) for an inquiry (Art. 2:344 and 2:345 BW).
- The 30% minority shareholder **exceeds this threshold** and therefore has standing.
- The Ondernemingskamer may:
  - Order an **inquiry** (enquete) into the policy and affairs of the BV (Art. 2:345 BW).
  - Grant **immediate provisional measures** (onmiddellijke voorzieningen, Art. 2:349a BW), including:
    - Suspension of the conflicted director;
    - Temporary appointment of an independent director;
    - Temporary deviation from the articles of association;
    - Prohibition on executing the transaction (if not yet completed).
  - If **wanbeleid** (mismanagement) is established, the Ondernemingskamer may (Art. 2:355 BW):
    - Annul the board resolution approving the transaction;
    - Dismiss the conflicted director;
    - Temporarily appoint independent directors;
    - Order other measures to remedy the mismanagement.

#### c. Reasonableness and Fairness (Art. 2:8 BW)
- All persons involved in the organization of the BV (including shareholders and directors) must behave towards each other in accordance with **reasonableness and fairness** (redelijkheid en billijkheid).
- A related party transaction approved without proper conflict management may violate this standard.
- The minority shareholder can invoke Art. 2:8 lid 2 BW to argue that a rule or resolution does not apply insofar as this would be **unacceptable** (onaanvaardbaar) under the standards of reasonableness and fairness.

#### d. Derivative Action / Direct Action
- Dutch law does not have a formal statutory derivative action like common law jurisdictions.
- However, the minority shareholder can:
  - Request the AVA to hold the director liable under Art. 2:9 BW;
  - Pursue a direct tort claim under Art. 6:162 BW if the shareholder suffered personal damage;
  - Use the inquiry procedure as a practical substitute.

### 3. Assessment of the Transaction Itself

The EUR 500K transaction with the director's other company must be assessed on its substantive merits:

#### Arm's Length Principle
- Was the transaction conducted at **fair market terms** (zakelijke voorwaarden)?
- Were comparable offers obtained from third parties?
- Was an independent valuation performed?

#### Corporate Benefit (Vennootschappelijk belang)
- Under Art. 2:239 BW, the board must manage the BV in its interest and that of its enterprise (belang van de vennootschap en de daarmee verbonden onderneming).
- The transaction must serve the **corporate interest** -- not the personal interest of the conflicted director.
- The Ondernemingskamer has consistently held that the corporate interest encompasses the interests of all stakeholders, including minority shareholders.

#### Dutch Corporate Governance Code (Best Practice)
- Although the Code applies directly only to listed companies, its principles on related party transactions serve as best-practice guidance:
  - **Chapter 2 (Conflicts of Interest)**: Directors must report conflicts; conflicted directors may not participate in deliberation or decision-making.
  - **Chapter 4 (Related Party Transactions)**: Related party transactions must be announced and subject to supervisory board approval above the materiality threshold.
- For a private BV, these principles can inform what constitutes proper governance.
