## Overview

The Wet bescherming bedrijfsgeheimen (Wbb) is the direct Dutch implementation of EU Directive 2016/943/EU. The relationship between the two instruments is straightforward from a constitutional standpoint: the Directive sets the minimum harmonized standard; the Wbb gives it domestic legal force. For practical compliance purposes, however, understanding how the two interact — and where the Wbb may go beyond or fall short of the Directive's requirements — is essential.

## 1. EU Directive 2016/943 — The Trade Secrets Directive

### Legal Basis and Purpose

Directive 2016/943/EU (the "TSD") was adopted on 8 June 2016 under Art. 114 TFEU (approximation of laws for the internal market). Its purpose is to harmonize the protection of trade secrets across EU Member States, which previously had widely divergent national frameworks.

**Key policy objectives:**
- Create a uniform minimum standard of trade secret protection across the EU
- Balance protection of legitimate commercial secrecy with freedom of information, whistleblower rights, and mobility of workers
- Provide harmonized civil law remedies (injunctions, damages, seizure)
- Establish clear definitions to enable cross-border enforcement

**Transposition deadline**: 9 June 2018

### Core Definitions (Art. 2 TSD)

**Trade secret** under Art. 2(1) TSD means information that:
1. Is **secret** — not generally known among or readily accessible to persons in circles that normally deal with such information
2. Has **commercial value because it is secret**
3. Has been subject to **reasonable steps** by the person in control to keep it secret

This three-part definition was adopted verbatim in Art. 1 Wbb and forms the cornerstone of both the EU and Dutch frameworks.

### Lawful vs. Unlawful Acts (Arts. 3–5 TSD)

The TSD distinguishes between:
- **Lawful acts**: Reverse engineering, independent discovery, disclosure under statutory obligation, whistleblowing, journalism — these are explicitly carved out from unlawful conduct
- **Unlawful acquisition**: Unauthorized access, copying, or appropriation
- **Unlawful use/disclosure**: Using or disclosing trade secrets obtained through unlawful means or in breach of a duty to maintain secrecy

## 2. Dutch Implementation — Wet bescherming bedrijfsgeheimen (Wbb)

### Legislative History

The Wbb was adopted on 17 October 2018 and entered into force on 23 October 2018 (Stb. 2018, 369). It implements the TSD into Dutch law with minimal deviation.

**Structure of the Wbb:**
- **Art. 1 Wbb** — Definition of "bedrijfsgeheim" (trade secret) — mirrors Art. 2(1) TSD
- **Art. 2 Wbb** — Lawful acquisition and disclosure (mirrors TSD Art. 3) — reverse engineering, independent discovery, etc.
- **Art. 3 Wbb** — Unlawful acquisition, use, and disclosure — mirrors TSD Art. 4
- **Art. 4 Wbb** — Exceptions: journalism, public interest, whistleblowing — mirrors TSD Art. 5
- **Arts. 5–8 Wbb** — Remedies: injunction (verbod), recall and destruction, damages, publication of judgment
- **Art. 1019ia-1019ib Rv** (Code of Civil Procedure) — Procedural protection of confidentiality in litigation (processuele geheimhouding)

### The Three-Part Definition in Practice

Dutch courts have begun developing case law on the Wbb's three-part definition:

1. **Rb. Den Haag 17 juli 2019 (ECLI:NL:RBDHA:2019:7161)** — One of the first applications of the Wbb. The court assessed whether the claimant's customer data and pricing strategies qualified as "bedrijfsgeheimen." It confirmed that all three elements must be present and applied cumulatively. The burden of proof rests on the trade secret holder.

2. **Rb. Midden-Nederland 5 februari 2020 (ECLI:NL:RBMNE:2020:428)** — The "reasonable steps" requirement. The court held that a general confidentiality clause in an employment contract, without further technical or organizational measures (e.g., access controls, encryption, classification policies), may be insufficient to demonstrate that reasonable protective steps were taken. This has significant compliance implications.

3. **Gerechtshof Den Haag 17 november 2020 (ECLI:NL:GHDHA:2020:2127)** — Scope of remedies. The court addressed the interaction between Wbb remedies (Art. 5–8 Wbb) and the general tort law framework (Art. 6:162 BW). The court confirmed that Wbb remedies are available alongside — not instead of — general tort claims.

## 3. Relationship Between the Directive and the Wbb

### Nature of the Relationship: Full Harmonization vs. Minimum Harmonization

Directive 2016/943 is a **minimum harmonization** directive. This means:
- Member States must meet the minimum standards set by the Directive
- Member States **may** provide more extensive protection (Recital 10 TSD), but only to the extent that this does not conflict with other EU law obligations (e.g., GDPR, free movement)
- The Directive harmonizes definitions and remedies but leaves some procedural aspects to national law (see Art. 16 TSD: Member States may provide for more favorable disclosure rules to protect whistleblowers)

**In Dutch law**: The Wbb is a close transposition. The Dutch legislature chose not to introduce significantly expanded protection beyond the Directive's minimum. This means that the Wbb and the TSD are for all practical purposes co-extensive.

### EU Law Supremacy and Direct Effect of the Directive

Since the Wbb correctly implements the TSD, questions of direct effect of the Directive itself are largely academic for the Netherlands. However, two scenarios are relevant:

1. **If the Wbb is applied incorrectly**: If a Dutch court misinterprets the Wbb in a way that falls below the TSD's minimum standard, the directive-conforming interpretation obligation (richtlijnconforme interpretatie — Marleasing, C-106/89) requires the court to read the Wbb in line with the TSD's purpose.

2. **If there is a gap in the Wbb**: For any aspect of trade secret protection that the Wbb does not cover but the TSD requires, the TSD provisions may be invoked directly against the State (vertical direct effect) if they are clear, precise, and unconditional.

### Role of Art. 93/94 Grondwet

The monist Dutch constitutional framework (Art. 93/94 Grondwet) means that:
- The TSD, as implemented by the Wbb, is part of Dutch domestic law
- If the Wbb fails to implement an aspect of the TSD correctly, a Dutch court may apply Art. 94 Grondwet to set aside the conflicting Dutch provision and apply the TSD directly
- In practice, since the Wbb is a close transposition, this scenario is unlikely but possible for edge cases

## 4. Practical Compliance: What Your Company Needs to Do

### Dual Compliance Framework

Your company must comply with both instruments, but in practice this means **complying with the Wbb** — which, if correctly applied, ensures simultaneous compliance with the TSD.

**The key practical steps:**

#### 4.1 Define and Classify Trade Secrets

- Inventory what information your company considers to be trade secrets (the three-part definition applies: secret, commercial value, reasonable steps)
- Classify information categories: trade secrets vs. ordinary confidential information (important — NDAs can protect both, but only Wbb remedies apply to trade secrets)
- Document the classification and its rationale

#### 4.2 Implement "Reasonable Steps" (Redelijke Maatregelen)

This is the most litigation-prone element. Following Rb. Midden-Nederland (2020), a general confidentiality clause is not sufficient. Reasonable steps should include:

**Technical measures:**
- Access controls and need-to-know restrictions
- Encryption of sensitive data
- Audit trails and monitoring
- Secure disposal of confidential materials

**Organizational measures:**
- Trade secret policies and procedures
- Employee training on confidentiality obligations
- NDAs with employees, contractors, and business partners
- Exit interview protocols for departing employees with trade secret access

**Legal/contractual measures:**
- Well-drafted NDAs referencing Wbb protection
- Contractual non-disclosure provisions in supplier and customer agreements
- IP assignment and confidentiality provisions in employment contracts

#### 4.3 Ensure NDAs Are Aligned with Wbb and TSD

The TSD and Wbb do not require an NDA for protection of trade secrets — but NDAs provide **additional contractual protection** that complements the statutory framework. Key alignment points:

- The NDA's definition of "confidential information" should cover information that qualifies as a trade secret under Art. 1 Wbb as a subset
- Ensure the NDA's exceptions (carve-outs) align with the TSD's lawful acts (Art. 3 TSD / Art. 2 Wbb): independent development, reverse engineering, public domain, and mandatory disclosure
- The NDA's whistleblowing exception should comply with Art. 5 TSD (which protects whistleblowers acting in good faith) — an NDA clause that prohibits all disclosure to regulatory authorities may be void to the extent it conflicts with the TSD's whistleblower protections

#### 4.4 Litigation Strategy

If trade secrets are misappropriated, your company can pursue remedies under:

1. **The Wbb** (Arts. 5–8):
   - Prohibition of use or disclosure (verbod op gebruik of openbaarmaking)
   - Recall and destruction of misappropriating goods or documents
   - Damages (schadevergoeding) — including lost profits and unjust enrichment
   - Publication of the judgment

2. **The NDA** (if applicable):
   - Breach of contract claim (wanprestatie, Art. 6:74 BW)
   - Penalty clause (boetebeding, Arts. 6:91–6:94 BW)
   - Injunction via kort geding (Art. 254 Rv)

3. **General tort law** (Art. 6:162 BW):
   - Available where the misappropriating party is not bound by an NDA
   - The Wbb does not exclude tort claims (Gerechtshof Den Haag 2020)

4. **Procedural protection** during litigation:
   - Art. 1019ib Rv (processuele geheimhouding): parties can request that the court limit access to evidence containing trade secrets — critical to avoid the paradox of exposing a trade secret in litigation to prove it is a trade secret

## 5. Specific Areas of Divergence or Complexity

### 5.1 Whistleblower Protections

Art. 5 TSD protects disclosure of trade secrets where the disclosure is necessary to reveal misconduct, wrongdoing, or illegal activity, provided the person acts in the public interest (whistleblowing). The Wbb implements this in Art. 4 Wbb. This interacts with the Dutch Wet bescherming klokkenluiders (Wbk — Whistleblower Protection Act), which was updated in 2023 to implement EU Directive 2019/1937. Companies must ensure their trade secret policies do not inadvertently create barriers to lawful whistleblowing.

### 5.2 Employee Mobility

Art. 1(3) TSD explicitly preserves Member States' freedom to apply own rules on:
- Employment contracts
- Professional mobility
- Confidentiality obligations of employees

This means the Wbb does not override Dutch employment law provisions on employee mobility (Art. 7:611, Art. 7:653 BW on non-competes). The TSD/Wbb provides a floor for trade secret protection but does not create an EU-level non-compete framework.

### 5.3 Statute of Limitations

The TSD does not specify a limitation period for trade secret claims. Under Dutch law, the general tort limitation period of 5 years (Art. 3:310 BW) applies to Wbb claims.

### 5.4 Criminal Law Dimension

The TSD is a civil law instrument only — it does not require Member States to criminalize trade secret misappropriation. However, Dutch criminal law may provide additional protection under Art. 273 Wetboek van Strafrecht (Sr) for certain forms of trade secret theft, particularly involving employees.
