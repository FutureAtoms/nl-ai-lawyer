## Applicable EU Law

### EU Trade Secrets Directive 2016/943

The **EU Trade Secrets Directive (Directive 2016/943)** was adopted on 8 June 2016 and had a transposition deadline of 9 June 2018. Its objectives are to:

1. Harmonize the definition of trade secrets across the EU
2. Establish minimum standards of protection against unlawful acquisition, use, and disclosure
3. Provide uniform civil law remedies for trade secret holders
4. Balance protection with freedom of movement of workers and freedom of information

The directive defines a **trade secret** using a three-part cumulative test (Art. 2(1) Directive 2016/943):
- The information is **secret** (not generally known or readily accessible in the relevant circles)
- The information has **commercial value** because it is secret
- The holder has taken **reasonable steps** to keep it secret

This definition is directly mirrored in Dutch law.

## Interaction with Dutch Law

### The Wet bescherming bedrijfsgeheimen (Wbb)

The **Wet bescherming bedrijfsgeheimen (Wbb)** is the Dutch statute that implements Directive 2016/943. The Wbb entered into force on 23 October 2018, slightly after the directive's transposition deadline, but is now fully in force.

**Relationship between Directive 2016/943 and the Wbb:**

The Wbb is a **full harmonization** implementing statute. This means:

1. **The Wbb tracks the directive closely**: The definitions, prohibited acts, exceptions, and remedies in the Wbb correspond directly to those in Directive 2016/943. The Dutch legislature chose to implement the directive with minimal deviation.

2. **The directive sets a floor, not a ceiling**: Directive 2016/943 is a minimum harmonization instrument in some respects (particularly the definition of trade secrets and the remedies framework), meaning Member States may provide broader protection, but may not provide lesser protection than the directive requires.

3. **Richtlijnconforme interpretatie (consistent interpretation)**: Dutch courts are obliged to interpret the Wbb in conformity with the text and purpose of Directive 2016/943. When a Wbb provision is ambiguous, the CJEU's interpretation of the directive's corresponding provision is authoritative.

4. **CJEU preliminary rulings are binding**: If the CJEU issues a preliminary ruling interpreting Directive 2016/943, Dutch courts applying the Wbb must follow that interpretation.

## Direct Effect Analysis

The **Trade Secrets Directive 2016/943** has been properly implemented in Dutch law through the **Wbb**. As a result, the question of direct effect of the directive itself does not normally arise in Dutch proceedings — companies and courts apply the Wbb, not the directive directly.

However, the directive remains relevant in two ways:

1. **Interpretive tool** (richtlijnconforme interpretatie): Dutch courts interpret the Wbb in light of the directive's text and purpose. The directive is thus operative in every Wbb case, even if not directly invoked.

2. **Gap-filling**: If the Wbb does not address a specific situation covered by the directive, the directive's provisions may be invoked directly (vertical direct effect against the State) or used to guide consistent interpretation (against private parties).

Since the Wbb implements the directive, **horizontal disputes** (between private companies) are governed by the Wbb, which is national law. There is no need to invoke the directive's horizontal direct effect (which directives do not have in any event — Marshall, C-152/84; Faccini Dori, C-91/92).

## Practical Consequences

**For your company's compliance with both the Wbb and Directive 2016/943:**

### 1. The Wbb IS the directive in Dutch law

For day-to-day compliance purposes, **focus on the Wbb**. Complying with the Wbb means complying with Directive 2016/943. The two instruments are substantively equivalent — the richtlijn (directive) and the wet (implementing statute) use the same definitions and remedies framework.

### 2. Three-Part Trade Secret Test

Your information qualifies as a **bedrijfsgeheim** (trade secret) under both the Wbb and the directive only if it meets all three criteria:
- **Secret**: Not generally known or readily accessible to persons in the relevant business circles
- **Commercial value because of secrecy**: The information derives value from being undisclosed (competitive advantage, investment in R&D, customer data, pricing strategies, formulas, etc.)
- **Reasonable protective steps**: Active measures such as: NDAs with employees and partners, access controls (technical and physical), information classification policies, employee training, and confidentiality provisions in contracts

### 3. Scope of Protection

The Wbb (like the directive) protects against:
- **Unlawful acquisition** (diefstal, hacking, breach of confidentiality obligations)
- **Unlawful use or disclosure** (using or sharing trade secrets acquired unlawfully or in breach of an obligation)

The Wbb does NOT protect against **independent development** (reverse engineering or independent parallel development) — this is an express exception under Art. 3 Wbb / Art. 5 Directive 2016/943.

### 4. Relationship to NDAs

The Wbb provides **statutory** protection independent of any NDA. An NDA provides **additional contractual** protection that may cover:
- Information not qualifying as a trade secret under the Wbb
- Obligations beyond the statutory framework (e.g., higher damages, specific return/destruction obligations)
- Procedural advantages (agreed dispute resolution, penalty clauses)

**Practical recommendation**: Maintain both Wbb compliance (adequate protective measures) AND well-drafted NDAs with all counterparties to whom confidential information is disclosed.

### 5. Remedies Available Under Both Frameworks

Under the Wbb (implementing the directive), your company can claim:
- **Injunctions** (verbod op gebruik of openbaarmaking) — including on an urgent basis (kort geding)
- **Recall and destruction** of products derived from trade secret misappropriation
- **Damages** (schadevergoeding) — actual losses plus unjust enrichment
- **Publication** of the court's judgment
- **Confidentiality in proceedings** (Art. 1019ib Rv) — protecting trade secrets from becoming public during litigation

### 6. Interaction with EU Law — Ongoing Obligation

The Netherlands is obliged to maintain full compliance with Directive 2016/943. If a CJEU judgment interprets the directive in a way that conflicts with the current Wbb, the Dutch legislator must amend the Wbb accordingly. In the meantime, Dutch courts are obliged to interpret the Wbb consistently with any CJEU ruling.

### 7. Cross-Border Enforcement

The harmonization achieved by Directive 2016/943 means that trade secret protection across EU Member States is now substantially uniform. For cross-border trade secret misappropriation, the omzetting (implementatie) of the directive in other Member States provides comparable protection throughout the EU, facilitating enforcement in multiple jurisdictions.
