## Legal Framework

The core legal framework governing this situation is:

- **Art. 7:669 lid 3 sub d BW (d-grond)** — Disfunctioneren (dysfunctional performance / underperformance): the ground for dismissal at the kantonrechter when an employee is not meeting job requirements
- **Art. 7:669 lid 3 sub i BW (i-grond, since WAB 2020)** — Cumulatiegrond: allows combining two or more partial grounds (c through h), where no single ground is fully met
- **Art. 7:669 lid 3 sub g BW (g-grond)** — Verstoorde arbeidsverhouding (disrupted working relationship): potentially relevant if the situation has deteriorated
- **Art. 7:673 BW** — Transitievergoeding (transition payment): mandatory on employer-initiated termination
- **Art. 7:900 BW** — Vaststellingsovereenkomst (settlement agreement): mutual consent route
- **Art. 7:670b BW** — Cooling-off period for employee after signing a vaststellingsovereenkomst
- **Art. 7:672 BW** — Statutory notice periods

The WWZ (Wet Werk en Zekerheid, 2015) introduced the closed dismissal system (gesloten ontslagstelsel), requiring employers to prove one of the statutory grounds. The WAB (Wet Arbeidsmarkt in Balans, 2020) added the i-grond cumulation ground.

## Analysis

### Critical Problem: The Documentation Gap

The most urgent issue is that you have had only **verbal discussions** about performance with no written documentation. This is a serious — potentially fatal — obstacle to any contested dismissal route under Dutch law.

Under consistent kantonrechter case law (including Rb. Amsterdam 7 March 2016, ECLI:NL:RBAMS:2016:1357 and Rb. Midden-Nederland 4 October 2017, ECLI:NL:RBMNE:2017:4993), a dismissal for disfunctioneren (Art. 7:669 lid 3 sub d BW) requires the employer to demonstrate:

1. The employee was **clearly informed** of the underperformance (schriftelijk en concreet)
2. The employee was given a **reasonable opportunity to improve** through a formal verbetertraject (performance improvement plan)
3. The verbetertraject was **concrete and measurable** — with specific goals, timelines, support, and evaluation moments
4. The employer provided adequate **coaching, guidance, and resources**
5. The underperformance is **not caused** by insufficient training, unclear instructions, or poor working conditions
6. A **sufficient dossier** (dossieropbouw) exists to demonstrate all of the above

Your situation — with only verbal discussions over an undefined period — does not meet these requirements. A kantonrechter is highly likely to deny a dissolution request submitted on the current facts.

### Overview of Available Options

#### Option 1: Kantonrechter Route via d-grond (Disfunctioneren) — Currently Not Available

As explained above, the d-grond requires a documented dossier and completed verbetertraject. Given that no documentation exists, this route is **not currently viable**. The path forward requires first building the dossier (see Practical Steps below).

Timeline to viability: typically **3–6 months minimum** from initiation of the formal verbetertraject.

#### Option 2: UWV Ontslagvergunning Route — Generally Not Applicable Here

The UWV route covers only two grounds:
- **a-grond**: Business economic reasons (redundancy)
- **b-grond**: Long-term illness (2+ years)

Underperformance falls under the kantonrechter route. The UWV route is not available for disfunctioneren. It could theoretically be used if the company undergoes genuine restructuring that eliminates the employee's position — but this requires a bona fide business economic reason and strict procedural compliance (afspiegelingsbeginsel). If the restructuring were seen as a pretext for dismissing an underperformer, courts may scrutinize it heavily.

#### Option 3: Vaststellingsovereenkomst (VSO) — Mutual Consent — Recommended First Step

A **vaststellingsovereenkomst** (also called a beëindigingsovereenkomst) under Art. 7:900 BW is a negotiated settlement agreement that avoids litigation. It is by far the most common and commercially efficient route for dismissals in the Netherlands.

Key features:
- **No judicial involvement required** — purely contractual
- Employee retains **WW (unemployment benefit) eligibility** if the employer takes initiative for termination (employee must not have been the one to actively seek termination)
- Employee has a **14-day cooling-off period** (bedenktijd) after signing, during which they may revoke consent without reason (Art. 7:670b lid 2 BW). If the employer fails to inform the employee of this right, the cooling-off period extends to 21 days
- Standard elements: agreed termination date, transitievergoeding payment (or agreed severance), final settlement of outstanding claims, return of company property, reference letter, non-disparagement clause, release of any non-compete (if applicable)

**Practical note**: Given the lack of documentation, the VSO is likely the most pragmatic starting point. However, the employee has **no obligation to agree**. If they decline, you must build a proper dossier before pursuing litigation.

#### Option 4: Kantonrechter Route via i-grond (Cumulatiegrond) — Since WAB 2020

The WAB introduced the i-grond (Art. 7:669 lid 3 sub i BW), allowing the kantonrechter to grant dissolution based on a **combination of two or more partial grounds** (c through h), even where no single ground is fully met.

For example: partial disfunctioneren (d-grond) + partial verstoorde arbeidsverhouding (g-grond).

**Risk**: If the kantonrechter grants dissolution on the i-grond, they **may** award up to **50% extra transitievergoeding** on top of the regular amount. This adds to the financial cost of dismissal.

For the i-grond, documentation is still required — the kantonrechter will want to see at least partial dossier on each ground relied upon.

## Employee Rights / Employer Obligations

### Notice Period (Art. 7:672 BW)
For an employee with 8 years of service, the statutory notice period for the employer is:
- **5–10 years of service → 2 months' notice**

The notice period is calculated from the date the kantonrechter grants dissolution (or from the agreed termination date in a VSO). The kantonrechter reduces the notice period by the time the dissolution procedure took (Art. 7:671b lid 9 sub b BW), subject to a minimum of 1 month.

### Transitievergoeding (Art. 7:673 BW)
Under the WAB (since 1 January 2020), the transition payment accrues from day one of employment:
- **Rate**: 1/3 monthly salary per year of service (pro rata for partial years)
- **Calculation for 8 years**: approximately 2.67 months' gross salary (8 years × 1/3)
- **Maximum (2024)**: EUR 94,000 or one year's salary, whichever is higher
- The transitievergoeding is **mandatory** in all employer-initiated dismissals (including VSO where the employer effectively drives the process)
- Not due only if: employee is seriously culpable (ernstig verwijtbaar), employee under 18 working under 12 hours/week, retirement age termination, or bankruptcy

### Billijke Vergoeding Risk
If the kantonrechter finds the employer **seriously culpable** (ernstig verwijtbaar) in how it handled the dismissal — for example, if it pressured the employee, deliberately created an unworkable situation, or constructed a sham PIP — the court may award an additional **billijke vergoeding** (fair compensation) on top of the transitievergoeding (Art. 7:671b lid 9 sub c BW). There is no statutory cap on the billijke vergoeding (HR 30 June 2017, ECLI:NL:HR:2017:1187, New Hairstyle).

### Discrimination Risk
With 8 years of tenure, review whether the employee belongs to a protected group (age, disability, pregnancy, etc.) or whether dismissal could be perceived as discriminatory. Performance-based dismissal of an employee in a protected category requires particularly robust documentation.

## Practical Steps

### Immediate Priority: Start Building the Dossier

1. **Schedule a formal performance review meeting** — do this in writing (invitation by email or letter), stating that the meeting is about performance concerns
2. **Document the meeting in writing** — produce a written record (gespreksverslag) of the meeting and its outcomes; have the employee sign it or note that they declined to sign
3. **Issue a written performance warning** — a formal written document stating:
   - Specific, concrete performance shortfalls (not vague assessments)
   - The expected standard of performance (what "good performance" looks like in this role)
   - The time period of underperformance
4. **Initiate a formal verbetertraject (improvement plan)** — the plan must include:
   - Specific, measurable improvement goals (SMART)
   - Timeline (typically 2–4 months depending on role complexity)
   - Resources, coaching, and support the employer will provide
   - Evaluation moments (e.g., monthly check-ins)
   - Clear statement of consequences if improvement is insufficient
5. **Monitor and document all evaluation moments** — written reports of each review meeting
6. **Evaluate at end of verbetertraject** — if improvement is insufficient, document this in writing with specific examples

### Parallel Track: Explore VSO Possibility

7. **Consult an employment lawyer** before approaching the employee about a VSO — the approach and offer terms must be carefully calibrated
8. **Consider whether to make a VSO proposal** — this can be done at any stage but is often most effective either early (before tensions escalate) or after completion of the verbetertraject
9. **Ensure VSO terms include transitievergoeding** at minimum; additional amounts are negotiable but set a realistic expectation

### Process Integrity

10. **Check for applicable CAO provisions** — certain CAOs have additional procedural requirements or enhanced severance for longer-tenured employees
11. **Consult the ondernemingsraad (works council)** if applicable — while individual dismissals do not require works council advice under Art. 25 WOR, some CAOs or company policies may require consultation
12. **Engage mediation if appropriate** — especially if the relationship has deteriorated (g-grond elements). Documented mediation attempts strengthen the dossier
