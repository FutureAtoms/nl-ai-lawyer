## Analysis

### Part 1: Kennismigrant Salary Threshold

#### Who is a Kennismigrant?
A **kennismigrant** (highly skilled migrant) is a third-country national (from outside the EU/EEA/Switzerland) who is employed in the Netherlands by a **recognized sponsor** (erkend referent) and who earns a salary meeting the applicable salary threshold. India is a third country for this purpose.

The employer must first obtain **erkend referent** status from the IND. Most companies hiring internationally will need to apply for this status if they have not already done so.

#### Salary Thresholds for Kennismigranten (2025 figures — verify with IND for current year)

The salary threshold is set by the IND and updated **annually on 1 January**. The following are the 2025 indicative thresholds (exclusive of 8% holiday allowance; verify current rates at IND.nl):

| Category | Minimum Gross Monthly Salary (2025 indicative) |
|----------|-----------------------------------------------|
| General kennismigrant (30+ years old) | EUR 5,688 per month (approx.) |
| General kennismigrant (under 30 years old) | EUR 4,171 per month (approx.) |
| Orientation year permit (zoekjaar) after Dutch university | EUR 2,989 per month (approx.) |
| Scientific researcher / teacher at university or research institute | Different threshold; verify with IND |
| EU Blue Card | EUR 5,688 per month (approx., or 1.5x average salary if higher) |

**Important**: These figures are updated annually. The **2025 thresholds effective 1 January 2025** should be confirmed directly with the IND (www.ind.nl) or the employer's immigration counsel. The 30% ruling (belastingvoordeel voor expats) does not affect the minimum salary threshold calculation — the gross salary must meet the threshold without taking the 30% ruling into account.

#### Applicable Threshold for a Senior Developer from India

For a senior developer (typically 30+ years old given the seniority level), the **general kennismigrant threshold for age 30+** applies. As of 2025, this is approximately **EUR 5,688 per month gross** (exclusive of the 8% holiday allowance). The salary specified in the employment contract must be at or above this level.

**Note on the 30% ruling (expatregeling)**: If the employee qualifies for the 30% ruling (loonbelasting voordeel), the tax benefits are significant but the gross salary for IND purposes must still meet the minimum threshold without the benefit of the ruling.

#### IND Process Overview
1. Employer must be an **erkend referent** (recognized sponsor) — apply at IND if not yet registered
2. Employer files an **mvv (machtiging tot voorlopig verblijf)** application and/or a **residence permit for kennismigrant** on behalf of the employee
3. IND checks: erkend referent status, salary threshold compliance, employment contract, qualifications
4. Processing time: typically **2–4 weeks** for erkend referent sponsors (regular procedure); fast track not always available
5. Upon approval: employee receives a combined residence and work permit (gecombineerde vergunning voor verblijf en arbeid — GVVA)
6. The GVVA is employer-specific — if the employee changes employer, a new application is needed

### Part 2: Non-Compete Clause in a Fixed-Term Contract

#### General Rule: Significant Restriction Since WWZ (2015)

Since 1 January 2015 (Art. 7:653 lid 2 BW, as amended by the WWZ), including a **concurrentiebeding** (non-compete clause) in a **fixed-term employment contract** (arbeidsovereenkomst voor bepaalde tijd) is **in principle not permitted**, unless:

> The employer includes a **written justification** (schriftelijke motivering) in the non-compete clause itself, explaining why the clause is necessary due to **substantial business or service interests** (zwaarwegende bedrijfs- of dienstbelangen) that specifically require the protection.

This is a significant departure from the rules applicable to indefinite contracts, where a non-compete clause can be included without specific justification (provided it meets the writing requirement and the employee is an adult).

#### Requirements for a Valid Non-Compete in a Fixed-Term Contract

For the concurrentiebeding to be valid in a fixed-term contract, the following requirements must ALL be met:

1. **In writing** (Art. 7:653 lid 1 sub a BW) — the clause must be explicitly included in the written employment contract
2. **Employee is an adult** (18+) — (Art. 7:653 lid 1 sub b BW)
3. **Contemporaneous written justification** (Art. 7:653 lid 2 BW) — the motivation must be:
   - **Provided at the time of contract conclusion** (not added later or retrospectively)
   - **Part of the contract itself** — the justification must be in the concurrentiebeding clause, not in a separate document or policy
   - **Specific** to this employee in this role — a generic statement that "competition would harm our business interests" is insufficient
   - **Explain why this specific employee in this specific role** needs the protection (e.g., access to trade secrets, client relationships, proprietary technology, specific know-how)

#### What the Justification Must Contain (Case Law)

Per **Hof Arnhem-Leeuwarden 24 September 2019, ECLI:NL:GHARL:2019:7739**, the written justification must:
- Be provided **in the clause itself** at the time of concluding the contract
- Be **sufficiently specific**: a generic recitation of the statutory text ("because we have substantial business interests") is insufficient
- Explain **why this specific employee in this specific role** requires a non-competition restriction
- Identify the **specific business interests** at stake: for a senior developer, this could be access to proprietary AI/ML architecture, source code, client technical relationships, unique algorithmic methods, trade secrets, or specialized technical knowledge that is difficult to replicate

For a **senior developer** working on custom AI models and software systems, there are strong arguments that substantial business interests exist (access to proprietary codebase, training data strategies, technical architecture, client-specific implementations). However, the justification must still be explicitly articulated in the contract clause itself.

#### Court's Power to Set Aside or Moderate (Art. 7:653 lid 3 BW)

Even if the clause is formally valid (written + motivated), the court may **set aside (vernietigen) or moderate** the non-compete clause if:
- The employee is **unreasonably disadvantaged** relative to the employer's interest
- Circumstances have changed significantly since the contract was signed
- The scope (geographic area, duration, type of activities) is disproportionate

Courts will weigh:
- Duration of the restriction (1–2 years is common; longer is riskier)
- Geographic scope (Netherlands-wide vs. global; narrower is more enforceable)
- Specificity of prohibited activities (narrower = more enforceable)
- Impact on employee's ability to find work in their field
- Whether the employee has highly specialized skills in a niche market

#### Compensation for Upholding the Clause (Art. 7:653 lid 5 BW)

If the court upholds the non-compete clause and it significantly limits the employee's ability to find work, the court **may** award the employee **compensation (vergoeding)** for the duration of the restriction. This is at the court's discretion and is not automatic.

#### Non-Solicitation Clause (Relatiebeding)

A **relatiebeding** (non-solicitation of clients/relations) is treated as a form of concurrentiebeding under Dutch case law and is subject to the same requirements, including the written justification requirement for fixed-term contracts. A relatiebeding must also specify which relations are covered (not a blanket restriction on all clients).

#### Planned Legislative Change (Note as of 2024/2025)
The Dutch government has been working on a legislative proposal to further restrict or regulate non-compete clauses, including potentially limiting their duration and requiring compensation. As of early 2026, verify whether new legislation has entered into force. Employment lawyers should be consulted for the latest position.

## Practical Recommendations

### For the Kennismigrant Hire

1. **Verify IND erkend referent status** — confirm your company is a recognized sponsor; if not, initiate the application immediately (adds 4–6 weeks)
2. **Confirm current salary threshold** — check IND.nl for the 2026 salary threshold applicable at the time of the permit application (thresholds increase annually)
3. **Set the salary at or above the threshold in the employment contract** — the contract must specify the gross monthly salary excluding 8% holiday allowance
4. **Ensure the contract meets the Art. 7:655 BW information requirements** — written contract with all mandatory terms
5. **Consult an immigration lawyer (immigratierecht specialist)** — IND procedures for kennismigranten are procedurally intensive; professional assistance reduces delay and rejection risk
6. **Note the employer-specific nature of the GVVA** — if the employee transfers to a different employer entity, a new permit is needed

### For the Non-Compete Clause in the Fixed-Term Contract

7. **Draft the justification as part of the concurrentiebeding clause itself** — do not put it in a separate document, appendix, or HR policy
8. **Make the justification specific** — identify the actual business interests at stake for this role: e.g., "This employee will have access to [specific proprietary AI model architecture], [specific client technical implementations], and [specific trade secrets], the disclosure or competitive use of which would cause substantial harm to the company's competitive position because [specific reason]"
9. **Scope the restriction narrowly** — limit by duration (e.g., 12 months), geographic area (e.g., the Netherlands), and type of activity (e.g., developing similar AI models for direct competitors in [specific sector]) to maximize enforceability
10. **Consider a relatiebeding** as an alternative or supplement — restricting solicitation of specific identified clients may be more enforceable and less disruptive than a broad non-compete
11. **Have the contract reviewed by an employment lawyer** before signing — given the technical requirements, errors in the motivation are a common cause of non-compete invalidity
