## Legal Framework

The primary legal basis for this situation is:

- **Art. 7:669 lid 3 sub d BW** (d-grond / disfunctioneren): Dissolution on the basis of dysfunctional performance - the employee demonstrably does not meet the requirements for the position, provided the employer has informed the employee of this and has given the employee a reasonable opportunity to improve.
- **Art. 7:673 BW** - Transitievergoeding (transition payment) - mandatory upon termination by the employer.
- **Art. 7:900 BW** - Vaststellingsovereenkomst (mutual settlement agreement) as an alternative route.
- **Wet Werk en Zekerheid (WWZ, 2015)** and **Wet Arbeidsmarkt in Balans (WAB, 2020)** govern the current dismissal framework.
- **Art. 7:669 lid 3 sub i BW (i-grond / cumulatiegrond, since WAB 2020)**: Allows combining two or more partial grounds if none individually fully meets the required threshold.

## Analysis

### The Core Problem: Insufficient Documentation (Dossieropbouw)

This is the most significant risk factor in your situation. Under Dutch law, a dismissal for disfunctioneren (the d-grond) requires a comprehensive and well-documented dossier. You have confirmed that performance discussions to date have been **verbal only, with nothing documented**. This is a serious obstacle to any contested dismissal route.

Dutch case law is consistent and strict on this point. As established in **Rb. Amsterdam 7 March 2016 (ECLI:NL:RBAMS:2016:1357)**, the employer must demonstrate all of the following before a kantonrechter will grant dissolution on the d-grond:

1. The employee has been **clearly and explicitly notified** of the specific performance shortcomings.
2. The employee has been given a **genuine and reasonable opportunity to improve** - the verbetertraject (improvement plan / PIP).
3. The improvement trajectory was **sufficiently concrete**, with measurable, SMART goals and a clear timeframe.
4. The employer provided **adequate coaching, guidance, and support** during the trajectory.
5. The underperformance is **not caused** by insufficient training, poor working conditions, unclear job requirements, or illness.

Additionally, from **Rb. Midden-Nederland 4 October 2017 (ECLI:NL:RBMNE:2017:4993)**: The verbetertraject must be genuine. A performance improvement plan designed to fail, or setting unrealistic targets, is grounds not only for dismissal denial but also for a billijke vergoeding (fair compensation) award against the employer.

**With only verbal discussions and no documented dossier**, a kantonrechter is very likely to deny a dissolution request based on the d-grond in its current state.

## Dismissal Routes Available

### Option 1: Build a Proper Dossier, Then Proceed via Kantonrechter (D-grond)

This is the legally sound, but time-intensive, route for a genuine performance case.

**Steps required:**
1. **Formalize the performance concerns in writing**: Issue a written notification (schriftelijke waarschuwing or functioneringsgesprek verslag) that clearly identifies the specific shortcomings in measurable terms.
2. **Initiate a formal verbetertraject (improvement plan)**: A written PIP with:
   - Specific, measurable, attainable, realistic, and time-bound (SMART) performance targets
   - The timeframe (typically 2-6 months, depending on the nature and seniority of the role)
   - Support measures offered by the employer (coaching, training, supervision)
   - Agreed review dates and escalation process
3. **Document all meetings**: Written records of all performance discussions, with the employee signing for receipt (or a note that they declined to sign).
4. **Conduct interim evaluations**: Written evaluations at agreed review dates, documenting progress or lack thereof.
5. **Issue a final warning** if targets are not met at the end of the verbetertraject.
6. **After the verbetertraject concludes unsatisfactorily**: File a dissolution petition (verzoekschrift) with the kantonrechter under Art. 7:669 lid 3 sub d BW.

**Timeline estimate**: Realistically 4-9 months to build a defensible dossier, followed by 4-6 weeks for the kantonrechter procedure.

**Outcome if granted**: Kantonrechter sets a termination date. Employer owes transitievergoeding. No billijke vergoeding if the employer has acted properly.

### Option 2: Negotiated Settlement (Vaststellingsovereenkomst)

A vaststellingsovereenkomst (VSO) / beëindigingsovereenkomst is a mutual written agreement to terminate the employment. This is frequently the most practical and commercially efficient route, particularly given the current absence of a documented dossier.

**Key elements to negotiate:**
- Termination date (rekening houdend met opzegtermijn)
- Transitievergoeding (at minimum - see below for calculation)
- Any additional settlement payment (vergoeding) to reflect the weak dossier position
- Outplacement support
- Positive reference letter (getuigschrift)
- Release of any non-compete or other post-contractual obligations
- Final settlement of all claims (finale kwijting)

**Employee rights**: The employee has a statutory **14-day cooling-off period (bedenktijd)** to revoke the signed agreement (Art. 7:670b lid 2 BW). If the employer fails to mention this right in the VSO, the period extends to 21 days.

**WW (unemployment benefit)**: For WW eligibility, the VSO must be structured so the employee is not treated as having taken the initiative to terminate. Standard VSO language typically achieves this.

**Advantage**: Avoids litigation risk, faster, controllable cost, and avoids the time and reputational risk of kantonrechter proceedings.

**Risk**: The weak dossier position means the employee or their legal representative may demand a higher settlement than the statutory minimum transitievergoeding.

### Option 3: UWV Route

The UWV ontslagvergunning route is **not applicable** for performance-based dismissal. The UWV route is reserved exclusively for:
- **A-grond** (bedrijfseconomische redenen / redundancy)
- **B-grond** (langdurige arbeidsongeschiktheid / 2+ years of illness)

Dismissal for disfunctioneren must go through the **kantonrechter** route.

### Option 4: I-grond (Cumulatie - Combined Grounds, since WAB 2020)

If the performance issues are accompanied by elements of a disrupted employment relationship (verstoorde arbeidsverhouding, g-grond), neither of which individually meets the full threshold, the employer may petition the kantonrechter under the **i-grond** (Art. 7:669 lid 3 sub i BW). This allows combining partial d-grond and partial g-grond (or other grounds).

**Important**: If the kantonrechter grants dissolution on the i-grond, it may award up to **50% additional transitievergoeding** on top of the statutory amount.

## Employee Rights / Employer Obligations

### Notice Period (Opzegtermijn)
For 8 years of service, the statutory employer notice period is **2 months** (Art. 7:672 BW: 5-10 years = 2 months). This period may be modified by an applicable CAO.

### Prohibited Dismissal (Opzegverboden)
Before proceeding, verify that no dismissal prohibition applies:
- The employee is not currently on sick leave (opzegverbod bij ziekte - first 2 years: Art. 7:670 lid 1 BW)
- The employee is not pregnant or on maternity/parental leave
- The employee is not a works council member

**Critical note on illness**: If the employee becomes ill during the dismissal process, the opzegverbod bij ziekte will apply, and proceedings will typically be suspended. Employers sometimes experience employees reporting sick once a termination process begins. Ensure the employee's health and work situation is checked before commencing formal steps.

### Redeployment Obligation (Herplaatsingsplicht)
Before filing for dissolution, the employer must investigate whether the employee can be **redeployed in a suitable alternative position** (passende functie) within the company or group (Art. 7:669 lid 1 BW). Document this investigation. If alternatives exist, the employer may be required to offer them before dissolution is granted.

### Works Council (Ondernemingsraad)
If your organization has a works council (ondernemingsraad), be aware that individual dismissal decisions are generally not subject to formal WOR advice rights (Art. 25 WOR applies to collective/organizational decisions). However, if the dismissal is part of a broader reorganization, WOR involvement may be required.

## Financial Implications

### Transitievergoeding (Mandatory Transition Payment)

Under Art. 7:673 BW (post-WAB): 1/3 monthly salary per year of service, accruing from day one.

**Indicative calculation for 8 years of service:**
- Formula: 8 years x (1/3 monthly salary) = 2.67 monthly salaries
- Based on: gross monthly salary including fixed allowances, average variable pay, and 8% vacation allowance
- **Maximum (2024)**: EUR 94,000 or 1 year's salary if higher
- Applies to both kantonrechter dissolution and if agreed in the VSO
- Note: the transitievergoeding is the **statutory minimum** in a VSO; the employee may negotiate for more given the weak dossier position.

**Example**: If the employee earns EUR 5,000 gross/month (excluding vacation allowance), the indicative transitievergoeding would be approximately EUR 13,333 (before tax adjustments).

### Billijke Vergoeding Risk
If a kantonrechter dissolution is sought with an inadequate dossier, or if steps are taken that constitute ernstig verwijtbaar handelen (seriously culpable employer conduct), the court may award a **billijke vergoeding** in addition to the transitievergoeding. This amount is uncapped and determined by the court based on all circumstances of the case (New Hairstyle, ECLI:NL:HR:2017:1187). This is a significant financial risk that underscores the need for proper dossier documentation.

### I-grond Additional Cost
If dissolution is granted on the i-grond: up to 50% additional transitievergoeding may be awarded by the kantonrechter.

In dismissal situations - particularly those involving performance and a long-standing employment relationship - mediation is frequently advisable and may be required or strongly expected by the court before litigation. Consider engaging a registered mediator (NMI/MfN) to explore whether the situation can be resolved without kantonrechter proceedings.

## Important Warnings

1. **Documentation gap is the critical risk**: Without a proper dossier and documented verbetertraject, a kantonrechter will almost certainly deny a d-grond dissolution request. Do not proceed to court without adequate documentation.

2. **Do not construct a false dossier**: Attempting to backdate documents or create a sham verbetertraject designed to fail is likely to be identified by the court and may result in a billijke vergoeding award, reputational damage, and potentially disciplinary consequences.

3. **Illness risk**: Be alert to the possibility that the employee may report sick once dismissal discussions begin. Illness triggers an opzegverbod and may complicate or delay proceedings significantly.

4. **Age and long tenure considerations**: Courts tend to consider the employee's prospects for finding new employment. An 8-year employee who may be in an older age cohort may face difficulty finding comparable work. This is relevant both for the billijke vergoeding assessment and for VSO negotiation.

5. **CAO provisions may apply**: The applicable CAO (if any) may contain additional requirements for dismissal procedures, enhanced severance arrangements, or notice periods. Always check the applicable CAO before proceeding.
