## Part 1: Kennismigrant Salary Threshold

### What is the Kennismigrant Scheme?

The **kennismigrant** (highly skilled migrant) residence permit is a Dutch immigration scheme that allows Dutch employers (recognized sponsors - erkend referenten) to bring in highly qualified workers from outside the European Economic Area (EEA) under an expedited procedure. The IND processes these applications, typically within two weeks for recognized sponsors.

### Salary Thresholds (Loondrempel)

The kennismigrant scheme requires that the employee receives a **minimum gross monthly salary** that is set by the IND and adjusted annually (typically on 1 January each year). These thresholds are set by the Besluit uitvoering Wet arbeid vreemdelingen (BuWav) and related ministerial orders.

**As of January 2025 (most recently verified figures - verify current 2026 thresholds with IND):**

| Category | Gross Monthly Salary (excluding vacation allowance) |
|----------|-----------------------------------------------------|
| Kennismigrant (general, age 30+) | EUR 5,331 per month |
| Kennismigrant (reduced rate, age under 30) | EUR 3,909 per month |
| Recent graduates (zoekjaar / orientation year) | EUR 2,801 per month |
| EU Blue Card | EUR 5,688 per month |

**Important notes:**
- These thresholds are adjusted annually on 1 January. As of the date of this analysis (March 2026), the applicable 2026 thresholds must be verified directly with the IND website (ind.nl) or via the published ministerial order.
- The salary requirement relates to the **gross monthly salary excluding holiday allowance (vakantiebijslag)**. Holiday allowance of 8% is not counted toward the threshold.
- The salary must be paid regularly each month; variable or performance-based components that are not guaranteed may not be counted.
- The employer must be a **recognized sponsor (erkend referent)** to use the kennismigrant scheme. If not yet recognized, the employer must first apply for this status from the IND before sponsoring a kennismigrant.

### Employer Obligations as Sponsor

As a recognized sponsor for a kennismigrant, the employer has ongoing obligations under the Vreemdelingenwet, including:
- Ensuring the employee continues to meet the salary threshold throughout employment
- Notifying the IND within 4 weeks of changes in the employment relationship (termination, salary reduction below threshold, change of function)
- Retaining relevant records for at least 5 years
- Cooperation with IND supervision and audits

Failure to comply can result in revocation of the recognized sponsor status and fines.

## Part 2: Concurrentiebeding (Non-Compete Clause) in a Fixed-Term Contract

### The Statutory Rule: Art. 7:653 BW

The **concurrentiebeding** (non-compete clause) is governed by Art. 7:653 BW. The WWZ (effective 1 January 2015) introduced a fundamental restriction on non-compete clauses in fixed-term contracts:

**Art. 7:653 lid 2 BW states:**

> A non-competition clause in an employment contract for a definite period (bepaalde tijd) is only valid if the employer includes in the contract itself a written **motivering** (justification / motivation) stating that the clause is necessary due to weighty business or service interests (zwaarwegende bedrijfs- of dienstbelangen).

### What This Means in Practice

In a fixed-term contract (bepaalde tijd), a standard non-compete clause is **in principle null and void (nietig) unless accompanied by a specific, written justification** in the contract itself.

The justification must:
1. Be **included in the contract itself** at the time of signing - it cannot be added retrospectively.
2. Be **sufficiently specific** - a generic reference to business interests or mere repetition of the statutory text is insufficient.
3. **Explain why this specific employee in this specific role** requires a non-compete restriction (i.e., why the employee's access to particular knowledge, client relationships, or trade secrets creates a weighty business interest that justifies restricting their post-employment freedom).

This requirement was authoritatively interpreted by the **Gerechtshof Arnhem-Leeuwarden (24 September 2019, ECLI:NL:GHARL:2019:7739)**, which held that a motivation must be role- and person-specific and go beyond generic language.

### For a Senior Developer in a Kennismigrant Context

A **senior developer** would typically have access to:
- Proprietary technical architecture, code, and systems
- Client-specific integrations or data
- Strategic product roadmaps
- Specific technical methodologies or trade secrets

This type of access may provide sufficient grounds for a valid motivering, but it must be documented and substantiated in the contract language. The motivation should, at minimum, address:
- What specific confidential information or business interests the employee will have access to
- Why those interests are substantial enough to outweigh the employee's freedom of movement after the contract
- What the actual restriction covers (geographic scope, sector, duration, activities covered)

### General Requirements for All Non-Compete Clauses (Regardless of Contract Type)

Even in an indefinite contract, the concurrentiebeding must:
- Be agreed in **writing** (in the employment contract or an annex specifically incorporated by reference)
- The employee must be **18 years or older**

### Court Moderation

Even a validly concluded concurrentiebeding may be **entirely or partially set aside (geheel of gedeeltelijk vernietigd)** by the court under Art. 7:653 lid 3 BW if:
- The employee is unreasonably disadvantaged in relation to the employer's legitimate interest
- Circumstances have changed materially since the clause was agreed

The court may also award **compensation (vergoeding) to the employee** while upholding the non-compete if it imposes a significant restriction (Art. 7:653 lid 5 BW).

## Practical Steps

1. **Salary threshold verification**: Before finalizing the offer, confirm the current 2026 IND salary thresholds at ind.nl. Ensure the agreed gross monthly salary (excluding vacation allowance) meets the applicable threshold for the employee's age category (30+ or under 30).

2. **Recognized sponsor status**: Confirm that your company already holds recognized sponsor (erkend referent) status. If not, initiate the IND application well in advance (processing times vary).

3. **IND application**: For the kennismigrant residence permit, the recognized sponsor files the application with the IND. The employee simultaneously applies for a Temporary Residence Permit (MVV / machtiging tot voorlopig verblijf) at the Dutch embassy in India if required. Processing for recognized sponsors is typically 2 weeks.

4. **Contract drafting - non-compete**: If a concurrentiebeding is desired in the fixed-term contract, engage an employment lawyer to draft a specific, tailored motivering that:
   - Identifies the specific confidential information and proprietary knowledge the senior developer will access
   - Explains the business interest in restricting post-employment competition
   - Specifies the scope, geographic area, and duration of the restriction (proportionality)

5. **Balance proportionality**: A non-compete for a senior developer is more defensible if limited in scope (specific sector or clients), geographic area (Netherlands, or specific regions), and duration (typically 6-12 months). An overbroad clause creates nullification risk.

6. **Review applicable CAO**: If a CAO (e.g., CAO ICT) applies, check whether it contains any provisions regarding concurrentiebedingen or whether its salary scales interact with the kennismigrant threshold.

7. **30% ruling**: For a senior developer from India, consider whether the **30% ruling** (30%-faciliteit, administered by the Belastingdienst) applies. This tax facility allows eligible highly skilled migrants to receive 30% of their gross salary tax-free. It affects the net cost calculation and may be relevant to the salary offer strategy. This falls within the **dutch-tax-law** skill area.
