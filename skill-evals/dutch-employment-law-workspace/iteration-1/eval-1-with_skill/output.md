## Legal Framework

The dismissal of an underperforming employee in the Netherlands is governed by:

- **BW Boek 7, Titel 10** (Art. 7:669 lid 3 sub d BW) -- the "d-grond" (disfunctioneren / dysfunctional performance)
- **Wet Werk en Zekerheid (WWZ, 2015)** -- introduced the closed system of dismissal grounds (gesloten ontslagstelsel), meaning the employer must prove one of the statutory grounds (a through h)
- **Wet Arbeidsmarkt in Balans (WAB, 2020)** -- introduced the i-grond (cumulatiegrond), allowing combination of partial grounds
- **Art. 7:673 BW** -- transitievergoeding (transition payment)
- **Art. 7:672 BW** -- opzegtermijnen (notice periods)

## Analysis

### The Core Problem: Documentation Gap

Based on the facts presented -- 8 years of service, indefinite contract, only verbal discussions about performance, and nothing documented -- the employer faces a **significant obstacle** to any dismissal route based on underperformance. Dutch courts take the documentation requirement for disfunctioneren (d-grond) very seriously. Below is a structured analysis of each available route.

### Route 1: Kantonrechter Ontbinding on D-grond (Disfunctioneren)

This is the primary legal route for performance-based dismissal. To succeed before the kantonrechter under the d-grond (Art. 7:669 lid 3 sub d BW), the employer must demonstrate **all** of the following:

1. **The employee has been clearly informed of the performance issues** -- written warnings, formal performance reviews, documented conversations. Verbal discussions alone are almost certainly insufficient. Courts routinely reject d-grond petitions where the employee was never formally notified in writing.

2. **The employee has been given a genuine opportunity to improve (verbetertraject)** -- a structured performance improvement plan (PIP / verbeterplan) with:
   - Clear, measurable objectives
   - A reasonable timeframe (typically 3-6 months, sometimes longer for long-tenured employees)
   - Adequate support (coaching, training, mentoring)
   - Regular progress evaluations (documented)

3. **The dysfunctioning is not caused by insufficient training or poor working conditions** -- the employer must have provided the tools and training needed.

4. **There is no suitable alternative position** (herplaatsingsmogelijkheden) within a reasonable period (Art. 7:669 lid 1 BW). For an employee with 8 years of service, the court will expect a serious effort to explore redeployment.

**Current status assessment**: With only verbal discussions and no documentation, the employer is **not in a position to file a successful d-grond petition** at this time. A kantonrechter would almost certainly reject the request, potentially awarding costs to the employee.

### Route 2: Kantonrechter Ontbinding on G-grond (Verstoorde Arbeidsverhouding)

If the performance discussions have led to a strained working relationship, the g-grond (disrupted working relationship, Art. 7:669 lid 3 sub g BW) may be considered. However:

- The disruption must be **serious and lasting** (ernstig en duurzaam verstoord)
- The employer must demonstrate that recovery is not possible
- Mediation should have been attempted
- The employer should not have created the disruption itself (courts look unfavorably on employers who manufacture a g-grond to circumvent d-grond requirements)

**Warning**: Courts are alert to employers using the g-grond as a "back door" when they lack a proper disfunctioneringsdossier.

### Route 3: I-grond (Cumulatiegrond -- WAB 2020)

Since the WAB (2020), the i-grond allows the kantonrechter to combine two or more partial grounds (from c through h). For example, partial disfunctioneren (d) plus partial verstoorde arbeidsverhouding (g). However:

- Each ground must be at least **partially** substantiated
- If dismissal is granted on the i-grond, the kantonrechter may award up to **50% extra transitievergoeding** on top of the regular amount
- Without any documentation, even the i-grond will be difficult to establish

### Route 4: Vaststellingsovereenkomst (Mutual Consent / Settlement Agreement)

This is often the **most practical route** in situations where the formal dossier is weak. Key elements:

- Written settlement agreement (beeindigingsovereenkomst) under Art. 7:900 BW
- Employee has a **14-day cooling-off period** (bedenktijd) to revoke acceptance (Art. 7:670b lid 2 BW); if employer fails to mention this right, the period extends to 21 days
- To preserve the employee's eligibility for **WW (unemployment benefits)**, the agreement must be structured so that the initiative for termination comes from the employer (not the employee)
- Standard terms typically include: termination date (respecting notice period), transition payment, final settlement, positive reference letter, release from non-compete (if applicable), outplacement support
- This route requires the employee's willingness to negotiate

### Route 5: UWV Route -- NOT Applicable

The UWV route is reserved for the a-grond (business economic reasons) and b-grond (long-term illness after 2 years). Underperformance does not qualify for UWV processing.

## Employee Rights / Employer Obligations

### Transitievergoeding (Transition Payment -- Art. 7:673 BW)

Regardless of the route chosen (except if the employee is found to be seriously culpable, which is rare in pure performance cases), the employer owes a transition payment:

- **Calculation**: 1/3 monthly salary per year of service (pro rata for partial years)
- **For 8 years of service**: approximately 2.67 months' gross salary (including vacation allowance of 8% and any fixed components)
- **Maximum (2024)**: EUR 94,000 or one year's salary if higher
- Since WAB (2020), the transition payment accrues from day 1

### Notice Period (Art. 7:672 BW)

For 8 years of service (5-10 year bracket):
- **Employer notice period**: 2 months
- **Employee notice period**: 1 month (unless otherwise agreed)
- If proceeding via kantonrechter, the procedural time may be deducted from the notice period (minimum 1 month remains)

### Opzegverboden (Prohibited Dismissals -- Art. 7:670 BW)

Verify that the employee is not protected by any prohibition on dismissal:
- Not currently on sick leave
- Not pregnant or on parental leave
- Not a works council member
- Not engaged in union activities

## Practical Steps

Given the current weak documentation position, the employer should take the following approach:

### Immediate Actions (Weeks 1-2)
1. **Assess the situation honestly** -- Is there a genuine performance issue, or has the employer failed to manage expectations? Consider consulting a Dutch employment lawyer (arbeidsrechtadvocaat).
2. **Check for an applicable CAO** -- CAO provisions may affect dismissal procedures, notice periods, and severance arrangements.
3. **Verify no opzegverbod applies** -- Confirm the employee is not ill, pregnant, or otherwise protected.

### Building the Dossier (Months 1-3)
4. **Document the performance issues in writing** -- Send the employee a formal letter or email summarizing the concerns that have been discussed verbally to date. Be specific, factual, and measurable.
5. **Conduct a formal performance review (functioneringsgesprek / beoordelingsgesprek)** -- Rate performance against objective criteria. Document the outcome in writing and have the employee sign for receipt.
6. **Start a verbetertraject (performance improvement plan)** with:
   - Clear, measurable objectives
   - Realistic timeline (typically 3-6 months; given 8 years of service, courts may expect a longer period)
   - Support offered (coaching, training, reduced workload, mentoring)
   - Regular check-in moments (every 2-4 weeks), documented in writing
   - Clear consequences if improvement is not achieved

### During the Verbetertraject (Months 3-9)
7. **Conduct and document regular evaluations** -- Are objectives being met? What support has been provided? What is the employee's response?
8. **Explore herplaatsingsmogelijkheden (redeployment)** -- Are there alternative suitable positions within the organization?
9. **Consider mediation** if the relationship becomes strained -- this strengthens any future g-grond argument and demonstrates good faith.

### After the Verbetertraject
10. **If insufficient improvement**: the dossier should now support a d-grond petition to the kantonrechter, or provide leverage for a vaststellingsovereenkomst negotiation.
11. **If improvement occurs**: the issue is resolved; continue to monitor and document.

### Alternative: Negotiate a Vaststellingsovereenkomst Now
12. If both parties prefer a swift resolution, the employer can propose a **vaststellingsovereenkomst** immediately. This avoids the need to build a full dossier, but typically requires a more generous financial offer to incentivize the employee's cooperation. Common elements include:
    - Transition payment (at minimum the statutory amount)
    - Additional severance (often negotiated above the statutory amount when the dossier is weak)
    - Outplacement budget
    - Positive reference
    - Release from non-compete or non-solicitation clauses
    - Retention of company equipment (phone, laptop) as a goodwill gesture

### Note on Mediation
In dismissal situations, mediation may be required or is strongly advisable. Courts expect that the employer has made reasonable efforts to resolve the situation before seeking judicial dissolution. Consider engaging a mediator (MfN-registermediator) early in the process.
