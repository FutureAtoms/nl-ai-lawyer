- **Finding**: Confidential information is defined as "business information." This is an extremely vague and unclear term with no boundaries or further specification.
- **Market Standard**: A well-drafted NDA under Dutch law provides a clear, specific definition. Common formulations include:
  - Specific categories (technical data, trade secrets, customer lists, financial projections, pricing strategies, R&D data, etc.)

### 2. Scope of Obligations

### 3. Standard Exceptions / Carve-outs

- **Rating**: RED
- **Finding**: No exceptions or carve-outs are mentioned. Assuming none are present.
- **Market Standard**: Every well-drafted NDA under Dutch law includes at minimum these four standard exceptions (uitzonderingen) from the confidentiality obligation:

  1. **Public domain** (openbaar domein): Information that is or becomes publicly available through no fault of the receiving party. Without this carve-out, you could theoretically be held to a confidentiality obligation for information that becomes a published news story.

  2. **Prior knowledge** (voorkennis): Information already known to you at the time of disclosure. Without this, you are prohibited from using or sharing information you legitimately possessed before entering the NDA.

  3. **Independent development** (onafhankelijke ontwikkeling): Information you develop independently without using the disclosing party's information. Particularly critical for companies conducting R&D.

  4. **Third-party disclosure** (derdenverkrijging): Information received from a third party who is not under a confidentiality obligation regarding that information.

  **Additionally missing — legally compelled disclosure**: You must also have the right (and often the legal obligation) to disclose information when required by law — for example, pursuant to a court order under Art. 843a Rv (exhibitieplicht), a Wwft (anti-money laundering) reporting obligation, a request from tax authorities (AWR), or supervisory requirements from AFM or DNB. An NDA cannot override these statutory obligations, but the absence of an express carve-out creates contractual risk and friction when compliance is required.

- **Action**: **Reject / Negotiate** — All four standard carve-outs plus a legally compelled disclosure provision must be added. This is non-negotiable for a commercially workable NDA.
