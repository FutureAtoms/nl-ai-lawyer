## Analysis

### 1. Share Capital — Flex-BV and the No-Minimum Rule

Since the introduction of the **Flex-BV** on 1 October 2012, the Dutch BV has **no minimum share capital**. The previous minimum of EUR 18,000 was abolished. Shares can now be issued with a nominal value as low as **EUR 0.01** per share (or in any other currency).

Practical options for a 60/40 split:

| Option | Total shares | Founder A (60%) | Founder B (40%) |
|--------|-------------|-----------------|-----------------|
| Simple | 100 shares @ EUR 0.01 | 60 shares | 40 shares |
| Common | 1,000 shares @ EUR 0.01 | 600 shares | 400 shares |
| Multiple classes | Class A + Class B | Can attach different rights per class |

**Recommendation**: Issue 100 ordinary shares at EUR 0.01 nominal value each, with Founder A holding 60 and Founder B holding 40. Total issued share capital: EUR 1.00. This is simple, clean, and fully Flex-BV compliant. There is no requirement for a bank deposit confirmation for cash contributions since Flex-BV.

**Important**: Although there is no minimum statutory capital, you should ensure the BV has sufficient working capital to operate. The **uitkeringstest** (Art. 2:216 BW) — requiring board approval before any distribution — acts as the creditor protection mechanism in lieu of minimum capital.

### 2. Corporate Governance Structure

#### 2.1 Management Board (Bestuur)

Under **Art. 2:239 BW**, the bestuur (management board) is charged with managing the BV. Both founders as directors is straightforward and common for startup BVs.

**Recommended structure**:

- **Two directors**: Founder A (60% shareholder) and Founder B (40% shareholder), both appointed as zelfstandig bevoegd (independently authorized to represent the BV) OR gezamenlijk bevoegd (jointly authorized) for transactions above a certain amount.
- **Signing authority at KVK**: Register both directors at the KVK (Kamer van Koophandel) with their respective signing authority (tekenbevoegdheid). For startups, many founders register both directors as zelfstandig bevoegd for operational efficiency, but restrict large transactions (above a threshold, e.g., EUR 25,000) to require joint authorization — this can be recorded in the statuten or internal board rules.
- **Director appointments** (Art. 2:242 BW): By the general meeting of shareholders (AVA) — in the statuten you can specify that the AVA appoints directors by absolute majority.

**Compensation**: Director remuneration is determined by the AVA unless delegated (Art. 2:245 BW).

#### 2.2 Supervisory Board vs. Advisory Board

Under Art. 2:250 BW, the statuten may provide for a **Raad van Commissarissen (RvC)** — a supervisory board with statutory authority to supervise the management board and with potential approval rights for certain transactions.

However, for an early-stage BV, the question is whether you want:

**Option A: Formal Raad van Commissarissen (RvC)**
- Statutory supervisory body under Art. 2:250–2:253 BW
- Members appointed by the AVA
- RvC supervises management; has statutory powers (e.g., can request information, has certain veto rights if granted in statuten)
- Fiduciary duties apply to RvC members (Art. 2:9 BW analogously; WBTR)
- Conflict of interest rules apply (Art. 2:239 lid 6 BW applies to directors; WBTR extends analogous rules to supervisory board members)
- Adds governance overhead; usually appropriate once the company has significant revenue or investors

**Option B: Raad van Advies (Advisory Board)**
- No statutory basis in BW Boek 2
- Created by contract (either in the statuten or by a separate advisory board charter/reglement)
- Members have advisory-only role; no statutory duties or powers
- No fiduciary duties under Dutch corporate law (unless also directors)
- More flexible: meet as needed, no quorum rules unless set internally
- Preferred for most early-stage BVs that want experienced advisors without formal governance obligations

**Recommendation for this BV**: Establish a **Raad van Advies (advisory board)** rather than a formal RvC. This gives you the benefit of experienced guidance without statutory complexity. Formalize the advisory board in a **Reglement Raad van Advies** (advisory board charter) that sets out:
- Composition (number of members, appointment procedure)
- Frequency and format of meetings
- Topics on which the board may be consulted
- Compensation of advisory board members
- Confidentiality obligations
- Duration of appointments and dismissal

If in the future you bring in institutional investors or the company grows substantially, you can establish a formal RvC at that time via an amendment to the statuten.

#### 2.3 Decision-Making and Deadlock

With a 60/40 split and both founders as directors, the majority shareholder (60%) controls the AVA and — if voting mirrors shareholding — controls the board as well. However:

- **Supermajority requirements**: For certain important decisions, you should require a higher quorum (e.g., 75% or 80% of the votes) — this gives Founder B (40%) a practical veto on fundamental matters. Include this in both the statuten and the aandeelhoudersovereenkomst.
- **Board deadlock**: If both directors must act jointly and they disagree, consider a deadlock mechanism in the SHA (see below).
- **Reserved matters**: List specific matters that require unanimous board approval (or AVA approval at a higher threshold) — e.g., changes to the business plan, new share issuances, major asset disposals, incurring debt above a threshold.

### 3. KVK Registration

Under the **Handelsregisterwet 2007**, the BV must be registered at the **Kamer van Koophandel (KVK)** within **8 days** of incorporation (i.e., within 8 days of the execution of the notarial deed of incorporation).

**Registration requirements**:
- Company name and any trade names (handelsnamen)
- Legal form: Besloten Vennootschap (BV)
- Registered office (statutaire zetel) — must be in the Netherlands
- SBI code (business activity classification)
- Names and details of directors and their signing authority (tekenbevoegdheid)
- Share capital details
- UBO (Ultimate Beneficial Owner) registration: Both founders hold more than 25%, so both must be registered as UBOs in the KVK UBO register (Wet registratie uiteindelijk belanghebbenden)

**KVK registration fee**: EUR 75 (one-time fee, as of 2025)

**Post-registration steps**:
1. Obtain VAT number (btw-nummer) from the Belastingdienst
2. Register for payroll taxes if employing staff (loonheffing)
3. Open a business bank account (most banks require a KVK uittreksel and notarial deed)
4. Register in the UBO register within 8 days of incorporation

### 4. Content of the Aandeelhoudersovereenkomst (Shareholders' Agreement / SHA)

The **aandeelhoudersovereenkomst (SHA)** is a contractual agreement between the shareholders (and optionally the BV itself as a party). It supplements the statuten and governs the internal relationship between the shareholders. Unlike the statuten, the SHA is private (not registered at KVK) and is binding only on the parties to it.

**Key provisions to include**:

#### a) Governance and Voting Rights
- Confirm the 60/40 share split and any class rights
- Decision-making procedures at AVA level
- Supermajority requirements: list "reserved matters" requiring more than simple majority (e.g., 75% or unanimous):
  - Amendment of statuten
  - Issue of new shares
  - Sale of substantially all assets
  - Changes to dividend policy
  - Approval of annual budget / business plan
  - Incurring debt above a threshold
  - Entry into related-party transactions above a threshold
  - Liquidation or dissolution

#### b) Board Governance
- Director appointment rights (which shareholder can nominate which director)
- Reserved matters requiring unanimous board approval
- Board meeting frequency, quorum, and voting procedures
- Matters requiring AVA approval (as opposed to board-only approval)
- Deadlock resolution mechanism (see below)

#### c) Share Transfer Restrictions (Blokkeringsregeling)
Since the Flex-BV abolished the mandatory blokkeringsregeling, you should include your agreed transfer restrictions in either the statuten or the SHA (or both):
- **Right of first refusal** (voorkeursrecht): If a shareholder wishes to transfer shares, the other shareholder has the right to purchase the shares on the same terms first.
- **Lock-up period**: Neither founder may transfer shares for a defined period (e.g., 2 years) without the other's consent — encourages commitment and prevents early exit.
- **Drag-along right**: If Founder A (60%) wants to sell the company, Founder A can compel Founder B to also sell their shares to the same buyer on the same terms (protects buyer from minority holdouts).
- **Tag-along right**: If Founder A sells their shares, Founder B has the right to "tag along" and sell their shares at the same price/terms (protects minority from being left behind with a new majority shareholder).
- **Good leaver / Bad leaver** provisions: Define what happens to a departing founder's shares — if they leave as a "good leaver" (illness, death, mutual consent), they retain shares or receive fair value; if they leave as a "bad leaver" (breach, competing, unilateral resignation), their shares may be repurchased at par/nominal value.

#### d) Dividend Policy
- Agree on a dividend distribution policy (e.g., minimum % of distributable profits to be distributed annually, subject to the uitkeringstest under Art. 2:216 BW)
- Or agree that profits are retained for reinvestment with unanimous approval required for distributions

#### e) Non-Compete and Non-Solicitation
- Duration and geographic scope of any non-compete obligation for each founder while a shareholder and for a defined period after exit
- Non-solicitation of employees and clients

#### f) Intellectual Property
- Confirm that all IP created by the founders in connection with the business is assigned to the BV (if not already done at incorporation)
- Confirm that the BV owns the codebase, trademarks, domain names, etc.

#### g) Deadlock
- Define what constitutes a deadlock (e.g., board cannot agree on a reserved matter after two rounds of voting)
- Deadlock resolution procedure: could include mediation, a casting vote mechanism, or ultimately a buy-sell (Texas shootout / shotgun) clause:
  - **Texas shootout**: Either party can trigger the mechanism by offering to buy the other's shares at a specified price. The other party can either accept (sell) or reverse the transaction (buy the triggering party's shares at the same price). This aligns incentives and prevents abuse.

#### h) Exit
- IPO or trade sale process: agree on procedures for a liquidity event
- Consent required for external financing / venture capital investment (typically unanimous or supermajority)
- Right of first refusal for the BV to acquire shares before external sale

#### i) Dispute Resolution
- Governing law: Dutch law
- Dispute resolution: arbitration (NAI — Nederlands Arbitrage Instituut) or mediation before litigation; if litigation, Rotterdam or Amsterdam District Court (whichever is agreed)
- The SHA should specify that disputes about shareholder rights are governed by the SHA first, and the statuten second

### 5. Statuten vs. SHA — Key Differences

| Feature | Statuten | Aandeelhoudersovereenkomst (SHA) |
|---------|----------|----------------------------------|
| Legal basis | Art. 2:177 BW | Contract (overeenkomst) |
| Form | Notarial deed; public (KVK) | Private document; not registered |
| Binding on third parties | Yes (once registered) | No (only parties to the SHA) |
| Enforceability against BV | Yes | Only if BV is a party |
| Amendment | Requires AVA resolution + notaris | Written agreement of parties |
| Successor shareholders | Yes | Requires accession clause |

**Practical tip**: Include an **accession clause** (toetredingsbeding) in the SHA requiring any new shareholder to sign an accession agreement accepting the SHA as a condition of share transfer. This ensures the SHA remains binding after share transfers.

## Key Risks

1. **Governance deadlock**: With two founders and a 60/40 split, the minority founder (40%) has little formal protection without a carefully drafted SHA. Without supermajority provisions and deadlock mechanisms, the 60% founder controls everything.

2. **No minimum capital**: Without the EUR 18,000 minimum, there is no guaranteed buffer for creditors. Ensure the BV is adequately capitalized for its initial operations. Directors are personally liable under the uitkeringstest (Art. 2:216 lid 3 BW) if they approve distributions that leave the BV unable to pay creditors.

3. **Founder exit without protections**: Without good leaver/bad leaver provisions and lock-up periods, a departing founder could sell their stake to an unwanted third party.

4. **IP not transferred**: If founders created IP before incorporation without a formal assignment agreement, the IP may remain personally owned. Remedy this at incorporation via an IP assignment agreement.

5. **WBTR compliance**: Post-1 July 2021, all directors must comply with the conflict of interest rules under Art. 2:239 lid 6 BW. If either founder also controls another company or has other business interests, document how conflicts are managed.

## Recommendations

1. **Use a notaris and a corporate lawyer in parallel**: The notaris handles incorporation; a corporate lawyer should draft the SHA. Do not rely on template statuten alone for a multi-founder BV.

2. **Flex-BV with 100 shares at EUR 0.01**: Simple, cost-effective, and allows easy recapitalization in future funding rounds.

3. **Establish a Raad van Advies** (not a formal RvC) initially, with the option to convert to a formal supervisory board if institutional investors join.

4. **Register both founders at KVK** with appropriate signing authority — consider joint authorization for amounts above EUR 25,000 (or an appropriate threshold for your business scale).

5. **The SHA is critical**: Invest time and legal fees in a comprehensive aandeelhoudersovereenkomst. It is far cheaper to draft it correctly at incorporation than to resolve a shareholder dispute later. Minimum SHA provisions: share transfer restrictions, reserved matters (supermajority), deadlock mechanism, good leaver/bad leaver, non-compete, and dividend policy.

6. **Plan for dilution**: If you anticipate future investment rounds, include an anti-dilution mechanism and pre-emption rights (voorkeursrecht) in the SHA or statuten for new share issuances.

7. **UBO registration**: Both founders (60% and 40%) exceed the 25% threshold and must be registered as UBOs. Failure to register is an offense under the Handelsregisterwet.

8. **Tax structuring**: Consider whether a holding company (persoonlijke holdings) structure is appropriate for each founder — this can have significant income tax (Box 2) and VPB implications. Consult a tax advisor (belastingadviseur) at incorporation.
