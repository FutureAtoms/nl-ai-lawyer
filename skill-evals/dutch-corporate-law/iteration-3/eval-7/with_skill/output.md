## Analysis

### 1. Minimum Share Capital — Flex-BV

**Since the Flex-BV Act (1 October 2012), there is NO minimum share capital requirement for a Dutch BV.**

Specifically:
- **Minimum share value**: EUR 0.01 per share (or any fraction thereof)
- **Pre-2012 rule** (no longer applicable): The old BV required a minimum paid-in capital of EUR 18,000. This requirement was abolished entirely by the Flex-BV Act.
- **Practical recommendation**: While EUR 0.01 is legally sufficient, founders typically capitalize their BV at a more substantial amount for operational, credibility, and banking purposes. Common starting capital in a two-founder technology/services BV is EUR 100 – EUR 50,000 (issued share capital). The specific amount is a commercial decision, not a legal requirement.
- **Legal basis**: Art. 2:178 BW (notarial deed requirement) and Art. 2:191 BW (share issuance); no minimum capital mandate post Flex-BV.

**No bank certificate is required** for cash contributions under the Flex-BV regime (only for contributions in kind / inbreng in natura does an accountant's statement remain required — Art. 2:204a BW).

### 2. Share Structure for a 60/40 Founder Split

For a 60/40 equity split between two founders, multiple approaches are available:

**Option A — Simple ordinary shares (aandelen gewone)**:
- Issue 60 ordinary shares to Founder A and 40 ordinary shares to Founder B (or any multiple thereof)
- Each share carries 1 vote per share (Art. 2:217 BW default)
- Founder A has 60% voting control
- Both founders share profits at 60/40

**Option B — Equal voting with unequal economics (if desired)**:
- Issue priority shares (prioriteitsaandelen) or preference shares (preferente aandelen) to differentiate voting rights from economic rights
- Since Flex-BV 2012, shares without voting rights (stemrechtloze aandelen) or shares without profit rights (winstrechtloze aandelen) are permitted (but not both simultaneously — Art. 2:228 BW)
- This enables more complex governance structures

**Recommended for standard 60/40 setup**: Option A (simple ordinary shares) is the most transparent and operationally straightforward approach. The 60/40 split means Founder A has a controlling majority for all resolutions requiring a simple majority, but NOT for supermajority decisions (typically 2/3 or 3/4 of votes, as specified in the statuten or SHA).

**Blokkeringsregeling (share transfer restriction)**:
Since Flex-BV 2012, a blokkeringsregeling (requiring board approval or first-offer to co-shareholders on share transfers) is no longer mandatory but is strongly recommended for a two-founder startup. Without it, either founder can transfer their shares to a third party without restriction (Art. 2:195 BW). The SHA should also address transfer restrictions.

### 3. Governance Structure Recommendation

#### A. Management Board (Bestuur) — Both Founders as Directors

**Recommended structure**:
- Both Founder A and Founder B appointed as managing directors (bestuurders) of the BV
- **Signing authority (vertegenwoordigingsbevoegdheid)**: Register at KVK whether directors are:
  - *Gezamenlijk bevoegd* (joint authority): Both must sign together for all acts — provides mutual check but operationally cumbersome
  - *Zelfstandig bevoegd* (individual authority): Each can act alone — operationally flexible but requires trust
  - *Zelfstandig bevoegd tot EUR X* (individual up to amount, joint above): A common middle-ground approach (e.g., individual up to EUR 25,000, joint above)

**Practical recommendation**: For day-to-day operations, individual authority up to a defined threshold, with joint authority required for significant transactions (above EUR 10,000-25,000, real estate transactions, hiring/firing senior staff, new debt obligations above EUR X). These thresholds can be set in the SHA rather than requiring amendment to the KVK registration.

**Legal basis**: Art. 2:239-240 BW (board management and representation), Art. 2:242 BW (director appointment by general meeting / AVA).

**WBTR (2021) — Conflict of Interest**:
Under Art. 2:239 lid 6 BW (post-WBTR), if a director has a personal interest that conflicts with the company's interest, that director must abstain from deliberation and voting on the relevant decision. This is critical for a two-director board: if Founder A has a conflict, and Founder A must abstain, Founder B alone makes the decision — and vice versa. The statuten should address this scenario explicitly, including whether the remaining director has authority to act alone when the other is conflicted.

#### B. Advisory Board (Raad van Advies)

An advisory board (raad van advies) is not a statutory body under BW Boek 2. It is a purely contractual/voluntary governance body. This is different from a:
- **Raad van Commissarissen (RvC)**: Statutory supervisory board with formal oversight and approval powers over the bestuur (Art. 2:250 BW) — not what you want at the founding stage
- **One-tier board (Monistisch bestuur)**: Non-executive directors formally on the board with supervisory duties (Art. 2:239a BW) — a more formalized structure

**Recommendation**: At the founding stage, an advisory board (raad van advies) is the appropriate structure:
- Members are advisors, not directors — they have no fiduciary duties or legal liability as directors
- The BV is not legally bound by the advisory board's recommendations
- Composition is flexible: can include investors, sector experts, mentors
- No KVK registration required for advisory board members (unless they also have signing authority)
- Governed by the SHA or a separate advisory board charter (adviesraad reglement)
- Advisory board members are typically compensated with equity (option plan) or modest cash retainer

**If, in future, stronger governance is desired**: Consider adding a formal RvC or converting to a one-tier board (Art. 2:239a BW) — but this adds cost and formality not typically warranted at founding stage.

#### C. General Meeting of Shareholders (Algemene Vergadering van Aandeelhouders — AVA)

The AVA is the highest decision-making body of the BV. Key decisions requiring AVA resolution:
- Appointment and dismissal of directors (Art. 2:242, 2:244 BW)
- Amendment of statuten (Art. 2:230 BW — requires notarial deed)
- Profit distribution / dividend (Art. 2:216 BW — subject to uitkeringstest)
- Major structural decisions (merger, demerger, dissolution)
- Approval of the annual accounts (Art. 2:210 lid 3 BW)
- Any decisions reserved to the AVA in the statuten or SHA

### 4. KVK Registration Requirements

The BV must be registered at the KVK (Kamer van Koophandel — Chamber of Commerce) within 8 days of incorporation (Handelsregisterwet 2007, Art. 18).

**Registration process**:
1. Engage a Dutch notaris to draft the notariële akte van oprichting (deed of incorporation) including the statuten
2. Notarial deed executed (akte verleden) — the BV legally exists from this moment
3. KVK registration: typically done by the notaris on behalf of the founders
4. KVK number (8-digit) assigned
5. RSIN number assigned (for government/tax correspondence)
6. UBO-register filing: Both founders likely qualify as UBOs (each holds >25% of shares)

**Costs (indicative)**:
- Notaris fees: EUR 500 – 2,000+ depending on complexity and notary
- KVK registration: EUR 75 (one-time, as of 2024)
- UBO registration: No separate fee (included with KVK)

**Post-registration steps**:
- Obtain btw-nummer (VAT number) from Belastingdienst
- Register for payroll tax (loonbelasting) if employing staff
- Open business bank account (requires KVK uittreksel; banks may require additional KYC documentation)
- Register UBOs in the UBO-register

### 5. Aandeelhoudersovereenkomst (SHA) — Key Provisions

The aandeelhoudersovereenkomst (shareholder agreement / SHA) is a private agreement between the shareholders that supplements the statuten. It is not filed with the KVK and remains confidential between the parties.

**Why an SHA is essential for a 60/40 two-founder BV**:
- The statuten govern the company vis-à-vis the outside world and all shareholders; the SHA governs the founders' relationship with each other in detail
- The SHA can contain provisions that are not practically put in statuten (e.g., day-to-day operational matters, vesting schedules, dispute resolution mechanisms)
- The SHA is immediately binding and easier to amend (by mutual agreement) than the statuten (which require a notarial deed amendment under Art. 2:230 BW)

**Recommended SHA Provisions**:

#### a. Governance and Decision-Making
- **Reserved matters**: Define decisions that require either unanimity (both founders must agree) or qualified majority (e.g., 75%), regardless of the voting shares. Example reserved matters:
  - Issuing new shares or granting options (dilution protection)
  - Incurring debt above EUR X
  - Entering into material contracts above EUR X
  - Hiring/firing senior management
  - Amendments to the statuten
  - Merger, demerger, sale of the company
  - Related-party transactions above EUR X
  - Dividend distribution / profit retention

#### b. Founder Vesting (Cliff and Vesting Schedule)
- **Highly recommended**: Even in a 60/40 split, both founders' shares should be subject to vesting. Standard Dutch startup practice: 4-year vesting with a 1-year cliff (25% vests after 12 months, remaining 75% monthly over 36 months).
- Mechanism: The SHA includes a "good leaver / bad leaver" scheme:
  - **Good leaver** (resigned after cliff, died, permanent disability): Retains vested shares at fair market value
  - **Bad leaver** (resigned before cliff, dismissed for cause): Must offer unvested shares back at nominal value or a discounted price

#### c. Share Transfer Restrictions
- **Right of first refusal (ROFR)**: If a founder wants to sell their shares, the other founder (and/or the company) must be offered first right of refusal at the same price.
- **Tag-along right (meekooprecht)**: If Founder A sells to a third party, Founder B has the right to sell their shares on the same terms (tag-along / meekopen). This is critical protection for the 40% minority founder.
- **Drag-along right (meenemenrecht)**: If Founder A (majority) agrees to sell the company, Founder B (minority) must also sell their shares on the same terms — this enables a clean exit.
- **Lock-up**: Shares cannot be transferred for an initial period (e.g., 24 months from incorporation) without the other founder's consent.

#### d. Protection of Minority Founder (40% holder)
The 40% holder does not have a statutory blocking minority (which in Dutch law requires 1/3+ of votes for decisions requiring 2/3 majority). The SHA should therefore explicitly protect the minority founder:
- Require unanimity for the reserved matters listed above
- Veto rights over issuance of new shares that would dilute below a threshold
- Board representation rights: as long as a founder holds >X% of shares, they retain the right to be on the board (unilateral appointment/removal right)
- Information rights: regular financial reporting obligations beyond the statutory minimum

#### e. Deadlock Resolution
A two-person board and a 60/40 shareholder structure can lead to deadlocks on decisions requiring unanimity. The SHA should include a deadlock resolution mechanism:
- **Cooling-off period**: 30-60 day negotiation/mediation period
- **Mediation**: Independent mediator agreed by both parties
- **Buy-sell (Texas shootout) mechanism**: Either founder can offer to buy the other's shares at a stated price; the other must either accept (sell) or buy at the same price. This creates mutually balanced pressure to set a fair price.
- **Arbitration**: Final fallback dispute resolution (NAI arbitration is common for Dutch startup SHAs)

#### f. Non-Compete and Confidentiality
- **Non-compete during employment/directorship**: Both founders agree not to engage in competing activities while active in the BV
- **Post-termination non-compete**: Maximum 1-2 years for Dutch B2B context (proportionality under Art. 6:248 lid 2 BW); must be specific in scope and geography
- **Confidentiality**: Perpetual obligation regarding company information
- **IP assignment**: All IP created by founders in the context of the BV should be assigned to (or confirmed to be owned by) the BV. Under Dutch law, IP created by an independent contractor does not automatically transfer — explicit written assignment per Art. 2 Auteurswet is required.

#### g. Exit and Liquidity Provisions
- **Exit mechanism**: Define events that constitute exit triggers (IPO, sale of >50% of shares, asset sale, merger)
- **Liquidation preference**: If outside investors participate in later rounds, the SHA should address preference shares and liquidation waterfall
- **Drag-along threshold**: Define the minimum sale consideration at which drag-along can be triggered
- **Exit timeline**: Optional provision specifying a default exit timeline (e.g., if no exit within 7 years, either founder can trigger a buy-sell mechanism)

## Key Risks

1. **No SHA / inadequate SHA**: The most common and most costly mistake in founder BVs. Without a robust SHA, disputes about decision-making, exit, and founder departures are governed by the minimal statutory defaults — which are often inadequate for startup/growth company situations.

2. **No vesting**: If a founder leaves early with 60% or 40% of the shares, the remaining founder(s) and the company are severely impaired. Vesting is essential.

3. **WBTR conflict of interest with two-director board**: If both founders are directors and one has a conflict, the remaining director must act alone. If both have conflicts simultaneously (e.g., in a related-party transaction they both benefit from), a third person must represent the company or the AVA must intervene. Statuten and SHA should address this explicitly.

4. **Dilution without protection**: Future financing rounds can dilute both founders. The SHA should include pre-emption rights on new share issuances (Art. 2:206a BW provides default pre-emption rights but these can be modified or waived).

5. **Tax structuring**: A direct BV ownership structure without a personal holding company (persoonlijke holding) may be suboptimal for the founders from an income tax perspective. Common Dutch startup structure: each founder holds their shares through a personal BV (persoonlijke holding-BV), which owns shares in the operating BV. This enables income optimization through dividend timing. Consult a belastingadviseur before incorporating.

## Recommendations

1. **Incorporate using a notaris** with experience in startup/entrepreneurial BV incorporations. Many Dutch notarissen offer standardized BV incorporation packages with Flex-BV-compatible statuten.
2. **Draft a comprehensive SHA immediately**: Do not defer the SHA until later. The SHA should be signed concurrently with or immediately after incorporation.
3. **Consider personal holding structure**: Before incorporating the operating BV, consult a tax advisor on whether a holding BV structure is advisable.
4. **Include vesting in the SHA**: 4-year vesting with 1-year cliff is standard practice in the Dutch startup ecosystem.
5. **Register both founders at KVK** with appropriate (individual up to amount, joint above) signing authority.
6. **Establish an advisory board** by adopting an adviesraad reglement as an annex to the SHA or as a separate document — keep it informal and low-cost at the founding stage.
7. **Consider option pool**: If the BV expects to attract employees or advisors with equity compensation, establish an option pool (ESOP or equity plan under Dutch law, typically via synthetic options / phantom equity or actual option rights under Art. 2:191a BW) early.
