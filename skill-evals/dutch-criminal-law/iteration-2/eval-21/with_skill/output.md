## Legal Framework

### The Core Offense: Valsheid in Geschrift (Art. 225 Sr)

**Artikel 225 Sr** criminalizes the making, falsifying, or using of false documents (geschriften) with the intent to use them as genuine and unforged. The provision applies to:

- **Art. 225 lid 1 Sr**: Making or falsifying a document capable of serving as proof, with the intent (oogmerk) to use it or have it used as genuine and unforged – maximum penalty: **6 years imprisonment** or a 5th category fine (EUR 90,000).
- **Art. 225 lid 2 Sr**: Intentionally using a document known to be false or falsified, with the same intent – same maximum penalty of 6 years.

**Application to false invoices**: False invoices (valselijk opgemaakte facturen) are paradigmatic examples of "geschriften" under art. 225 Sr. The Hoge Raad confirmed in HR 22 april 2014, ECLI:NL:HR:2014:953 that electronic documents and digital invoices also qualify as a "geschrift" if they are intended to serve as proof in legal dealings. False invoicing in the context of a financial fraud investigation was directly addressed in HR 7 februari 2017, ECLI:NL:HR:2017:168, where the HR addressed the relationship between valsheid in geschrift (art. 225 Sr) and fiscal fraud (art. 69 AWR) when false documents support fraudulent tax returns.

**Key element – oogmerk**: The offense requires the specific intent (oogmerk) to use the document as genuine and unforged (HR 14 mei 1957, NJ 1957/472 – Oogmerk-arrest). This subjective element distinguishes criminal forgery from innocent administrative errors.

### Corporate Criminal Liability: Art. 51 Sr

The question of whether your **company itself** (the rechtspersoon) faces criminal liability is governed by **art. 51 Sr** – the cornerstone of corporate criminal liability in the Netherlands.

**Art. 51 lid 1 Sr** expressly provides that criminal offenses can be committed by natural persons *and* by legal entities (rechtspersonen), including BVs, NVs, and other corporate forms.

**Art. 51 lid 2 Sr** further provides that if an offense is committed by a legal entity, prosecution can be brought against:
1. **The legal entity itself** (de rechtspersoon), **and/or**
2. **Those who ordered the commission** (opdracht geven) of the offense, **and/or**
3. **Those who had de facto control** (feitelijke leiding geven) over the prohibited conduct

This means the company, the financial director, and potentially other officers may all face prosecution simultaneously or alternatively.

### Attribution Test: Drijfmest Criteria

The leading authority for attributing criminal conduct to a legal entity is **HR 21 oktober 2003, ECLI:NL:HR:2003:AF7938 (Drijfmest-arrest)**. Under the Drijfmest criteria, the FIOD and the Openbaar Ministerie (OM) will assess whether the conduct of the financial director can "reasonably be attributed" (redelijkerwijs worden toegerekend) to your company by examining non-exhaustive factors:

1. Did the conduct (submission of false invoices) occur within the **normal business operations** of the company?
2. Did the company **benefit** from the false invoicing (e.g., reduced tax liability, inflated reimbursement claims, improper payments)?
3. Did the company have **beschikkingsmacht** (control power) over whether the conduct occurred?
4. Did the company **accept** the conduct or similar conduct, including by failing to implement adequate controls or compliance measures?

The **IJzerdraad-arrest (HR 23 februari 1954, NJ 1954/378)** established the foundational criteria of "beschikken en aanvaarden," which have been superseded but not abandoned by the Drijfmest standard.

In a case involving a financial director submitting false invoices in the course of their duties, the attribution criteria are likely to be met: the conduct occurred in the sphere of the company's financial operations, served or appeared to serve the company's interests, and the company either had inadequate controls (acceptance by omission) or may have knowingly benefited.

### Feitelijk Leidinggeven by Other Officers

Beyond the financial director themselves, other officers and directors may face liability as **feitelijk leidinggevers** under art. 51 lid 2 sub 2 Sr if the **Slavenburg II criteria** (HR 16 december 1986, ECLI:NL:HR:1986:AC9607) are met:

- They were **aware** (or had reason to be aware) of the false invoicing;
- They were in a **position to prevent or stop** the conduct;
- They **consciously accepted** (bewust aanvaard) the substantial chance (aanmerkelijke kans) that the offense was being committed, including by failing to act (omission).

The Hoge Raad refined these criteria in HR 1 juni 2021, ECLI:NL:HR:2021:818, confirming that passive directors who fail to take preventive measures can also be held liable as feitelijk leidinggevers, even without active involvement.

## The FIOD Investigation

The **FIOD** (Fiscale Inlichtingen- en Opsporingsdienst) is the Dutch Tax and Financial Investigation Service, operating under the Ministry of Finance. The FIOD specializes in fiscal fraud, financial crime, and economic offenses, and conducts investigations under the supervision of the Functioneel Parket – the specialized prosecution office for complex financial, economic, and environmental crime.

### The Raid (Doorzoeking)

The FIOD raid (doorzoeking) will have been authorized by an onderzoeksrechter (examining magistrate) or by the officier van justitie under the powers of the Wetboek van Strafvordering (Sv). During the raid:

- The FIOD may **seize documents, computers, servers, and other data carriers** under art. 94 and 96 Sv.
- The FIOD may **question employees** – but employees who are suspects have the **right to remain silent** (zwijgrecht, art. 29 Sv). The cautie (caution/Miranda warning) must be given before any interrogation of a suspect.
- **Legal professional privilege (verschoningsrecht)**: Documents subject to legal privilege (i.e., communications between the company and its lawyers) may not be seized – HR 1 juli 2014, ECLI:NL:HR:2014:1563. If the FIOD attempts to seize such documents, the company's lawyer must immediately assert the privilege.
- **Nemo tenetur**: The company cannot be compelled to create new documents or compile information that does not already exist, but pre-existing documents may be required to be produced – HR 18 juni 2019, ECLI:NL:HR:2019:965.

### Post-Raid Procedure

Following the raid, the FIOD will analyze seized materials and prepare a process-verbaal (official report) for the Functioneel Parket. The OM then decides whether to:
1. Prosecute the company and/or individuals (dagvaarding/summons);
2. Offer a settlement (transactie under art. 74 Sr) to avoid prosecution;
3. Decline prosecution (seponering).

For large corporate fraud cases, the OM may offer a **hoge transactie** (high-value corporate settlement) – the Dutch equivalent of a deferred prosecution agreement. ING Bank's EUR 775 million settlement in 2018 for Wwft violations illustrates the scale these settlements can reach.

## Rights of the Company and Its Employees

- **Right to remain silent**: All suspects (including the financial director and any other employees who become suspects) have the right to remain silent (art. 29 Sv). No adverse inference may be drawn from silence.
- **Right to counsel**: The suspect has the right to consult a lawyer before police questioning (**Salduz rights** – derived from ECHR art. 6). The company itself should instruct legal counsel immediately.
- **Legal privilege**: Communications with the company's lawyers are protected by the verschoningsrecht (art. 218 Sv).
- **Notification rights**: The company has the right to be informed of the charges (art. 27c Sv).
