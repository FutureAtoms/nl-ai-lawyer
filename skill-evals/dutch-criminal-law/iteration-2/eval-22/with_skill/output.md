## Legal Framework

### Overview: Environmental Criminal Law in the Netherlands

Environmental criminal law (milieustrafrecht) in the Netherlands sits primarily within the framework of the **Wet op de Economische Delicten (WED)** – the Economic Offenses Act. The WED does not define environmental offenses itself but designates violations of environmental statutes as "economische delicten" and applies a separate, often stricter, sentencing framework.

The employee's report (melding) that your factory may be violating environmental waste disposal regulations is a serious matter that can trigger multiple overlapping enforcement mechanisms: administrative (bestuursrechtelijk), civil, and criminal.

### Key Statutory Provisions

#### 1. Wet op de Economische Delicten (WED)

The WED is the primary framework for criminal enforcement of environmental law in the Netherlands:

- **Art. 1 WED**: Designates violations of listed environmental laws as economische delicten, categorizing them as either misdrijf (felony) or overtreding (misdemeanor), depending on intent.
- **Art. 1a WED**: Aggravating circumstances that increase penalties, including repeat offenses, deliberate risk-taking (opzet), and severe environmental harm.
- **Art. 6 WED**: Maximum penalties:
  - For misdrijven (intentional violations): up to **6 years imprisonment** and/or a 5th category fine (EUR 90,000) or **10% of annual turnover** (whichever is higher).
  - For overtredingen (negligent violations): up to 1 year detention and/or a 4th category fine (EUR 22,500).
- **Art. 7 WED**: Additional sanctions including business closure (stillegging onderneming), confiscation (verbeurdverklaring), and publication of the judgment (openbaarmaking vonnis).

#### 2. Omgevingswet and Wet Milieubeheer

Since **1 January 2024**, the **Omgevingswet** has replaced much of the previously fragmented environmental regulatory framework, including large parts of the Wet milieubeheer (Wm):

- **Art. 1.11 Omgevingswet**: The **general zorgplicht** (duty of care) – all parties engaging in activities that affect the physical environment must take adequate measures to prevent or limit adverse effects. This provision can independently serve as the basis for criminal prosecution.
- **Former Art. 10.1 Wet milieubeheer**: The predecessor zorgplicht provision, still referenced in transitional cases and ongoing investigations predating the Omgevingswet.
- The Wet milieubeheer (Wm) provisions on afvalstoffen (waste substances) remain referenced in WED designations in specific sectors during the transitional period.

#### 3. Wetboek van Strafrecht – Endangerment Provisions

For the most serious forms of environmental damage, the Wetboek van Strafrecht contains direct criminal prohibitions:

- **Art. 173a Sr**: Causing pollution that results in death, injury, or irreversible environmental damage – maximum **6 years imprisonment** or EUR 900,000 fine (5th category). No actual harm is required if there was imminent danger.
- **Art. 173b Sr**: Endangerment by releasing dangerous substances into water, air, or soil – maximum **3 years imprisonment** or EUR 450,000 fine.

#### 4. Transboundary Waste Rules (EVOA)

If your factory engages in any transboundary shipment of waste, **EVOA (EC Regulation 1013/2006)** – the European Waste Shipment Regulation – imposes strict permit and notification requirements. Violations constitute WED offenses and can attract criminal prosecution regardless of whether harm actually occurred.

## Categories of Potential Environmental Offenses

Depending on what the employee's report concerns, the following categories of environmental offenses (afvalstoffendelicten and verontreinigingsdelicten) may be relevant:

### Illegal Waste Disposal (Afvalstoffendelicten)
- Unauthorized collection, transport, treatment, or disposal (storten/lozen) of waste without the required environmental permit (milieuvergunning or omgevingsvergunning)
- Mixing prohibited waste streams
- Fraudulent waste classification or documentation

### Pollution Offenses (Verontreiniging)
- Illegal discharge into soil (bodem), surface water, or groundwater without a permit
- Exceeding permitted emission limits (emissiegrenswaarden)
- Failure to report a soil contamination (meldingsplicht schending)

### Operating Without or in Violation of Required Permits
- Operating a Category A or B installation without or in breach of the required environmental permit
- Violating specific permit conditions (e.g., hours of operation, waste categories, discharge volumes)

The Hoge Raad confirmed in **HR 23 december 2003, ECLI:NL:HR:2003:AN7818** that operating in breach of permit conditions constitutes an economisch delict under the WED, even where environmental harm is minimal. In **HR 21 april 2009, ECLI:NL:HR:2009:BH2684 (Chemie-Pack)**, the HR further ruled that the general zorgplicht (duty of care) under the Wet milieubeheer can serve as an independent basis for criminal prosecution, even when the specific conduct is not covered by a particular permit condition.

## Corporate Criminal Liability

Your company (as a rechtspersoon) can be held criminally liable under **art. 51 Sr** for environmental violations committed by its employees or officers, provided the conduct can be attributed to the company using the **Drijfmest criteria** (HR 21 oktober 2003, ECLI:NL:HR:2003:AF7938):

1. Did the illegal waste disposal occur within the **normal business operations** of the factory?
2. Did the company **benefit** from the conduct (e.g., cost savings by avoiding proper waste disposal fees)?
3. Did the company have **beschikkingsmacht** (control) over whether the conduct occurred?
4. Did the company **accept or tolerate** the conduct, including by failing to implement adequate environmental compliance controls?

In addition, factory managers or directors who were aware of the violations and failed to intervene may be prosecuted as **feitelijk leidinggevers** under art. 51 lid 2 Sr, applying the Slavenburg II criteria (HR 16 december 1986, ECLI:NL:HR:1986:AC9607): awareness of the conduct, position to prevent it, and conscious acceptance of the risk that violations were occurring.

## The Duty of Care (Zorgplicht)

Factories and industrial operators in the Netherlands carry a broad environmental **zorgplicht** (Art. 1.11 Omgevingswet; formerly Art. 10.1 Wm). This duty requires:

1. Assessing the environmental impact of activities;
2. Implementing technically and economically feasible preventive measures;
3. Maintaining transparent operational procedures and environmental records;
4. Reporting environmental incidents to the competent authority (bevoegd gezag).

A breach of the zorgplicht can independently support a criminal negligence (schuld) charge, even if no specific permit provision was violated. This was confirmed in Chemie-Pack and Rb. Rotterdam 19 november 2013, ECLI:NL:RBROT:2013:9116 (large-scale illegal waste dumping, where the court imposed substantial fines on both the corporate defendant and its feitelijk leidinggever).

## Penalties and Sanctions

### Criminal Penalties (WED)

| Violation Type | Classification | Max. Imprisonment | Max. Fine |
|---|---|---|---|
| Intentional (opzettelijk) | Misdrijf | 6 years | EUR 900,000 or 10% annual turnover |
| Negligent (schuld) | Overtreding | 1 year detention | EUR 22,500 |
| Art. 173a Sr (serious pollution) | Misdrijf | 6 years | EUR 900,000 |
| Art. 173b Sr (endangerment) | Misdrijf | 3 years | EUR 450,000 |

### Additional Sanctions (Art. 7 WED)

- **Stillegging onderneming**: Closure of the factory or specific production activity (temporary or permanent) – this is a devastating sanction for an operational factory.
- **Ontneming wederrechtelijk voordeel**: Confiscation of profits from illegal waste disposal (e.g., savings from not paying proper waste disposal costs).
- **Verbeurdverklaring**: Confiscation of assets, vehicles, equipment used in the offense.
- **Openbaarmaking vonnis**: Publication of the criminal judgment at the company's expense.

### Administrative Penalties (Parallel Track)

In addition to criminal enforcement, administrative enforcement bodies (omgevingsdiensten, ILT) can impose:
- Administrative fines (bestuurlijke boete) and/or administrative penalties (bestuursdwang/dwangsom)
- Suspension or withdrawal of environmental permits

Note: The **una via principle** (Art. 5:44 Awb / Art. 243 lid 2 Sv) applies: if an administrative penalty has already been imposed for the same violation, criminal prosecution for the same facts is generally barred, and vice versa. The enforcement route must be chosen before a final decision is made.
