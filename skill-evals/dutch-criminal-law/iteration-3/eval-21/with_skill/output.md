## Legal Framework: Valsheid in Geschrift (Art. 225 Sr)

### Elements of the Offense

Under **Art. 225 lid 1 Sr**, the offense of valsheid in geschrift consists of:

1. **Making a false or falsified written document** (het valselijk opmaken of vervalsen van een geschrift):
   - Creating a document containing false information, OR
   - Altering an existing document to make it contain false information.
2. **The document must be intended to serve as proof** (bestemd om tot bewijs van enig feit te dienen):
   - Invoices are archetypal documents serving as proof of a business transaction, tax deduction, or payment obligation. Invoices qualify as a "geschrift" within the meaning of Art. 225 Sr.
   - Electronic documents (digital invoices, emails) also qualify (HR 22 april 2014, ECLI:NL:HR:2014:953).
3. **Intent to use the document as genuine and unforged** (oogmerk om het als echt en onvervalst te gebruiken of door anderen te doen gebruiken):
   - The oogmerk (purpose/intent) is the subjective element. The person creating the false document must intend that it be used as genuine (HR 14 mei 1957, NJ 1957/472).
   - In the context of false invoices, this oogmerk is almost invariably present when the invoices are used for payment claims, tax filings, or accounting purposes.

**Art. 225 lid 2 Sr** additionally covers the offense of **intentionally using** a false document as genuine, or intentionally having such use made by another - meaning even if the financial director did not personally create the false invoices, using them knowingly is itself a crime.

**Maximum penalty** (Art. 225 lid 1 Sr): **6 years imprisonment** and/or a fine (4th category: EUR 22,500 for natural persons; for legal entities, up to the 5th or 6th category, or 10% of annual turnover under WED).

## Corporate Criminal Liability (Art. 51 Sr)

### Art. 51 Sr: The Legal Foundation

**Art. 51 lid 1 Sr** provides that criminal offenses can be committed not only by natural persons but also by **legal entities** (rechtspersonen). A BV, NV, stichting, or association can be the perpetrator of a criminal offense.

**Art. 51 lid 2 Sr** provides that if a criminal offense is committed by a legal entity, prosecution may be directed against:
1. **The legal entity itself**, AND/OR
2. **Those who gave the order** (opdrachtgevers) for the commission of the offense, AND/OR
3. **Those who had de facto control** (feitelijke leiding) over the prohibited conduct.

The Openbaar Ministerie (OM/prosecution) can choose to prosecute any one, several, or all of these parties.

### When Is an Offense Attributed to the Legal Entity?

The **Drijfmest-arrest** (HR 21 oktober 2003, ECLI:NL:HR:2003:AF7938) established the modern standard for attributing criminal conduct to a legal entity. Attribution is based on a flexible test ("redelijkerwijs toerekenen" - can reasonably be attributed), considering:

1. **Did the conduct occur within the normal business operations of the company?** (in de sfeer van de rechtspersoon)
   - If the financial director submitted false invoices in the course of his role managing the company's finances, this criterion is met.
2. **Did the company benefit from the conduct?**
   - False invoices typically serve to inflate costs, reduce taxable profit, obtain unjustified payments, or conceal fraud. If the company financially benefited, this factor favors attribution.
3. **Did the company have control (beschikkingsmacht) over whether the conduct occurred?**
   - The company, through its management structure, board, and supervisory mechanisms, had the power to prevent or allow the conduct.
4. **Did the company accept the conduct, or similar conduct?** (IJzerdraad criteria, refined by Drijfmest)
   - This includes failing to implement adequate controls, compliance systems, or oversight that would have detected and prevented the false invoicing. Even passive acceptance (by failing to prevent what was known or should have been known) can satisfy this criterion.

**The critical question for your company**: Was the false invoicing by the financial director part of the company's normal operations, did the company benefit, and did the company's management structure accept or fail to prevent it? If yes to multiple factors, the company faces significant corporate criminal liability.

## Personal Liability of Other Directors and Officers (Feitelijk Leidinggeven - Art. 51 lid 2 Sr)

Beyond the financial director (who is directly suspected), **other directors, board members, or managers** of the company may face personal criminal liability as **feitelijk leidinggevers** under Art. 51 lid 2 Sr if they:
- Were aware of the false invoicing (or had reason to be aware), AND
- Were in a position to prevent or stop the conduct, AND
- Consciously accepted (bewust aanvaarden) the substantial chance that the offense would occur - including by failing to act (Slavenburg II, HR 16 december 1986, ECLI:NL:HR:1986:AC9607; refined by HR 1 juni 2021, ECLI:NL:HR:2021:818).

**Warning**: Even directors who were not directly involved in creating the false invoices may face feitelijk leidinggeven liability if they were in a position of oversight and failed to detect or prevent the fraud. Passive failure to act can suffice.

## The FIOD Raid: Immediate Practical Implications

The **FIOD** (Fiscale Inlichtingen- en Opsporingsdienst) is the specialized Dutch investigation service for tax and financial crimes. A FIOD raid (doorzoeking) is an extremely serious development that signals the OM/FIOD already have sufficient suspicion to justify a search warrant and document seizure.

### What to Expect

- **Document and data seizure**: FIOD has likely seized financial records, email correspondence, invoices, accounting systems, and possibly computers and servers.
- **Questioning of employees**: FIOD officers may question employees during or after the raid. All employees have the right to remain silent (cautie/zwijgrecht) under Art. 29 Sv. They should NOT answer questions without consulting a lawyer first.
- **Suspect status of financial director**: The financial director is a suspect (verdachte) under Art. 27 Sv and has specific rights, including the right to remain silent and the right to a lawyer (Salduz rights).
- **Company status**: The company itself may already be treated as a suspect (verdachte rechtspersoon) or become one as the investigation develops.

### Legal Professional Privilege (Verschoningsrecht)

Documents that are subject to legal professional privilege (verschoningsrecht - Art. 218 Sv) - specifically communications with a lawyer (advocaat) - cannot be seized. If privileged documents were taken, the company's lawyer must immediately assert privilege to have those documents returned (HR 1 juli 2014, ECLI:NL:HR:2014:1563).

### Nemo Tenetur (Right Against Self-Incrimination)

Under the nemo tenetur principle, neither individuals nor the company can be compelled to actively create or compile incriminating documents for the FIOD. Pre-existing documents that must be produced under legal obligation may be required, but compelled active self-incrimination is not permitted (HR 18 juni 2019, ECLI:NL:HR:2019:965).
