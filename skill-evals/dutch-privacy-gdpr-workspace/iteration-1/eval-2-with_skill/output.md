## Compliance Analysis

### Right of Access (Inzagerecht) -- Art. 15 AVG

#### What Art. 15 AVG Requires

Under **Art. 15(1) AVG**, the data subject has the right to obtain from the controller:

1. **Confirmation** as to whether personal data concerning him or her are being processed
2. **Access** to the personal data, and the following information:
   - (a) The **purposes** of the processing
   - (b) The **categories** of personal data concerned
   - (c) The **recipients** or categories of recipients to whom data have been or will be disclosed (including recipients in third countries or international organizations)
   - (d) Where possible, the **envisaged retention period**, or the criteria used to determine it
   - (e) The existence of the right to request **rectification, erasure, restriction**, or to **object** to processing
   - (f) The right to lodge a **complaint** with the Autoriteit Persoonsgegevens (AP)
   - (g) Where data are not collected from the data subject, any available information about the **source** of the data
   - (h) The existence of **automated decision-making**, including profiling (Art. 22 AVG), and meaningful information about the logic involved, as well as the significance and envisaged consequences

Under **Art. 15(3) AVG**, the controller shall provide a **copy** of the personal data undergoing processing. The first copy must be provided **free of charge**; for further copies, a reasonable fee based on administrative costs may be charged.

#### Scope: What Must Be Provided

The right of access applies to **all personal data** concerning the former employee. This includes:

**Must be provided (in principle):**
- **Personnel file** (personeelsdossier): employment contract, salary records, leave records, correspondence
- **Performance reviews** (beoordelingsverslagen / functioneringsverslagen): These are personal data about the former employee and fall within the scope of Art. 15 AVG. Dutch case law and AP guidance confirm that performance reviews constitute personal data of the employee being reviewed.
- **Emails about the former employee**: Emails that contain personal data about the former employee are within scope, even if the employee was not a sender or recipient. This includes emails where the former employee is discussed, evaluated, or mentioned in a substantive way.
- **Sick leave records** (verzuimgegevens): Factual absence data (not medical details, which may be subject to additional rules)
- **Training records**
- **Any other data in which the former employee is identified or identifiable**

#### Deadline (Termijn)

Under **Art. 12(3) AVG**, the controller must respond to the access request **without undue delay** and in any event **within one month (1 maand)** of receipt of the request.

- **Extension**: The deadline may be extended by a maximum of **two additional months (2 maanden)** if the request is complex or the controller has received a large number of requests. The controller must:
  - Inform the data subject of the extension **within one month** of receipt of the original request
  - Provide reasons for the delay

- **Total maximum response time**: **3 months** (1 month + 2 months extension)

- **Practical note**: Given that this involves a former employee's comprehensive request (including emails, which may require significant search effort), an extension may be justified, but only if the controller notifies within the initial 1-month period.

- **AP enforcement**: The AP considers timely response to data subject requests a compliance priority. Failure to respond within the statutory timeframe is itself a violation of Art. 12(3) AVG. Under the AP's practical compliance tips: "Respond to data subject requests promptly: Within 1 month (extendable by 2 months)."

### Exceptions and Limitations on the Right of Access

The right of access is **not absolute**. Several exceptions may apply, but they must be interpreted **restrictively** (narrow interpretation, as the right of access is a fundamental right):

#### 1. Rights and Freedoms of Others (Art. 15(4) AVG)

**Art. 15(4) AVG**: The right to obtain a copy of personal data shall **not adversely affect the rights and freedoms of others**.

This is the most relevant exception for this case:

- **Internal emails containing personal data of third parties**: If emails about the former employee also contain personal data of other individuals (colleagues, managers, clients), providing unredacted copies could infringe on the rights and freedoms of those third parties.
- **Solution**: The controller may **redact** (anonimiseren / zwart lakken) personal data of third parties before providing the copies. The controller may NOT refuse the entire request solely because third-party data is present.
- **Performance reviews containing opinions of managers/colleagues**: The opinions and assessments expressed by managers and colleagues about the former employee are **personal data of the former employee** (not of the assessor). However, if the identity of the assessor is confidential (e.g., in a confidential feedback process), the controller may redact identifying information of the assessor under Art. 15(4), while still providing the substance of the assessment.

#### 2. UAVG-Specific Exceptions (Art. 41 UAVG)

**Art. 41 UAVG** provides exemptions from the right of access for:
- Processing by intelligence and security services
- Processing for journalistic, academic, artistic, or literary purposes (Art. 43 UAVG)

These exemptions are **not applicable** to a standard employer-employee context.

#### 3. Manifestly Unfounded or Excessive Requests (Art. 12(5) AVG)

Under **Art. 12(5) AVG**, the controller may:
- Charge a **reasonable fee** (redelijke vergoeding), or
- **Refuse** to act on the request

if the request is **manifestly unfounded** (kennelijk ongegrond) or **excessive** (buitensporig), in particular because of its repetitive character.

The burden of proof lies with the **controller** to demonstrate that the request is manifestly unfounded or excessive. A first request from a former employee for all their personal data is generally **not** manifestly unfounded or excessive.

#### 4. Legal Claims Privilege

There is no general exception in the AVG for documents that may be relevant to legal proceedings. However:
- **Documents subject to legal professional privilege (verschoningsrecht)** of an external lawyer (advocaat) are not personal data of the former employee and need not be disclosed.
- **Internal legal advice** from in-house counsel does not enjoy the same absolute privilege as external advocate communications, but may be subject to the Art. 15(4) exception regarding rights and freedoms of others.

#### 5. Disproportionate Effort

The AVG does not provide a general "disproportionate effort" exception for access requests. However, in practice:
- The controller must make **reasonable efforts** to search for and identify all personal data.
- If the volume of data is very large, the controller may ask the data subject to **specify the request** (Art. 12(6) AVG -- Recital 63 AVG): the controller may request that the data subject specify the information or processing activities to which the request relates, particularly if the controller processes a large quantity of data.
- The controller **cannot** refuse the request merely because it is burdensome or time-consuming.

### Specific Analysis: Performance Reviews

Performance reviews (beoordelingsverslagen, functioneringsverslagen) are **personal data** of the former employee under Art. 4(1) AVG. This includes:

- **Factual assessments**: Competency ratings, targets, achievement evaluations
- **Subjective opinions**: Manager opinions about the employee's performance, behavior, potential
- **Conclusions and recommendations**: Promotion decisions, development plans, warnings

Dutch case law (including decisions by the AP and Dutch courts) has consistently held that performance evaluations must be provided under Art. 15 AVG. The fact that they contain subjective opinions does not exclude them from the scope of "personal data."

**Important**: The controller must provide the **substance** of the reviews. The controller may redact:
- Personal data of third parties (names of other employees mentioned)
- The identity of confidential feedback providers (if a specific confidentiality commitment was made), while still providing the content of the feedback

### Specific Analysis: Emails About the Former Employee

Emails are within scope if they contain personal data about the former employee. This includes:
- Emails **sent to or from** the former employee (clearly personal data)
- Emails **about** the former employee (where the employee is discussed, even if not a party to the email)
- **Internal communications** (between managers, HR) discussing the employee

**Practical challenges**:
- Searching email systems can be technically complex and time-consuming
- Emails often contain data about multiple individuals
- The controller should use **reasonable search methods** (keyword searches on the employee's name, employee ID, etc.)
- **Redaction** of third-party personal data is required before disclosure

**Not within scope**:
- Emails that merely mention the employee's name incidentally (e.g., in a CC list or meeting invitation) without containing substantive personal data about them
- Purely internal notes that do not contain personal data about the former employee (e.g., general policy discussions)

## Risk Assessment

### AP Enforcement Risk: **MEDIUM-HIGH**

- The AP considers timely and complete response to data subject rights as a core compliance obligation.
- **Employee data** is an AP priority enforcement area: "Employee data -- Employee monitoring, HR analytics."
- Failure to respond within the 1-month deadline (or extended 3-month deadline) or providing an incomplete response can result in:
  - A **complaint** (klacht) by the former employee to the AP
  - AP investigation and potential corrective measures
  - Fines under Art. 83(5)(b) AVG for infringement of data subject rights (up to EUR 20 million or 4% of annual worldwide turnover)
  - The former employee may also bring a **civil claim** before the Dutch courts (Art. 79 AVG)

### Practical Risk Factors

- Former employees often exercise access rights in the context of **disputes** (e.g., unfair dismissal, discrimination claims). Incomplete or delayed responses can escalate legal proceedings.
- The AP has the power to **order compliance** with data subject requests (Art. 58(2)(c) AVG) and impose **penalty payments** (last onder dwangsom) for ongoing non-compliance.

## Recommendations

1. **Acknowledge the request promptly** -- Confirm receipt of the inzageverzoek in writing. If you anticipate needing an extension, communicate this within 1 month with reasons.

2. **Comply within 1 month** (Art. 12(3) AVG) -- This is the primary deadline. Only use the 2-month extension if genuinely needed due to complexity, and notify the data subject.

3. **Conduct a comprehensive search** for all personal data of the former employee:
   - Personnel file (HR system)
   - Performance management system
   - Email systems (search on name, employee ID)
   - Shared drives and document management systems
   - Any other systems where personal data may be stored

4. **Provide performance reviews in full** -- These are personal data of the former employee. Redact only personal data of identifiable third parties (other employees named in the reviews). Do not withhold the substance of the evaluations.

5. **Provide relevant emails** -- Search for and provide emails containing substantive personal data about the former employee. Apply redaction of third-party personal data (Art. 15(4) AVG).

6. **Apply redaction, not refusal** -- Where exceptions apply (third-party rights), redact the specific protected information rather than refusing to provide the entire document.

7. **Document your decisions** -- For each category of data, document:
   - What was searched
   - What was found
   - What was provided
   - What was withheld and on what legal basis (with reference to specific AVG/UAVG articles)

8. **Provide the required supplementary information** -- In addition to the personal data itself, provide the information listed in Art. 15(1)(a)-(h) AVG (purposes, categories, recipients, retention periods, rights, etc.).

9. **Provide the data in an accessible format** -- Art. 15(3) AVG requires provision of a copy. If the request is made electronically, provide the information in a commonly used electronic form (Art. 12(3) AVG), unless the data subject requests otherwise.

10. **Consult a qualified privacy lawyer or DPO** -- Given the complexity (former employee, potential dispute context, emails about the employee), professional legal consultation is strongly recommended to navigate the exceptions and ensure compliance.
