# With-Skill Response: Dutch Real Estate Law - Test 2

**Prompt:** "My tenant hasn't paid rent for 3 months on a 7:290 commercial property. What's the procedure to terminate the lease and evict them under Dutch law?"

---

## Real Estate Law Analysis

**Property Type**: Commercial - Middenstandsbedrijfsruimte (Art. 7:290 BW)
**Transaction Type**: Lease termination and eviction for non-payment
**Legal Question**: What is the procedure to terminate a 7:290 commercial lease and evict a tenant who has not paid rent for 3 months?

## Applicable Legal Framework

- **BW Boek 7, Titel 4, Afdeling 6 (Art. 7:290-310)** - Middenstandsbedrijfsruimte (commercial retail/hospitality rental)
- **Art. 6:265 BW** - Ontbinding (dissolution) of contracts for breach
- **Art. 6:82 BW** - Ingebrekestelling (notice of default)
- **Art. 7:296 BW** - Termination grounds for 7:290 commercial leases
- **Art. 556 Rv (Wetboek van Burgerlijke Rechtsvordering)** - Enforcement of eviction (ontruiming)

## Analysis

### The 7:290 Regime: Strong Tenant Protection

Commercial premises qualifying under Art. 7:290 BW (retail spaces, restaurants, cafes, hotels, and other publicly accessible business premises) enjoy significant tenant protection under Dutch law. This includes:

- **Minimum lease duration**: 5 + 5 years (Art. 7:292 BW)
- **Limited grounds for termination by landlord** during the first 10 years (Art. 7:296 BW)
- **Rent review rights** (Art. 7:303 BW)
- **Indeplaatsstelling** (tenant substitution, Art. 7:307 BW)

However, non-payment of rent is a **serious breach** that can lead to lease termination even within the protected 10-year period.

### Procedure for Termination and Eviction

#### Step 1: Ingebrekestelling (Formal Notice of Default)

Before you can pursue ontbinding (dissolution) of the lease, you must put the tenant formally in default:

- Send a written **ingebrekestelling** (notice of default) per Art. 6:82 BW
- Clearly state:
  - The overdue rent amounts and periods (3 months)
  - A reasonable deadline (termijn) for the tenant to pay (typically 14 days)
  - That you will pursue ontbinding and ontruiming (eviction) if payment is not received
- **Method**: Send by registered mail (aangetekende brief) and regular mail to ensure receipt. A deurwaardersexploot (bailiff's writ) provides the strongest proof of delivery.

#### Step 2: Court Proceedings (Ontbindingsprocedure)

If the tenant fails to pay within the stated deadline, you must go to court. Under Dutch law, only a court can terminate a lease for a 7:290 property:

**Option A: Bodemprocedure (Regular Proceedings)**
- File a dagvaarding (summons) with the kantonrechter (sub-district court)
- Request:
  1. **Ontbinding** (dissolution) of the lease agreement based on Art. 6:265 BW (the tenant's non-payment constitutes a material breach - tekortkoming in de nakoming)
  2. **Ontruiming** (eviction) of the premises
  3. **Payment of rent arrears** (achterstallige huur) and future rent until the lease is terminated or the premises are vacated
  4. **Statutory interest** (wettelijke handelsrente, Art. 6:119a BW for commercial contracts)
  5. **Costs** (buitengerechtelijke incassokosten and proceskosten)

**Option B: Kort Geding (Summary Proceedings)**
- If urgency requires it, you can file a kort geding to obtain a provisional eviction order
- The voorzieningenrechter can order ontruiming on a provisional basis
- This is faster (typically scheduled within 2-4 weeks) but the decision is provisional
- A kort geding is particularly useful if you fear the tenant will cause further damage or if there is a pattern of non-payment

**In practice**: Many landlords pursue both a kort geding for quick eviction and a bodemprocedure for definitive lease termination and collection of all claims.

#### Step 3: Court Assessment

The court will assess:
- Whether the non-payment constitutes a **tekortkoming** (breach) that justifies ontbinding
- The **severity** of the breach (3 months of non-payment is generally considered sufficient to justify ontbinding in case law)
- Whether the tenant has offered to cure the breach (e.g., payment plan)
- Whether ontbinding is proportionate given all circumstances

**Case law**: Dutch courts generally consider non-payment of 3 or more months' rent a sufficiently serious breach to justify ontbinding of a commercial lease, unless there are exceptional extenuating circumstances (such as a dispute about the condition of the premises or force majeure).

#### Step 4: Enforcement of Eviction (Ontruiming)

Once you have a court judgment (vonnis) ordering ontruiming:
- The judgment must be served by a deurwaarder (bailiff) on the tenant
- The tenant is given a deadline to vacate voluntarily (typically 14 days, unless the court specifies otherwise)
- If the tenant does not vacate: the deurwaarder will carry out the eviction, if necessary with police assistance
- Costs of eviction can be recovered from the tenant

### Recovery of Rent Arrears

In parallel with the termination procedure, you can pursue recovery of:
- **Outstanding rent** (achterstallige huur)
- **Statutory commercial interest** (wettelijke handelsrente, Art. 6:119a BW)
- **Extrajudicial collection costs** (buitengerechtelijke incassokosten, per the Besluit vergoeding voor buitengerechtelijke incassokosten)
- **Contractual penalty** (if the lease contains a boetebeding for late payment -- ROZ model leases typically include such a clause)

## Practical Requirements

1. **Engage a lawyer** (huurrechtadvocaat) to handle the court proceedings
2. **Document everything**: Keep records of all rent payments, correspondence, and the ingebrekestelling
3. **Check the lease agreement**: Review the ROZ model or custom lease for specific provisions regarding non-payment, boeteclausule, and bank guarantee/waarborgsom
4. **Check for a bank guarantee or waarborgsom**: If the tenant provided a bank guarantee or deposit, you can draw on this to cover the arrears (subject to the terms of the guarantee)
5. **Consider mediation**: Some courts encourage mediation before proceeding to a full hearing

## Risks and Protections

| Risk | Description |
|------|-------------|
| Tenant cures the breach | If the tenant pays before the court hearing, the court may deny ontbinding |
| Proportionality defense | Tenant may argue the breach is insufficient for ontbinding (unlikely with 3 months arrears) |
| Empty judgment | If tenant has no assets, collection of arrears may be difficult |
| Squatter risk | After eviction, premises should be secured promptly |
| Contractual obligations | Landlord must check whether there are any contractual barriers to termination |

## Recommendations

1. **Act promptly**: Send the ingebrekestelling immediately; non-payment of 3 months is already a strong ground for ontbinding
2. **Engage a huurrechtadvocaat**: Commercial lease termination requires court proceedings; professional legal representation is strongly recommended
3. **Consider kort geding**: If speed is essential, pursue summary proceedings alongside the regular procedure
4. **Draw on security**: If a bank guarantee or waarborgsom was provided, initiate the claim process
5. **Secure the premises**: After obtaining an eviction order, promptly secure the property to prevent unauthorized re-entry
6. **Assess solvency**: Consider whether the tenant has assets to satisfy a judgment for rent arrears; if not, factor this into your strategy (costs of prolonged litigation vs. expected recovery)
