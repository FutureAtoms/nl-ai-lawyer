# With-Skill Response: Dutch Real Estate Law - Test 1

**Prompt:** "I'm buying an apartment in Amsterdam. The makelaar says there's erfpacht (leasehold) from the gemeente. The canon hasn't been revised in 20 years. What should I watch out for?"

---

## Real Estate Law Analysis

**Property Type**: Residential (woonruimte) - Apartment (appartementsrecht)
**Transaction Type**: Purchase with erfpacht (leasehold)
**Legal Question**: What are the risks and considerations when purchasing an apartment on erfpacht land in Amsterdam where the canon has not been revised for 20 years?

## Applicable Legal Framework

- **BW Boek 5, Titel 7 (Art. 5:85-5:100)** - Erfpacht (leasehold)
- **BW Boek 5, Titel 9** - Appartementsrecht (apartment rights)
- **Art. 3:89 BW** - Transfer requirements for registered property
- **Kadasterwet** - Registration at kadaster
- **Amsterdam municipal erfpacht system** (Gemeentelijke Erfpachtvoorwaarden Amsterdam)

## Analysis

### What is Erfpacht?

Erfpacht (leasehold) is a limited property right (beperkt zakelijk recht) under Art. 5:85 BW. It grants the erfpachter (leaseholder) the right to hold and use someone else's immovable property (in this case, the land owned by the Gemeente Amsterdam). In return, the erfpachter pays a periodic fee called the **canon** to the landowner (the municipality).

When you buy an apartment on erfpacht land, you are purchasing the appartementsrecht (apartment right), which includes a share in the erfpacht right -- you do NOT own the land itself. The Gemeente Amsterdam retains ownership of the underlying land.

### Amsterdam's Erfpacht System

Amsterdam has a unique and extensive erfpacht system. The municipality is one of the largest landowners in the Netherlands and leases land to property owners through erfpacht. Amsterdam has historically used two main systems:

1. **Voortdurende erfpacht (continuous leasehold)** -- Older system where the canon is fixed for periods (typically 50 or 75 years) and is subject to revision (canonherziening) at the end of each period.

2. **Eeuwigdurende erfpacht (perpetual leasehold)** -- Introduced by Amsterdam from 2016 onward. Under this system, homeowners can convert to a fixed canon that never changes (afgekocht for the eternal duration), providing certainty.

### Critical Risk: Canon Revision (Canonherziening)

The fact that the canon has not been revised in 20 years is a major red flag. Here is why:

- Under the older voortdurende erfpacht system, the canon is periodically revised -- typically every 50 or 75 years, depending on the erfpacht conditions (erfpachtvoorwaarden).
- When the canon is due for revision, the Gemeente recalculates it based on the **current land value** (grondwaarde). Given Amsterdam's dramatic property price increases over recent decades, a canon revision can result in an **enormous increase** -- potentially from a few hundred euros per year to thousands of euros per year.
- If the revision has simply not yet occurred but is approaching (or overdue), you could face a sudden and significant cost increase shortly after purchase.

**Key questions to investigate:**
- **When was the erfpacht established?** Check the erfpachtakte (leasehold deed) and the erfpachtvoorwaarden (leasehold conditions) for the revision date.
- **Which erfpachtvoorwaarden apply?** Amsterdam has multiple sets of conditions (Algemene Bepalingen -- AB 1915, AB 1934, AB 1937, AB 1955, AB 1966, AB 1994, AB 2000, AB 2016). The applicable set determines the revision mechanism.
- **Is the canon currently afgekocht (commuted)?** If the canon was previously paid in a lump sum (afkoop), it may be that the afkoop period is expiring, triggering a new canon calculation.
- **Is conversion to eeuwigdurende erfpacht possible?** Since 2016, Amsterdam has offered the option to convert to perpetual leasehold, which provides cost certainty. Check the conversion offer (overstapregeling) and calculate whether it is financially attractive.

### Financial Impact

A canon revision can have a substantial impact on:
- Your monthly housing costs (the new canon must be paid in addition to any mortgage)
- The market value of the apartment (high erfpacht costs reduce property value)
- Your mortgage capacity (banks factor in erfpacht costs when determining how much you can borrow)

### Due Diligence Steps

Before purchasing, you should:

1. **Obtain the erfpachtakte and erfpachtvoorwaarden** from the kadaster and review them carefully (or have a notaris review them)
2. **Check the canon revision date** -- is a revision imminent?
3. **Request an indication of the new canon** from the Gemeente Amsterdam (Grondzaken department)
4. **Investigate the option to convert to eeuwigdurende erfpacht** and calculate the cost (afkoopsom)
5. **Factor erfpacht costs into your total cost of ownership** -- mortgage lenders will require this
6. **Check with your mortgage advisor** whether the erfpacht situation affects your borrowing capacity

## Practical Requirements

- **Notaris (notary)**: A notaris is legally required for the transfer of the appartementsrecht (Art. 3:89 BW). The notaris will also conduct title research, including review of the erfpacht conditions. The notaris should flag any upcoming canon revision.
- **Makelaar (real estate agent)**: Your makelaar should provide full information about the erfpacht situation, including the applicable conditions and any pending revision.
- **Kadaster**: The erfpacht right, its conditions, and the canon are registered at the kadaster.
- **Gemeente Amsterdam Grondzaken**: Contact them directly for information on the current canon, upcoming revision, and conversion options.

## Risks and Protections

| Risk | Description |
|------|-------------|
| Canon revision shock | Canon could increase dramatically upon revision based on current land values |
| Reduced property value | High erfpacht costs reduce the effective value and resale price of the apartment |
| Mortgage limitations | Banks may reduce your borrowing capacity due to high erfpacht costs |
| Conversion costs | Converting to eeuwigdurende erfpacht may require a substantial lump sum |
| VvE complexity | In an apartment complex, the erfpacht is held collectively by the VvE; individual conversion may depend on VvE decision |

## Recommendations

1. **Do not proceed** until you have full clarity on the erfpacht conditions, the canon revision date, and the expected new canon amount
2. **Engage a notaris** early in the process to review the erfpacht situation
3. **Request a conversion calculation** from the Gemeente Amsterdam to understand the cost of switching to eeuwigdurende erfpacht
4. **Include an ontbindende voorwaarde** (resolutive condition) in the koopovereenkomst related to erfpacht if there is uncertainty about the future canon
5. **Consult a financial advisor** to understand the total cost of ownership including erfpacht
6. **Check the VvE situation** -- the VvE splitsingsakte will indicate how erfpacht obligations are divided among apartment owners
