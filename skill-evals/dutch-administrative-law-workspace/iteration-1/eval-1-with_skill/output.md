# With-Skill Response: Dutch Administrative Law - Test 1

**Prompt:** "The gemeente rejected our building permit application (omgevingsvergunning). We think the decision is wrong. What are our options and deadlines?"

---

## Administrative Law Analysis

**Decision**: Rejection of omgevingsvergunning (building/environmental permit) application
**Administrative Body**: Gemeente (municipality) - College van Burgemeester en Wethouders (B&W)
**Legal Basis**: Omgevingswet (Environmental and Planning Act, in force since 1 January 2024, replacing the former Wabo)
**Deadline**: 6 weeks from the day after the decision was sent/published

## Standing Analysis

As the applicant for the omgevingsvergunning, you are clearly an **belanghebbende** (interested party) under Art. 1:2 Awb. The applicant for a permit is always considered directly affected by the decision to reject that application. Your standing to file bezwaar (and later beroep) is not in question.

## Grounds for Challenge

The rejection of an omgevingsvergunning can be challenged on several grounds. You should assess which of the following apply to your situation:

### 1. Procedural Defects (Formele gebreken)
- **Art. 3:2 Awb (Zorgvuldigheidsbeginsel)**: Did the gemeente carefully investigate all relevant facts? Were all relevant aspects of your application properly assessed?
- **Art. 3:46-47 Awb (Motiveringsbeginsel)**: Is the decision properly and sufficiently reasoned? A rejection must clearly explain why the application does not meet the requirements.
- **Hearing**: Were you given an opportunity to supplement your application or respond to concerns before the decision?
- **Advisory opinions**: If an advisory body (welstandscommissie, brandweer, etc.) was consulted, was their advice properly followed or reasonably deviated from?

### 2. Factual Errors (Feitelijke onjuistheden)
- Did the gemeente rely on incorrect facts about your property, plans, or the applicable zoning rules (omgevingsplan)?
- Were relevant facts or circumstances overlooked?

### 3. Legal Errors (Juridische onjuistheden)
- Did the gemeente correctly apply the applicable provisions of the **Omgevingswet** and the local **omgevingsplan** (zoning/environmental plan)?
- Were the correct assessment criteria (beoordelingskader) used?
- If the activity is in principle permitted under the omgevingsplan, the permit should generally be granted (gebonden beschikking) unless specific refusal grounds apply
- Did the gemeente incorrectly classify the activity or apply the wrong standards?

### 4. Proportionality and Reasonableness
- **Art. 3:4 Awb (Evenredigheidsbeginsel)**: Are the consequences of the rejection proportionate to the interests served by the refusal?
- **Belangenafweging**: Did the gemeente properly weigh all relevant interests (your interest in the permit vs. spatial planning, environmental, and third-party interests)?

### 5. General Principles of Good Governance (ABBB)
- **Vertrouwensbeginsel** (legitimate expectations): Were you given assurances by municipality officials that the permit would be granted? If so, the gemeente may be bound by those representations.
- **Gelijkheidsbeginsel** (equality): Were similar applications in comparable situations granted?

## Procedure

### Option 1: Bezwaar (Objection) - Primary Route

This is the first and mandatory step in most cases.

**Deadline**: **6 weeks** from the day after the date on which the decision was sent to you (Art. 6:7-6:8 Awb). This deadline is strictly enforced -- missing it will almost certainly result in your bezwaar being declared inadmissible (niet-ontvankelijk).

**How to file**:
1. Submit a written bezwaarschrift (objection letter) to the **same administrative body** (College van B&W) that made the decision
2. The bezwaarschrift must contain (Art. 6:5 Awb):
   - Your name and address
   - The date
   - A description of the decision you are objecting to (attach a copy of the decision)
   - The grounds (reasons) for your objection
   - Your signature

**Pro forma bezwaar**: If the 6-week deadline is approaching and you need more time to prepare your grounds, you can file a **pro forma bezwaarschrift** stating "grounds to follow" and request additional time to supplement your objection. This preserves your right while giving you time to prepare a comprehensive argument.

**Hearing**: You have the right to be heard (Art. 7:2 Awb) before the gemeente decides on your bezwaar. This is your opportunity to explain your position orally.

**Decision timeline**: The gemeente must decide on your bezwaar within **6 weeks** after the bezwaar period expires (or 12 weeks if an advisory committee is used, Art. 7:10 Awb), with a possible 6-week extension.

**Full reconsideration**: The bezwaar procedure involves a full reconsideration (heroverweging, Art. 7:11 Awb) of the original decision -- the gemeente reconsiders both the facts and the law. This means the outcome could be different from the original decision.

### Option 2: Beroep (Appeal to Court) - After Bezwaar

If the bezwaar is rejected (or the outcome is unsatisfactory):

- File **beroep** (appeal) with the **bestuursrechter** (administrative judge) at the competent **Rechtbank** within **6 weeks** of the decision on bezwaar
- The court will independently review the legality of the decision
- Court fee (griffierecht): EUR 50 for natural persons, EUR 371 for legal entities (2024 rates)
- The court can annul the decision (vernietigen), instruct the gemeente to make a new decision, or resolve the matter itself

### Option 3: Voorlopige Voorziening (Provisional Measures)

If the rejection has urgent consequences (e.g., construction deadlines, contractual obligations, financial losses from delay):

- You can request a **voorlopige voorziening** (provisional measure) from the **voorzieningenrechter** at the Rechtbank
- This can be filed during the bezwaar phase (Art. 8:81 Awb)
- The voorzieningenrechter can suspend the rejection or order the gemeente to issue a temporary permit
- Requires demonstration of **spoedeisend belang** (urgent interest)
- Can be combined with the main procedure (kortsluiting, Art. 8:86 Awb) -- the judge may decide the entire case at the provisional measures hearing

### Option 4: Hoger Beroep (Higher Appeal)

If the Rechtbank rules against you:
- Appeal to the **Afdeling bestuursrechtspraak van de Raad van State (ABRvS)** within 6 weeks
- The ABRvS is the highest administrative court for spatial planning and environmental matters

### Alternative: Revised Application

Depending on the grounds for rejection, you may also consider:
- **Submitting a revised application** that addresses the municipality's concerns
- **Requesting a preliminary consultation** (vooroverleg) with the gemeente before reapplying
- This can be pursued in parallel with bezwaar

## Practical Steps

1. **Check the deadline immediately**: Count 6 weeks from the day after the date of the decision. If in doubt, file a pro forma bezwaar right away.
2. **Read the decision carefully**: Examine the reasoning (motivering) and the legal provisions cited. This tells you exactly why the gemeente rejected your application.
3. **Check the rechtsmiddelenclausule**: The decision should contain a clause at the bottom explaining how to object (if not, the deadline may be extended due to verschoonbare termijnoverschrijding, but do not rely on this).
4. **Gather supporting evidence**: Technical reports, expert opinions, comparable permits granted to others, and correspondence with the gemeente.
5. **Consult a specialist**: An omgevingsrecht (environmental law) or bestuursrecht (administrative law) lawyer can assess the strength of your case and draft the bezwaarschrift.
6. **Consider legal aid**: If you are a natural person with limited income, you may be eligible for government-funded legal aid (gesubsidieerde rechtsbijstand / toevoeging via the Raad voor Rechtsbijstand).

## Provisional Measures

If the delay caused by the bezwaar and beroep procedures would cause irreversible harm or significant financial damage:
- A voorlopige voorziening is advisable
- This can be filed concurrently with bezwaar
- The voorzieningenrechter can hear the case within 2-4 weeks
