- **Issues**:

  - The clause does not: (a) define what constitutes "marketing activities"; (b) specify a claims procedure or notice requirement; (c) require the Distributor's involvement in the defense; (d) cap the indemnification amount; (e) exclude losses caused by the Supplier's own actions or instructions.

  - Standard market position: indemnities should be specific to defined activities, subject to the overall liability cap, and exclude situations where the claiming party (Supplier) is itself at fault.
- **Recommendation**: Reject this clause in its current form. Negotiate: (1) limitation of indemnity to claims arising solely from the Distributor's own gross negligence or willful misconduct; (2) exclusion of claims arising from Supplier's marketing materials, instructions, or product defects; (3) subject the indemnity to a cap equivalent to the greater of annual fees or EUR [agreed amount]; (4) include a claims procedure with notice, cooperation, and control rights; (5) expressly include a duty to mitigate (schadebeperkingsplicht) under Art. 6:101 BW.

### 2. Non-Compete Clause (Concurrentiebeding) - 5 Years, Entire EU

**Clause text**: *"Distributor shall not sell competing products for 5 years after termination in the entire EU."*

- **Risk Level**: RED
- **Summary**: The non-compete clause imposes a prohibition on competing for 5 years post-termination across the entire European Union. This clause is both legally disproportionate under Dutch civil law and raises serious concerns under EU and Dutch competition law (mededingingsrecht).
- **Legal Basis**:
  - Art. 6:248 lid 2 BW (beperkende werking redelijkheid en billijkheid)
  - Art. 7:443 BW (maximum 2-year non-compete for commercial agents)
  - Art. 101 TFEU (prohibition on anti-competitive agreements)
  - Art. 6 Mededingingswet (Mw) - Dutch cartel prohibition
  - EU Vertical Block Exemption Regulation (VBER) Regulation (EU) 2022/720, Art. 5 (post-term non-compete restrictions)
  - nl-contract-playbook Section 6: Non-Compete (RED flag: unlimited duration/geography)
  - sector-playbook Section 2: Distribution/Agency - Non-Compete Clauses (maximum 2 years per Art. 7:443 BW)
- **Issues**:

  **A. Civil Law Analysis (Dutch BW)**:
  - Under Dutch law, commercial non-compete clauses must be proportionate in scope, duration, and geography to be enforceable. A 5-year duration is **excessive** (onredelijk) and significantly exceeds the market-standard range of 1-2 years post-termination.
  - For commercial agents specifically, Art. 7:443 BW imposes a statutory maximum of 2 years. While this provision may not directly apply to a distribution agreement (as opposed to an agency agreement), Dutch courts apply the proportionality principle analogously.
  - An EU-wide geographic scope for a non-compete is disproportionate unless the Distributor operated across the entire EU - even then, a 5-year EU-wide restriction is extraordinary.
  - Courts may apply Art. 6:248 lid 2 BW to partially or fully set aside this clause, or may moderate it to a proportionate scope (conversion/partial annulment - partiële vernietiging).

  **B. Competition Law Analysis (Mededinging)**:
  - This clause triggers competition law (mededingingsrecht) analysis under both Dutch and EU law.
  - Under the EU Vertical Block Exemption Regulation (VBER, Regulation (EU) 2022/720), post-term non-compete obligations are only block-exempted for a maximum of **1 year** post-termination (Art. 5(1)(b) VBER). A 5-year post-term non-compete falls **outside the VBER safe harbor** entirely.
  - Under Art. 101 TFEU and Art. 6 Mededingingswet, vertical agreements that restrict competition (including non-competes) are prohibited unless they meet the requirements for exemption. A 5-year EU-wide post-term non-compete would need to satisfy the conditions of Art. 101(3) TFEU individually, which is a high threshold.
  - The Autoriteit Consument & Markt (ACM), the Dutch competition authority, actively enforces mededingingsrecht. Fines can reach up to 10% of annual global turnover.
  - This clause presents a significant **kartel** risk if the parties' combined market share exceeds 30% in any relevant market.
  - Even below the 30% threshold, the clause falls outside the VBER and is therefore presumptively a restriction of competition under Art. 101(1) TFEU / Art. 6 Mw.

  **Practical Consequence**: Dutch courts will almost certainly refuse to enforce a 5-year EU-wide post-term non-compete on both proportionality (Art. 6:248 BW) and competition law grounds. There is a risk that the competition law violation renders the entire non-compete clause void (Art. 3:40 BW - nietig), potentially without severability if the clause is integral to the agreement structure.
- **Recommendation**: Renegotiate to a maximum 1-year post-termination non-compete limited to the specific territory/market in which the Distributor actively operated. If the Supplier insists on broader protection, seek guidance from a competition law specialist before signing. Do not sign this clause as currently drafted.
