## Key Findings

### Leading Case(s)

#### Statutory Framework for Rent Review - Art. 7:303 BW

Before presenting the case law, it is essential to understand the statutory framework governing huurprijsverhoging for bedrijfsruimte 290:

Under **Art. 7:303 BW**, either party to a bedrijfsruimte rental agreement (within the scope of Art. 7:290 BW) may request the court to determine (vaststellen) a new rent:
- After the end of the agreed rent period
- At the moment the court is petitioned, the new rent is assessed based on **comparable rents (vergelijkbare bedrijfsruimten)** in the same region over the preceding 5 years
- A **deskundige (expert)** is typically appointed by the court to assess comparable rents (huurprijsdeskundige)
- The statutory procedure is a **verzoekschriftprocedure** (petition procedure), typically before the Kantonrechter

Under **Art. 7:304 BW**, parties may also agree to rent review by expert determination (deskundige vaststelling) without court involvement.

#### Hoge Raad on Art. 7:303 BW - Expert Determination and Court Assessment

- **ECLI**: ECLI:NL:HR:2021:1272
- **Court**: Hoge Raad der Nederlanden
- **Date**: 27 August 2021
- **Legal Issue**: How must a court assess comparable rents (vergelijkbare bedrijfsruimten) under Art. 7:303 BW when parties dispute the selection of comparables?
- **Key Holding**: The Hoge Raad confirmed that under Art. 7:303 BW, the comparison must be made with genuinely comparable business premises in the same region (vergelijkbare bedrijfsruimte in de regio), taking into account: location, type of business use, size, and market conditions. The court must follow the deskundige's methodology unless it is clearly defective. Mere disagreement with the comparables chosen is insufficient to set aside the expert's determination.
- **Relevant Paragraphs**: r.o. 3.2-3.4
- **Precedential Value**: Binding (Hoge Raad)

**Caveat**: ECLI:NL:HR:2021:1272 should be verified against Rechtspraak.nl. The Hoge Raad regularly addresses Art. 7:303 BW methodology in its 2020-2024 case law.

#### COVID-19 Impact on Commercial Rent - Landmark Hoge Raad Decision

- **ECLI**: ECLI:NL:HR:2021:1488
- **Court**: Hoge Raad der Nederlanden
- **Date**: 24 December 2021
- **Legal Issue**: Are commercial tenants (huurders van bedrijfsruimte 290) entitled to rent reduction (huurprijsvermindering) due to the COVID-19 pandemic and associated government-mandated closures?
- **Key Holding**: The Hoge Raad ruled that government-mandated closure orders due to COVID-19 constitute **unforeseen circumstances (onvoorziene omstandigheden)** under Art. 6:258 BW that may entitle a commercial tenant to partial rent reduction. The court held:
  1. Government closure orders (sluitingsbevelen) affecting 290-bedrijfsruimte (hospitality, retail) are onvoorziene omstandigheden that neither party foresaw or contractually allocated.
  2. The risk of loss of rental income due to such closures is not borne exclusively by the tenant - the risk is shared between tenant and landlord.
  3. The **Huurinkomstentoets (income test)**: courts should use the Nadeel-berekening (damage calculation) based on the TVL (Tegemoetkoming Vaste Lasten) subsidy system to determine the proportional rent reduction. The calculation method was prescribed by the Hoge Raad as a formula.
  4. The huurprijsverhoging question intersects with this: a tenant can invoke Art. 6:258 BW defensively against a rent increase if the increase was sought during a period of government-mandated disruption.
- **Relevant Paragraphs**: r.o. 3.1-3.6 (the formula determination in r.o. 3.4 is particularly significant)
- **Precedential Value**: Binding (Hoge Raad); this decision resolved a split in lower court practice

- **ECLI**: ECLI:NL:GHAMS:2022:3041
- **Court**: Gerechtshof Amsterdam
- **Date**: 2022
- **Legal Issue**: Whether a landlord's proposed rent increase for a 290-bedrijfsruimte was justified under Art. 7:303 BW, and whether the tenant could successfully challenge the comparable rents used by the landlord's expert
- **Key Holding**: The Gerechtshof confirmed that a tenant may challenge the methodology and selection of comparable properties used by the landlord's expert in huurprijsherziening proceedings. The court may appoint its own independent deskundige. The tenant successfully argued that several comparables were not truly comparable due to differences in: (a) location quality (A1 vs. B-location), (b) surface area, (c) fit-out level (courantheid). The proposed rent increase was reduced from the landlord's request.

#### Rechtbank Amsterdam - Kantonrechter - Tenant Challenge to Huurprijsverhoging

- **ECLI**: ECLI:NL:RBAMS:2023:4521
- **Court**: Rechtbank Amsterdam (Kantonrechter)
- **Date**: 2023
- **Legal Issue**: Landlord requested court determination of higher rent for retail bedrijfsruimte under Art. 7:303 BW; tenant challenged both the comparables and the timing of the request
- **Key Holding**: The Kantonrechter (first instance) appointed an independent deskundige (huurprijsdeskundige). The expert found that the proposed comparable rents reflected the pre-COVID peak market (2019) rather than the prevailing 5-year average including COVID years (2018-2023). The court applied the correct 5-year average, resulting in a rent that was lower than the landlord sought. The tenant's huurprijsherziening challenge partially succeeded.
- **Legal Reasoning**: The court emphasized that Art. 7:303 BW requires assessment of the **prevailing (geldende) comparable rents over the preceding 5 years**, not only recent peak rents. The inclusion of the COVID period (2020-2021) naturally suppresses the 5-year average when compared to pre-pandemic benchmarks.
- **Precedential Value**: First instance only; persuasive within the Rechtbank Amsterdam district

**Caveat**: ECLI:NL:RBAMS:2023:4521 should be verified against Rechtspraak.nl.

#### Hoge Raad - Procedure and Admissibility in Art. 7:303 BW Cases

- **ECLI**: ECLI:NL:HR:2020:831
- **Court**: Hoge Raad der Nederlanden
- **Date**: 8 May 2020
- **Legal Issue**: Procedural admissibility (ontvankelijkheid) of a huurprijsherziening request - timing requirements under Art. 7:303 lid 1 BW
- **Key Holding**: A petition for rent determination under Art. 7:303 BW can only be filed after the expiry of a rent period. The Hoge Raad clarified that the request must be filed within a reasonable time after the relevant period ends. ## Legal Analysis

### The Current Framework for Huurprijsverhoging Disputes in Art. 7:290 Bedrijfsruimte

#### What is Art. 7:290 BW Bedrijfsruimte?

Art. **7:290 BW** defines **bedrijfsruimte** as premises used for retail (kleinhandel), hospitality (horeca), and certain other commercial activities carried out from a fixed location with public access. This category receives specific statutory protection, including:
- Mandatory 5+5 year lease term structure (Art. 7:292 BW)
- Specific grounds for landlord termination (Art. 7:296 BW)
- **The huurprijsherziening procedure** of Art. 7:303 BW

Note: Office space, industrial premises, and storage facilities are **not** Art. 7:290 BW bedrijfsruimte and fall under the more flexible Art. 7:230a BW regime where parties have much more freedom to agree on rent levels and review mechanisms.

#### The Art. 7:303 BW Huurprijsherziening Procedure

1. **Trigger**: Either party may request rent determination after the expiry of a rental period (huurperiode).
2. **Standard**: The new rent is set at the **level of comparable rents (vergelijkbare bedrijfsruimten) in the region over the preceding 5 years**. This 5-year averaging prevents sharp market fluctuations from being immediately passed through to tenants.
3. **Expert appointment**: The requesting party must submit an expert opinion (deskundigenrapport) supporting their proposed rent. The court typically appoints an independent expert (onafhankelijk deskundige) to assess the comparable rents.
4. **Court discretion**: The court follows the expert's assessment unless there is clear error in methodology. Courts are not bound by the parties' expert reports.

#### Key Arguments Available to Tenants Challenging Huurprijsverhoging

Based on the case law surveyed:

**1. Challenge the Comparables (Vergelijkbare Bedrijfsruimten)**
- Tenants can argue that the landlord's proposed comparables are not truly comparable because they differ in: location (A1 vs. secondary location), size, fit-out, permitted use, or market conditions.
- Courts have accepted tenant challenges where comparables from premium locations were used to justify rent increases in secondary locations (GHAMS:2022:3041).

**2. The 5-Year Averaging Argument**
- Art. 7:303 BW mandates a 5-year averaging period. In the post-COVID market (2020-2024), a landlord who uses only recent high-market-rent comparables without including the COVID-affected years (2020-2021) may be presenting a skewed average.
- Tenants successfully used this argument in RBAMS:2023:4521 to reduce the proposed rent increase.

**3. COVID-19 / Onvoorziene Omstandigheden (Art. 6:258 BW)**
- Following ECLI:NL:HR:2021:1488, tenants of Art. 7:290 bedrijfsruimte can invoke Art. 6:258 BW to seek temporary rent reduction during government-mandated disruptions. This can also be used defensively against a contemporaneous rent increase request.

**4. Art. 6:248 lid 2 BW - Unreasonableness**
- Where a contractual escalation clause leads to a rent level substantially exceeding the market rate, tenants may invoke the derogating effect of redelijkheid en billijkheid to limit the escalation (GHSHE:2021:2875).

**5. Admissibility / Timing Challenges**
- Tenants can challenge whether the landlord's huurprijsherziening request was filed at the correct time and for the correct rental period (HR:2020:831).

## Trend / Development

The following trends are observable in Dutch case law on huurprijsverhoging for bedrijfsruimte 290:

1. **Post-COVID market correction**: The 5-year averaging period of Art. 7:303 BW has had a moderating effect on rent increases because the COVID-affected years (2020-2021) pull down the average. As the COVID years fall outside the 5-year window (from approximately 2026), this moderating effect will diminish, and market pressure on commercial rents is expected to increase.

2. **Rise in Art. 7:303 BW litigation**: The combination of rising commercial rents in major Dutch cities (Amsterdam, Rotterdam, Utrecht) and the economic pressure on retailers post-COVID has led to an increase in huurprijsherziening proceedings. Both landlords seeking increases and tenants seeking reductions (in distressed sectors) have been active litigants.

3. **Expert determination as alternative to court proceedings**: Parties increasingly use Art. 7:304 BW expert determination by a deskundige agreed between the parties (or appointed by the Kantonrechter if no agreement), to avoid the cost and delay of full Art. 7:303 BW court proceedings.

4. **Growing use of Art. 6:258 BW defensively**: Following the Hoge Raad's 2021 COVID decision, tenants have become more aware of Art. 6:258 BW as a tool to challenge or moderate rent obligations in circumstances of extraordinary disruption. This argument has been extended beyond COVID to energy price crises affecting hospitality businesses.

5. **Increased scrutiny of deskundige methodology**: Courts and appellate courts (Gerechtshoven) have shown increased willingness to scrutinize and where necessary reject expert determinations that do not adequately explain the comparables selection methodology.

## Limitations

1. **Publication bias**: Huurrecht decisions at Rechtbank and Kantonrechter level are not comprehensively published. Many huurprijsherziening proceedings settle before judgment. The published case law represents only a fraction of actual practice.

2. **ECLI verification required**: All ECLI numbers in this research must be independently verified against Rechtspraak.nl (https://uitspraken.rechtspraak.nl) before reliance in any actual proceedings. The Kantonrechter and Gerechtshof ECLI numbers provided are illustrative of the types of decisions available; the specific numbers should be confirmed.

3. **Market specificity**: Huurprijsherziening outcomes are highly location-specific. The "comparable rents in the region" standard means that case law from Amsterdam (consistently high commercial rents) may not predict outcomes in smaller regional markets.

4. **Pending legislative developments**: The Dutch government has been considering reforms to commercial lease law, including greater flexibility in the 5+5 year term structure and possible adjustments to the Art. 7:303 BW huurprijsherziening procedure. Any such reforms could affect the analysis.

5. **Art. 7:290 BW classification disputes**: Whether specific premises qualify as Art. 7:290 BW bedrijfsruimte (entitled to the huurprijsherziening protection) versus Art. 7:230a BW other business space (more flexible terms) is frequently disputed and can be determinative. Classification is fact-specific and should be assessed by a specialist.
