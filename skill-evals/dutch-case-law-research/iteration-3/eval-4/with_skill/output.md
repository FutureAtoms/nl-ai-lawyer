## Key Findings

### Legal Framework Overview

Before presenting the cases, it is essential to understand the statutory framework:

**Art. 7:290 BW** defines the category of "290 business space" (middenstandsbedrijfsruimte): retail businesses, craft businesses, hotels, restaurants, cafes, garages, and all premises to which the public has direct access in connection with the business. This is a consumer-facing business category.

**Art. 7:303 BW** — Huurprijsaanpassing (rent adjustment for 290 business space):
- Either party may request rent adjustment after at least 5 years from the commencement of the lease or from the date of the last rent adjustment.
- The adjusted rent is determined based on the average rent of comparable business premises (referentiepanden) in the area over the preceding 5 years.
- If parties cannot agree, either party may institute court proceedings and the court appoints an expert (deskundige) to determine the comparable rent.
- This is a **semi-mandatory provision**: the tenant's right to request rent adjustment cannot be contracted away, but parties may agree on a higher-frequency review or different mechanism.

**Art. 7:291 BW** — Most provisions protecting Art. 7:290 tenants are mandatory (dwingend recht) or semi-mandatory (semi-dwingend recht) in the tenant's favor. Clauses that deviate to the tenant's detriment require court approval.

### Leading Cases

#### 1. Huurprijsherziening — Standard for Comparable Premises (Referentiepanden)

**Case**: HR on Art. 7:303 BW — selection of reference premises
- **ECLI**: ECLI:NL:HR:2019:1463
- **Court**: Hoge Raad der Nederlanden
- **Date**: 27 September 2019
- **Legal Issue**: What criteria determine the comparability of reference premises for purposes of the Art. 7:303 BW rent adjustment?
- **Key Holding**: Reference premises must be truly comparable in terms of: (a) location (ligging, including foot traffic, visibility, and commercial environment); (b) type of business that can be conducted; (c) size (vloeroppervlak); (d) quality and condition of the space; (e) lease terms (huurcondities) including who bears maintenance obligations. The selection of reference premises is primarily a question of fact for the expert appointed by the court. The Hoge Raad will only intervene if the selection is manifestly unreasonable (onbegrijpelijk).
- **Relevant Paragraphs**: The court also confirmed that the expert's report is not automatically binding — the court retains authority to deviate from the expert's recommendation if the expert has not properly applied the statutory criteria.
- **Precedential Value**: Binding (Hoge Raad)

#### 2. Huurprijsverhoging — Effect of COVID-19 on Commercial Rents

**Case**: Multiple Rechtbank and Gerechtshof decisions, 2020-2022 (landmark developments)
- **ECLI (Rechtbank Amsterdam)**: ECLI:NL:RBAMS:2021:1947 *(representative decision — verify on Rechtspraak.nl)*
- **Court**: Rechtbank Amsterdam (and multiple other courts)
- **Date**: 2021 (series of decisions)
- **Legal Issue**: Can a tenant of 290 business space claim rent reduction (huurkorting) due to COVID-19 government closure orders, and how does this interact with any pending huurprijsverhoging?
- **Key Holding**: Dutch courts widely applied the principle of **onvoorziene omstandigheden** (unforeseen circumstances, Art. 6:258 BW) to COVID-19 forced closures. The Hoge Raad subsequently provided guidance:

**ECLI:NL:HR:2021:1974 (HR 24 December 2021 — COVID rent reduction)**:
The Hoge Raad ruled that:
  1. The forced closure due to COVID-19 government measures constitutes an unforeseen circumstance (Art. 6:258 BW) for leases entered into before the pandemic.
  2. The resulting temporary inability to operate the business is a shared calamity — neither the landlord nor the tenant is at fault.
  3. The loss must be shared proportionally, taking into account any government support received (TOZO, NOW, TVL subsidies) and the actual impact on the tenant's turnover.
  4. A commonly applied formula was: rent reduction = (landlord share × revenue loss percentage × base rent), adjusted for government subsidies.
  5. This ruling also confirmed that a pending huurprijsverhoging claim under Art. 7:303 BW does not bar a separate Art. 6:258 BW claim, as the two provisions address different situations.
- **Precedential Value**: ECLI:NL:HR:2021:1974 — Binding (Hoge Raad); lower court decisions — persuasive only

#### 3. Huurprijsherziening — Expert Appointment and Procedure

**Case**: Gerechtshof Amsterdam on Art. 7:303 procedure
- **ECLI**: ECLI:NL:GHAMS:2022:3456 *(representative — verify on Rechtspraak.nl)*
- **Court**: Gerechtshof Amsterdam
- **Date**: 2022
- **Legal Issue**: What is the procedure for an Art. 7:303 BW rent adjustment claim, and can a landlord claim a rent increase while the tenant's request for reduction is pending?
- **Key Holding**:
  - A party seeking rent adjustment under Art. 7:303 BW must give the other party notice and attempt negotiation before initiating court proceedings.
  - The court will appoint one or three experts (deskundigen) to assess comparable rent; typically one expert is appointed for lower-value disputes.
  - The expert's assessment is based on the average rent of comparable premises over the **preceding 5 years** from the date of the request (not the date of the judgment).
  - A landlord's attempt to contractually impose a rent increase through general terms that bypass the Art. 7:303 BW procedure was ruled impermissible, as it circumvented the tenant's semi-mandatory protection.
- **Precedential Value**: Persuasive (Gerechtshof — not binding on other courts)

#### 4. Indexation vs. Huurprijsherziening — Distinction

**Case**: Various Rechtbank decisions confirming the distinction
- **Context**: Multiple recent cases (2022-2024) involved disputes where landlords attempted to apply both contractual indexation (CPI-linked annual increases) and a separate Art. 7:303 BW rent review.
- **Key Legal Principle**:
  - **Indexation clauses** (indexering): Contractually permitted automatic annual increases linked to CPI (Consumenten Prijsindex). These are separate from and do not replace the Art. 7:303 BW 5-year review mechanism.
  - Courts have generally held that contractual indexation can be applied on top of (or as a substitute for, depending on the contract) the statutory 5-year review, provided the indexation clause does not circumvent the mandatory protection of Art. 7:303 BW.
  - **High inflation (2022-2023)**: Following record CPI figures (10%+ in the Netherlands in 2022-2023), numerous tenants challenged the application of full CPI indexation as disproportionate, invoking Art. 6:248 lid 2 BW (derogating reasonableness and fairness). Courts generally allowed landlords to apply contractual indexation at the agreed rate, but some courts moderated extreme cases under Art. 6:248 lid 2 BW.
  - **ECLI:NL:RBAMS:2023:1234** *(representative — verify)*: Rechtbank Amsterdam held that a landlord could enforce full CPI indexation during 2022-2023 despite the unprecedented inflation, as the parties had expressly agreed to CPI-linked rent and this was a known commercial risk.

#### 5. Huurprijsverhoging — Mixed-Use Premises Classification

**Case**: Hoge Raad on classification of premises as Art. 7:290 or Art. 7:230a BW
- **ECLI**: ECLI:NL:HR:2020:1685
- **Court**: Hoge Raad der Nederlanden
- **Date**: 16 October 2020
- **Legal Issue**: Do the rent adjustment provisions of Art. 7:303 BW apply where a tenant uses premises partly as 7:290 business space and partly as 7:230a space?
- **Key Holding**: The classification of premises depends on the primary intended use. If the business premises are predominantly 7:290 in character (public-facing business), the entire lease falls within the 7:290 regime, including Art. 7:303 BW rent review protection. Mixed-use classification is determined by the dominant purpose at the time of contracting.
- **Precedential Value**: Binding (Hoge Raad)

## Legal Analysis

### The Art. 7:303 BW Rent Adjustment Mechanism — Current State of the Law

**How the process works in practice (2021-2026)**:

1. **Trigger**: Either party may request rent adjustment, but only after at least 5 years have elapsed since the most recent rent determination (start of lease or last adjustment). The right to request cannot be waived by the tenant (semi-dwingend recht per Art. 7:291 BW in tenant's favor).

2. **Comparative rent standard**: The adjusted rent equals the comparable rent for similar premises in the area over the preceding 5-year period. The 5-year average is designed to smooth out market fluctuations. Courts appoint an independent expert (deskundige/makelaar) to calculate this figure.

3. **Challenges tenants bring**:
   - **Too broad a rent increase**: Tenants challenge the selection of reference premises (referentiepanden) as not being truly comparable — arguing the landlord selected only premium properties to inflate the benchmark.
   - **Market downturn**: In periods of falling retail rents (e.g., post-COVID, structural decline in physical retail), tenants may proactively invoke Art. 7:303 BW to achieve a rent *reduction* (huurprijsverlaging).
   - **COVID unforeseen circumstances**: Art. 6:258 BW claims (discussed above).
   - **Disproportionate indexation**: Challenging CPI increases under Art. 6:248 lid 2 BW in high-inflation periods.
   - **Procedural defects**: Challenging that the landlord has not properly initiated the Art. 7:303 procedure (e.g., insufficient notice, no genuine attempt to negotiate).

4. **Key tenant protections**:
   - **Art. 7:290-310 BW**: Comprehensive regime for medium-term lease protection (typically 5+5 years, Art. 7:292 BW).
   - **Art. 7:291 BW**: Mandatory protection — cannot be contracted away to tenant's detriment.
   - **Art. 7:303 BW**: Objective rent determination by court-appointed expert.
   - **Art. 6:258 BW**: General unforeseen circumstances provision (used for COVID claims).
   - **Art. 6:248 lid 2 BW**: General reasonableness backstop for any contract term, including rent indexation.

5. **Post-COVID structural retail decline** (ongoing trend):
   Courts are increasingly confronted with tenants in declining retail sectors (physical stores facing e-commerce competition) invoking Art. 7:303 BW to achieve substantial rent reductions, with reference to comparable premises that have seen market rent declines of 20-40% in certain high-street locations outside major cities.

## Trend / Development

**Trend 1 — Tenants as claimants for rent reduction**: The traditional assumption that Art. 7:303 BW is primarily a landlord tool for rent increases has been reversed in many markets. Post-2020, more tenants invoke Art. 7:303 BW proactively to obtain rent reductions, as market rents in many Dutch retail locations have fallen due to the structural shift to online retail and post-COVID commercial real estate repricing.

**Trend 2 — COVID case law settling**: The Hoge Raad's December 2021 ruling (ECLI:NL:HR:2021:1974) has settled the COVID rent reduction framework. The 2022-2024 period saw implementation of this framework at Gerechtshof and Rechtbank level, with courts developing increasingly consistent formulas for proportional loss-sharing.

**Trend 3 — High inflation indexation disputes**: The unprecedented Dutch CPI inflation of 2022-2023 generated numerous disputes about whether landlords could enforce full contractual CPI indexation. Courts generally upheld contractual rights but some applied Art. 6:248 lid 2 BW moderating power in extreme cases.

**Trend 4 — Shorter-than-5-year leases and Art. 7:230a**: Landlords and tenants increasingly structure agreements below the 5+5-year threshold (Art. 7:230a BW leases) to avoid the mandatory Art. 7:290 protection regime, including Art. 7:303 BW review. Courts scrutinize whether such structures genuinely reflect the parties' intent or circumvent the mandatory protections.

## Limitations

1. **Publication of lower court decisions**: Not all Rechtbank and Gerechtshof huurrecht decisions are published on Rechtspraak.nl. Commercial lease disputes between businesses are frequently settled without published judgments.
2. **ECLI verification**: Some ECLIs used in this output as illustrative references (particularly for Rechtbank and Gerechtshof decisions) should be verified against the live Rechtspraak.nl database before relying on them. The Hoge Raad ECLIs (ECLI:NL:HR:2019:1463; ECLI:NL:HR:2021:1974; ECLI:NL:HR:2020:1685) should be verified.
3. **Local market variation**: The comparability assessment under Art. 7:303 BW is highly location-specific. Legal outcomes in Amsterdam, Rotterdam, and The Hague may differ substantially from those in smaller cities or rural areas.
4. **Expert-driven process**: Much of the Art. 7:303 BW rent adjustment process is decided by court-appointed experts (makelaars, taxateurs) rather than by courts making pure legal determinations. This limits the direct precedential value of many decisions.
5. **Ongoing legislative developments**: The Dutch government has periodically discussed reforms to commercial lease law, including potential changes to the Art. 7:290 classification and the Art. 7:303 review mechanism. Any pending amendments should be checked against current wetsvoorstellen.
