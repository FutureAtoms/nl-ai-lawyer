# Legal Briefing on Dutch Law Topic

Generate a structured legal briefing on a specific Dutch law topic, synthesizing legislation, case law, and practical implications.

## Skill and Tools

Use all available MCP tools as needed:
- `legislation_search` -- to find relevant statutes and regulations
- `legislation_fetch` -- to retrieve full text of specific provisions
- `caselaw_search_rechtspraak` -- to find leading case law
- `caselaw_fetch_uitspraak` -- to retrieve full text of key decisions

## Workflow

### Step 1: Scope and Depth

- Parse the `topic` to determine the legal area and specific subject
- If `depth` is "summary": produce a concise briefing (1-2 pages equivalent) with key points only
- If `depth` is "detailed" or not specified: produce a comprehensive briefing with full analysis

### Step 2: Research Phase

Conduct research using all available MCP tools:

1. **Legislation Research:**
   - Search for primary legislation (wet in formele zin)
   - Search for secondary legislation (AMvB, ministeriële regeling)
   - Search for relevant EU regulations and directives
   - Identify the current text and any recent amendments

2. **Case Law Research:**
   - Search for Hoge Raad (Supreme Court) decisions -- these are leading precedents
   - Search for relevant Gerechtshof (Court of Appeal) decisions
   - Search for recent Rechtbank (District Court) decisions showing current application
   - Identify the key standard cases (standaardarresten)

3. **Contextual Research:**
   - Identify relevant parliamentary history (parlementaire geschiedenis) if significant
   - Note relevant policy documents or guidance from regulators

### Step 4: Quality Checks

Before presenting the briefing:
- Verify all ECLI numbers are correctly formatted
- Confirm article references are accurate
- Ensure the analysis is balanced and presents multiple perspectives where relevant
- Check that Dutch legal terminology is used correctly
- Verify that cited legislation is current (not repealed or superseded)
