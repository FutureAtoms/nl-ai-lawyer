# Search Dutch Legislation (Wetgeving)

Search and retrieve Dutch legislation, statutes, and regulations.

## Skill and Tools

Use skill: **dutch-legislation-lookup**

Use MCP tools:
- `legislation_search` -- search for Dutch laws and regulations
- `legislation_fetch` -- retrieve the full text of a specific law or article
- `caselaw_search_rechtspraak` -- to find case law interpreting the legislation

## Workflow

### Step 1: Query Interpretation

- Identify whether the user is searching for:
  - A specific law by name (e.g., "Burgerlijk Wetboek", "Wet werk en zekerheid")
  - A specific article (e.g., "art. 6:162 BW", "artikel 7:669 BW")
  - A legal topic (e.g., "huurrecht", "ontslagrecht", "aansprakelijkheid")
- If the query is in English, translate to the Dutch legal equivalent
- Common law abbreviations:
  - BW = Burgerlijk Wetboek (Civil Code)
  - Sv = Wetboek van Strafvordering (Code of Criminal Procedure)
  - Sr = Wetboek van Strafrecht (Penal Code)
  - Rv = Wetboek van Burgerlijke Rechtsvordering (Code of Civil Procedure)
  - Awb = Algemene wet bestuursrecht (General Administrative Law Act)
  - WWZ = Wet werk en zekerheid (Work and Security Act)
  - WAB = Wet arbeidsmarkt in balans (Balanced Labour Market Act)
  - AVG = Algemene verordening gegevensbescherming (GDPR)
  - UAVG = Uitvoeringswet AVG (GDPR Implementation Act)
  - Wft = Wet op het financieel toezicht (Financial Supervision Act)

### Step 2: Execute Search

Use `legislation_search` with:
- The search query (use Dutch legal terminology)
- Apply `area` filter if provided. Common legal areas:
  - Privaatrecht (private law)
  - Arbeidsrecht (employment law)
  - Ondernemingsrecht (corporate law)
  - Bestuursrecht (administrative law)
  - Strafrecht (criminal law)
  - Belastingrecht (tax law)
  - Huurrecht (tenancy law)
  - Gezondheidsrecht (health law)
  - Milieurecht (environmental law)
  - IE-recht (intellectual property law)

### Step 4: Context and Related Provisions

For the most relevant results:
- Identify related articles within the same section/title
- Note any transitional provisions (overgangsrecht)
- Check for implementing regulations (uitvoeringsbesluiten, ministeri\u00eble regelingen)
- Identify any EU directives or regulations that the Dutch law implements
- Note any pending amendments or recent changes (wetswijzigingen)

### Step 5: Case Law Cross-Reference

Use `caselaw_search_rechtspraak` to find:
- Leading cases (standaardarresten) interpreting the legislation
- Recent applications of the provision
- Present 2-3 most relevant cases with ECLI references
