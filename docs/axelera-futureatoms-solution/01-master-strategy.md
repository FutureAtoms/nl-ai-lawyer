## Why This Structure Is Legally Stronger

### 1) Side-business restrictions are not unlimited under Dutch law

- Your current employment contract Clause 13 requires consent for ancillary activities.
- Dutch law now states that a clause prohibiting or limiting outside work is void unless justified by an objective reason (BW 7:653a).
- Practical implication: Axelera can set objective restrictions (confidentiality, conflict, working-time/safety), but should not impose a blanket ban on all FutureAtoms activity.

### 2) Non-compete must remain proportionate

- Your Clause 10 is broad (worldwide, one year, broad business scope).
- BW 7:653 allows judicial moderation/destruction if unfairly restrictive relative to employer interests.
- Practical implication: negotiate a narrower non-compete tied to direct competitive card-level solutions, not all AI software.

### 3) IP ownership should be split by scope and creation context

- Employer ownership in NL can attach to works created within employment duties (Auteurswet Art. 7; software employer copyright governed by Art. 7 Aw — Art. 45h concerns rental rights, not employer ownership).
- Employee invention rules under ROW Art. 12 are scope-sensitive and can be contractually adjusted.
- Practical implication: define pre-existing FutureAtoms IP and carve it out; transfer/assign only defined Wingman deliverables.

### 4) Penalty clause should be rebalanced

- Your Clause 16 combines high fixed penalties with daily penalties and separate damages claims.
- Employee penalty clauses are constrained (BW 7:650 and 7:651), and courts can moderate disproportionate penalties.
- Practical implication: cap exposure and avoid full cumulation with damages for same breach.

### 5) Immigration/citizenship continuity is usually better under employee path

- IND naturalisation baseline requires 5 years lawful residence and a valid permit with non-temporary purpose (or permanent/long-term EU status) at key moments in process.
- Switching to contractor can create permit-structure uncertainty and timing risk if current basis is highly-skilled-migrant sponsorship.
- Practical implication: keep employee status unless an immigration lawyer confirms a safe permit conversion timeline.
