# Schedule 3: Restricted Competitive Field

**Referenced by:** Proposed Addendum v2, Article 2.3
**Date:** 5 March 2026
**Status:** Draft -- subject to execution of Addendum

---

## Definition of Restricted Competitive Field

Standalone applications whose primary purpose is programming Axelera-branded AI accelerator cards (Metis and successor products) using the Voyager SDK.

---

## Scope Clarification

This restriction applies **only** to products that are:

1. **Standalone applications** -- i.e., marketed or distributed as dedicated, purpose-built tools
2. **Primarily purposed** for programming Axelera-branded hardware -- i.e., the principal function of the application is Axelera card programming
3. **Targeting Axelera products specifically** -- i.e., Metis and successor products using the Voyager SDK

All three elements must be present for a product to fall within the Restricted Competitive Field.

---

## Explicit Exclusions

The following categories of activity are **expressly excluded** from the Restricted Competitive Field and are not restricted by the Addendum:

### 1. General-Purpose Software Tools

Software tools, platforms, IDEs, frameworks, or libraries that are general-purpose in nature and not primarily targeted at Axelera hardware, even if they may incidentally support Axelera hardware alongside other platforms. This includes but is not limited to:

- Vendor-neutral semiconductor design platforms (e.g., ChipOS)
- General-purpose code editors, IDEs, and developer productivity tools
- Workflow automation and orchestration frameworks
- AI/ML development frameworks and platforms

### 2. Non-Axelera Hardware Platforms

Products developed for hardware platforms manufactured by third parties, including but not limited to:

- FPGAs from any manufacturer
- ASICs designed by third parties
- Accelerator cards from other vendors (e.g., NVIDIA, AMD, Intel, Qualcomm)
- Embedded processors, microcontrollers, and SoCs from any manufacturer
- Raspberry Pi and similar development boards

### 3. Semiconductor Design Workflows for Non-Axelera Chips

Tools, platforms, and services related to semiconductor design that do not primarily target Axelera products, including but not limited to:

- RTL verification and simulation tools
- Physical design (floorplanning, placement, CTS, routing) tools
- Design rule checking (DRC/LVS/ERC) tools
- Timing analysis tools
- CPU/processor design tools

### 4. Consulting, Training, and Professional Services

- Technical consulting for any client on any hardware platform
- Training courses, workshops, and educational materials
- Advisory and freelance services unrelated to the Restricted Competitive Field
- Speaking engagements, conference presentations, and publications

### 5. Open-Source Contributions

- Contributions to open-source projects in any field
- Publication of open-source tools, libraries, and frameworks
- Academic research and publications
- Community participation and knowledge sharing

### 6. Other FutureAtoms Business Domains

All FutureAtoms business activities outside semiconductor design, including but not limited to:

- Healthcare (Swaastik)
- Music generation (BevyBeats)
- Professional networking AI (Zaphy)
- Workflow automation (Agentic Control)
- RTL generation (SystemVerilogGPT)
- Wellness (Yuj)
- Mental health (Savitri)
- Computer vision (AdaptiveVision)

## Legal Basis for Narrow Scope

This Restricted Competitive Field is narrowly defined in accordance with:

- **Article 7:653a BW**: restrictions on side activities must be justified by objective reasons and proportionate to the legitimate business interest
- **Counter-Proposal Article D1.2**: the non-competition obligation is a *nevenwerkzaamhedenbeding* (side activities restriction), not a post-employment *concurrentiebeding*, and must be interpreted restrictively
- **Proportionality principle**: the restriction is limited to the specific Wingman use case (Axelera card programming) and does not extend to the broader semiconductor or technology field
